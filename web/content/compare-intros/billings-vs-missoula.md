# Billings vs Missoula: Which Montana City is Right for You?

Billings and Missoula are Montana's two largest cities, but they couldn't feel more different. Billings is the Magic City, a sprawling, commerce-driven hub on the eastern plains with 117,116 people, a strong energy and healthcare economy, and a surprisingly mild climate for Montana. Missoula is the Garden City, a progressive, mountain-ringed university town of 77,757 where the Clark Fork River runs through downtown and the trailheads are practically in the backyard.

## Who Should Move to Billings?

*   Professionals in energy, healthcare, or agriculture who want Montana's strongest job market
*   Families looking for a full-service city with big-box retail, major hospitals, and good schools
*   People who want lower housing costs than Bozeman or Missoula without sacrificing urban amenities
*   Anyone who prefers a warmer, drier climate and doesn't need mountains right out the door

## Who Should Move to Missoula?

*   Young professionals, students, and academics drawn to the University of Montana's energy
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want world-class hiking, skiing, and river access within minutes of downtown
*   People who value a walkable, arts-rich downtown with independent restaurants, breweries, and live music
*   Anyone who wants a progressive, culturally active community in a dramatic mountain setting

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where Billings pulls ahead. The median home price in Billings is around $390,654, compared to $554,389 in Missoula, a difference of over $160,000. Rent is also more manageable in Billings at roughly $1,383 per month, versus $1,486 in Missoula.

The income picture is closer than you might expect. Billings actually edges out Missoula on median household income, $71,855 versus $65,329, which means your dollar stretches noticeably further on the eastern side of the state.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Billings feels like a working city that happens to be in Montana, practical, unpretentious, and built around commerce and community. It has the restaurants, the events, and the infrastructure of a real regional hub, but without the trendy polish of Bozeman or Missoula. Missoula, by contrast, has an unmistakable energy: farmers markets on the river, packed breweries on a Tuesday, and a general sense that everyone is either training for something or just got back from it. If you want Montana with a city pulse, Billings delivers. If you want Montana with a mountain soul, Missoula is hard to beat.

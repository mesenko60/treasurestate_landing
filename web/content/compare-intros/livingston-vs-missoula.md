# Missoula vs Livingston: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Missoula and Livingston are both Montana communities with strong artistic and outdoor identities, but they operate at very different scales. Missoula is the Garden City, a university city of 77,757 with a vibrant downtown, a dense outdoor recreation landscape, and a cultural scene that draws people from across the region. Livingston is the Windiest City in Montana, a small, storied town of 8,040 in the Paradise Valley, where the Yellowstone River flows cold and clear, the Absaroka Range rises to the south, and a long tradition of artists, writers, and fly fishers gives the place a character that is genuinely hard to find elsewhere.

## Who Should Move to Missoula?

*   Students, academics, and professionals connected to the University of Montana
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 65 fishing access sites, 49 trailheads, and 2 wilderness areas within 30 miles
*   People who want a larger city with a full range of amenities, hospitals, major retailers, a regional airport, in a mountain setting
*   Anyone who values a progressive, culturally active community with live music, independent restaurants, and a walkable downtown

## Who Should Move to Livingston?

*   Artists, writers, and creative professionals drawn to Livingston's long-established arts and literary community
*   Fly fishing enthusiasts who want direct access to the Yellowstone River, one of Montana's finest, right in town
*   Remote workers and retirees who want a small, character-rich community close to Yellowstone National Park
*   People who want the feel of a small Montana town with a surprisingly rich cultural life and stunning mountain scenery

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Missoula is the more expensive city, though the gap is narrower than many expect. The median home price in Missoula is around $554,389, compared to $509,477 in Livingston, a difference of about $45,000. Rent, however, runs higher in Livingston at about $1,783 per month versus $1,486 in Missoula, which reflects the town's desirability relative to its limited housing supply.

Median household incomes are nearly identical: $65,329 in Missoula versus $65,187 in Livingston. For a town of 8,000 with a limited local job market, Livingston's housing costs reflect its appeal to remote workers and second-home buyers more than its economic base.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Missoula is a full-service mountain city with a university heartbeat, always busy, always engaged, with a cultural calendar that rivals cities twice its size. Livingston is a small town with an outsized reputation, known for its wind, its writers, its fly fishing, and its proximity to one of the world's great national parks. The historic downtown, the railroad heritage, and the backdrop of the Absarokas give Livingston a sense of place that is genuinely irreplaceable. If you want Montana with full city services and a large outdoor recreation inventory, Missoula is the answer. If you want Montana with soul, history, and the Yellowstone River out your back door, Livingston makes a compelling case.

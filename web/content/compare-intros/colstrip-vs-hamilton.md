# Colstrip vs Hamilton, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Colstrip and Hamilton offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Colstrip is a purpose-built energy town in eastern Montana with 2,055 people, its massive coal-fired power plant, its excellent, company-built community amenities, and quick access to Castle Rock Lake and the surrounding pine hills. Hamilton, with 4,659 residents, is a scenic hub of the Bitterroot Valley known for its historic downtown, its relatively mild climate, and the Bitterroot Mountains and the Bitterroot River.

## Who Should Move to Colstrip?

* Workers in the energy, mining, or industrial sectors looking for high-paying employment.
* Families seeking excellent, well-funded local schools and community parks.
* People looking for very affordable housing combined with high average salaries.
* Those who appreciate a practical, hardworking community with a strong industrial identity.

## Who Should Move to Hamilton?

* Retirees and remote workers drawn to the stunning scenery and milder weather of the 'Banana Belt'.
* Fly fishing enthusiasts who want to live near the renowned waters of the Bitterroot River.
* People seeking a vibrant small-town community with a historic main street, local breweries, and community events.
* Outdoor adventurers who want immediate access to the rugged canyons and trails of the Bitterroot Selway Wilderness.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Colstrip is around $219,986, while in Hamilton it is closer to $516,033, a difference of roughly $296,047, making Colstrip significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Colstrip averages about $632 per month, compared to roughly $958 in Hamilton.

Colstrip's overall cost of living differs from Hamilton's. Colstrip offers higher average salaries compared to Hamilton, with a median household income of $86,000 compared to Hamilton's $52,917.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Colstrip is a unique, industrious town built specifically to support the local energy industry. It boasts surprisingly excellent parks, schools, and recreational facilities for its size, offering a practical, high-income lifestyle for those working in the energy sector. Hamilton offers a highly desirable blend of spectacular mountain scenery and a charming, active community. The pace is relaxed but the town is lively, with a downtown that serves as a gathering place for locals and visitors alike, all set against the towering peaks of the Bitterroot Range.

# Glasgow vs Helena, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Glasgow and Helena offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Glasgow is a historic railroad town on the Hi-Line with 3,224 people, its position as a regional hub for northeastern Montana, its proximity to Fort Peck Dam, and quick access to Fort Peck Lake and the Charles M. Russell National Wildlife Refuge. Helena, with just 33,885 residents, is a historic state capital known for its government sector, its gold rush architecture, and Mount Helena and the Missouri River lakes.

## Who Should Move to Glasgow?

* Workers in agriculture, government, or the railroad industry looking for stable employment in northeastern Montana.
* Anglers and boaters who want to live near Fort Peck Lake, one of the premier walleye fisheries in the country.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, isolated community.
* Hunters and outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the vast, uncrowded public lands of the Missouri River Breaks.

## Who Should Move to Helena?

* Government workers and professionals.
* Families seeking a stable, safe community.
* Mountain bikers and hikers who want trails right out their back door.
* People who appreciate historic architecture and a central location.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Glasgow is around $195,000, while in Helena it is closer to $425,000, a difference of roughly $230,000, making Glasgow significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Glasgow averages about $750 per month, compared to roughly $1,100 in Helena.

Glasgow's overall cost of living differs from Helena's. Glasgow offers lower average salaries compared to Helena, with a median household income of $55,000 compared to Helena's $68,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Glasgow is a resilient, isolated community that serves as the commercial and social center for a massive swath of northeastern Montana. It offers a quiet, slow-paced lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture, the railroad, and the incredible recreational opportunities at nearby Fort Peck Lake. Helena is a stable, professional town driven by state government, with a quiet downtown and immediate access to mountain trails.

# East Helena vs Choteau, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

East Helena and Choteau offer two very different Montana lifestyles. East Helena is a growing residential community in the Helena Valley with 2,264 people, its industrial heritage, its rapid recent growth as a suburb of the capital, and quick access to Canyon Ferry Lake and the Elkhorn Mountains. Choteau, with 1,721 residents, is a scenic gateway town to the Rocky Mountain Front known for its spectacular views of the Front, its rich dinosaur paleontology history, and the Bob Marshall Wilderness and Freezout Lake.

## Who Should Move to East Helena?

* Families looking for a strong local school district and a tight-knit community feel.
* Professionals working in Helena who want a slightly quieter, more residential environment.
* People who appreciate a town with a strong working-class heritage that is actively reinventing itself.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want quick access to Canyon Ferry Lake and the surrounding national forest.

## Who Should Move to Choteau?

* Outdoor enthusiasts who want uncrowded access to the Bob Marshall Wilderness.
* Birders and wildlife watchers drawn to the spectacular migrations at nearby Freezout Lake.
* People seeking a quiet, affordable community with some of the best mountain views in the state.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in East Helena is around $430,196, while in Choteau it is closer to $288,323, a difference of roughly $141,873, making Choteau significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in East Helena averages about $1,020 per month, compared to roughly $713 in Choteau.

East Helena's overall cost of living differs from Choteau's. East Helena offers lower average salaries compared to Choteau, with a median household income of $53,611 compared to Choteau's $53,929.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

East Helena is transitioning from its historic roots as a smelter town into a rapidly growing, family-friendly suburb of Helena. It offers a quieter, slightly more affordable alternative to the capital city while maintaining its own distinct, blue-collar identity. Choteau is a quiet, stunningly located town that serves as the Front Porch of the Rockies. It offers a slow-paced, affordable lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture, paleontology, and the incredible wilderness right in its backyard.

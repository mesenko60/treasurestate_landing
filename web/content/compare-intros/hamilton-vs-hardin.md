# Hamilton vs Hardin: Which Montana Town Is Right for You?

Hamilton and Hardin sit on opposite sides of Montana and offer two very different versions of small-town life. Hamilton, the Banana Belt of Montana in the Bitterroot Valley, is a town of 4,659 people known for its mild climate, the Bitterroot River, and one of the most extensive outdoor recreation networks of any small town in the state. Hardin, Where the Rivers Meet in Big Horn County, is a town of 3,742 people near the confluence of the Bighorn and Little Bighorn rivers, known for the Little Bighorn Battlefield, the Bighorn River's world-class tailwater trout fishery, and the living cultural heritage of the Crow Nation.

## Who Should Move to Hamilton?

- Outdoor enthusiasts who want the Bitterroot National Forest, 47 trailheads, 55 lakes, and 25 fishing access points all within 30 miles
- Remote workers, retirees, or families who want a mild, sunny climate in a beautiful valley setting with solid amenities
- Fly fishers and hikers who want a wide variety of rivers, trails, and backcountry access in one of western Montana's most scenic valleys
- People drawn to a vibrant small-town community with a strong arts and outdoor culture

## Who Should Move to Hardin?

- History enthusiasts drawn to Little Bighorn Battlefield and the rich cultural heritage of the Crow Nation
- Anglers who want the Bighorn River, one of Montana's premier tailwater trout fisheries, close at hand
- People looking for very affordable housing and a low cost of living in a quiet, rural community
- Those who want to experience the unique culture of southeastern Montana, including the annual Crow Fair

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Hamilton is significantly more expensive. The typical home value is $516,033 compared to $204,573 in Hardin, a difference of over $310,000. Rent is also higher in Hamilton at $958 per month versus $726 in Hardin. Hardin's median household income of $60,463 is actually higher than Hamilton's $52,917, making Hardin notably more affordable in real terms. For buyers on a tight budget, Hardin offers some of the most accessible housing in Montana. Hamilton's nearest airport is in Missoula, 56 miles away. Hardin's is in Billings, 54 miles to the west.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

These two towns are separated by the full width of Montana and by almost everything else. Hamilton is a lush, green valley town with a mild climate, a strong outdoor culture, and a growing population of remote workers and retirees. Hardin is a quieter, more traditional eastern Montana community shaped by the Bighorn River, the Little Bighorn Battlefield, and the Crow Nation's presence. Hamilton has 194 recreational sites within 30 miles to Hardin's 20, but Hardin's Bighorn River and its historical significance give it a character that is genuinely distinctive in Montana.

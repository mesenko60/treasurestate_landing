# Laurel vs Hamilton: Which Montana Town is Right for You?

Laurel and Hamilton are both mid-sized Montana communities with similar populations and comparable climates, but they sit in completely different mountain valleys and offer very different lifestyles. Laurel is the City Between the Yellowstone and the Rimrocks, a working-class Yellowstone Valley community of 7,215 in Yellowstone County at 3,307 feet, with Billings Logan International Airport 18 miles away, 60 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 21 fishing access sites, 10 museums, 6 state parks, 5 golf courses, 4 historic sites, 3 nature reserves, 2 disc golf courses, 1 river, and a 1,800-student school district. Hamilton is the Banana Belt of Montana, a Bitterroot Valley community of 4,659 in Ravalli County at 3,573 feet, known for its mild winters and proximity to the Bitterroot National Forest, with the nearest airport in Missoula 56 miles away, 194 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 47 trailheads, 55 lakes, 32 campgrounds, 25 fishing access sites, 5 nature reserves, 3 disc golf courses, 3 historic sites, 1 wilderness area, 1 wildlife refuge, and a 1,800-student school district.

## Who Should Move to Laurel?

*   Working families and professionals who want easy access to Billings, the largest city in Montana, just 18 miles away, with its hospitals, employers, shopping, and airport, while living in a more affordable community
*   Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want 21 fishing access sites on the Yellowstone River, 6 state parks, 10 museums, and 5 golf courses within 30 miles, with the Rimrocks and the Yellowstone Valley as a backdrop
*   Buyers who want an affordable Montana community close to a major city, with a typical home value of $369,826 and rent averaging $1,160 per month
*   Families who want a 1,800-student school district, 4 historic sites, and a genuine working-class Montana community with strong Yellowstone Valley character

## Who Should Move to Hamilton?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 47 trailheads, 55 lakes, 32 campgrounds, 25 fishing access sites, 5 nature reserves, a wilderness area, and a wildlife refuge all within 30 miles in the Bitterroot Valley, one of the most trail-rich corridors in Montana
*   People who want a mild western Montana climate, with January average highs of 40 degrees and annual snowfall of just 19.8 inches, making it one of the most temperate communities in the state
*   Buyers who want to live in the Bitterroot Valley with a typical home value of $516,033 but dramatically lower rent averaging just $958 per month, making it an attractive option for renters
*   Retirees and remote workers who want a quieter Bitterroot Valley community with a 1,800-student school district, 6 museums, and easy access to Missoula's full range of services 56 miles north

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Hamilton is more expensive to buy into than Laurel. The typical home value in Hamilton is $516,033, compared to $369,826 in Laurel, about $146,000 more. Rent tells the opposite story: Hamilton averages $958 per month versus $1,160 in Laurel, making Hamilton the better monthly value for renters.

Median household income is higher in Laurel at $66,382 versus $52,917 in Hamilton. Laurel's higher income reflects its proximity to Billings and its more diverse employment base. Hamilton's lower income reflects the Bitterroot Valley's more rural, service-oriented economy, but its lower rent makes it accessible to residents across a wide range of income levels.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Laurel is a working-class Yellowstone Valley community where the railroad, the refinery, the Rimrocks, and proximity to Billings define daily life. Hamilton is a quieter, more traditional western Montana community where the Bitterroot River, the Selway-Bitterroot Wilderness, and a mild climate define daily life. The outdoor recreation gap is dramatic: Hamilton has 194 sites within 30 miles versus Laurel's 60, including 47 trailheads and 55 lakes that Laurel cannot match. Laurel has 21 fishing access sites, 10 museums, and 6 state parks that reflect its deep Yellowstone Valley roots. If you want an affordable working-class community with easy access to Billings and strong Yellowstone Valley outdoor access, Laurel is a practical and solid choice. If you want a mild, trail-rich Bitterroot Valley community with extraordinary outdoor diversity and a quieter pace of life, Hamilton is one of the most compelling small towns in western Montana.

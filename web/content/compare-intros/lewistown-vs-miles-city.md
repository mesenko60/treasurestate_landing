# Miles City vs Lewistown, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Miles City and Lewistown offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Miles City is a ranching community on the eastern plains with 8,412 people, the livestock industry, rodeo culture, and quick access to the wide-open Yellowstone River country. Lewistown, with just 6,204 residents, is a historic agricultural center known for its charming stone architecture, its position at the geographic center of Montana, and the surrounding island mountain ranges and Spring Creek.

## Who Should Move to Miles City?

* Ranchers, livestock producers, and people whose careers and lifestyle are rooted in Eastern Montana's agricultural economy.
* Those seeking very affordable housing in a community where the typical home costs less than half what it does in many western Montana towns.
* Remote workers or retirees who want quiet, uncrowded living without the cost pressures that come with proximity to major tourist hubs.
* People who appreciate the authentic cowboy heritage of the Yellowstone Valley and the Bucking Horse Sale rodeo tradition.

## Who Should Move to Lewistown?

* Retirees or remote workers who want a quiet, peaceful lifestyle in a classic Montana setting.
* People looking for affordable housing and a lower overall cost of living.
* Those who prefer a tight-knit, traditional small-town community with a strong agricultural heritage.
* History buffs and outdoor enthusiasts who enjoy uncrowded access to the Snowy, Judith, and Moccasin mountains.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Miles City is around $233,769, while in Lewistown it is closer to $268,126, a difference of roughly $34,357, making Miles City significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Miles City averages about $750 per month, compared to roughly $896 in Lewistown.

Miles City's overall cost of living differs from Lewistown's. Miles City offers higher average salaries compared to Lewistown, with a median household income of $60,732 compared to Lewistown's $44,195.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Miles City is quiet and agricultural in character, with a lifestyle shaped by ranching seasons and rodeo. It sits along the Yellowstone River, looking east toward the open plains. Lewistown is quiet, slow-paced, and friendly in that classic Montana way. People wave when they drive by, and life revolves around ranching, community events, and the pristine waters of Big Spring Creek that flow right through the center of town.

# Bigfork vs Great Falls: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Bigfork and Great Falls offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Bigfork is a Village by the Bay, a small community of 5,000 on the eastern shore of Flathead Lake in Flathead County at 2,940 feet elevation, with 176 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 32 lakes, 27 campgrounds, and Glacier National Park nearby. Great Falls is the Electric City, a community of 60,000 in Cascade County at 3,340 feet on the Missouri River, with 45 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 10 museums, 8 fishing access sites, 6 waterfalls, a wildlife refuge, and a community shaped by Malmstrom Air Force Base and the Lewis and Clark National Historic Trail.

## Who Should Move to Bigfork?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 27 campgrounds, 25 trailheads, 28 fishing access sites, 32 lakes, 16 state parks, and Glacier National Park all within 30 miles
*   Affluent buyers and retirees who want a small, scenic lakeside community with a median household income of $82,648 and a quieter pace of life
*   People who want a genuine village atmosphere on Flathead Lake with a summer arts festival, a community theater, and one of the most beautiful natural settings in Montana
*   Anyone who wants western Montana's mountain and lake scenery as their daily backdrop

## Who Should Move to Great Falls?

*   Budget-conscious buyers who want a mid-sized Montana city at a significantly lower price, with a typical home value of $328,554 versus Bigfork's $758,435
*   Military families and veterans connected to Malmstrom Air Force Base, which anchors Great Falls's economy and community identity
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want the Missouri River, 8 fishing access sites, 6 waterfalls, 5 state parks, a wildlife refuge, and the Lewis and Clark National Historic Trail all within 30 miles
*   Families who want a mid-sized Montana city with a 10,000-student school district, a regional airport, and a full range of services at a much lower cost of living

## Cost of Living Breakdown

The gap between these two towns is very large. The typical home value in Bigfork is around $758,435, compared to $328,554 in Great Falls, more than double the price. Rent is lower in Bigfork: Bigfork averages about $1,103 per month, while Great Falls comes in at roughly $1,340.

The median household income in Bigfork is $82,648, compared to $63,934 in Great Falls. Bigfork residents earn considerably more on average, but the home price gap is so large that Great Falls offers a dramatically more accessible path to homeownership.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Bigfork is a small lakeside village where the arts, outdoor recreation, and Flathead Lake define daily life. Great Falls is a mid-sized Montana city with a strong military presence, a Lewis and Clark heritage, and a community built around the Missouri River and the surrounding high plains. If you want a quiet, scenic village on one of Montana's most beautiful lakes and are prepared to pay for it, Bigfork is exceptional. If you want a mid-sized Montana city with a lower cost of living, a strong community identity, and easy access to the Missouri River's outdoor recreation, Great Falls is a solid and underappreciated choice.

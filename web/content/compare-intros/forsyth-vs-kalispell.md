# Forsyth vs Kalispell, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Forsyth and Kalispell offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Forsyth is a historic railroad town on the Yellowstone River with 1,630 people, its beautiful, historic county courthouse, its position as a transportation and agricultural hub, and quick access to the Yellowstone River and the eastern plains. Kalispell, with 26,110 residents, is a commercial center of the Flathead Valley known for its retail and medical facilities, its historic downtown, and Flathead Lake and Glacier National Park.

## Who Should Move to Forsyth?

* Workers in agriculture, transportation, or local services looking for a stable community.
* People seeking very affordable housing and a low overall cost of living.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle on the eastern plains.
* Hunters and anglers who want uncrowded access to the Yellowstone River.

## Who Should Move to Kalispell?

* Families who want access to Glacier National Park without the resort town prices.
* Professionals working in healthcare or retail.
* People who want a mix of historic charm and modern conveniences.
* Outdoor lovers who want to be near Flathead Lake.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Forsyth is around $208,690, while in Kalispell it is closer to $550,000, a difference of roughly $341,310, making Forsyth significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Forsyth averages about $743 per month, compared to roughly $1,400 in Kalispell.

Forsyth's overall cost of living differs from Kalispell's. Forsyth offers lower average salaries compared to Kalispell, with a median household income of $58,375 compared to Kalispell's $60,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Forsyth is a quiet, practical community in eastern Montana, defined by the railroad, agriculture, and the Yellowstone River. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle with a strong sense of local history. Kalispell is the practical, bustling heart of the Flathead Valley, balancing its role as a commercial hub with incredible access to natural wonders.

# Billings vs Butte: Which Montana City is Right for You?

Billings and Butte are both proud, working Montana cities with deep histories, but they occupy very different corners of the state, literally and figuratively. Billings is the Magic City, Montana's largest city at 117,116 people, a commerce-driven hub on the eastern plains with a mild climate and a diversified modern economy. Butte is the Richest Hill on Earth, a gritty, storied mining city of 34,494 perched at 5,741 feet in the mountains of southwestern Montana, with one of the most colorful and complex histories of any city in the American West.

## Who Should Move to Billings?

*   Professionals in healthcare, energy, or agriculture who want Montana's broadest job market
*   Families seeking a full-service city with strong schools, major medical centers, and lower elevation winters
*   People who want affordable housing relative to Bozeman or Missoula, with full urban amenities
*   Anyone who prefers a warmer, drier climate and easy access to Yellowstone Country

## Who Should Move to Butte?

*   History buffs and architecture lovers drawn to Butte's extraordinary Victorian-era Uptown district
*   People seeking genuinely affordable housing in a Montana city with real character and community pride
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want access to hot springs, mountain lakes, and wilderness within 30 miles
*   Remote workers and retirees who want a tight-knit, authentic Montana community without resort-town prices

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Butte is one of Montana's most affordable cities. The median home price in Butte is around $274,163, roughly $116,000 less than Billings' $390,654. Rent is comparable, with Butte averaging about $1,318 per month versus $1,383 in Billings.

The trade-off is income. Billings' median household income of $71,855 is notably higher than Butte's $57,633, reflecting the larger city's more diverse and higher-paying job market. For remote workers or retirees, though, Butte's affordability is a genuine advantage.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Billings feels like a city on the move, modern, practical, and always expanding. Butte feels like a city that has already lived several lifetimes and is comfortable with that. The Uptown historic district is unlike anything else in Montana, with ornate copper-boom-era architecture that tells the story of a place that was once one of the most important industrial cities in the country. Billings is where you go to build something new; Butte is where you go to be part of something old and irreplaceable.

# Laurel vs Helena: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Laurel and Helena are both central Montana communities with similar climates, but they differ considerably in size, history, and what they offer day to day. Laurel is the City Between the Yellowstone and the Rimrocks, a quiet, working-class community of 7,215 in Yellowstone County with affordable housing, 21 fishing access sites within 30 miles, and easy access to Billings. Helena is the Queen City of the Rockies, Montana's state capital at 33,100 people, with 10 historic sites, 3 hot springs, 28 campgrounds, and 107 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, and a civic identity shaped by gold rush history and state government.

## Who Should Move to Laurel?

*   Budget-conscious families and buyers who want Yellowstone County living with a typical home value around $369,826, significantly below Helena's $466,393
*   People who want a quiet, small-town community with Billings' full-service amenities just 18 miles away
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 21 fishing access sites and 6 state parks along the Yellowstone River corridor
*   Anyone who wants a low-key, practical Montana lifestyle without the costs of a state capital city

## Who Should Move to Helena?

*   State and federal government employees, lobbyists, and professionals in Montana's public sector
*   History enthusiasts drawn to Helena's gold rush heritage, the Last Chance Gulch walking mall, and 10 historic sites within 30 miles
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 28 campgrounds, 11 trailheads, 3 hot springs, and 10 lakes within 30 miles
*   Families who want a mid-sized Montana city with a 7,800-student school district and a broad range of services

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Laurel is significantly more affordable on both housing and rent. The typical home value in Laurel is around $369,826, compared to $466,393 in Helena, a gap of nearly $97,000. Rent follows the same pattern: Laurel averages about $1,160 per month, while Helena comes in at roughly $1,489, a difference of over $300 per month.

The income figures are similar. The median household income in Laurel is $66,382, compared to $69,341 in Helena. Helena residents earn slightly more, driven in part by government employment, but the cost premium is real and meaningful for buyers and renters alike.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Laurel is a practical, quiet river town where the Yellowstone is close and the pace is slow. Helena is a city with a story to tell, from the gold rush that built its ornate Victorian neighborhoods to the state capitol dome that anchors its skyline, and a downtown that has been revitalized around the historic Last Chance Gulch pedestrian mall. If you want affordable, low-key Montana living near Billings, Laurel is a smart choice. If you want a mid-sized Montana city with genuine historical character, a strong outdoor recreation footprint, and a civic energy that comes with being the state capital, Helena is worth the premium.

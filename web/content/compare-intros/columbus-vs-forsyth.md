# Columbus vs Forsyth, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Columbus and Forsyth offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Columbus is a scenic river town in the Stillwater Valley with 2,015 people, its position at the confluence of the Yellowstone and Stillwater rivers, its proximity to the Stillwater Mine, and quick access to the Absaroka-Beartooth Wilderness. Forsyth, with 1,630 residents, is a historic railroad town on the Yellowstone River known for its beautiful, historic county courthouse, its position as a transportation and agricultural hub, and the Yellowstone River and the eastern plains.

## Who Should Move to Columbus?

* Workers in the mining or industrial sectors looking for steady, high-paying employment.
* Anglers and boaters who want immediate access to two of Montana's premier rivers.
* People seeking a scenic, small-town lifestyle with easy highway access to the amenities of Billings.
* Outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the rugged beauty of the Absaroka-Beartooth Wilderness.

## Who Should Move to Forsyth?

* Workers in agriculture, transportation, or local services looking for a stable community.
* People seeking very affordable housing and a low overall cost of living.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle on the eastern plains.
* Hunters and anglers who want uncrowded access to the Yellowstone River.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Columbus is around $428,574, while in Forsyth it is closer to $208,690, a difference of roughly $219,884, making Forsyth significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Columbus averages about $926 per month, compared to roughly $743 in Forsyth.

Columbus's overall cost of living differs from Forsyth's. Columbus offers higher average salaries compared to Forsyth, with a median household income of $62,500 compared to Forsyth's $58,375.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Columbus is a beautiful, thriving town that balances a strong industrial economy driven by the nearby palladium mine with spectacular outdoor recreation. It offers a relaxed, scenic lifestyle with excellent access to both the mountains and the city of Billings. Forsyth is a quiet, practical community in eastern Montana, defined by the railroad, agriculture, and the Yellowstone River. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle with a strong sense of local history.

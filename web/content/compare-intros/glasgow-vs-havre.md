# Havre vs Glasgow, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Havre and Glasgow offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Havre is a historic railroad and agricultural hub on the Hi-Line with 9,362 people, its deep roots in the railroad industry, the Havre Beneath the Streets underground tour, and quick access to the nearby Bear Paw Mountains and Fresno Reservoir. Glasgow, with 3,224 residents, is a historic railroad town on the Hi-Line known for its position as a regional hub for northeastern Montana, its proximity to Fort Peck Dam, and Fort Peck Lake and the Charles M. Russell National Wildlife Refuge.

## Who Should Move to Havre?

* Workers in agriculture, education, or the railroad industry looking for steady employment.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a community with all essential amenities.
* Those who appreciate the stark beauty of the northern plains and the close-knit feel of a Hi-Line town.
* History buffs interested in the unique underground history and nearby frontier battlefields.

## Who Should Move to Glasgow?

* Workers in agriculture, government, or the railroad industry looking for stable employment in northeastern Montana.
* Anglers and boaters who want to live near Fort Peck Lake, one of the premier walleye fisheries in the country.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, isolated community.
* Hunters and outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the vast, uncrowded public lands of the Missouri River Breaks.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Havre is around $224,252, while in Glasgow it is closer to $195,000, a difference of roughly $29,252, making Glasgow significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Havre averages about $925 per month, compared to roughly $750 in Glasgow.

Havre's overall cost of living differs from Glasgow's. Havre offers higher average salaries compared to Glasgow, with a median household income of $58,000 compared to Glasgow's $55,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Havre is a resilient, hardworking community that serves as the commercial center for a vast stretch of northern Montana. It offers a practical, unpretentious lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture and the railroad. Glasgow is a resilient, isolated community that serves as the commercial and social center for a massive swath of northeastern Montana. It offers a quiet, slow-paced lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture, the railroad, and the incredible recreational opportunities at nearby Fort Peck Lake.

# Belgrade vs Billings: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Belgrade and Billings represent two very different entry points into Montana life. Belgrade is the Hub of the Gallatin Valley, a fast-growing suburb of 12,509 on the western edge of Bozeman's orbit, where new neighborhoods are going up fast, Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport is five minutes away, and 104 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles put world-class fishing, hiking, and skiing practically at your doorstep. Billings is the Magic City, Montana's largest city at 117,116 people, a sprawling commercial and healthcare hub on the eastern plains where the economy is diverse, the climate is mild, and the lifestyle is more city than mountain town.

## Who Should Move to Belgrade?

*   Families and remote workers who want Gallatin Valley access at a lower price than Bozeman proper, with strong schools and a fast-growing community
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want a ski area, 21 fishing access sites, and 16 trailheads all within 30 miles
*   People who want quick airport access without paying Bozeman's premium home prices
*   Anyone drawn to the energy of a booming small city that still feels like a neighborhood

## Who Should Move to Billings?

*   Professionals in healthcare, energy, agriculture, or retail who want Montana's strongest and most diverse job market
*   Families who want a full-service city with major hospitals, big-box retail, and 16,000-student school district
*   People who prefer a warmer, drier climate with mild winters by Montana standards
*   Anyone who wants urban amenities and a lower cost of living than western Montana's resort-adjacent towns

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing tells the biggest part of the story here. The typical home value in Belgrade is around $544,626, compared to $390,654 in Billings, making Billings meaningfully more affordable despite being the larger city. Rent follows the same pattern: Belgrade averages about $2,206 per month, while Billings comes in at roughly $1,383.

The income gap helps explain Belgrade's higher prices. The median household income in Belgrade is $88,896, well above Billings at $71,855, reflecting the Gallatin Valley's tech and professional economy. But in Billings, your dollar simply stretches further on everyday expenses.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Belgrade feels like a town in transition, growing fast and building out, with a practical, family-oriented character that sits comfortably between Bozeman's polish and small-town Montana's simplicity. Billings, by contrast, is a fully formed city with the restaurants, events, and infrastructure of a regional hub, but without the trendy edge of the western corridor. If you want the Gallatin Valley lifestyle with room to breathe, Belgrade is a smart landing spot. If you want Montana's most complete city experience at a lower price, Billings makes a compelling case.

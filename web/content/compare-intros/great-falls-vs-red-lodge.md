# Red Lodge vs Great Falls, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Red Lodge and Great Falls offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Red Lodge is a historic mountain town and gateway to Yellowstone with 2,336 people, its vibrant, historic downtown, its position at the base of the Beartooth Highway, and quick access to Red Lodge Mountain and the Beartooth Mountains. Great Falls, with just 60,403 residents, is a historic river city known for its military base, its rich Lewis and Clark history, and the Missouri River and Giant Springs.

## Who Should Move to Red Lodge?

* Outdoor enthusiasts who want immediate access to skiing, hiking, and the Beartooth Wilderness.
* People seeking a lively, historic mountain town with a strong sense of community.
* Retirees and remote workers drawn to the spectacular scenery and small-town charm.
* Those who want to live in a gateway community to Yellowstone National Park.

## Who Should Move to Great Falls?

* Military families stationed at Malmstrom AFB.
* People seeking affordable urban living in Montana.
* History buffs interested in the Lewis and Clark expedition.
* Those who enjoy river recreation right in town.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Red Lodge is around $550,000, while in Great Falls it is closer to $285,000, a difference of roughly $265,000, making Great Falls significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Red Lodge averages about $1,200 per month, compared to roughly $950 in Great Falls.

Red Lodge's overall cost of living differs from Great Falls's. Red Lodge offers higher average salaries compared to Great Falls, with a median household income of $58,000 compared to Great Falls's $55,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Red Lodge is a vibrant, historic mountain town that balances its role as a tourist destination with a strong, year-round local community. It offers spectacular access to the Beartooth Mountains and a lively downtown filled with shops, restaurants, and local events. Great Falls is a grounded, affordable city with a strong military presence and a deep connection to the Missouri River.

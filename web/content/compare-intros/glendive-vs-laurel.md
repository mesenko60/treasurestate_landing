# Laurel vs Glendive: Which Montana Town is Right for You?

Laurel and Glendive are both mid-sized Montana communities in the central and eastern parts of the state, but they serve very different residents and offer different access to services and outdoor recreation. Laurel is the City Between the Yellowstone and the Rimrocks, a working-class Yellowstone Valley community of 7,215 in Yellowstone County at 3,307 feet, with Billings Logan International Airport 18 miles away, 60 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 21 fishing access sites, 10 museums, 6 state parks, 5 golf courses, 4 historic sites, 3 nature reserves, 2 disc golf courses, 1 river, and a 1,800-student school district. Glendive is the Paddlefish Capital of the World, a small eastern Montana oil and agricultural community of 4,873 in Dawson County at 2,083 feet on the Yellowstone River, with Glendive Airport 6 miles away, 30 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 7 viewpoints, 6 museums, 4 fishing access sites, 3 campgrounds, 3 lakes, 2 state parks, 1 disc golf course, 1 golf course, 1 historic site, 1 nature reserve, and a 1,000-student school district.

## Who Should Move to Laurel?

*   Working families and professionals who want easy access to Billings, the largest city in Montana, just 18 miles away, with its hospitals, employers, shopping, and airport, while living in a more affordable community
*   Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want 21 fishing access sites on the Yellowstone River, 6 state parks, 10 museums, and 5 golf courses within 30 miles, with the Rimrocks and the Yellowstone Valley as a backdrop
*   Buyers who want an affordable Montana community close to a major city, with a typical home value of $369,826 and rent averaging $1,160 per month
*   Families who want a 1,800-student school district, 4 historic sites, and a genuine working-class Montana community with strong Yellowstone Valley character

## Who Should Move to Glendive?

*   Paddlefish anglers and outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the Yellowstone River's unique paddlefish snagging season, one of the most unusual and celebrated fishing traditions in Montana, along with 4 fishing access sites and Makoshika State Park within 30 miles
*   Fossil hunters and geology enthusiasts who want access to Makoshika State Park, Montana's largest state park, known for its dramatic badlands formations and significant dinosaur fossil discoveries including Triceratops and Tyrannosaurus rex
*   Budget-conscious buyers who want one of the most affordable housing markets in Montana, with a typical home value of $185,447 and rent averaging just $832 per month
*   Oil and gas industry workers, agricultural workers, and anyone who wants a quiet, affordable eastern Montana community with its own small airport 6 miles away and a 1,000-student school district

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Laurel is significantly more expensive than Glendive. The typical home value in Laurel is $369,826, compared to $185,447 in Glendive, exactly double. Rent is also higher in Laurel at $1,160 per month versus $832 in Glendive.

Median household income is slightly higher in Glendive at $71,063 versus $66,382 in Laurel, reflecting the oil and gas industry wages that support Glendive's economy. Glendive offers some of the most affordable housing of any Montana community its size, making homeownership accessible to buyers on a modest budget.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Laurel is a working-class Yellowstone Valley community where the railroad, the refinery, the Rimrocks, and proximity to Billings define daily life. Glendive is a quiet eastern Montana river town where the Yellowstone River, Makoshika State Park's badlands, the paddlefish season, and the oil patch define community identity. Laurel has 21 fishing access sites, 10 museums, and 6 state parks within 30 miles that reflect its position in the heart of the Yellowstone Valley. Glendive has 7 viewpoints and Makoshika State Park, which offers some of the most dramatic and otherworldly landscapes in Montana. If you want a working-class community with easy access to Billings and strong Yellowstone Valley outdoor access, Laurel is a solid and practical choice. If you want an affordable eastern Montana river town with unique fossil country, badlands scenery, and a genuine small-town character, Glendive is one of the most distinctive communities in the state.

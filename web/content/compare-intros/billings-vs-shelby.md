# Shelby vs Billings, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Shelby and Billings offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Shelby is a historic railroad and agricultural town with 3,169 people, its position on the Hi-Line, its famous 1923 Dempsey-Gibbons heavyweight title fight, and quick access to the Sweet Grass Hills and Lake Elwell. Billings, with just 117,445 residents, is a Montana's largest city known for its major medical centers, its sprawling retail sector, and the Rimrocks and Yellowstone River.

## Who Should Move to Shelby?

* Workers in the agriculture, railroad, or energy sectors looking for steady employment on the Hi-Line.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, rural setting.
* Those who appreciate the stark beauty of the northern plains and the close-knit community of a small agricultural town.
* History buffs interested in the unique legacy of the 1923 heavyweight championship fight.

## Who Should Move to Billings?

* Professionals seeking corporate or medical careers.
* Families wanting large school districts and diverse activities.
* People who prefer urban conveniences over small-town quiet.
* Those who want easy access to a major regional airport.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Shelby is around $185,000, while in Billings it is closer to $375,000, a difference of roughly $190,000, making Shelby significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Shelby averages about $700 per month, compared to roughly $1,200 in Billings.

Shelby's overall cost of living differs from Billings's. Shelby offers lower average salaries compared to Billings, with a median household income of $58,000 compared to Billings's $65,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Shelby is a resilient, hardworking town on the northern plains, where life is shaped by the railroad, agriculture, and the vast open spaces of the Hi-Line. It offers a quiet, practical lifestyle far removed from the state's major urban centers. Billings is a bustling regional hub with a true city feel, offering extensive amenities and a fast-paced environment.

# Manhattan vs Glasgow, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Manhattan and Glasgow offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Manhattan is a rapidly growing agricultural town in the Gallatin Valley with 2,288 people, its historic role as the Seed Potato Capital of the World, its booming residential growth, and quick access to the Gallatin River and the Bridger Mountains. Glasgow, with 3,224 residents, is a historic railroad town on the Hi-Line known for its position as a regional hub for northeastern Montana, its proximity to Fort Peck Dam, and Fort Peck Lake and the Charles M. Russell National Wildlife Refuge.

## Who Should Move to Manhattan?

* Professionals working in Bozeman who want a smaller-town feel and are willing to commute.
* Families looking for excellent local schools and a safe, tight-knit community.
* People who appreciate a town that still honors its agricultural heritage despite rapid modernization.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want immediate access to the world-class fishing of the Gallatin River.

## Who Should Move to Glasgow?

* Workers in agriculture, government, or the railroad industry looking for stable employment in northeastern Montana.
* Anglers and boaters who want to live near Fort Peck Lake, one of the premier walleye fisheries in the country.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, isolated community.
* Hunters and outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the vast, uncrowded public lands of the Missouri River Breaks.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Manhattan is around $652,619, while in Glasgow it is closer to $195,000, a difference of roughly $457,619, making Glasgow significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Manhattan averages about $1,700 per month, compared to roughly $750 in Glasgow.

Manhattan's overall cost of living differs from Glasgow's. Manhattan offers higher average salaries compared to Glasgow, with a median household income of $69,671 compared to Glasgow's $55,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Manhattan blends its deep agricultural roots with its new reality as a highly desirable, expensive suburb of Bozeman. It maintains a charming, historic downtown and a strong community identity, even as it experiences significant growth and rising costs. Glasgow is a resilient, isolated community that serves as the commercial and social center for a massive swath of northeastern Montana. It offers a quiet, slow-paced lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture, the railroad, and the incredible recreational opportunities at nearby Fort Peck Lake.

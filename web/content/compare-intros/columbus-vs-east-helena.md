# East Helena vs Columbus, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

East Helena and Columbus offer two very different Montana lifestyles. East Helena is a growing residential community in the Helena Valley with 2,264 people, its industrial heritage, its rapid recent growth as a suburb of the capital, and quick access to Canyon Ferry Lake and the Elkhorn Mountains. Columbus, with 2,015 residents, is a scenic river town in the Stillwater Valley known for its position at the confluence of the Yellowstone and Stillwater rivers, its proximity to the Stillwater Mine, and the Absaroka-Beartooth Wilderness.

## Who Should Move to East Helena?

* Families looking for a strong local school district and a tight-knit community feel.
* Professionals working in Helena who want a slightly quieter, more residential environment.
* People who appreciate a town with a strong working-class heritage that is actively reinventing itself.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want quick access to Canyon Ferry Lake and the surrounding national forest.

## Who Should Move to Columbus?

* Workers in the mining or industrial sectors looking for steady, high-paying employment.
* Anglers and boaters who want immediate access to two of Montana's premier rivers.
* People seeking a scenic, small-town lifestyle with easy highway access to the amenities of Billings.
* Outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the rugged beauty of the Absaroka-Beartooth Wilderness.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in East Helena is around $430,196, while in Columbus it is closer to $428,574, a difference of roughly $1,622, making Columbus significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in East Helena averages about $1,020 per month, compared to roughly $926 in Columbus.

East Helena's overall cost of living differs from Columbus's. East Helena offers lower average salaries compared to Columbus, with a median household income of $53,611 compared to Columbus's $62,500.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

East Helena is transitioning from its historic roots as a smelter town into a rapidly growing, family-friendly suburb of Helena. It offers a quieter, slightly more affordable alternative to the capital city while maintaining its own distinct, blue-collar identity. Columbus is a beautiful, thriving town that balances a strong industrial economy driven by the nearby palladium mine with spectacular outdoor recreation. It offers a relaxed, scenic lifestyle with excellent access to both the mountains and the city of Billings.

# Dillon vs Bozeman: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Dillon and Bozeman are both western Montana communities at high elevation, but they differ enormously in size, cost, and character. Dillon is Montana's Hometown, a small Beaverhead County community of 3,880 at 5,095 feet, known for its quiet ranching character, the Beaverhead River, 24 fishing access sites, 16 campgrounds, 5 state parks, 2 hot springs, and the University of Montana Western. Bozeman is the Gallatin Gateway, one of Montana's fastest-growing cities with 58,000 residents in Gallatin County at 4,826 feet, with 139 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 32 fishing access sites, 16 trailheads, 16 waterfalls, 16 lakes, 13 campgrounds, 12 museums, 9 golf courses, 7 boat launches, 2 hot springs, a national forest, a ski area, and Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport 10 miles away.

## Who Should Move to Dillon?

*   Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want 24 fishing access sites, 16 campgrounds, 5 state parks, 2 hot springs, 8 lakes, and the Beaverhead River all within 30 miles
*   Remote workers, retirees, and families who want a genuinely quiet, small-town Montana lifestyle at a dramatically lower cost than Bozeman, with a typical home value of $375,585 and rent averaging just $821 per month
*   People who want a small university town with the University of Montana Western providing community events, sports, and cultural programming
*   Anyone drawn to southwestern Montana's wide-open spaces, ranching culture, and the Big Hole Valley just to the west

## Who Should Move to Bozeman?

*   Young professionals, students, and families who want one of Montana's most dynamic and fastest-growing cities, with Montana State University, a thriving tech sector, a lively downtown, and a strong job market with a median household income of $79,903
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 32 fishing access sites, 16 trailheads, 16 waterfalls, 16 lakes, 13 campgrounds, 2 hot springs, a national forest, and a ski area all within 30 miles, plus Yellowstone National Park about 90 miles south
*   Families who want an 8,500-student school district, Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport 10 miles away, and a community with the full range of services of a growing mid-sized city
*   Anyone who wants Montana's most cosmopolitan small city, with excellent restaurants, a strong arts scene, and a community that attracts people from across the country

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Bozeman is dramatically more expensive than Dillon. The typical home value in Dillon is around $375,585, compared to $715,781 in Bozeman, nearly double. Rent is dramatically higher in Bozeman: Dillon averages about $821 per month, while Bozeman comes in at roughly $2,113, more than two and a half times higher.

Median household income is considerably higher in Bozeman at $79,903 versus $51,458 in Dillon. Even with higher incomes, Bozeman's housing costs are extraordinary. Dillon is dramatically more affordable for both buyers and renters.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Dillon is a small southwestern Montana town where the Beaverhead River, ranching culture, and the University of Montana Western define community life. Bozeman is a rapidly growing university city where Montana State University, a booming tech economy, and a world-class outdoor recreation culture define daily life. Bozeman has a cosmopolitan energy and a density of activity that Dillon does not, with a thriving restaurant scene, a strong arts community, and constant growth. If you want a quiet, affordable southwestern Montana lifestyle with excellent fishing and outdoor access, Dillon is one of Montana's most underrated small towns. If you want Montana's fastest-growing and most dynamic city, Bozeman is one of the most desirable places to live in the American West.

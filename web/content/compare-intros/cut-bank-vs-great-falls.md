# Cut Bank vs Great Falls, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Cut Bank and Great Falls offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Cut Bank is a windy agricultural town on the Hi-Line with 3,056 people, its proximity to the Blackfeet Indian Reservation, its agricultural and energy economy, and quick access to the eastern edge of Glacier National Park. Great Falls, with just 60,403 residents, is a historic river city known for its military base, its rich Lewis and Clark history, and the Missouri River and Giant Springs.

## Who Should Move to Cut Bank?

* Workers in agriculture, energy, or government looking for affordable living on the Hi-Line.
* People seeking a quiet, resilient community with a low cost of living.
* Those who want to live near Glacier National Park without paying resort-town prices.
* Individuals who appreciate the stark, windy beauty of the northern plains.

## Who Should Move to Great Falls?

* Military families stationed at Malmstrom AFB.
* People seeking affordable urban living in Montana.
* History buffs interested in the Lewis and Clark expedition.
* Those who enjoy river recreation right in town.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Cut Bank is around $175,000, while in Great Falls it is closer to $285,000, a difference of roughly $110,000, making Cut Bank significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Cut Bank averages about $650 per month, compared to roughly $950 in Great Falls.

Cut Bank's overall cost of living differs from Great Falls's. Cut Bank offers lower average salaries compared to Great Falls, with a median household income of $52,000 compared to Great Falls's $55,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Cut Bank is a tough, practical town known for its wind and its hardworking residents. It serves as a gateway between the vast agricultural plains of the Hi-Line and the dramatic peaks of the Rocky Mountain Front, offering a quiet, affordable lifestyle. Great Falls is a grounded, affordable city with a strong military presence and a deep connection to the Missouri River.

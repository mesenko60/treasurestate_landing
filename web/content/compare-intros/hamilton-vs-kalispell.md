# Hamilton vs Kalispell: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Hamilton and Kalispell are both western Montana communities in river valleys surrounded by mountains, but they sit in different corners of the state and offer distinct lifestyles. Hamilton is the Banana Belt of Montana, a small Bitterroot Valley community of 4,659 in Ravalli County at 3,573 feet, known for its mild winters, 194 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 55 lakes, 47 trailheads, 32 campgrounds, 25 fishing access sites, and the Bitterroot National Forest at its doorstep. Kalispell is the Gateway to Glacier Country, a community of 29,886 in Flathead County at 2,959 feet in the Flathead Valley, with 189 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 28 fishing access sites, 28 campgrounds, 27 lakes, 26 trailheads, 19 ski areas, 15 state parks, 10 golf courses, a national park, and Glacier Park International Airport 10 miles away.

## Who Should Move to Hamilton?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 47 trailheads, 55 lakes, 32 campgrounds, 25 fishing access sites, a hot spring, a national forest, and a wilderness area all within 30 miles
*   People who want a small, quiet Bitterroot Valley community with a genuinely mild winter climate, where January average highs reach 40 degrees, compared to Kalispell's 34 degrees
*   Remote workers, retirees, and families who want a peaceful western Montana lifestyle at dramatically lower monthly costs than Kalispell, with Missoula's full range of services 56 miles north
*   Anyone drawn to the Bitterroot Valley's combination of mountain scenery, river access, and the laid-back character of a small agricultural community

## Who Should Move to Kalispell?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 19 ski areas, 28 fishing access sites, 28 campgrounds, 26 trailheads, 27 lakes, 15 state parks, 4 boat launches, and Glacier National Park all within 30 miles
*   Families and professionals who want a mid-sized Flathead Valley community with a 4,200-student school district, Glacier Park International Airport 10 miles away, and Whitefish and Glacier National Park as regular weekend destinations
*   People who want a growing western Montana city with a strong outdoor recreation economy, a lively downtown, and easy access to Flathead Lake and Glacier National Park
*   Anyone who wants to be within an hour of both Glacier National Park and Whitefish Mountain Resort

## Cost of Living Breakdown

These two towns are surprisingly close in home prices. The typical home value in Hamilton is around $516,033, compared to $541,638 in Kalispell, only about 5% more. Rent is dramatically lower in Hamilton: Hamilton averages about $958 per month, while Kalispell comes in at roughly $2,086, more than double.

Median household income is higher in Kalispell at $61,590 versus $52,917 in Hamilton. Hamilton's dramatically lower rent makes it the more affordable monthly lifestyle despite similar home prices.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Hamilton is a small Bitterroot Valley town where the river, the mountains, and the mild climate define daily life. Kalispell is a mid-sized Flathead Valley city where Glacier National Park, Flathead Lake, and a booming outdoor recreation economy define community life. Both towns have exceptional outdoor access, but Kalispell's proximity to Glacier National Park and its 19 ski areas within 30 miles give it a different outdoor character. If you want the Bitterroot Valley's mild climate, quiet, and outdoor access at a dramatically lower monthly cost, Hamilton is a compelling choice. If you want the Flathead Valley's Glacier country access, a larger community, and more services, Kalispell is one of Montana's fastest-growing and most desirable places to live.

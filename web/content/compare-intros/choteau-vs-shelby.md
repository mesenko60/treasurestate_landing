# Choteau vs Shelby, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Choteau and Shelby offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Choteau is a scenic gateway town to the Rocky Mountain Front with 1,721 people, its spectacular views of the Front, its rich dinosaur paleontology history, and quick access to the Bob Marshall Wilderness and Freezout Lake. Shelby, with 3,169 residents, is a historic railroad and agricultural town known for its position on the Hi-Line, its famous 1923 Dempsey-Gibbons heavyweight title fight, and the Sweet Grass Hills and Lake Elwell.

## Who Should Move to Choteau?

* Outdoor enthusiasts who want uncrowded access to the Bob Marshall Wilderness.
* Birders and wildlife watchers drawn to the spectacular migrations at nearby Freezout Lake.
* People seeking a quiet, affordable community with some of the best mountain views in the state.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle.

## Who Should Move to Shelby?

* Workers in the agriculture, railroad, or energy sectors looking for steady employment on the Hi-Line.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, rural setting.
* Those who appreciate the stark beauty of the northern plains and the close-knit community of a small agricultural town.
* History buffs interested in the unique legacy of the 1923 heavyweight championship fight.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Choteau is around $288,323, while in Shelby it is closer to $185,000, a difference of roughly $103,323, making Shelby significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Choteau averages about $713 per month, compared to roughly $700 in Shelby.

Choteau's overall cost of living differs from Shelby's. Choteau offers lower average salaries compared to Shelby, with a median household income of $53,929 compared to Shelby's $58,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Choteau is a quiet, stunningly located town that serves as the Front Porch of the Rockies. It offers a slow-paced, affordable lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture, paleontology, and the incredible wilderness right in its backyard. Shelby is a resilient, hardworking town on the northern plains, where life is shaped by the railroad, agriculture, and the vast open spaces of the Hi-Line. It offers a quiet, practical lifestyle far removed from the state's major urban centers.

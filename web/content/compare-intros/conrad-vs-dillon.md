# Conrad vs Dillon, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Conrad and Dillon offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Conrad is a quiet agricultural town on the northern plains with 2,318 people, its position as the seat of Pondera County, its strong farming and ranching economy, and quick access to the Rocky Mountain Front and Lake Frances. Dillon, with 3,880 residents, is a historic ranching and college town known for the University of Montana Western, its deep agricultural roots, and the Beaverhead River and surrounding Pioneer Mountains.

## Who Should Move to Conrad?

* Workers in agriculture or local services looking for a quiet, stable community.
* People seeking very affordable housing far removed from the state's busy tourist hubs.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle.
* Hunters and anglers who want uncrowded access to the plains and the nearby Rocky Mountain Front.

## Who Should Move to Dillon?

* Students, faculty, and staff associated with the University of Montana Western.
* Ranchers and agricultural workers who want to live in a community that still deeply values its livestock heritage.
* Anglers seeking world-class trout fishing on the Beaverhead and Big Hole rivers.
* People looking for a genuine, unpretentious western town with a stable economy and access to vast public lands.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Conrad is around $244,817, while in Dillon it is closer to $375,585, a difference of roughly $130,768, making Conrad significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Conrad averages about $888 per month, compared to roughly $821 in Dillon.

Conrad's overall cost of living differs from Dillon's. Conrad offers lower average salaries compared to Dillon, with a median household income of $47,348 compared to Dillon's $51,458.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Conrad is a classic, quiet Montana farming community where life revolves around the agricultural seasons and local school sports. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle with spectacular views of the Rocky Mountain Front to the west. Dillon strikes a unique balance between a traditional cowboy town and a small college community. It remains deeply tied to the ranching industry, but the presence of the university adds a layer of cultural and educational activity, creating a grounded, authentic Montana atmosphere.

# Havre vs Bozeman, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Havre and Bozeman offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Havre is a historic railroad and agricultural hub on the Hi-Line with 9,362 people, its deep roots in the railroad industry, the Havre Beneath the Streets underground tour, and quick access to the nearby Bear Paw Mountains and Fresno Reservoir. Bozeman, with 54,539 residents, is a booming tech and university hub known for Montana State University, its high-tech economy, and the Bridger Mountains and Big Sky.

## Who Should Move to Havre?

* Workers in agriculture, education, or the railroad industry looking for steady employment.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a community with all essential amenities.
* Those who appreciate the stark beauty of the northern plains and the close-knit feel of a Hi-Line town.
* History buffs interested in the unique underground history and nearby frontier battlefields.

## Who Should Move to Bozeman?

* Tech workers and entrepreneurs.
* Students and faculty at MSU.
* Affluent outdoor enthusiasts.
* People who want a highly active, progressive mountain town.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Havre is around $224,252, while in Bozeman it is closer to $750,000, a difference of roughly $525,748, making Havre significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Havre averages about $925 per month, compared to roughly $2,000 in Bozeman.

Havre's overall cost of living differs from Bozeman's. Havre offers lower average salaries compared to Bozeman, with a median household income of $58,000 compared to Bozeman's $70,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Havre is a resilient, hardworking community that serves as the commercial center for a vast stretch of northern Montana. It offers a practical, unpretentious lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture and the railroad. Bozeman is fast-paced, highly educated, and outdoor-obsessed, with a premium cost of living and a constant influx of new residents.

# Wolf Point vs Belgrade, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Wolf Point and Belgrade offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Wolf Point is a historic agricultural town on the Fort Peck Indian Reservation with 2,517 people, its position as the largest community on the reservation, its deep roots in farming and ranching, and quick access to the Missouri River and the vast eastern plains. Belgrade, with just 12,509 residents, is a rapidly growing hub in the Gallatin Valley known for proximity to Bozeman, the Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport, and the Bridger Mountains and Gallatin River.

## Who Should Move to Wolf Point?

* People seeking some of the most affordable housing in the state.
* Workers in agriculture, tribal government, or local services.
* Those who appreciate a quiet, isolated lifestyle on the high plains.
* Hunters and anglers who want uncrowded access to the Missouri River and surrounding lands.

## Who Should Move to Belgrade?

* Families and professionals who work in Bozeman but want slightly more affordable housing options.
* Frequent travelers who appreciate being minutes away from Montana's busiest airport.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want quick access to the Bridger Mountains, Big Sky, and blue-ribbon trout streams.
* People looking for a community with a strong local identity that still benefits from the economic engine of the Gallatin Valley.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Wolf Point is around $145,000, while in Belgrade it is closer to $544,626, a difference of roughly $399,626, making Wolf Point significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Wolf Point averages about $600 per month, compared to roughly $2,206 in Belgrade.

Wolf Point's overall cost of living differs from Belgrade's. Wolf Point offers lower average salaries compared to Belgrade, with a median household income of $42,000 compared to Belgrade's $88,896.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Wolf Point is a quiet, resilient community deeply tied to the Fort Peck Indian Reservation and the agricultural economy of northeastern Montana. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle far removed from the state's major urban centers. Belgrade feels like a busy, practical town that is absorbing much of the Gallatin Valley's growth. It has its own distinct community pride, centered around local schools and events, but its daily rhythm is closely tied to the economic hum of neighboring Bozeman and the constant activity at the airport.

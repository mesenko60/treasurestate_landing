# Big Sky vs Billings: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Big Sky and Billings are about as different as two Montana communities can be. Big Sky is the Biggest Skiing in America, a resort community of 3,591 in Gallatin County at 6,319 feet in central Montana, known for Big Sky Resort, 23 trailheads, 17 waterfalls, 18 lakes, 3 hot springs, 2 wilderness areas, a national forest, and 140 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles. Billings is the Magic City, Montana's largest city with 117,116 residents in Yellowstone County at 3,123 feet in eastern Montana, with 53 outdoor recreation sites, 9 museums, 9 golf courses, 16 fishing access sites, 5 historic sites, 4 state parks, Billings Logan International Airport 5 miles away, and a 16,000-student school district.

## Who Should Move to Big Sky?

*   Skiers, snowboarders, and mountain sports enthusiasts who want Big Sky Resort, one of the largest ski areas in North America with over 5,800 acres of skiable terrain, right in their backyard
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 23 trailheads, 17 waterfalls, 18 lakes, 19 fishing access sites, 3 hot springs, 2 wilderness areas, and a national forest all within 30 miles
*   High-income remote workers and affluent buyers who can afford Big Sky's extraordinary real estate market, with a typical home value of $1,819,905 and a median household income of $103,625
*   People who want a small, exclusive resort community lifestyle where outdoor recreation is the primary focus and the mountains are always visible from your front door

## Who Should Move to Billings?

*   Professionals and families who want Montana's largest city with the most complete job market, healthcare system, retail base, and urban amenities in the state
*   People who want Billings Logan International Airport 5 miles away, a 16,000-student school district, 9 museums, 9 golf courses, and a city with a median household income of $71,855
*   Anyone who wants a relatively affordable Montana city lifestyle, with a typical home value of $390,654 and rent averaging $1,383 per month, dramatically lower than Big Sky
*   Families and retirees who want a full-service Montana city with hospitals, universities, a vibrant downtown, and easy access to the Beartooth Mountains and Yellowstone Country to the south

## Cost of Living Breakdown

The gap between Big Sky and Billings is extraordinary. The typical home value in Big Sky is $1,819,905, compared to $390,654 in Billings, making Billings more than four times more affordable. Rent is closer: Big Sky averages $1,712 per month, while Billings comes in at $1,383, a more modest difference.

Median household income is significantly higher in Big Sky at $103,625 versus $71,855 in Billings. Big Sky's income reflects its affluent resort community demographics, but even with higher incomes, its real estate prices are in a completely different category from any other Montana community.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Big Sky is a world-class mountain resort community where the ski mountain, the wilderness, and a high-income outdoor lifestyle define daily life. Billings is Montana's commercial capital, where business, healthcare, agriculture, and a full urban lifestyle define community identity. Big Sky has 140 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles and one of the great ski resorts in North America, but almost no urban amenities and a school district of just 300 students. Billings has everything a large Montana city can offer, from a regional hospital system to a thriving downtown arts scene, but its outdoor recreation access is modest by Montana standards. These two communities serve completely different needs, and the choice between them comes down to whether you want a resort lifestyle in the mountains or a full-service city life on the eastern Montana plains.

# Butte vs Anaconda: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Butte and Anaconda are neighboring communities in southwestern Montana with deeply intertwined histories, Butte mined the copper and Anaconda smelted it, and they remain two of the most historically distinctive communities in the American West. Butte is the Richest Hill on Earth, a city of 34,494 perched at 5,741 feet, with a Victorian-era Uptown district, 17 museums within 30 miles, and a civic identity forged in the fires of the copper industry. Anaconda is the Smelter City, a smaller community of 9,421 at 5,279 feet in Deer Lodge County, where the iconic smokestack of the former Washoe Smelter still towers over town and the Old Works Golf Course, designed by Jack Nicklaus on the former smelter grounds, is one of the most unusual golf courses in the country.

## Who Should Move to Butte?

*   History lovers drawn to Butte's extraordinary Victorian-era Uptown district, the Berkeley Pit, and one of the densest concentrations of historic architecture in the Mountain West
*   Families and professionals who want a larger community with more services, better school enrollment, and a broader job market
*   People who want hot springs, mountain lakes, and wilderness within easy reach, Butte has 4 hot springs within 30 miles
*   Anyone who wants a working-class Montana city with a national profile and a mythology that is genuinely unlike anything else

## Who Should Move to Anaconda?

*   Remote workers and retirees seeking some of Montana's most affordable housing, median rent of just $661 per month
*   History enthusiasts drawn to Anaconda's copper-boom heritage, the Anaconda Smelter Stack State Park, and the Copper Village Museum
*   Outdoor lovers who want 48 lakes within 30 miles, Fairmont Hot Springs, Georgetown Lake, and the Pintler Wilderness
*   People who want a quiet, tight-knit community with a fascinating industrial history and a surprisingly rich outdoor recreation inventory

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Home prices are remarkably similar: the median home price in Butte is around $274,163, compared to $283,380 in Anaconda, a difference of less than $10,000. Where the two communities diverge sharply is in rent: about $1,318 per month in Butte versus just $661 in Anaconda, the lowest rent of any of the top 10 Montana towns.

Butte's median household income of $57,633 is higher than Anaconda's $49,533, reflecting Butte's larger job market. For anyone with remote income or a fixed budget, however, Anaconda's combination of ultra-low rent and 134 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, including 48 lakes, is a remarkable value proposition.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Butte and Anaconda are two sides of the same copper coin, communities whose fates were bound together by the Anaconda Copper Mining Company for nearly a century. Butte is larger, louder, and more defiant; Anaconda is quieter, more contemplative, and in some ways more at peace with its past. The Old Works Golf Course is a perfect symbol of Anaconda's reinvention, a world-class golf course built on the bones of an industrial giant. Both communities are authentically and irreplaceably Montana; the choice is simply a matter of scale and temperament.

# Colstrip vs Lewistown, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Colstrip and Lewistown offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Colstrip is a purpose-built energy town in eastern Montana with 2,055 people, its massive coal-fired power plant, its excellent, company-built community amenities, and quick access to Castle Rock Lake and the surrounding pine hills. Lewistown, with 6,204 residents, is a historic agricultural center known for its charming stone architecture, its position at the geographic center of Montana, and the surrounding island mountain ranges and Spring Creek.

## Who Should Move to Colstrip?

* Workers in the energy, mining, or industrial sectors looking for high-paying employment.
* Families seeking excellent, well-funded local schools and community parks.
* People looking for very affordable housing combined with high average salaries.
* Those who appreciate a practical, hardworking community with a strong industrial identity.

## Who Should Move to Lewistown?

* Retirees or remote workers who want a quiet, peaceful lifestyle in a classic Montana setting.
* People looking for affordable housing and a lower overall cost of living.
* Those who prefer a tight-knit, traditional small-town community with a strong agricultural heritage.
* History buffs and outdoor enthusiasts who enjoy uncrowded access to the Snowy, Judith, and Moccasin mountains.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Colstrip is around $219,986, while in Lewistown it is closer to $268,126, a difference of roughly $48,140, making Colstrip significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Colstrip averages about $632 per month, compared to roughly $896 in Lewistown.

Colstrip's overall cost of living differs from Lewistown's. Colstrip offers higher average salaries compared to Lewistown, with a median household income of $86,000 compared to Lewistown's $44,195.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Colstrip is a unique, industrious town built specifically to support the local energy industry. It boasts surprisingly excellent parks, schools, and recreational facilities for its size, offering a practical, high-income lifestyle for those working in the energy sector. Lewistown is quiet, slow-paced, and friendly in that classic Montana way. People wave when they drive by, and life revolves around ranching, community events, and the pristine waters of Big Spring Creek that flow right through the center of town.

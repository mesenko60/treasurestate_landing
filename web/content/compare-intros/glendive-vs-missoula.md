# Glendive vs Missoula: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Glendive and Missoula sit at opposite ends of Montana in nearly every sense. Glendive is the Paddlefish Capital of the World, a community of 4,873 in Dawson County in eastern Montana at 2,083 feet elevation, with 30 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles and Makoshika State Park at its edge. Missoula is the Garden City, a university city of 77,757 in Missoula County in western Montana at 3,215 feet, with 211 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 65 fishing access sites, 49 trailheads, 22 campgrounds, 10 museums, 9 historic sites, 2 hot springs, and the Clark Fork River running through its heart.

## Who Should Move to Glendive?

*   Budget-conscious buyers who want Montana at a very low price, with a typical home value of $185,447 and median rent of just $832 per month
*   Outdoor enthusiasts and anglers who want Makoshika State Park, the Yellowstone River, and some of the best paddlefish snagging in the country right in their backyard
*   People who want a small, quiet eastern Montana community with a genuine small-town character and a slower pace of life
*   Remote workers or retirees who want affordable housing and a community where $71,063 in median household income goes a very long way

## Who Should Move to Missoula?

*   Young professionals, students, and families who want a university city with good job opportunities, a 9,000-student school district, and Missoula Montana Airport 7 miles away
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 65 fishing access sites, 49 trailheads, 22 campgrounds, 13 lakes, 2 hot springs, and a ski area all within 30 miles
*   People who love a lively downtown with craft breweries, live music, farmers markets, and a cultural scene that punches well above Missoula's size
*   Anyone who wants western Montana's mountains, rivers, and forests as their daily backdrop

## Cost of Living Breakdown

The gap between these two towns is very large. The typical home value in Glendive is around $185,447, compared to $554,389 in Missoula, nearly three times the price. Rent is lower in Glendive: Glendive averages about $832 per month, while Missoula comes in at roughly $1,486.

Interestingly, median household income is slightly higher in Glendive at $71,063 versus $65,329 in Missoula. Glendive residents earn more on average and pay far less for housing, making it one of the most affordable lifestyles in Montana relative to income.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Glendive is a small eastern Montana town where the Yellowstone River, Makoshika State Park, and the paddlefish season define the calendar. Missoula is a vibrant university city where the Clark Fork River, the Rattlesnake Wilderness, and a thriving arts and food scene define daily life. These two towns represent opposite ends of the Montana spectrum. If you want affordability, quiet, and the raw eastern Montana landscape, Glendive is a genuine option. If you want a lively, connected western Montana city with exceptional outdoor access and a university energy, Missoula is one of the best places to live in the state.

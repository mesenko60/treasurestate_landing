# Lewistown vs Bozeman: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Lewistown and Bozeman are two of the most different communities in Montana, separated not just by distance but by price, pace, and purpose. Lewistown is the Geographic Center of Montana, a quiet ranching community of 6,204 in Fergus County with affordable housing, 17 campgrounds within 30 miles, and a traditional small-town character. Bozeman is the Gallatin Gateway, a fast-growing city of 58,000 in Gallatin County at 4,826 feet elevation, with 139 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 32 fishing access sites, 16 trailheads, a major university, and one of the strongest economies in the Mountain West.

## Who Should Move to Lewistown?

*   Retirees and remote workers who want a quiet, affordable small-town lifestyle at a fraction of Bozeman's price, with a typical home value around $268,126 versus Bozeman's $715,781
*   People who want a traditional, tight-knit ranching community with a genuine small-town character and no resort-corridor crowds
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 17 campgrounds, 7 fishing access sites, and the Big Snowy Mountains within 30 miles
*   Anyone who wants to live at the geographic heart of Montana, where the land is wide and the pace is slow

## Who Should Move to Bozeman?

*   Professionals, entrepreneurs, and tech workers drawn to Bozeman's booming economy and a median household income of $79,903
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 32 fishing access sites, 16 trailheads, 16 lakes, a ski area, and 139 total recreation sites within 30 miles
*   Students and academics connected to Montana State University, one of the state's premier research institutions
*   Families who want a full-service city with an 8,500-student school district, a regional airport, and a vibrant downtown

## Cost of Living Breakdown

The gap between these two towns is one of the widest in Montana. The typical home value in Lewistown is around $268,126, compared to $715,781 in Bozeman, nearly three times the price. Rent is equally dramatic: Lewistown averages about $896 per month, while Bozeman comes in at roughly $2,113, more than double.

The income gap is significant but does not close the housing gap. The median household income in Bozeman is $79,903, compared to $44,195 in Lewistown. Bozeman residents earn considerably more, but the housing premium is so large that affordability remains a genuine challenge for many buyers.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Lewistown is a town where the ranching calendar still shapes community life, the downtown has a quiet, historic character, and the nearest major city is over 100 miles away. Bozeman is one of the fastest-growing cities in the country, a place where Montana State University drives innovation, the downtown is packed with restaurants and breweries, and the Gallatin Valley's mountain scenery makes it one of the most desirable places to live in the Mountain West. These two towns serve entirely different needs. If you want affordable, quiet, traditional Montana, Lewistown is one of the best values in the state. If you want a booming economy, world-class outdoor recreation, and a city with genuine momentum, Bozeman is hard to argue with.

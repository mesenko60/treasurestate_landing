# Havre vs Kalispell, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Havre and Kalispell offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Havre is a historic railroad and agricultural hub on the Hi-Line with 9,362 people, its deep roots in the railroad industry, the Havre Beneath the Streets underground tour, and quick access to the nearby Bear Paw Mountains and Fresno Reservoir. Kalispell, with 26,110 residents, is a commercial center of the Flathead Valley known for its retail and medical facilities, its historic downtown, and Flathead Lake and Glacier National Park.

## Who Should Move to Havre?

* Workers in agriculture, education, or the railroad industry looking for steady employment.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a community with all essential amenities.
* Those who appreciate the stark beauty of the northern plains and the close-knit feel of a Hi-Line town.
* History buffs interested in the unique underground history and nearby frontier battlefields.

## Who Should Move to Kalispell?

* Families who want access to Glacier National Park without the resort town prices.
* Professionals working in healthcare or retail.
* People who want a mix of historic charm and modern conveniences.
* Outdoor lovers who want to be near Flathead Lake.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Havre is around $224,252, while in Kalispell it is closer to $550,000, a difference of roughly $325,748, making Havre significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Havre averages about $925 per month, compared to roughly $1,400 in Kalispell.

Havre's overall cost of living differs from Kalispell's. Havre offers lower average salaries compared to Kalispell, with a median household income of $58,000 compared to Kalispell's $60,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Havre is a resilient, hardworking community that serves as the commercial center for a vast stretch of northern Montana. It offers a practical, unpretentious lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture and the railroad. Kalispell is the practical, bustling heart of the Flathead Valley, balancing its role as a commercial hub with incredible access to natural wonders.

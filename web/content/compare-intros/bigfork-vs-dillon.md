# Bigfork vs Dillon: Which Montana Town Is Right for You?

Bigfork and Dillon are both small western Montana towns, but they occupy very different corners of the state and offer very different lifestyles. Bigfork, A Village by the Bay on Flathead Lake, is a resort-adjacent community of 5,000 people with a thriving arts scene, world-class fishing, and premium real estate. Dillon, Montana's Hometown in the Beaverhead Valley, is a high-desert ranching and university town of 3,880 people, home to the University of Montana Western and surrounded by some of the finest fly fishing rivers in the country.

## Who Should Move to Bigfork?

- Outdoor enthusiasts who want Flathead Lake, Glacier National Park, and a wide variety of fishing, boating, and hiking options
- Arts and culture lovers who want a vibrant summer theater and arts scene in a small-town setting
- Retirees or remote workers who want a beautiful lakeside community with a polished, welcoming atmosphere
- Those who want the full northwestern Montana experience, from skiing to lake recreation to wildlife watching

## Who Should Move to Dillon?

- Fly fishers who want access to the Beaverhead, Big Hole, and Ruby rivers, among the most storied trout streams in Montana
- Remote workers, retirees, or students who want a quiet, affordable university town with a genuine small-town feel
- People drawn to wide-open high-desert landscapes, big skies, and a slower, more traditional pace of life
- History enthusiasts interested in Lewis and Clark country and the ranching heritage of southwest Montana

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Bigfork is dramatically more expensive. The typical home value is $758,435 compared to $375,585 in Dillon, a difference of over $380,000. Rent follows the same pattern, with Bigfork at $1,103 per month and Dillon at $821. Bigfork's median household income of $82,648 is considerably higher than Dillon's $51,458, but the housing cost gap is large enough that Dillon remains far more affordable in real terms.

Bigfork is served by Glacier Park International Airport in Kalispell, 22 miles away. Dillon's nearest airport is in Butte, 62 miles to the north.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Bigfork has the energy and polish of a destination community. The summer arts festival, the Bigfork Summer Playhouse, and the lakeside setting give it a vibrancy that is unusual for a town of its size. Dillon is quieter and more self-contained, a place where the university, the ranching community, and the fishing culture define the rhythm of life. Clark's Lookout State Park and the Beaverhead River are right in town, and the surrounding landscape is vast and unhurried. These are two towns that share a love of Montana's outdoors but almost nothing else in terms of setting, cost, or daily character.

Bigfork has 176 recreational sites within 30 miles, including 28 fishing access points, 32 lakes, 25 trailheads, and access to Glacier National Park. Dillon has 71 sites, anchored by 24 fishing access points, 16 campgrounds, 5 state parks, and 2 hot springs. For variety and volume, Bigfork leads. For river fishing and southwest Montana's wide-open spaces, Dillon is in its own category.

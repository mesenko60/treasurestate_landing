# Laurel vs Butte: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Laurel and Butte are both working-class Montana communities with deep roots and affordable housing, but they sit in very different landscapes and carry very different histories. Laurel is the City Between the Yellowstone and the Rimrocks, a community of 7,215 in the Yellowstone River valley with 21 fishing access sites within 30 miles and a quiet, practical character. Butte is the Richest Hill on Earth, a city of 34,494 in the mountains of Silver Bow County at 5,741 feet elevation, with one of the most dramatic industrial and cultural histories in the American West, 17 museums within 30 miles, and a proud, gritty identity that has survived the collapse of the copper industry and come out the other side.

## Who Should Move to Laurel?

*   Families and buyers who want Yellowstone County living with a typical home value around $369,826 and easy access to Billings 18 miles away
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 21 fishing access sites, 6 state parks, and the Yellowstone River corridor within 30 miles
*   People who want a warm-weather central Montana climate, with July highs averaging 88°F versus Butte's 78°F
*   Anyone who wants a quiet, low-key small town with a practical, working-class character

## Who Should Move to Butte?

*   History enthusiasts drawn to Butte's National Historic Landmark district, the Berkeley Pit, and 17 museums within 30 miles
*   Budget-conscious buyers who want western Montana at an affordable price, with a typical home value around $274,163
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 14 campgrounds, 4 hot springs, and 13 lakes within 30 miles
*   People who want a larger city with a strong sense of community identity and a cultural calendar anchored by events like the annual St. Patrick's Day celebration

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Butte is the more affordable option on housing. The typical home value in Laurel is around $369,826, compared to $274,163 in Butte, a gap of nearly $96,000. Rent is closer: Laurel averages about $1,160 per month, while Butte comes in at roughly $1,318, making Laurel slightly cheaper to rent in despite the higher home prices.

The income gap is notable. The median household income in Laurel is $66,382, compared to $57,633 in Butte. Laurel residents earn more on average, which helps offset the higher home prices relative to Butte.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Laurel is a quiet river town where the Yellowstone flows nearby and life revolves around family, work, and the outdoors. Butte is a city with a story, a place where the copper boom built ornate Victorian mansions and the bust left a Superfund site visible from the highway, and where the community has responded with a stubborn pride and a cultural revival that includes a thriving arts scene and a historic district unlike anything else in Montana. If you want affordable, low-key small-town life near Billings, Laurel is a solid choice. If you want a larger, historically rich city in western Montana at a very accessible price, Butte is one of the state's most underrated communities.

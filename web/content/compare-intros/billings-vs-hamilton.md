# Hamilton vs Billings: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Hamilton and Billings offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Hamilton is the Banana Belt of Montana, a small Bitterroot Valley community of 4,659 in Ravalli County at 3,573 feet, known for its mild winters, 194 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 55 lakes, 47 trailheads, 32 campgrounds, 25 fishing access sites, and the Bitterroot National Forest at its doorstep. Billings is the Magic City, Montana's largest city with 117,116 residents in Yellowstone County at 3,123 feet, with Billings Logan International Airport 5 miles away, a 16,000-student school district, 53 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, and the full range of services that come with being the commercial hub of the Northern Rockies.

## Who Should Move to Hamilton?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 47 trailheads, 55 lakes, 32 campgrounds, 25 fishing access sites, a hot spring, a national forest, and a wilderness area all within 30 miles
*   People who want a small, quiet Bitterroot Valley community with a genuinely mild winter climate, where January average highs reach 40 degrees
*   Remote workers, retirees, and families who want a peaceful western Montana lifestyle at a lower cost than Missoula, with Missoula's full range of services 56 miles north
*   Anyone drawn to the Bitterroot Valley's combination of mountain scenery, river access, and the laid-back character of a small agricultural community

## Who Should Move to Billings?

*   Professionals, families, and anyone who needs a full-service Montana city, with a 16,000-student school district, Billings Logan International Airport 5 miles away, and the full range of employment, healthcare, and retail that comes with Montana's largest city
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 16 fishing access sites, 9 golf courses, 4 state parks, 5 historic sites, and the Rimrocks trail system all within 30 miles
*   People who want a city with real momentum, a growing arts scene, a walkable downtown, and a community that has been reinvesting in itself for years
*   Anyone who wants to be within a few hours of both Yellowstone National Park and the Beartooth Highway

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Hamilton is considerably more expensive than Billings for buyers. The typical home value in Hamilton is around $516,033, compared to $390,654 in Billings, about 32% higher. Rent is lower in Hamilton: Hamilton averages about $958 per month, while Billings comes in at roughly $1,383.

Median household income is significantly higher in Billings at $71,855 versus $52,917 in Hamilton. Billings residents earn more on average and pay less for housing, making it the better value for buyers and earners alike.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Hamilton is a small Bitterroot Valley town where the river, the mountains, and the mild climate define daily life. People come here for the outdoor access and the quiet, and they stay because the Bitterroot Valley is genuinely beautiful and remarkably livable. Billings is a real city, with traffic, a hospital district, a performing arts center, and a community that functions as the commercial hub for a region stretching into Wyoming and the Dakotas. If you want the Bitterroot Valley's outdoor access and a slower pace of life, Hamilton is a compelling choice. If you want Montana's biggest city with the most services, the most jobs, and the most amenities, Billings is the clear answer.

# Missoula vs Great Falls: Which Montana City is Right for You?

Missoula and Great Falls are both significant Montana cities, but they occupy very different roles in the state's story. Missoula is the Garden City, a progressive, mountain-ringed university town of 77,757 where the Clark Fork River runs through downtown, the trailheads are everywhere, and the University of Montana gives the city a youthful, culturally active energy. Great Falls is the Electric City, a more traditional, working-class city of 60,000 on the Missouri River in central Montana, anchored by Malmstrom Air Force Base, the world-class C.M. Russell Museum, and a spectacular series of waterfalls that once stopped Lewis and Clark for nearly a month.

## Who Should Move to Missoula?

*   Young professionals, students, and academics drawn to the University of Montana's energy and the city's arts scene
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want world-class hiking, skiing, and river access within minutes of downtown
*   People who value a walkable, progressive community with independent restaurants, breweries, and live music
*   Anyone who wants a culturally vibrant mountain city with a strong sense of identity and community

## Who Should Move to Great Falls?

*   Military families stationed at or connected to Malmstrom Air Force Base
*   History and art lovers drawn to the C.M. Russell Museum and the Lewis and Clark National Historic Trail
*   Retirees and remote workers who want a quieter, more affordable city with a genuine small-town feel
*   Outdoor lovers who want easy access to the Missouri River trail system, the Rocky Mountain Front, and Glacier Country

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Great Falls is significantly more affordable than Missoula. The median home price in Great Falls is around $328,554, compared to $554,389 in Missoula, a difference of over $225,000. Rent is also lower in Great Falls at about $1,340 per month versus $1,486 in Missoula.

The income gap is surprisingly narrow. Missoula's median household income of $65,329 is only slightly higher than Great Falls' $63,934, which means Great Falls residents get considerably more financial breathing room despite earning nearly the same amount.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Missoula has an unmistakable energy, farmers markets on the river, packed breweries on a Tuesday, and a general sense that the mountains are always calling. It's a city that takes its outdoor culture seriously and wears its progressive values openly. Great Falls is quieter and more settled, a city where the Missouri River trail system is a genuine community treasure and the C.M. Russell Museum reminds you that this was once the heart of the American West's most celebrated artistic tradition. Both cities are authentically Montana, but Missoula is the mountain town and Great Falls is the river city.

# Libby vs Red Lodge, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Libby and Red Lodge offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Libby is a remote timber town in the Kootenai National Forest with 2,775 people, its deep roots in the logging industry, its stunning, rugged isolation, and quick access to the Kootenai River and Cabinet Mountains. Red Lodge, with just 2,336 residents, is a historic mountain town and gateway to Yellowstone known for its vibrant, historic downtown, its position at the base of the Beartooth Highway, and Red Lodge Mountain and the Beartooth Mountains.

## Who Should Move to Libby?

* Outdoor enthusiasts seeking uncrowded access to some of Montana's most rugged and remote wilderness.
* Retirees and remote workers looking for affordable mountain living far from major tourist hubs.
* People who appreciate a tight-knit, resilient community with a strong working-class heritage.
* Hunters, anglers, and hikers who want to live in the heart of the Kootenai National Forest.

## Who Should Move to Red Lodge?

* Outdoor enthusiasts who want immediate access to skiing, hiking, and the Beartooth Wilderness.
* People seeking a lively, historic mountain town with a strong sense of community.
* Retirees and remote workers drawn to the spectacular scenery and small-town charm.
* Those who want to live in a gateway community to Yellowstone National Park.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Libby is around $285,000, while in Red Lodge it is closer to $550,000, a difference of roughly $265,000, making Libby significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Libby averages about $850 per month, compared to roughly $1,200 in Red Lodge.

Libby's overall cost of living differs from Red Lodge's. Libby offers lower average salaries compared to Red Lodge, with a median household income of $45,000 compared to Red Lodge's $58,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Libby is a resilient, isolated town tucked into the far northwest corner of the state. It is defined by its timber heritage and its incredible, uncrowded access to the Kootenai River and Cabinet Mountains, offering a quiet, rugged lifestyle. Red Lodge is a vibrant, historic mountain town that balances its role as a tourist destination with a strong, year-round local community. It offers spectacular access to the Beartooth Mountains and a lively downtown filled with shops, restaurants, and local events.

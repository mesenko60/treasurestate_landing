# Conrad vs Columbus, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Conrad and Columbus offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Conrad is a quiet agricultural town on the northern plains with 2,318 people, its position as the seat of Pondera County, its strong farming and ranching economy, and quick access to the Rocky Mountain Front and Lake Frances. Columbus, with 2,015 residents, is a scenic river town in the Stillwater Valley known for its position at the confluence of the Yellowstone and Stillwater rivers, its proximity to the Stillwater Mine, and the Absaroka-Beartooth Wilderness.

## Who Should Move to Conrad?

* Workers in agriculture or local services looking for a quiet, stable community.
* People seeking very affordable housing far removed from the state's busy tourist hubs.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle.
* Hunters and anglers who want uncrowded access to the plains and the nearby Rocky Mountain Front.

## Who Should Move to Columbus?

* Workers in the mining or industrial sectors looking for steady, high-paying employment.
* Anglers and boaters who want immediate access to two of Montana's premier rivers.
* People seeking a scenic, small-town lifestyle with easy highway access to the amenities of Billings.
* Outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the rugged beauty of the Absaroka-Beartooth Wilderness.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Conrad is around $244,817, while in Columbus it is closer to $428,574, a difference of roughly $183,757, making Conrad significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Conrad averages about $888 per month, compared to roughly $926 in Columbus.

Conrad's overall cost of living differs from Columbus's. Conrad offers lower average salaries compared to Columbus, with a median household income of $47,348 compared to Columbus's $62,500.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Conrad is a classic, quiet Montana farming community where life revolves around the agricultural seasons and local school sports. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle with spectacular views of the Rocky Mountain Front to the west. Columbus is a beautiful, thriving town that balances a strong industrial economy driven by the nearby palladium mine with spectacular outdoor recreation. It offers a relaxed, scenic lifestyle with excellent access to both the mountains and the city of Billings.

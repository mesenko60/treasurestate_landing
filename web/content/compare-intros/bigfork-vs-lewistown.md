# Lewistown vs Bigfork: Which Montana Town is Right for You?

Lewistown and Bigfork are both small Montana communities with similar populations, but they represent very different lifestyles and price points. Lewistown is the Geographic Center of Montana, a historic ranching and agricultural community of 6,204 in Fergus County at 3,934 feet, with the nearest airport in Great Falls 115 miles away, 37 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 17 campgrounds, 7 fishing access sites, 3 lakes, 3 nature reserves, 2 golf courses, 2 museums, 2 state parks, 1 hot spring, and a 1,200-student school district. Bigfork is A Village by the Bay, a resort and arts community of 5,000 in Flathead County at 2,940 feet on the eastern shore of Flathead Lake, with Glacier Park International Airport in Kalispell 22 miles away, 176 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 32 lakes, 28 fishing access sites, 27 campgrounds, 25 trailheads, 16 state parks, 10 museums, 9 golf courses, 8 nature reserves, 6 historic sites, 4 disc golf courses, 4 boat launches, 1 national forest, 1 national park, 1 river, 1 ski area, and a 900-student school district.

## Who Should Move to Lewistown?

*   Retirees and remote workers who want a quiet, self-contained central Montana community with a genuine small-town character, affordable housing, and wide-open central Montana skies
*   History buffs and people drawn to Montana's ranching and agricultural heritage, with the Central Montana Historical Society and Museum, the Lewistown Art Center, and the surrounding Judith Basin country offering a deep connection to the region's past
*   Budget-conscious buyers who want one of the most affordable housing markets in Montana, with a typical home value of $268,126 and rent averaging just $896 per month
*   Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want 17 campgrounds, 7 fishing access sites, 3 nature reserves, 2 state parks, and a hot spring within 30 miles in the heart of central Montana's ranch country

## Who Should Move to Bigfork?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts and lake lovers who want 32 lakes, 28 fishing access sites, 27 campgrounds, 25 trailheads, 16 state parks, 8 nature reserves, and Glacier National Park all within 30 miles, with Flathead Lake at their doorstep
*   Arts and culture enthusiasts who want a community known for its summer theater, galleries, and boutique shops along Electric Avenue, in one of the most scenic small towns in western Montana
*   Affluent buyers and retirees who want a resort-quality lifestyle on Flathead Lake, with a typical home value of $758,435 and a median household income of $82,648, in a community that draws visitors from across the country
*   Golfers and outdoor recreation seekers who want 9 golf courses, 25 trailheads, 4 boat launches, and a ski area all within 30 miles, with Flathead Lake as the centerpiece of daily life

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Bigfork is dramatically more expensive than Lewistown. The typical home value in Bigfork is $758,435, compared to $268,126 in Lewistown, nearly three times higher. Rent is also higher in Bigfork at $1,103 per month versus $896 in Lewistown.

Median household income is much higher in Bigfork at $82,648 versus $44,195 in Lewistown. Bigfork's higher income reflects its resort community character and the affluent retiree and remote worker population that has settled on Flathead Lake. Lewistown's dramatically lower home prices make homeownership accessible to buyers across a wide range of income levels.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Lewistown is a quiet, traditional central Montana community where ranching, agriculture, the Judith Basin, and a deep sense of place define daily life. Bigfork is a polished resort village on Flathead Lake where summer theater, gallery walks, boating, and a vibrant arts scene set the tone. The outdoor recreation gap is enormous: Bigfork has 176 sites within 30 miles versus Lewistown's 37, including 25 trailheads, 32 lakes, and a ski area that Lewistown does not have. Lewistown has 17 campgrounds, a hot spring, and the wide-open central Montana landscape that defines its identity. If you want an affordable, quiet central Montana community with genuine small-town character and a deep connection to Montana's ranching heritage, Lewistown is one of the most underrated places to live in the state. If you want a resort-quality lakeside village with extraordinary outdoor access, an arts scene, and Flathead Lake as your backyard, Bigfork is one of the most distinctive small towns in Montana.

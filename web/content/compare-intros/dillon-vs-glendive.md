# Glendive vs Dillon: Which Montana Town Is Right for You?

Glendive and Dillon are both small, affordable Montana towns with strong outdoor identities, but they occupy very different corners of the state. Glendive, the Paddlefish Capital of the World on the Yellowstone River in far eastern Montana, is a prairie town of 4,873 people known for Makoshika State Park and paddlefish snagging season. Dillon, Montana's Hometown in the Beaverhead Valley of southwest Montana, is a high-desert ranching and university town of 3,880 people, home to the University of Montana Western and surrounded by some of the finest fly fishing rivers in the country.

## Who Should Move to Glendive?

- People looking for very affordable housing and a low cost of living in a quiet, friendly community
- Anglers who want the Yellowstone River and the unique experience of paddlefish snagging season each spring
- Fossil and geology enthusiasts who want Makoshika State Park, Montana's largest state park, as a backyard
- Those who prefer eastern Montana's wide-open prairie landscapes and a traditional, community-centered way of life

## Who Should Move to Dillon?

- Fly fishers who want access to the Beaverhead, Big Hole, and Ruby rivers, among the most storied trout streams in Montana
- Remote workers, retirees, or students who want a quiet, affordable university town with a genuine small-town feel
- People drawn to wide-open high-desert landscapes, big skies, and a slower, more traditional pace of life
- History enthusiasts interested in Lewis and Clark country and the ranching heritage of southwest Montana

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Both towns are among the most affordable in Montana, though Dillon is more expensive. The typical home value in Dillon is $375,585 compared to $185,447 in Glendive, a difference of about $190,000. Rent is nearly identical, with Glendive at $832 per month and Dillon at $821. Glendive's median household income of $71,063 is higher than Dillon's $51,458, making Glendive the more affordable option in real terms. Both towns have their own airports nearby, with Glendive's just 6 miles away and Dillon's nearest in Butte, 62 miles north.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Glendive and Dillon are both quiet, self-contained communities where outdoor life and local tradition define the rhythm of daily living. Glendive's identity is shaped by the Yellowstone River, the badlands of Makoshika, and the annual paddlefish season. Dillon's is shaped by the Beaverhead River, the University of Montana Western, and the vast ranching landscape of the Big Hole and Beaverhead valleys. Both towns have a genuine, unpretentious Montana character, but they offer very different landscapes and very different fishing experiences.

Glendive has 30 recreational sites within 30 miles, anchored by Makoshika State Park, 4 fishing access points, and 7 scenic viewpoints. Dillon has 71 sites, including 24 fishing access points, 16 campgrounds, 5 state parks, and 2 hot springs. Dillon offers more variety overall, particularly for river fishing and backcountry access. Glendive's Makoshika State Park is one of Montana's most remarkable landscapes and a genuine draw for visitors and residents alike.

# Bigfork vs Helena: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Bigfork and Helena offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Bigfork is a Village by the Bay, a small community of 5,000 on the eastern shore of Flathead Lake in Flathead County at 2,940 feet elevation, with 176 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 32 lakes, 27 campgrounds, and Glacier National Park nearby. Helena is the Queen City of the Rockies, Montana's state capital with 33,100 residents in Lewis and Clark County at 4,039 feet, with 107 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 28 campgrounds, 10 historic sites, 9 museums, 3 hot springs, and a community shaped by government, history, and the Continental Divide.

## Who Should Move to Bigfork?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 27 campgrounds, 25 trailheads, 28 fishing access sites, 32 lakes, 16 state parks, and Glacier National Park all within 30 miles
*   Affluent buyers and retirees who want a small, scenic lakeside community with a median household income of $82,648 and a mild climate, with January lows averaging 23°F versus Helena's 17°F
*   People who want a genuine village atmosphere on Flathead Lake with a summer arts festival, a community theater, and one of the most beautiful natural settings in Montana
*   Anyone who wants western Montana's mountain and lake scenery as their daily backdrop

## Who Should Move to Helena?

*   Government employees, professionals, and families who want Montana's state capital, with a 7,800-student school district, Helena Regional Airport, and the stability that comes with a government-anchored economy
*   Budget-conscious buyers who want a Montana city at a significantly lower price, with a typical home value of $466,393 versus Bigfork's $758,435
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 28 campgrounds, 11 trailheads, 10 fishing access sites, 3 hot springs, 10 historic sites, and the Mount Helena trail system all within 30 miles
*   History enthusiasts drawn to Helena's gold rush heritage, the Cathedral of Saint Helena, Last Chance Gulch, and one of the most architecturally rich downtowns in Montana

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Bigfork is considerably more expensive than Helena. The typical home value in Bigfork is around $758,435, compared to $466,393 in Helena, a difference of nearly $300,000. Rent is lower in Bigfork: Bigfork averages about $1,103 per month, while Helena comes in at roughly $1,489.

The median household income in Bigfork is $82,648, compared to $69,341 in Helena. Bigfork residents earn more on average, but the home price gap is large enough that Helena offers a much more accessible path to homeownership for most buyers.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Bigfork is a small lakeside village where the arts, outdoor recreation, and Flathead Lake define daily life. Helena is a mid-sized capital city with a gold rush history, a walkable downtown built along Last Chance Gulch, and a community that balances government work with serious outdoor recreation on the Continental Divide. If you want a quiet, scenic village on one of Montana's most beautiful lakes, Bigfork is exceptional. If you want a mid-sized Montana city with a rich history, a stable economy, and easy access to the Helena National Forest and the Elkhorn Mountains, Helena is a strong and often underappreciated choice.

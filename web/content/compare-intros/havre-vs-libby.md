# Havre vs Libby, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Havre and Libby offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Havre is a historic railroad and agricultural hub on the Hi-Line with 9,362 people, its deep roots in the railroad industry, the Havre Beneath the Streets underground tour, and quick access to the nearby Bear Paw Mountains and Fresno Reservoir. Libby, with 2,775 residents, is a remote timber town in the Kootenai National Forest known for its deep roots in the logging industry, its stunning, rugged isolation, and the Kootenai River and Cabinet Mountains.

## Who Should Move to Havre?

* Workers in agriculture, education, or the railroad industry looking for steady employment.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a community with all essential amenities.
* Those who appreciate the stark beauty of the northern plains and the close-knit feel of a Hi-Line town.
* History buffs interested in the unique underground history and nearby frontier battlefields.

## Who Should Move to Libby?

* Outdoor enthusiasts seeking uncrowded access to some of Montana's most rugged and remote wilderness.
* Retirees and remote workers looking for affordable mountain living far from major tourist hubs.
* People who appreciate a tight-knit, resilient community with a strong working-class heritage.
* Hunters, anglers, and hikers who want to live in the heart of the Kootenai National Forest.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Havre is around $224,252, while in Libby it is closer to $285,000, a difference of roughly $60,748, making Havre significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Havre averages about $925 per month, compared to roughly $850 in Libby.

Havre's overall cost of living differs from Libby's. Havre offers higher average salaries compared to Libby, with a median household income of $58,000 compared to Libby's $45,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Havre is a resilient, hardworking community that serves as the commercial center for a vast stretch of northern Montana. It offers a practical, unpretentious lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture and the railroad. Libby is a resilient, isolated town tucked into the far northwest corner of the state. It is defined by its timber heritage and its incredible, uncrowded access to the Kootenai River and Cabinet Mountains, offering a quiet, rugged lifestyle.

# Hamilton vs Anaconda: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Hamilton and Anaconda are both small western Montana communities with strong outdoor access and industrial heritage, but they differ significantly in climate, cost, and character. Hamilton is the Banana Belt of Montana, a small Bitterroot Valley community of 4,659 in Ravalli County at 3,573 feet, known for its mild winters, 194 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 55 lakes, 47 trailheads, 32 campgrounds, 25 fishing access sites, and the Bitterroot National Forest at its doorstep. Anaconda is the Smelter City, a community of 9,421 in Deer Lodge County at 5,279 feet, with 134 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 48 lakes, 19 campgrounds, 16 museums, 9 nature reserves, 9 trailheads, 8 fishing access sites, 6 golf courses, a ski area, and the iconic Anaconda Smelter Stack State Park.

## Who Should Move to Hamilton?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 47 trailheads, 55 lakes, 32 campgrounds, 25 fishing access sites, a hot spring, a national forest, and a wilderness area all within 30 miles
*   People who want a small, quiet Bitterroot Valley community with a genuinely mild winter climate, where January average highs reach 40 degrees, compared to Anaconda's 33 degrees
*   Remote workers, retirees, and families who want a peaceful western Montana lifestyle with Missoula's full range of services 56 miles north
*   Anyone drawn to the Bitterroot Valley's combination of mountain scenery, river access, and the laid-back character of a small agricultural community

## Who Should Move to Anaconda?

*   Budget-conscious buyers who want western Montana at a very low price, with a typical home value of $283,380 and median rent of just $661 per month, compared to Hamilton's $516,033 and $958
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 48 lakes, 19 campgrounds, 9 trailheads, 8 fishing access sites, 6 golf courses including the nationally recognized Old Works Golf Course, a hot spring at Fairmont, and a ski area all within 30 miles
*   History enthusiasts drawn to Anaconda's extraordinary copper smelting heritage, the Anaconda Smelter Stack State Park, and 16 museums within 30 miles
*   People who want a small, affordable western Montana community with easy access to both Butte and Helena

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Anaconda is dramatically more affordable than Hamilton. The typical home value in Hamilton is around $516,033, compared to $283,380 in Anaconda, nearly double. Rent is also lower in Anaconda: Hamilton averages about $958 per month, while Anaconda comes in at just $661, the lowest in this comparison set.

Median household incomes are similar: $52,917 in Hamilton versus $49,533 in Anaconda. Anaconda residents earn slightly less on average but pay far less for housing, making it one of the most affordable lifestyles in western Montana.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Hamilton is a small Bitterroot Valley town where the river, the mountains, and the mild climate define daily life. Anaconda is a small copper smelting town where industrial heritage, the Continental Divide, and a community shaped by the Anaconda Company define community identity. Anaconda has a roughness and authenticity that comes from its mining and smelting past, along with serious outdoor access and some of the most affordable housing in western Montana. If you want the Bitterroot Valley's mild climate, quiet, and outdoor access, Hamilton is a compelling choice. If you want a community with a deep industrial heritage, western Montana's outdoor access, and dramatically lower housing costs, Anaconda is one of Montana's most affordable and most historically interesting small towns.

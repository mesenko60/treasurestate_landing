# Colstrip vs Red Lodge, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Colstrip and Red Lodge offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Colstrip is a purpose-built energy town in eastern Montana with 2,055 people, its massive coal-fired power plant, its excellent, company-built community amenities, and quick access to Castle Rock Lake and the surrounding pine hills. Red Lodge, with 2,336 residents, is a historic mountain town and gateway to Yellowstone known for its vibrant, historic downtown, its position at the base of the Beartooth Highway, and Red Lodge Mountain and the Beartooth Mountains.

## Who Should Move to Colstrip?

* Workers in the energy, mining, or industrial sectors looking for high-paying employment.
* Families seeking excellent, well-funded local schools and community parks.
* People looking for very affordable housing combined with high average salaries.
* Those who appreciate a practical, hardworking community with a strong industrial identity.

## Who Should Move to Red Lodge?

* Outdoor enthusiasts who want immediate access to skiing, hiking, and the Beartooth Wilderness.
* People seeking a lively, historic mountain town with a strong sense of community.
* Retirees and remote workers drawn to the spectacular scenery and small-town charm.
* Those who want to live in a gateway community to Yellowstone National Park.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Colstrip is around $219,986, while in Red Lodge it is closer to $550,000, a difference of roughly $330,014, making Colstrip significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Colstrip averages about $632 per month, compared to roughly $1,200 in Red Lodge.

Colstrip's overall cost of living differs from Red Lodge's. Colstrip offers higher average salaries compared to Red Lodge, with a median household income of $86,000 compared to Red Lodge's $58,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Colstrip is a unique, industrious town built specifically to support the local energy industry. It boasts surprisingly excellent parks, schools, and recreational facilities for its size, offering a practical, high-income lifestyle for those working in the energy sector. Red Lodge is a vibrant, historic mountain town that balances its role as a tourist destination with a strong, year-round local community. It offers spectacular access to the Beartooth Mountains and a lively downtown filled with shops, restaurants, and local events.

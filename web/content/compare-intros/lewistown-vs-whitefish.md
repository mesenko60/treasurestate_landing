# Lewistown vs Whitefish: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Lewistown and Whitefish represent perhaps the widest lifestyle and price gap of any comparison in this series. Lewistown is the Geographic Center of Montana, a quiet ranching community of 6,204 in Fergus County with a typical home value around $268,126 and a traditional small-town character. Whitefish is Stumptown, a resort town of 8,915 in Flathead County at 3,038 feet elevation, with 202 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 33 trailheads, 26 lakes, 19 ski areas, Glacier National Park nearby, and a typical home value of $840,135, one of the highest in the state.

## Who Should Move to Lewistown?

*   Retirees and remote workers who want a quiet, affordable small-town lifestyle at a fraction of Whitefish's price, with a typical home value around $268,126 versus $840,135
*   People who want a traditional, tight-knit ranching community with a genuine small-town character and none of the resort-town crowds or pricing
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 17 campgrounds, 7 fishing access sites, and the Big Snowy Mountains within 30 miles
*   Anyone who wants to live at the geographic heart of Montana, where the land is wide and the pace is genuinely slow

## Who Should Move to Whitefish?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 33 trailheads, 26 lakes, 22 fishing access sites, 19 ski areas, 46 campgrounds, and Glacier National Park all within 30 miles
*   Affluent buyers and resort-lifestyle seekers who want one of Montana's most desirable communities, with a median household income of $71,110 and a strong tourism economy
*   Skiers and mountain bikers who want Whitefish Mountain Resort as their home mountain
*   People who want a vibrant, walkable downtown with restaurants, galleries, and a year-round events calendar driven by both tourism and a tight-knit local community

## Cost of Living Breakdown

The gap between these two towns is extraordinary. The typical home value in Lewistown is around $268,126, compared to $840,135 in Whitefish, more than three times the price. Rent is equally dramatic: Lewistown averages about $896 per month, while Whitefish comes in at roughly $2,639, nearly three times as much.

The median household income in Whitefish is $71,110, compared to $44,195 in Lewistown. Whitefish residents earn considerably more on average, but the housing premium is so large that even high earners can find affordability challenging.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Lewistown is a town where the ranching calendar shapes daily life, the downtown has a quiet, historic character, and the wide-open central Montana landscape is the main draw. Whitefish is a resort town that has managed to retain a genuine community identity alongside its tourism economy, a place where the mountain and the lake define daily life and the downtown hums year-round. These two towns serve entirely different needs. If you want affordable, quiet, traditional Montana, Lewistown is one of the best values in the state. If you want one of Montana's most spectacular outdoor settings and are prepared to pay resort-market prices for it, Whitefish delivers in full.

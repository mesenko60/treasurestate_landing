# Columbus vs Sidney, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Columbus and Sidney offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Columbus is a scenic river town in the Stillwater Valley with 2,015 people, its position at the confluence of the Yellowstone and Stillwater rivers, its proximity to the Stillwater Mine, and quick access to the Absaroka-Beartooth Wilderness. Sidney, with 6,173 residents, is a bustling agricultural and energy hub known for its position in the Bakken oil field, its rich farming heritage in the Yellowstone Valley, and the Yellowstone River and eastern Montana badlands.

## Who Should Move to Columbus?

* Workers in the mining or industrial sectors looking for steady, high-paying employment.
* Anglers and boaters who want immediate access to two of Montana's premier rivers.
* People seeking a scenic, small-town lifestyle with easy highway access to the amenities of Billings.
* Outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the rugged beauty of the Absaroka-Beartooth Wilderness.

## Who Should Move to Sidney?

* Workers in the energy, agriculture, or transportation sectors looking for strong employment opportunities.
* People seeking a hardworking, practical community with a resilient local economy.
* Those who want affordable housing compared to western Montana, with higher average wages driven by the oil and gas industry.
* Hunters and anglers who appreciate the rugged, uncrowded access to the Yellowstone River and surrounding plains.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Columbus is around $428,574, while in Sidney it is closer to $241,500, a difference of roughly $187,074, making Sidney significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Columbus averages about $926 per month, compared to roughly $850 in Sidney.

Columbus's overall cost of living differs from Sidney's. Columbus offers lower average salaries compared to Sidney, with a median household income of $62,500 compared to Sidney's $75,200.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Columbus is a beautiful, thriving town that balances a strong industrial economy driven by the nearby palladium mine with spectacular outdoor recreation. It offers a relaxed, scenic lifestyle with excellent access to both the mountains and the city of Billings. Sidney is a dynamic, industrious town where the pace of life is closely tied to the energy markets and agricultural seasons. It has a practical, no-nonsense atmosphere, serving as a major economic engine for eastern Montana while maintaining its roots as a tight-knit farming community.

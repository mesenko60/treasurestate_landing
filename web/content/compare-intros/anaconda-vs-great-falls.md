# Great Falls vs Anaconda: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Great Falls and Anaconda are both historically significant Montana communities with working-class roots and genuine civic pride, but they occupy very different niches. Great Falls is the Electric City, a city of 60,000 on the Missouri River in central Montana, anchored by Malmstrom Air Force Base, the C.M. Russell Museum, and a river trail system that winds through town. Anaconda is the Smelter City, a small, mountain community of 9,421 in Deer Lodge County, where the copper-smelting past is still visible in the iconic smokestack that towers over town and the surrounding landscape offers 134 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles.

## Who Should Move to Great Falls?

*   Military families stationed at or connected to Malmstrom Air Force Base
*   Families and professionals who want a full-service city with a regional airport, larger job market, and a broader range of amenities
*   History and art lovers drawn to the C.M. Russell Museum and the Lewis and Clark National Historic Trail
*   People who want a larger community with more services, schools, and healthcare options

## Who Should Move to Anaconda?

*   Remote workers and retirees seeking some of Montana's most affordable housing in a scenic mountain setting
*   History enthusiasts drawn to Anaconda's copper-boom heritage and the Anaconda Smelter Stack State Park
*   Outdoor lovers who want hot springs at Fairmont, mountain lakes, wilderness, and ski access at a fraction of western Montana's typical cost
*   People who want a quiet, tight-knit community with a strong working-class identity and a fascinating industrial history

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Both cities are affordable by Montana standards, but Anaconda is the clear winner on housing costs. The median home price in Great Falls is around $328,554, while in Anaconda it's closer to $283,380, a difference of about $45,000. The rent gap is far more dramatic: about $1,340 per month in Great Falls versus just $661 in Anaconda, the lowest rent of any of the top 10 Montana towns.

Anaconda's median household income of $49,533 is significantly lower than Great Falls' $63,934, reflecting the limited local job market. For anyone with remote income or a pension, however, Anaconda's combination of low housing costs and 134 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles is a compelling proposition.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Great Falls is a working river city with a military heartbeat and a world-class art museum. It's practical, community-oriented, and proud of its Missouri River heritage. Anaconda is something more singular, a town that has made peace with its industrial past and found a new identity in its natural surroundings and its history. The Old Works Golf Course, built on the old smelter works by Jack Nicklaus, is a symbol of that reinvention. Both cities are authentically Montana; Great Falls offers more services and stability, while Anaconda offers more affordability, more mountain character, and a story that is genuinely unlike any other in the state.

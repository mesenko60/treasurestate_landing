# Malta vs Colstrip, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Malta and Colstrip offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Malta is a historic agricultural town on the Hi-Line with 2,100 people, its world-class dinosaur fossils, its deep ranching heritage, and quick access to the Bowdoin National Wildlife Refuge and the Missouri River Breaks. Colstrip, with 2,055 residents, is a purpose-built energy town in eastern Montana known for its massive coal-fired power plant, its excellent, company-built community amenities, and Castle Rock Lake and the surrounding pine hills.

## Who Should Move to Malta?

* Workers in agriculture, government, or local services looking for a quiet, stable community.
* People seeking very affordable housing and a low overall cost of living.
* Hunters, birders, and outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the vast, uncrowded public lands of northeastern Montana.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle on the high plains.

## Who Should Move to Colstrip?

* Workers in the energy, mining, or industrial sectors looking for high-paying employment.
* Families seeking excellent, well-funded local schools and community parks.
* People looking for very affordable housing combined with high average salaries.
* Those who appreciate a practical, hardworking community with a strong industrial identity.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Malta is around $244,540, while in Colstrip it is closer to $219,986, a difference of roughly $24,554, making Colstrip significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Malta averages about $693 per month, compared to roughly $632 in Colstrip.

Malta's overall cost of living differs from Colstrip's. Malta offers lower average salaries compared to Colstrip, with a median household income of $59,449 compared to Colstrip's $86,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Malta is a quiet, resilient community on the eastern Hi-Line, defined by agriculture, wide-open spaces, and its unique paleontology attractions. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle far from the state's urban centers. Colstrip is a unique, industrious town built specifically to support the local energy industry. It boasts surprisingly excellent parks, schools, and recreational facilities for its size, offering a practical, high-income lifestyle for those working in the energy sector.

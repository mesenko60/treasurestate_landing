# Havre vs Laurel, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Havre and Laurel offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Havre is a historic railroad and agricultural hub on the Hi-Line with 9,362 people, its deep roots in the railroad industry, the Havre Beneath the Streets underground tour, and quick access to the nearby Bear Paw Mountains and Fresno Reservoir. Laurel, with 7,215 residents, is a railroad and refinery town known for its industrial economy, its position as a transportation hub, and the Yellowstone River and nearby Beartooth Mountains.

## Who Should Move to Havre?

* Workers in agriculture, education, or the railroad industry looking for steady employment.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a community with all essential amenities.
* Those who appreciate the stark beauty of the northern plains and the close-knit feel of a Hi-Line town.
* History buffs interested in the unique underground history and nearby frontier battlefields.

## Who Should Move to Laurel?

* Industrial workers, railroad employees, and tradespeople looking for solid employment opportunities.
* Families who want a smaller school district and community feel while remaining just 15 minutes from Billings.
* Homebuyers seeking more affordable real estate than what is typically found in the Billings city limits.
* People who want easy access to the Beartooth Highway and the recreational opportunities south of the Yellowstone River.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Havre is around $224,252, while in Laurel it is closer to $369,826, a difference of roughly $145,574, making Havre significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Havre averages about $925 per month, compared to roughly $1,160 in Laurel.

Havre's overall cost of living differs from Laurel's. Havre offers lower average salaries compared to Laurel, with a median household income of $58,000 compared to Laurel's $66,382.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Havre is a resilient, hardworking community that serves as the commercial center for a vast stretch of northern Montana. It offers a practical, unpretentious lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture and the railroad. Laurel is a hardworking, blue-collar community where the local economy is driven by the CHS refinery and the Montana Rail Link yard. It offers a slower, more traditional small-town pace than Billings, but residents frequently commute to the larger city for shopping, dining, and entertainment.

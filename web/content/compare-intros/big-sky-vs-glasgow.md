# Glasgow vs Big Sky, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Glasgow and Big Sky offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Glasgow is a historic railroad town on the Hi-Line with 3,224 people, its position as a regional hub for northeastern Montana, its proximity to Fort Peck Dam, and quick access to Fort Peck Lake and the Charles M. Russell National Wildlife Refuge. Big Sky, with just 3,591 residents, is a premier alpine resort community known for the massive Big Sky Resort, its luxury real estate market, and Lone Mountain and the Gallatin Canyon.

## Who Should Move to Glasgow?

* Workers in agriculture, government, or the railroad industry looking for stable employment in northeastern Montana.
* Anglers and boaters who want to live near Fort Peck Lake, one of the premier walleye fisheries in the country.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, isolated community.
* Hunters and outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the vast, uncrowded public lands of the Missouri River Breaks.

## Who Should Move to Big Sky?

* Affluent buyers and second-home owners looking for luxury alpine real estate.
* Dedicated skiers and snowboarders who want access to some of the biggest and best terrain in North America.
* Hospitality and resort workers who are willing to navigate the high cost of living for the mountain lifestyle.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want to live in a high-altitude environment with immediate access to wilderness and Yellowstone National Park.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Glasgow is around $195,000, while in Big Sky it is closer to $1,819,905, a difference of roughly $1,624,905, making Glasgow significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Glasgow averages about $750 per month, compared to roughly $1,712 in Big Sky.

Glasgow's overall cost of living differs from Big Sky's. Glasgow offers lower average salaries compared to Big Sky, with a median household income of $55,000 compared to Big Sky's $103,625.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Glasgow is a resilient, isolated community that serves as the commercial and social center for a massive swath of northeastern Montana. It offers a quiet, slow-paced lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture, the railroad, and the incredible recreational opportunities at nearby Fort Peck Lake. Big Sky is not a traditional town but a sprawling, high-end resort community built around outdoor recreation and luxury living. The atmosphere is transient and seasonal, driven by tourism, skiing, and wealth, offering spectacular alpine access but lacking the historic, grounded feel of older Montana settlements.

# Big Timber vs Anaconda, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Big Timber and Anaconda offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Big Timber is a historic ranching community in the Sweet Grass Valley with 1,715 people, its deep, multi-generational ranching heritage, its classic, historic downtown architecture, and quick access to the Boulder River and the Crazy Mountains. Anaconda, with 9,491 residents, is a historic smelting town known for its towering smokestack, its working-class heritage, and Georgetown Lake and the Anaconda-Pintler Wilderness.

## Who Should Move to Big Timber?

* People seeking an authentic, traditional western community with a strong agricultural identity.
* Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want access to the pristine waters of the Boulder River.
* Those looking for a scenic, quiet lifestyle with relatively easy access to Bozeman or Billings.
* Retirees and remote workers drawn to the town's historic charm and spectacular mountain views.

## Who Should Move to Anaconda?

* Retirees and remote workers seeking very low housing costs.
* History buffs fascinated by Montana's copper era.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want uncrowded access to the Pintlers.
* People looking for a quiet, slow-paced mountain community.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Big Timber is around $422,291, while in Anaconda it is closer to $210,000, a difference of roughly $212,291, making Anaconda significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Big Timber averages about $1,009 per month, compared to roughly $750 in Anaconda.

Big Timber's overall cost of living differs from Anaconda's. Big Timber offers higher average salaries compared to Anaconda, with a median household income of $63,514 compared to Anaconda's $48,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Big Timber is a quintessential Montana ranching town, fiercely protective of its agricultural roots and stunning natural surroundings. It offers a quiet, authentic western lifestyle set against the dramatic backdrop of the Crazy Mountains. Anaconda is a quiet, resilient town with a deep industrial history, offering incredibly affordable access to some of Montana's best mountain recreation.

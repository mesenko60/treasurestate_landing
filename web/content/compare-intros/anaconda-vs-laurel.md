# Laurel vs Anaconda: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Laurel and Anaconda are both working-class Montana communities with affordable housing and strong outdoor access, but they sit in very different landscapes and carry very different histories. Laurel is the City Between the Yellowstone and the Rimrocks, a community of 7,215 in the Yellowstone River valley with 21 fishing access sites within 30 miles and a warm, dry central Montana climate. Anaconda is the Smelter City, a community of 9,421 in Deer Lodge County at 5,279 feet elevation, with 48 lakes within 30 miles, the world-famous Old Works Golf Course, Fairmont Hot Springs, and one of the most dramatic industrial histories in the American West.

## Who Should Move to Laurel?

*   Families and buyers who want central Montana living with a typical home value around $369,826 and easy access to Billings 18 miles away
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 21 fishing access sites and 6 state parks along the Yellowstone River corridor
*   People who want a warmer, drier climate, with July highs averaging 88°F versus Anaconda's 78°F and annual snowfall of just 15 inches versus Anaconda's 25 inches
*   Anyone who wants a quiet, practical small-town community near a major Montana city

## Who Should Move to Anaconda?

*   Budget-conscious buyers and retirees who want western Montana at a dramatically lower price, with a typical home value around $283,380 and rent averaging just $661 per month
*   History enthusiasts drawn to Anaconda's copper smelting heritage, the Anaconda Smelter Stack State Park, and 16 museums within 30 miles
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 48 lakes, 19 campgrounds, Fairmont Hot Springs, and a ski area all within 30 miles
*   Remote workers and retirees who want affordable, scenic western Montana without the resort-corridor price tag

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Anaconda is dramatically more affordable than Laurel on both housing and rent. The typical home value in Laurel is around $369,826, compared to $283,380 in Anaconda, a gap of nearly $87,000. The rent gap is even more striking: Laurel averages about $1,160 per month, while Anaconda comes in at roughly $661, less than half the price.

The income gap is significant. The median household income in Laurel is $66,382, compared to $49,533 in Anaconda. Laurel residents earn considerably more on average, but Anaconda's cost of living is so low that everyday expenses are a fraction of what they are in the Yellowstone corridor.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Laurel is a quiet, practical river town where the Yellowstone flows nearby and Billings is always close. Anaconda is a small city that has reinvented itself after the closure of the copper smelter, with a strong community identity, a remarkable outdoor footprint anchored by 48 lakes and Fairmont Hot Springs, and a cost of living that makes it genuinely attractive for people who want western Montana without the resort-corridor price. If you want central Montana living near Billings, Laurel is the practical choice. If you want affordable, historically rich small-town life in a spectacular mountain setting, Anaconda is one of Montana's most underrated communities.

# Wolf Point vs Livingston, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Wolf Point and Livingston offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Wolf Point is a historic agricultural town on the Fort Peck Indian Reservation with 2,517 people, its position as the largest community on the reservation, its deep roots in farming and ranching, and quick access to the Missouri River and the vast eastern plains. Livingston, with just 8,386 residents, is a historic railroad and arts town known for its literary heritage, its vibrant arts scene, and the Yellowstone River and Paradise Valley.

## Who Should Move to Wolf Point?

* People seeking some of the most affordable housing in the state.
* Workers in agriculture, tribal government, or local services.
* Those who appreciate a quiet, isolated lifestyle on the high plains.
* Hunters and anglers who want uncrowded access to the Missouri River and surrounding lands.

## Who Should Move to Livingston?

* Artists, writers, and creative professionals.
* Fly fishing enthusiasts who want to live on the Yellowstone River.
* People who want a small-town atmosphere with easy access to Bozeman.
* Those who appreciate historic downtown architecture.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Wolf Point is around $145,000, while in Livingston it is closer to $509,477, a difference of roughly $364,477, making Wolf Point significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Wolf Point averages about $600 per month, compared to roughly $1,783 in Livingston.

Wolf Point's overall cost of living differs from Livingston's. Wolf Point offers lower average salaries compared to Livingston, with a median household income of $42,000 compared to Livingston's $65,187.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Wolf Point is a quiet, resilient community deeply tied to the Fort Peck Indian Reservation and the agricultural economy of northeastern Montana. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle far removed from the state's major urban centers. Livingston is a windy, eclectic town that blends its gritty railroad past with a sophisticated arts community, serving as the scenic gateway to Yellowstone.

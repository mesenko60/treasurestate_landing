# Bigfork vs Butte: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Bigfork and Butte are both western Montana communities, but they sit in very different landscapes and carry very different price tags. Bigfork is a Village by the Bay, a small community of 5,000 on the eastern shore of Flathead Lake in Flathead County at 2,940 feet elevation, with 176 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 32 lakes, 27 campgrounds, and Glacier National Park nearby. Butte is the Richest Hill on Earth, a community of 34,494 in Silver Bow County at 5,741 feet, with 94 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 17 museums, 14 campgrounds, 4 hot springs, and a copper mining history that shaped the entire American West.

## Who Should Move to Bigfork?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 27 campgrounds, 25 trailheads, 28 fishing access sites, 32 lakes, 16 state parks, and Glacier National Park all within 30 miles
*   Affluent buyers and retirees who want a small, scenic lakeside community with a median household income of $82,648 and a mild climate, with January lows averaging 23°F versus Butte's 10°F
*   People who want a genuine village atmosphere on Flathead Lake with a summer arts festival, a community theater, and one of the most beautiful natural settings in Montana
*   Anyone who wants western Montana's mountain and lake scenery as their daily backdrop

## Who Should Move to Butte?

*   Budget-conscious buyers who want western Montana at a dramatically lower price, with a typical home value of $274,163 versus Bigfork's $758,435, less than half the cost
*   History enthusiasts drawn to Butte's extraordinary copper mining heritage, the Berkeley Pit, the Butte Historic District, 17 museums, and one of the most intact Victorian mining-era downtowns in the American West
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 14 campgrounds, 12 fishing access sites, 4 hot springs, 13 lakes, and a national forest all within 30 miles
*   People who want a mid-sized Montana community with a strong sense of place, a fiercely independent character, and easy access to Anaconda, Deer Lodge, and the Bitterroot Valley

## Cost of Living Breakdown

The gap between these two towns is very large. The typical home value in Bigfork is around $758,435, compared to $274,163 in Butte, nearly three times the price. Rent is lower in Bigfork: Bigfork averages about $1,103 per month, while Butte comes in at roughly $1,318.

The median household income in Bigfork is $82,648, compared to $57,633 in Butte. Bigfork residents earn considerably more on average, but the home price difference is so large that Butte offers one of the most accessible paths to homeownership in western Montana.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Bigfork is a small lakeside village where the arts, outdoor recreation, and Flathead Lake define daily life. Butte is a city with a story unlike any other in Montana, a place where the copper industry built an ornate Victorian downtown, the labor movement found some of its most passionate advocates, and the community has maintained a stubborn pride and a distinct Irish-Catholic identity that sets it apart from every other Montana city. If you want a quiet, scenic village on one of Montana's most beautiful lakes, Bigfork is exceptional. If you want western Montana at a fraction of the price, with one of the most fascinating historical identities in the American West, Butte is one of Montana's most underrated communities.

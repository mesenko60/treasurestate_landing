# Cut Bank vs Butte, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Cut Bank and Butte offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Cut Bank is a windy agricultural town on the Hi-Line with 3,056 people, its proximity to the Blackfeet Indian Reservation, its agricultural and energy economy, and quick access to the eastern edge of Glacier National Park. Butte, with just 34,768 residents, is a historic mining city known for its rich labor history, its stunning historic architecture, and the Continental Divide and surrounding national forests.

## Who Should Move to Cut Bank?

* Workers in agriculture, energy, or government looking for affordable living on the Hi-Line.
* People seeking a quiet, resilient community with a low cost of living.
* Those who want to live near Glacier National Park without paying resort-town prices.
* Individuals who appreciate the stark, windy beauty of the northern plains.

## Who Should Move to Butte?

* History lovers who appreciate historic homes and architecture.
* People looking for very affordable mountain living.
* Those who want a town with a strong, independent character.
* Engineering students attending Montana Tech.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Cut Bank is around $175,000, while in Butte it is closer to $250,000, a difference of roughly $75,000, making Cut Bank significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Cut Bank averages about $650 per month, compared to roughly $800 in Butte.

Cut Bank's overall cost of living differs from Butte's. Cut Bank offers higher average salaries compared to Butte, with a median household income of $52,000 compared to Butte's $50,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Cut Bank is a tough, practical town known for its wind and its hardworking residents. It serves as a gateway between the vast agricultural plains of the Hi-Line and the dramatic peaks of the Rocky Mountain Front, offering a quiet, affordable lifestyle. Butte is fiercely proud of its working-class roots, offering a unique, historic atmosphere that is distinct from the rest of the state.

# Columbus vs Shelby, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Columbus and Shelby offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Columbus is a scenic river town in the Stillwater Valley with 2,015 people, its position at the confluence of the Yellowstone and Stillwater rivers, its proximity to the Stillwater Mine, and quick access to the Absaroka-Beartooth Wilderness. Shelby, with 3,169 residents, is a historic railroad and agricultural town known for its position on the Hi-Line, its famous 1923 Dempsey-Gibbons heavyweight title fight, and the Sweet Grass Hills and Lake Elwell.

## Who Should Move to Columbus?

* Workers in the mining or industrial sectors looking for steady, high-paying employment.
* Anglers and boaters who want immediate access to two of Montana's premier rivers.
* People seeking a scenic, small-town lifestyle with easy highway access to the amenities of Billings.
* Outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the rugged beauty of the Absaroka-Beartooth Wilderness.

## Who Should Move to Shelby?

* Workers in the agriculture, railroad, or energy sectors looking for steady employment on the Hi-Line.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, rural setting.
* Those who appreciate the stark beauty of the northern plains and the close-knit community of a small agricultural town.
* History buffs interested in the unique legacy of the 1923 heavyweight championship fight.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Columbus is around $428,574, while in Shelby it is closer to $185,000, a difference of roughly $243,574, making Shelby significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Columbus averages about $926 per month, compared to roughly $700 in Shelby.

Columbus's overall cost of living differs from Shelby's. Columbus offers higher average salaries compared to Shelby, with a median household income of $62,500 compared to Shelby's $58,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Columbus is a beautiful, thriving town that balances a strong industrial economy driven by the nearby palladium mine with spectacular outdoor recreation. It offers a relaxed, scenic lifestyle with excellent access to both the mountains and the city of Billings. Shelby is a resilient, hardworking town on the northern plains, where life is shaped by the railroad, agriculture, and the vast open spaces of the Hi-Line. It offers a quiet, practical lifestyle far removed from the state's major urban centers.

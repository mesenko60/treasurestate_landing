# Billings vs Bozeman: Which Montana City is Right for You?

Billings and Bozeman are the two cities people most often debate when relocating to Montana, and for good reason, they represent two very different versions of what Montana life can look like. Billings is the Magic City, Montana's largest city at 117,116 people, a practical, commerce-driven hub with affordable housing and a strong job market. Bozeman is the Gallatin Gateway, a fast-growing university and outdoor recreation mecca of 58,000 that has become one of the most in-demand small cities in the American West, with housing prices to match.

## Who Should Move to Billings?

*   Professionals in healthcare, energy, agriculture, or logistics who want Montana's strongest job market
*   Families who want a full-service city with excellent schools, major hospitals, and lower housing costs
*   People who prefer a warmer, drier climate and easy access to Yellowstone Country and the Beartooths
*   Anyone who wants city-level amenities without the sticker shock of Montana's resort-adjacent towns

## Who Should Move to Bozeman?

*   Young professionals, tech workers, and entrepreneurs drawn to Bozeman's booming economy and startup culture
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want Bridger Bowl and Big Sky skiing, Gallatin River fishing, and endless trails at their doorstep
*   University of Montana State students, faculty, and the broader academic community
*   People who want a vibrant, walkable downtown with upscale dining, craft breweries, and a cosmopolitan feel

## Cost of Living Breakdown

The housing gap between these two cities is one of the starkest in Montana. The median home price in Billings is around $390,654, already a significant number, but in Bozeman it climbs to $715,781, nearly double. Rent follows the same trajectory: about $1,383 per month in Billings versus $2,113 in Bozeman.

Bozeman does offer higher salaries to partially offset those costs, with a median household income of $79,903 compared to Billings' $71,855. But the math still heavily favors Billings for anyone prioritizing financial breathing room.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Billings is a working Montana city, practical, unpretentious, and proud of it. It has everything a family or professional needs without the pressure of keeping up with a rapidly gentrifying real estate market. Bozeman is a different animal entirely: ambitious, outdoorsy, and increasingly expensive, with a downtown that feels more like a destination than a neighborhood. If you want Montana with room to breathe financially, Billings is the answer. If you want Montana at its most dynamic and are willing to pay for it, Bozeman is hard to argue with.

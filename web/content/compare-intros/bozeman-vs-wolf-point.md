# Wolf Point vs Bozeman, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Wolf Point and Bozeman offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Wolf Point is a historic agricultural town on the Fort Peck Indian Reservation with 2,517 people, its position as the largest community on the reservation, its deep roots in farming and ranching, and quick access to the Missouri River and the vast eastern plains. Bozeman, with just 54,539 residents, is a booming tech and university hub known for Montana State University, its high-tech economy, and the Bridger Mountains and Big Sky.

## Who Should Move to Wolf Point?

* People seeking some of the most affordable housing in the state.
* Workers in agriculture, tribal government, or local services.
* Those who appreciate a quiet, isolated lifestyle on the high plains.
* Hunters and anglers who want uncrowded access to the Missouri River and surrounding lands.

## Who Should Move to Bozeman?

* Tech workers and entrepreneurs.
* Students and faculty at MSU.
* Affluent outdoor enthusiasts.
* People who want a highly active, progressive mountain town.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Wolf Point is around $145,000, while in Bozeman it is closer to $750,000, a difference of roughly $605,000, making Wolf Point significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Wolf Point averages about $600 per month, compared to roughly $2,000 in Bozeman.

Wolf Point's overall cost of living differs from Bozeman's. Wolf Point offers lower average salaries compared to Bozeman, with a median household income of $42,000 compared to Bozeman's $70,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Wolf Point is a quiet, resilient community deeply tied to the Fort Peck Indian Reservation and the agricultural economy of northeastern Montana. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle far removed from the state's major urban centers. Bozeman is fast-paced, highly educated, and outdoor-obsessed, with a premium cost of living and a constant influx of new residents.

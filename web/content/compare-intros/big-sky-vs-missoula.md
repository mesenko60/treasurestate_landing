# Big Sky vs Missoula: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Big Sky and Missoula both attract outdoor enthusiasts, but they offer fundamentally different Montana lifestyles. Big Sky is the Biggest Skiing in America, a resort community of 3,591 in Gallatin County at 6,319 feet, known for Big Sky Resort, 23 trailheads, 17 waterfalls, 18 lakes, 3 hot springs, 2 wilderness areas, and 140 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles. Missoula is the Garden City, a university city of 77,757 in Missoula County at 3,215 feet in western Montana, with 211 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 65 fishing access sites, 49 trailheads, 22 campgrounds, 13 lakes, 10 museums, 9 historic sites, 8 state parks, 3 hot springs, 2 wilderness areas, a national forest, a ski area, Missoula Montana Airport 7 miles away, and a 9,000-student school district.

## Who Should Move to Big Sky?

*   Skiers, snowboarders, and mountain sports enthusiasts who want Big Sky Resort, one of the largest ski areas in North America with over 5,800 acres of skiable terrain, right in their backyard
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 23 trailheads, 17 waterfalls, 18 lakes, 19 fishing access sites, 3 hot springs, 2 wilderness areas, and a national forest all within 30 miles
*   High-income remote workers and affluent buyers who can afford Big Sky's extraordinary real estate market, with a typical home value of $1,819,905 and a median household income of $103,625
*   People who want a small, exclusive resort community lifestyle where outdoor recreation is the primary focus and the mountains define every aspect of daily life

## Who Should Move to Missoula?

*   University community enthusiasts, young professionals, and families who want the energy of a college town with the University of Montana, a vibrant arts scene, live music, craft breweries, and a bustling downtown
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 65 fishing access sites, 49 trailheads, 22 campgrounds, 13 lakes, 8 state parks, 3 hot springs, 2 wilderness areas, a national forest, and a ski area all within 30 miles
*   People who want a full-service western Montana city with Missoula Montana Airport 7 miles away, a 9,000-student school district, 10 museums, and a median household income of $65,329
*   Anyone who wants a lively, progressive Montana city with a strong arts and culture scene, weekly farmers markets, independent bookstores, and a community that feels genuinely engaged with its surroundings

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Big Sky is dramatically more expensive than Missoula. The typical home value in Big Sky is $1,819,905, compared to $554,389 in Missoula, more than three times higher. Rent is actually lower in Big Sky: $1,712 per month versus $1,486 in Missoula, a modest difference that reflects Big Sky's smaller rental market.

Median household income is higher in Big Sky at $103,625 versus $65,329 in Missoula. Even with significantly higher incomes, Big Sky's home prices are in a completely different category. Missoula is expensive by Montana standards, but it is dramatically more accessible than Big Sky for most buyers.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Big Sky is a world-class mountain resort community where the ski mountain, the wilderness, and a high-income outdoor lifestyle define daily life. Missoula is a vibrant university city where the Clark Fork River, the surrounding mountains, the University of Montana, and a strong arts and culture scene define community identity. Missoula has more total outdoor recreation sites than Big Sky, including 65 fishing access sites and 49 trailheads, but Big Sky has 17 waterfalls, 3 hot springs, and a ski resort that Missoula cannot match. If you want a resort lifestyle in the mountains with world-class skiing and wilderness access, Big Sky is extraordinary. If you want a lively western Montana city with a university, a thriving downtown, and outstanding outdoor access at a fraction of the cost, Missoula is one of the most livable cities in the Mountain West.

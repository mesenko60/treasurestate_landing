# Missoula vs Butte: Which Montana City is Right for You?

Missoula and Butte are both proud western Montana cities with strong identities, but they occupy very different places in the state's story. Missoula is the Garden City, a progressive university town of 77,757 where the Clark Fork River anchors a vibrant downtown and the outdoor recreation inventory is among the richest in the state. Butte is the Richest Hill on Earth, a gritty, historically extraordinary mining city of 34,494 perched at 5,741 feet, with a copper-boom architectural legacy, a fierce civic pride, and some of the most affordable housing of any Montana city its size.

## Who Should Move to Missoula?

*   Students, academics, and professionals tied to the University of Montana and its surrounding economy
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 65 fishing access sites, 49 trailheads, and 2 wilderness areas within 30 miles
*   People who value a progressive, culturally active city with a strong arts scene and independent businesses
*   Anyone who wants a walkable, river-centered downtown with a genuine sense of community

## Who Should Move to Butte?

*   History lovers and architecture enthusiasts drawn to Butte's extraordinary Victorian-era Uptown district
*   Remote workers and retirees seeking affordable housing in a mountain city with real character and community pride
*   People who want access to hot springs, mountain lakes, and wilderness without paying Missoula prices
*   Anyone who appreciates a working-class Montana city that has survived hard times with its identity intact

## Cost of Living Breakdown

The housing gap between these two cities is substantial. The median home price in Missoula is around $554,389, while in Butte it's closer to $274,163, exactly half the cost. Rent is also significantly lower in Butte at about $1,318 per month versus $1,486 in Missoula.

Missoula's median household income of $65,329 is higher than Butte's $57,633, but not by enough to close the gap in housing costs. For anyone with location-independent income, Butte's affordability is a compelling argument.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Missoula is polished and progressive, a city that takes its coffee, its trails, and its politics seriously. Butte is something else entirely, rawer, prouder, and more complicated. The Berkeley Pit, the Uptown historic district, the St. Patrick's Day parade that draws thousands to a city of 34,000, Butte has a mythology that Missoula simply doesn't. Both cities are authentically Montana, but Missoula is the university town and Butte is the mining town, and that distinction runs deeper than just history.

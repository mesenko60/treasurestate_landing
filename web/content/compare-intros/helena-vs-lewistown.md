# Lewistown vs Helena: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Lewistown and Helena are both central Montana communities, but they differ substantially in size, price, and the kind of life they offer. Lewistown is the Geographic Center of Montana, a quiet ranching community of 6,204 in Fergus County with affordable housing, 17 campgrounds within 30 miles, and a traditional small-town character. Helena is the Queen City of the Rockies, Montana's state capital with 33,100 residents in Lewis and Clark County at 4,039 feet elevation, with 107 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 10 historic sites, 28 campgrounds, and a civic identity shaped by gold rush history and state government.

## Who Should Move to Lewistown?

*   Retirees and remote workers who want a quiet, affordable small-town lifestyle with a typical home value around $268,126 compared to Helena's $466,393
*   People who want a traditional, tight-knit ranching community with a genuine small-town character and no state-capital bustle
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 17 campgrounds, 7 fishing access sites, and the Big Snowy Mountains within 30 miles
*   Anyone who wants to live at the geographic heart of Montana, where the pace is slow and the landscape is wide open

## Who Should Move to Helena?

*   State government employees, lobbyists, and public sector professionals drawn to Montana's capital city and its stable employment base
*   History enthusiasts who want 10 historic sites, 9 museums, and the Cathedral of Saint Helena all within 30 miles
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 28 campgrounds, 11 trailheads, 10 fishing access sites, 3 hot springs, and a national forest within 30 miles
*   Families who want a mid-sized Montana city with a 7,800-student school district, a regional airport, and full urban services

## Cost of Living Breakdown

The gap between these two towns is significant. The typical home value in Lewistown is around $268,126, compared to $466,393 in Helena, a difference of nearly $200,000. Rent diverges similarly: Lewistown averages about $896 per month, while Helena comes in at roughly $1,489, more than $590 more per month.

The income gap is notable. The median household income in Helena is $69,341, compared to $44,195 in Lewistown. Helena residents earn considerably more, driven in part by state government salaries and a broader professional economy, but the housing premium is substantial.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Lewistown is a town where the ranching calendar shapes daily life, the downtown has a quiet, historic character, and the wide-open central Montana landscape is the main draw. Helena is a capital city with a gold rush past, a Victorian downtown that has been carefully preserved, and a community that takes genuine pride in its role as the seat of Montana government. If you want affordable, quiet, traditional small-town Montana, Lewistown is one of the best values in the state. If you want a mid-sized Montana city with a rich history, a strong outdoor recreation footprint, and the stability of a government-anchored economy, Helena is a compelling choice.

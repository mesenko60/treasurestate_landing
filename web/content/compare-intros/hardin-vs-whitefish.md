# Hardin vs Whitefish: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Hardin and Whitefish are two Montana communities that could not be more different in price, character, or setting. Hardin is Where the Rivers Meet, a small Big Horn County community of 3,742 at 2,910 feet in eastern Montana, known for its proximity to the Little Bighorn Battlefield, the Bighorn River, 10 fishing access sites, 4 historic sites, and a quiet agricultural lifestyle. Whitefish is Stumptown, a northwest Montana resort community of 8,915 in Flathead County at 3,038 feet, with 202 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 46 campgrounds, 33 trailheads, 26 lakes, 22 fishing access sites, 19 ski areas, 8 state parks, 8 museums, 8 nature reserves, 7 golf courses, 7 waterfalls, 4 disc golf courses, 4 boat launches, 4 historic sites, 3 viewpoints, a national forest, a national park, a river, Glacier Park International Airport 9 miles away, and a 1,800-student school district.

## Who Should Move to Hardin?

*   History enthusiasts drawn to the Little Bighorn Battlefield National Monument 14 miles away, one of the most significant and evocative historic sites in the American West
*   Anglers who want 10 fishing access sites and the Bighorn River, one of Montana's premier blue-ribbon trout streams, within easy reach
*   Remote workers, retirees, and families who want a very affordable small-town lifestyle in eastern Montana, with a typical home value of just $204,573 and rent averaging $726 per month
*   People who want a quiet, traditional eastern Montana community with a strong Native American cultural heritage and a slower pace of life

## Who Should Move to Whitefish?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts and resort lifestyle seekers who want 46 campgrounds, 33 trailheads, 26 lakes, 22 fishing access sites, 19 ski areas, 8 state parks, 7 waterfalls, a national forest, and Glacier National Park all within 30 miles
*   Skiers and mountain sports enthusiasts who want Whitefish Mountain Resort, one of Montana's premier ski destinations, combined with a vibrant resort town with excellent dining, galleries, and a lively downtown
*   Affluent buyers and remote workers who can afford Whitefish's premium pricing, with a typical home value of $840,135 and a median household income of $71,110
*   Families and professionals who want Glacier Park International Airport 9 miles away, a 1,800-student school district, and a community that combines world-class outdoor recreation with a sophisticated small-town lifestyle

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Whitefish is dramatically more expensive than Hardin. The typical home value in Hardin is around $204,573, compared to $840,135 in Whitefish, more than four times higher. Rent is also dramatically higher in Whitefish: Hardin averages about $726 per month, while Whitefish comes in at roughly $2,639, more than three and a half times more.

Median household income is higher in Whitefish at $71,110 versus $60,463 in Hardin. Even with higher incomes, Whitefish's housing costs are extraordinary. Hardin is dramatically more affordable for both buyers and renters, making it one of the most affordable communities in Montana.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Hardin is a small eastern Montana town where the Bighorn River, the Little Bighorn Battlefield, and a quiet agricultural and ranching community define daily life. Whitefish is a northwest Montana resort town where Glacier National Park, Whitefish Mountain Resort, and a sophisticated outdoor and tourism economy define community life. Whitefish has a density of outdoor access, lakes, ski areas, and mountain scenery that is among the finest in North America, and its combination of a vibrant downtown with world-class skiing and Glacier National Park nearby makes it one of the most desirable small towns in the American West. If you want a very affordable small-town lifestyle with access to one of America's great historic battlefields and excellent trout fishing, Hardin is a compelling choice. If you want northwest Montana's premier resort community with 19 ski areas, 26 lakes, 33 trailheads, and Glacier National Park at your doorstep, Whitefish is in a class of its own.

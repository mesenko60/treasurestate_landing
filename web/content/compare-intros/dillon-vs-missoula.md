# Dillon vs Missoula: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Dillon and Missoula offer two very different versions of western Montana life. Dillon is Montana's Hometown, a small Beaverhead County community of 3,880 at 5,095 feet, known for its quiet ranching character, the Beaverhead River, 24 fishing access sites, 16 campgrounds, 5 state parks, 2 hot springs, and the University of Montana Western. Missoula is the Garden City, a vibrant university city of 77,757 in Missoula County at 3,215 feet, with 211 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 65 fishing access sites, 49 trailheads, 22 campgrounds, 13 lakes, 10 museums, 9 historic sites, 8 state parks, 2 hot springs, a national forest, 2 wilderness areas, and Missoula Montana Airport 7 miles away.

## Who Should Move to Dillon?

*   Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want 24 fishing access sites, 16 campgrounds, 5 state parks, 2 hot springs, 8 lakes, and the Beaverhead River all within 30 miles
*   Remote workers, retirees, and families who want a genuinely quiet, small-town Montana lifestyle at a very affordable price, with a typical home value of $375,585 and rent averaging just $821 per month
*   People who want a small university town with the University of Montana Western providing community events, sports, and cultural programming
*   Anyone drawn to southwestern Montana's wide-open spaces, ranching culture, and the Big Hole Valley just to the west

## Who Should Move to Missoula?

*   Young professionals, students, and families who want a vibrant university city with the University of Montana, a lively downtown, craft breweries, live music, weekly farmers markets, and a strong arts and culture scene
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 65 fishing access sites, 49 trailheads, 22 campgrounds, 13 lakes, 2 hot springs, a national forest, and 2 wilderness areas all within 30 miles
*   People who want a mid-sized western Montana city with a 9,000-student school district, Missoula Montana Airport 7 miles away, and a strong job market anchored by the University of Montana and a growing healthcare sector
*   Anyone who wants western Montana's most dynamic and culturally active community

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Missoula is considerably more expensive than Dillon. The typical home value in Dillon is around $375,585, compared to $554,389 in Missoula, about 48% higher. Rent is dramatically higher in Missoula: Dillon averages about $821 per month, while Missoula comes in at roughly $1,486, nearly double.

Median household income is higher in Missoula at $65,329 versus $51,458 in Dillon. Even with higher incomes, Missoula's housing costs are substantially greater. Dillon is dramatically more affordable for both buyers and renters.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Dillon is a small southwestern Montana town where the Beaverhead River, ranching culture, and the University of Montana Western define community life. Missoula is a mid-sized university city where the Clark Fork River, the University of Montana, and a strong arts and outdoor culture define daily life. Missoula has a density of activity and culture that Dillon does not, with busy sidewalks, a thriving restaurant scene, and constant community events. If you want a quiet, affordable southwestern Montana lifestyle with excellent fishing and outdoor access, Dillon is a compelling choice. If you want western Montana's most vibrant and culturally active city, Missoula is one of the most livable mid-sized cities in the American West.

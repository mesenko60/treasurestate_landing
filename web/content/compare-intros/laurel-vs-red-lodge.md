# Red Lodge vs Laurel, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Red Lodge and Laurel offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Red Lodge is a historic mountain town and gateway to Yellowstone with 2,336 people, its vibrant, historic downtown, its position at the base of the Beartooth Highway, and quick access to Red Lodge Mountain and the Beartooth Mountains. Laurel, with just 7,215 residents, is a railroad and refinery town known for its industrial economy, its position as a transportation hub, and the Yellowstone River and nearby Beartooth Mountains.

## Who Should Move to Red Lodge?

* Outdoor enthusiasts who want immediate access to skiing, hiking, and the Beartooth Wilderness.
* People seeking a lively, historic mountain town with a strong sense of community.
* Retirees and remote workers drawn to the spectacular scenery and small-town charm.
* Those who want to live in a gateway community to Yellowstone National Park.

## Who Should Move to Laurel?

* Industrial workers, railroad employees, and tradespeople looking for solid employment opportunities.
* Families who want a smaller school district and community feel while remaining just 15 minutes from Billings.
* Homebuyers seeking more affordable real estate than what is typically found in the Billings city limits.
* People who want easy access to the Beartooth Highway and the recreational opportunities south of the Yellowstone River.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Red Lodge is around $550,000, while in Laurel it is closer to $369,826, a difference of roughly $180,174, making Laurel significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Red Lodge averages about $1,200 per month, compared to roughly $1,160 in Laurel.

Red Lodge's overall cost of living differs from Laurel's. Red Lodge offers lower average salaries compared to Laurel, with a median household income of $58,000 compared to Laurel's $66,382.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Red Lodge is a vibrant, historic mountain town that balances its role as a tourist destination with a strong, year-round local community. It offers spectacular access to the Beartooth Mountains and a lively downtown filled with shops, restaurants, and local events. Laurel is a hardworking, blue-collar community where the local economy is driven by the CHS refinery and the Montana Rail Link yard. It offers a slower, more traditional small-town pace than Billings, but residents frequently commute to the larger city for shopping, dining, and entertainment.

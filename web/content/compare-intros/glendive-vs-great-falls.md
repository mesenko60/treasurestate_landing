# Glendive vs Great Falls: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Glendive and Great Falls are both mid-sized Montana communities with similar climates and a shared eastern plains character, but they differ significantly in scale and setting. Glendive is the Paddlefish Capital of the World, a community of 4,873 in Dawson County at 2,083 feet elevation in eastern Montana, with 30 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles and Makoshika State Park at its edge. Great Falls is the Electric City, a community of 60,000 in Cascade County at 3,340 feet in central Montana, with 45 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 10 museums, 8 fishing access sites, 6 waterfalls, 5 state parks, 2 rivers, and Malmstrom Air Force Base as a major economic anchor.

## Who Should Move to Glendive?

*   Budget-conscious buyers who want Montana at a very low price, with a typical home value of $185,447 and median rent of just $832 per month, compared to Great Falls' $328,554 and $1,340
*   Outdoor enthusiasts and anglers who want Makoshika State Park, the Yellowstone River, and some of the best paddlefish snagging in the country right in their backyard
*   People who want a small, quiet eastern Montana community with a genuine small-town character and a slower pace of life
*   Remote workers or retirees who want affordable housing and a community where $71,063 in median household income goes a very long way

## Who Should Move to Great Falls?

*   Military families and professionals who want a mid-sized Montana city anchored by Malmstrom Air Force Base, with a 10,000-student school district and Great Falls International Airport 5 miles away
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want the Missouri River, 8 fishing access sites, 6 waterfalls, 5 state parks, Giant Springs State Park, and the Lewis and Clark National Historic Trail all within 30 miles
*   Budget-conscious buyers who want a mid-sized Montana city at a moderate price, with a typical home value of $328,554, well below Bozeman or Missoula
*   History enthusiasts drawn to the C.M. Russell Museum, the Lewis and Clark Interpretive Center, and a community shaped by the Missouri River and the great falls that stopped the Lewis and Clark Expedition for a month

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Glendive is considerably more affordable than Great Falls. The typical home value in Glendive is around $185,447, compared to $328,554 in Great Falls, nearly double. Rent follows the same pattern: Glendive averages about $832 per month, while Great Falls comes in at roughly $1,340.

Median household income is higher in Glendive at $71,063 versus $63,934 in Great Falls. Glendive residents earn more on average and pay far less for housing, giving it one of the better affordability ratios in Montana.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Glendive is a small eastern Montana town where the Yellowstone River, Makoshika State Park, and the paddlefish season define the calendar. Great Falls is a mid-sized central Montana city where the Missouri River, the military, and a strong arts heritage centered on C.M. Russell define community life. If you want affordability and small-town quiet in eastern Montana, Glendive is hard to beat. If you want a mid-sized Montana city with more services, more employment options, and a river running through it, Great Falls is a solid and often underappreciated choice.

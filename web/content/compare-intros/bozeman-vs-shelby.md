# Shelby vs Bozeman, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Shelby and Bozeman offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Shelby is a historic railroad and agricultural town with 3,169 people, its position on the Hi-Line, its famous 1923 Dempsey-Gibbons heavyweight title fight, and quick access to the Sweet Grass Hills and Lake Elwell. Bozeman, with just 54,539 residents, is a booming tech and university hub known for Montana State University, its high-tech economy, and the Bridger Mountains and Big Sky.

## Who Should Move to Shelby?

* Workers in the agriculture, railroad, or energy sectors looking for steady employment on the Hi-Line.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, rural setting.
* Those who appreciate the stark beauty of the northern plains and the close-knit community of a small agricultural town.
* History buffs interested in the unique legacy of the 1923 heavyweight championship fight.

## Who Should Move to Bozeman?

* Tech workers and entrepreneurs.
* Students and faculty at MSU.
* Affluent outdoor enthusiasts.
* People who want a highly active, progressive mountain town.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Shelby is around $185,000, while in Bozeman it is closer to $750,000, a difference of roughly $565,000, making Shelby significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Shelby averages about $700 per month, compared to roughly $2,000 in Bozeman.

Shelby's overall cost of living differs from Bozeman's. Shelby offers lower average salaries compared to Bozeman, with a median household income of $58,000 compared to Bozeman's $70,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Shelby is a resilient, hardworking town on the northern plains, where life is shaped by the railroad, agriculture, and the vast open spaces of the Hi-Line. It offers a quiet, practical lifestyle far removed from the state's major urban centers. Bozeman is fast-paced, highly educated, and outdoor-obsessed, with a premium cost of living and a constant influx of new residents.

# Hardin vs Billings: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Hardin and Billings are both eastern Montana communities along the Yellowstone River corridor, but they are vastly different in size and what they offer. Hardin is Where the Rivers Meet, a small Big Horn County community of 3,742 at 2,910 feet, known for its proximity to the Little Bighorn Battlefield, the Bighorn River, 10 fishing access sites, 4 historic sites, and a quiet eastern Montana lifestyle. Billings is the Magic City, Montana's largest city with 117,116 residents in Yellowstone County at 3,123 feet, with 53 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 16 fishing access sites, 9 golf courses, 9 museums, 5 historic sites, 4 state parks, 2 disc golf courses, 2 campgrounds, 2 lakes, Billings Logan International Airport 5 miles away, and a 16,000-student school district.

## Who Should Move to Hardin?

*   History enthusiasts drawn to the Little Bighorn Battlefield National Monument 14 miles away, one of the most significant and evocative historic sites in the American West
*   Anglers who want 10 fishing access sites and the Bighorn River, one of Montana's premier blue-ribbon trout streams, within easy reach
*   Remote workers, retirees, and families who want a very affordable small-town lifestyle in eastern Montana, with a typical home value of just $204,573 and rent averaging $726 per month
*   People who want a quiet, traditional eastern Montana community with a strong Native American cultural heritage and a slower pace of life

## Who Should Move to Billings?

*   Professionals and families who want Montana's largest city, with the state's most diverse job market, Billings Logan International Airport 5 miles away, and a 16,000-student school district
*   People who want a full range of urban amenities in a Montana setting, with 9 golf courses, 9 museums, 4 state parks, a vibrant downtown, and a strong healthcare and energy sector economy
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 16 fishing access sites, 4 state parks, 2 campgrounds, and easy access to the Beartooth Highway and Yellowstone National Park to the south
*   Anyone who wants the convenience and services of a real city, with a median household income of $71,855 and a community that is growing and diversifying

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Billings is considerably more expensive than Hardin. The typical home value in Hardin is around $204,573, compared to $390,654 in Billings, nearly double. Rent is also substantially higher in Billings: Hardin averages about $726 per month, while Billings comes in at roughly $1,383, about 90% more.

Median household income is higher in Billings at $71,855 versus $60,463 in Hardin. Billings residents earn more, which helps offset the higher housing costs, but Hardin remains dramatically more affordable for both buyers and renters.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Hardin is a small eastern Montana town where the Bighorn River, the Little Bighorn Battlefield, and a quiet agricultural and ranching community define daily life. Billings is Montana's largest city, where a diverse economy, a strong healthcare sector, and a growing urban culture define daily life. Billings has a density of services, amenities, and opportunity that Hardin does not, with a real airport, a full range of shopping and dining, and a community that is large enough to feel like a genuine city while still being unmistakably Montana. If you want a very affordable small-town lifestyle with access to one of America's great historic sites and excellent trout fishing, Hardin is a compelling choice. If you want Montana's most complete city with the best job market, the most services, and the strongest infrastructure in the state, Billings is the clear answer.

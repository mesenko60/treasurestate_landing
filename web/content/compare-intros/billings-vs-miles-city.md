# Miles City vs Billings, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Miles City and Billings represent two very different scales of Eastern Montana life. Billings is Montana's largest city, with 117,116 people, a busy commercial corridor, a regional medical hub, and a lively arts and dining scene anchored by the Rimrocks. Miles City, known as the Cow Capital of Montana, is a proud ranching town of 8,412 on the Yellowstone River, where the Range Riders Museum, the Bucking Horse Sale, and wide-open prairie skies define the character of the place.

## Who Should Move to Billings?

People who want Montana's best job market and career options in healthcare, energy, and retail. Those who value a full range of restaurants, breweries, and entertainment without leaving the state. Families who want large school districts, multiple hospital systems, and suburban amenities. Anyone who wants a city feel while still being close to the Beartooth Mountains and Yellowstone country.

## Who Should Move to Miles City?

Ranchers, farmers, and anyone whose livelihood or lifestyle is tied to the land and livestock culture of Eastern Montana. People seeking very affordable housing in a community where neighbors still know each other by name. Remote workers or retirees who want a quiet, uncrowded town with genuine small-city character. History enthusiasts drawn to the deep cowboy and frontier heritage of the Yellowstone Valley.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the gap is most dramatic. The typical home in Billings costs $390,654, while in Miles City the figure is $233,769, a difference of more than $156,000. Rent follows the same pattern: Billings averages $1,383 per month compared to $750 in Miles City. Median household income in Billings is $71,855 versus $60,732 in Miles City, so Billings residents earn more but also pay considerably more to live there. Overall, Miles City's cost of living runs significantly lower across nearly every category.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Billings feels like a real regional city. It has traffic, chain restaurants alongside local favorites, a performing arts center, and the energy of a place that draws people from across a wide region for shopping, medical care, and events. Miles City is quieter and slower by design. The downtown has a genuine historic character, the Bucking Horse Sale each May draws visitors from across the West, and the pace of life reflects the ranching culture that built the town. If you want urban convenience, Billings wins easily. If you want affordable, unpretentious, and deeply rooted in Montana's cowboy tradition, Miles City is hard to beat.

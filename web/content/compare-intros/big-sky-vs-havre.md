# Havre vs Big Sky, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Havre and Big Sky offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Havre is a historic railroad and agricultural hub on the Hi-Line with 9,362 people, its deep roots in the railroad industry, the Havre Beneath the Streets underground tour, and quick access to the nearby Bear Paw Mountains and Fresno Reservoir. Big Sky, with 3,591 residents, is a premier alpine resort community known for the massive Big Sky Resort, its luxury real estate market, and Lone Mountain and the Gallatin Canyon.

## Who Should Move to Havre?

* Workers in agriculture, education, or the railroad industry looking for steady employment.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a community with all essential amenities.
* Those who appreciate the stark beauty of the northern plains and the close-knit feel of a Hi-Line town.
* History buffs interested in the unique underground history and nearby frontier battlefields.

## Who Should Move to Big Sky?

* Affluent buyers and second-home owners looking for luxury alpine real estate.
* Dedicated skiers and snowboarders who want access to some of the biggest and best terrain in North America.
* Hospitality and resort workers who are willing to navigate the high cost of living for the mountain lifestyle.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want to live in a high-altitude environment with immediate access to wilderness and Yellowstone National Park.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Havre is around $224,252, while in Big Sky it is closer to $1,819,905, a difference of roughly $1,595,653, making Havre significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Havre averages about $925 per month, compared to roughly $1,712 in Big Sky.

Havre's overall cost of living differs from Big Sky's. Havre offers lower average salaries compared to Big Sky, with a median household income of $58,000 compared to Big Sky's $103,625.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Havre is a resilient, hardworking community that serves as the commercial center for a vast stretch of northern Montana. It offers a practical, unpretentious lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture and the railroad. Big Sky is not a traditional town but a sprawling, high-end resort community built around outdoor recreation and luxury living. The atmosphere is transient and seasonal, driven by tourism, skiing, and wealth, offering spectacular alpine access but lacking the historic, grounded feel of older Montana settlements.

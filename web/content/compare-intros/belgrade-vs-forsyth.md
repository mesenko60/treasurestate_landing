# Forsyth vs Belgrade, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Forsyth and Belgrade offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Forsyth is a historic railroad town on the Yellowstone River with 1,630 people, its beautiful, historic county courthouse, its position as a transportation and agricultural hub, and quick access to the Yellowstone River and the eastern plains. Belgrade, with 12,509 residents, is a rapidly growing hub in the Gallatin Valley known for proximity to Bozeman, the Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport, and the Bridger Mountains and Gallatin River.

## Who Should Move to Forsyth?

* Workers in agriculture, transportation, or local services looking for a stable community.
* People seeking very affordable housing and a low overall cost of living.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle on the eastern plains.
* Hunters and anglers who want uncrowded access to the Yellowstone River.

## Who Should Move to Belgrade?

* Families and professionals who work in Bozeman but want slightly more affordable housing options.
* Frequent travelers who appreciate being minutes away from Montana's busiest airport.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want quick access to the Bridger Mountains, Big Sky, and blue-ribbon trout streams.
* People looking for a community with a strong local identity that still benefits from the economic engine of the Gallatin Valley.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Forsyth is around $208,690, while in Belgrade it is closer to $544,626, a difference of roughly $335,936, making Forsyth significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Forsyth averages about $743 per month, compared to roughly $2,206 in Belgrade.

Forsyth's overall cost of living differs from Belgrade's. Forsyth offers lower average salaries compared to Belgrade, with a median household income of $58,375 compared to Belgrade's $88,896.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Forsyth is a quiet, practical community in eastern Montana, defined by the railroad, agriculture, and the Yellowstone River. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle with a strong sense of local history. Belgrade feels like a busy, practical town that is absorbing much of the Gallatin Valley's growth. It has its own distinct community pride, centered around local schools and events, but its daily rhythm is closely tied to the economic hum of neighboring Bozeman and the constant activity at the airport.

# Glasgow vs Columbia Falls, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Glasgow and Columbia Falls offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Glasgow is a historic railroad town on the Hi-Line with 3,224 people, its position as a regional hub for northeastern Montana, its proximity to Fort Peck Dam, and quick access to Fort Peck Lake and the Charles M. Russell National Wildlife Refuge. Columbia Falls, with just 5,308 residents, is a gateway community to Glacier National Park known for its timber industry roots, its revitalized downtown, and the Flathead River and Glacier National Park.

## Who Should Move to Glasgow?

* Workers in agriculture, government, or the railroad industry looking for stable employment in northeastern Montana.
* Anglers and boaters who want to live near Fort Peck Lake, one of the premier walleye fisheries in the country.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, isolated community.
* Hunters and outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the vast, uncrowded public lands of the Missouri River Breaks.

## Who Should Move to Columbia Falls?

* Outdoor lovers who want to live minutes from the entrance to Glacier National Park.
* Families looking for a community with a strong local spirit and a growing downtown scene.
* People who work in the Flathead Valley but prefer a town with a slightly more blue-collar, unpretentious history than Whitefish.
* Those who want immediate access to the Flathead River for fishing, floating, and recreation.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Glasgow is around $195,000, while in Columbia Falls it is closer to $584,580, a difference of roughly $389,580, making Glasgow significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Glasgow averages about $750 per month, compared to roughly $1,003 in Columbia Falls.

Glasgow's overall cost of living differs from Columbia Falls's. Glasgow offers lower average salaries compared to Columbia Falls, with a median household income of $55,000 compared to Columbia Falls's $65,313.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Glasgow is a resilient, isolated community that serves as the commercial and social center for a massive swath of northeastern Montana. It offers a quiet, slow-paced lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture, the railroad, and the incredible recreational opportunities at nearby Fort Peck Lake. Columbia Falls has transitioned from a traditional timber town into a vibrant gateway community, balancing its working-class roots with a new influx of breweries, markets, and tourism. It feels less resort-focused than neighboring Whitefish, maintaining a strong, independent local character while serving as the front porch to Glacier National Park.

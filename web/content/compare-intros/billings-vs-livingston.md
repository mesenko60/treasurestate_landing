# Billings vs Livingston: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Billings and Livingston are both eastern-leaning Montana communities with deep ties to the Yellowstone River, but they offer very different lives. Billings is the Magic City, Montana's largest city at 117,116 people, a full-service regional hub with a diversified economy and all the amenities of a major city. Livingston is the Windiest City in Montana, a small, artsy, historically rich town of 8,040 in the Paradise Valley, where the Yellowstone River runs cold and clear, the wind comes howling down from the Absarokas, and the proximity to Yellowstone National Park shapes everything.

## Who Should Move to Billings?

*   Professionals who need access to Montana's largest and most diverse job market
*   Families seeking a full-service city with major hospitals, strong schools, and a wide range of housing options
*   People who want a warmer, drier climate with milder winters and easy access to Yellowstone Country
*   Anyone who values the infrastructure and amenities of a large regional city

## Who Should Move to Livingston?

*   Artists, writers, and creative professionals drawn to Livingston's long-established arts and literary community
*   Fly fishing enthusiasts who want some of the best Yellowstone River access in the state right in town
*   Remote workers and retirees who want a small, character-rich community with stunning mountain scenery
*   People who want to be close to Yellowstone National Park and the Paradise Valley without paying Bozeman prices

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Livingston has become notably more expensive in recent years, driven in part by its proximity to Bozeman and its appeal to remote workers. The median home price is around $509,477, roughly $120,000 more than Billings' $390,654. Rent also runs higher at about $1,783 per month versus $1,383 in Billings.

Income levels favor Billings, with a median household income of $71,855 compared to Livingston's $65,187. For a town of 8,000 people with a limited local job market, Livingston's housing costs reflect its desirability more than its economic base.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Billings has the energy and infrastructure of a regional capital, always busy, always expanding, with a commercial pulse that keeps the city humming. Livingston has a completely different character: it's a small town with a big reputation, known for its wind, its writers, its fly fishing, and its proximity to one of the world's great national parks. The historic downtown, the railroad heritage, and the stunning backdrop of the Absaroka Range give Livingston a sense of place that is genuinely hard to replicate. If you want Montana with full city services, Billings is the answer. If you want Montana with soul and scenery, Livingston makes a compelling case.

# Havre vs Helena, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Havre and Helena offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Havre is a historic railroad and agricultural hub on the Hi-Line with 9,362 people, its deep roots in the railroad industry, the Havre Beneath the Streets underground tour, and quick access to the nearby Bear Paw Mountains and Fresno Reservoir. Helena, with 33,885 residents, is a historic state capital known for its government sector, its gold rush architecture, and Mount Helena and the Missouri River lakes.

## Who Should Move to Havre?

* Workers in agriculture, education, or the railroad industry looking for steady employment.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a community with all essential amenities.
* Those who appreciate the stark beauty of the northern plains and the close-knit feel of a Hi-Line town.
* History buffs interested in the unique underground history and nearby frontier battlefields.

## Who Should Move to Helena?

* Government workers and professionals.
* Families seeking a stable, safe community.
* Mountain bikers and hikers who want trails right out their back door.
* People who appreciate historic architecture and a central location.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Havre is around $224,252, while in Helena it is closer to $425,000, a difference of roughly $200,748, making Havre significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Havre averages about $925 per month, compared to roughly $1,100 in Helena.

Havre's overall cost of living differs from Helena's. Havre offers lower average salaries compared to Helena, with a median household income of $58,000 compared to Helena's $68,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Havre is a resilient, hardworking community that serves as the commercial center for a vast stretch of northern Montana. It offers a practical, unpretentious lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture and the railroad. Helena is a stable, professional town driven by state government, with a quiet downtown and immediate access to mountain trails.

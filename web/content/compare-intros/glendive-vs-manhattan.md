# Manhattan vs Glendive, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Manhattan and Glendive offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Manhattan is a rapidly growing agricultural town in the Gallatin Valley with 2,288 people, its historic role as the Seed Potato Capital of the World, its booming residential growth, and quick access to the Gallatin River and the Bridger Mountains. Glendive, with 4,873 residents, is a rugged eastern Montana town known for its rich dinosaur fossil beds, its position as an agricultural and energy hub, and Makoshika State Park and the Yellowstone River.

## Who Should Move to Manhattan?

* Professionals working in Bozeman who want a smaller-town feel and are willing to commute.
* Families looking for excellent local schools and a safe, tight-knit community.
* People who appreciate a town that still honors its agricultural heritage despite rapid modernization.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want immediate access to the world-class fishing of the Gallatin River.

## Who Should Move to Glendive?

* Workers in the agriculture, railroad, or energy sectors looking for steady employment in eastern Montana.
* Homebuyers seeking some of the most affordable real estate in the state.
* People who appreciate the stark beauty of the badlands and the quiet isolation of the high plains.
* Paleontology enthusiasts and rockhounds drawn to the fossil-rich terrain of Makoshika State Park.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Manhattan is around $652,619, while in Glendive it is closer to $185,447, a difference of roughly $467,172, making Glendive significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Manhattan averages about $1,700 per month, compared to roughly $832 in Glendive.

Manhattan's overall cost of living differs from Glendive's. Manhattan offers lower average salaries compared to Glendive, with a median household income of $69,671 compared to Glendive's $71,063.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Manhattan blends its deep agricultural roots with its new reality as a highly desirable, expensive suburb of Bozeman. It maintains a charming, historic downtown and a strong community identity, even as it experiences significant growth and rising costs. Glendive is a practical, hardworking town set against the dramatic backdrop of the Montana badlands. It is far removed from the tourist crowds of the western mountains, offering a quiet, resilient community life where the local economy is tied to the land, the railroad, and the river.

# Lewistown vs Great Falls: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Lewistown and Great Falls are both central Montana communities, but they differ considerably in size, services, and what they offer residents. Lewistown is the Geographic Center of Montana, a quiet ranching community of 6,204 in Fergus County with affordable housing, 17 campgrounds within 30 miles, and a traditional small-town character. Great Falls is the Electric City, a city of 60,000 on the Missouri River in Cascade County, with 10 museums within 30 miles, 6 waterfalls, a strong military presence anchored by Malmstrom Air Force Base, and a regional economy that serves a wide swath of central Montana.

## Who Should Move to Lewistown?

*   Retirees and remote workers who want a quiet, affordable small-town lifestyle with a typical home value around $268,126 and rent averaging just $896 per month
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 17 campgrounds, 7 fishing access sites, and the Big Snowy Mountains within 30 miles
*   People who want a traditional, tight-knit ranching community with a genuine small-town character
*   Anyone who wants to live at the geographic center of Montana, away from larger-city costs and traffic

## Who Should Move to Great Falls?

*   Military families and veterans connected to Malmstrom Air Force Base, one of the city's largest employers
*   Families who want a mid-sized Montana city with a 10,000-student school district, a regional airport, and full urban services
*   History and culture enthusiasts drawn to the C.M. Russell Museum, the Lewis and Clark Interpretive Center, and 10 museums within 30 miles
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want the Missouri River, 6 waterfalls, 8 fishing access sites, and 5 state parks within 30 miles

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Lewistown is more affordable on housing, but the gap is smaller than many Montana comparisons. The typical home value in Lewistown is around $268,126, compared to $328,554 in Great Falls, a difference of about $60,000. Rent diverges more sharply: Lewistown averages about $896 per month, while Great Falls comes in at roughly $1,340, nearly $450 more per month.

The income gap is significant. The median household income in Great Falls is $63,934, compared to $44,195 in Lewistown. Great Falls residents earn considerably more, driven in part by military salaries and a broader professional economy.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Lewistown is a town where the ranching community shapes daily life, the pace is slow, and the downtown has a quiet, historic character. Great Falls is a working city on the Missouri River, with a downtown that has been reinvesting in itself, a strong arts community anchored by the C.M. Russell Museum, and a civic identity shaped by both its industrial past and its military present. If you want affordable, quiet, small-town Montana, Lewistown is a solid choice. If you want a mid-sized Montana city with a river, a regional economy, and a cultural footprint that includes one of the best Western art museums in the country, Great Falls is worth a serious look.

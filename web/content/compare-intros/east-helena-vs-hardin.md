# East Helena vs Hardin, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

East Helena and Hardin offer two very different Montana lifestyles. East Helena is a growing residential community in the Helena Valley with 2,264 people, its industrial heritage, its rapid recent growth as a suburb of the capital, and quick access to Canyon Ferry Lake and the Elkhorn Mountains. Hardin, with 3,742 residents, is a agricultural community near the Crow Reservation known for its proximity to the Little Bighorn Battlefield, its farming and ranching economy, and the Bighorn River and Yellowtail Dam.

## Who Should Move to East Helena?

* Families looking for a strong local school district and a tight-knit community feel.
* Professionals working in Helena who want a slightly quieter, more residential environment.
* People who appreciate a town with a strong working-class heritage that is actively reinventing itself.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want quick access to Canyon Ferry Lake and the surrounding national forest.

## Who Should Move to Hardin?

* Agricultural workers and farmers involved in the sugar beet and wheat industries of the Bighorn Valley.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, rural setting.
* History enthusiasts interested in the rich Native American culture and the legacy of the Little Bighorn.
* Anglers who want to be near the world-famous trout fishing below Yellowtail Dam.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in East Helena is around $430,196, while in Hardin it is closer to $204,573, a difference of roughly $225,623, making Hardin significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in East Helena averages about $1,020 per month, compared to roughly $726 in Hardin.

East Helena's overall cost of living differs from Hardin's. East Helena offers lower average salaries compared to Hardin, with a median household income of $53,611 compared to Hardin's $60,463.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

East Helena is transitioning from its historic roots as a smelter town into a rapidly growing, family-friendly suburb of Helena. It offers a quieter, slightly more affordable alternative to the capital city while maintaining its own distinct, blue-collar identity. Hardin is a quiet, culturally diverse agricultural center where the plains meet the Bighorn River. It is a town defined by farming, ranching, and its location bordering the Crow Indian Reservation, offering a slow-paced, rural lifestyle far from the state's major urban centers.

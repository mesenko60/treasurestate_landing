# Red Lodge vs Bozeman, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Red Lodge and Bozeman offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Red Lodge is a historic mountain town and gateway to Yellowstone with 2,336 people, its vibrant, historic downtown, its position at the base of the Beartooth Highway, and quick access to Red Lodge Mountain and the Beartooth Mountains. Bozeman, with just 54,539 residents, is a booming tech and university hub known for Montana State University, its high-tech economy, and the Bridger Mountains and Big Sky.

## Who Should Move to Red Lodge?

* Outdoor enthusiasts who want immediate access to skiing, hiking, and the Beartooth Wilderness.
* People seeking a lively, historic mountain town with a strong sense of community.
* Retirees and remote workers drawn to the spectacular scenery and small-town charm.
* Those who want to live in a gateway community to Yellowstone National Park.

## Who Should Move to Bozeman?

* Tech workers and entrepreneurs.
* Students and faculty at MSU.
* Affluent outdoor enthusiasts.
* People who want a highly active, progressive mountain town.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Red Lodge is around $550,000, while in Bozeman it is closer to $750,000, a difference of roughly $200,000, making Red Lodge significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Red Lodge averages about $1,200 per month, compared to roughly $2,000 in Bozeman.

Red Lodge's overall cost of living differs from Bozeman's. Red Lodge offers lower average salaries compared to Bozeman, with a median household income of $58,000 compared to Bozeman's $70,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Red Lodge is a vibrant, historic mountain town that balances its role as a tourist destination with a strong, year-round local community. It offers spectacular access to the Beartooth Mountains and a lively downtown filled with shops, restaurants, and local events. Bozeman is fast-paced, highly educated, and outdoor-obsessed, with a premium cost of living and a constant influx of new residents.

# Big Timber vs Billings, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Big Timber and Billings offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Big Timber is a historic ranching community in the Sweet Grass Valley with 1,715 people, its deep, multi-generational ranching heritage, its classic, historic downtown architecture, and quick access to the Boulder River and the Crazy Mountains. Billings, with 117,445 residents, is a Montana's largest city known for its major medical centers, its sprawling retail sector, and the Rimrocks and Yellowstone River.

## Who Should Move to Big Timber?

* People seeking an authentic, traditional western community with a strong agricultural identity.
* Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want access to the pristine waters of the Boulder River.
* Those looking for a scenic, quiet lifestyle with relatively easy access to Bozeman or Billings.
* Retirees and remote workers drawn to the town's historic charm and spectacular mountain views.

## Who Should Move to Billings?

* Professionals seeking corporate or medical careers.
* Families wanting large school districts and diverse activities.
* People who prefer urban conveniences over small-town quiet.
* Those who want easy access to a major regional airport.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Big Timber is around $422,291, while in Billings it is closer to $375,000, a difference of roughly $47,291, making Billings significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Big Timber averages about $1,009 per month, compared to roughly $1,200 in Billings.

Big Timber's overall cost of living differs from Billings's. Big Timber offers lower average salaries compared to Billings, with a median household income of $63,514 compared to Billings's $65,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Big Timber is a quintessential Montana ranching town, fiercely protective of its agricultural roots and stunning natural surroundings. It offers a quiet, authentic western lifestyle set against the dramatic backdrop of the Crazy Mountains. Billings is a bustling regional hub with a true city feel, offering extensive amenities and a fast-paced environment.

# Deer Lodge vs Bigfork, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Deer Lodge and Bigfork offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Deer Lodge is a historic ranching and prison town with 2,938 people, the Old Montana Prison Museum, its deep ranching heritage in the Powell Valley, and quick access to the Clark Fork River and the Flint Creek Range. Bigfork, with just 5,000 residents, is a picturesque village on Flathead Lake known for its thriving arts scene, its cherry orchards, and Flathead Lake and the Swan River.

## Who Should Move to Deer Lodge?

* History buffs who appreciate the town's unique museums and historic architecture.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, traditional western town.
* Workers in agriculture, government, or local services looking for a slow-paced lifestyle.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want uncrowded access to the Clark Fork River and surrounding mountains.

## Who Should Move to Bigfork?

* Retirees and affluent professionals looking for a scenic, upscale lakeside community.
* Boaters, sailors, and water sports enthusiasts who want to live on the largest natural freshwater lake in the West.
* Art lovers who appreciate the town's galleries, summer playhouse, and cultural events.
* People who desire a resort-like atmosphere with high-end dining and boutique shopping.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Deer Lodge is around $225,000, while in Bigfork it is closer to $758,435, a difference of roughly $533,435, making Deer Lodge significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Deer Lodge averages about $800 per month, compared to roughly $1,103 in Bigfork.

Deer Lodge's overall cost of living differs from Bigfork's. Deer Lodge offers lower average salaries compared to Bigfork, with a median household income of $48,000 compared to Bigfork's $82,648.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Deer Lodge is a quiet, deeply historic town that feels like a step back in time. It is defined by its agricultural roots and its unique history as the site of the original territorial prison, offering a slow-paced, affordable lifestyle in a beautiful valley setting. Bigfork feels like a curated resort village, where life centers around the water, the arts, and seasonal tourism. The downtown is polished and walkable, filled with galleries and cafes, offering a lifestyle that is significantly more upscale and leisure-focused than most Montana towns.

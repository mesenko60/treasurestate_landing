# Columbia Falls vs Glendive: Which Montana Town Is Right for You?

Columbia Falls and Glendive represent two very different corners of Montana. Columbia Falls, the Gateway to Glacier in the northwest, is a mountain town of 5,308 people surrounded by wilderness, ski areas, and the wild country of Glacier National Park. Glendive, the Paddlefish Capital of the World in the far east, is a prairie town of 4,873 people on the Yellowstone River, known for its dinosaur fossils at Makoshika State Park and its deep ties to ranching and agriculture.

## Who Should Move to Columbia Falls?

- Outdoor enthusiasts who want year-round mountain recreation, from skiing and hiking to paddling and wildlife watching
- Families seeking a strong school district and a genuine small-town community near Glacier National Park
- People who want western Montana's lush scenery and mild-by-Montana-standards winters
- Remote workers or retirees who want beauty and adventure without paying Whitefish prices

## Who Should Move to Glendive?

- People who love wide-open prairie landscapes, big skies, and a true eastern Montana way of life
- Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the Yellowstone River and paddlefish season
- Those looking for very affordable housing and a low cost of living in a tight-knit community
- History and fossil enthusiasts who want Makoshika State Park as a backyard

## Cost of Living Breakdown

The housing gap between these two towns is dramatic. The typical home value in Columbia Falls is $584,580, while in Glendive it is just $185,447, less than a third of the price. Rent reflects the same divide, with Columbia Falls averaging $1,003 per month compared to $832 in Glendive. Interestingly, Glendive's median household income of $71,063 is slightly higher than Columbia Falls at $65,313, making Glendive's affordability even more pronounced in real terms.

Columbia Falls is served by Glacier Park International Airport in Kalispell, 6 miles away. Glendive has its own small regional airport, also about 6 miles from town.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Columbia Falls is a mountain gateway town with energy and momentum. Glacier National Park draws visitors from around the world, and the town has grown into a lively base camp with good restaurants, local breweries, and a strong outdoor culture. Glendive is quieter and more remote, a place where the pace of life follows the seasons and the river. Paddlefish season in spring brings the town together, and Makoshika's badlands provide a striking, otherworldly backdrop. These are two towns that share a Montana identity but almost nothing else in terms of setting or lifestyle.

Columbia Falls has 214 recreational sites within 30 miles, including 48 campgrounds, 19 ski areas, and access to Glacier National Park. Glendive has 30 sites within range, anchored by Makoshika State Park, the Yellowstone River, and a handful of fishing access points. For sheer recreational volume, Columbia Falls is in a different category. For solitude, fossils, and river fishing, Glendive has its own quiet appeal.

# Laurel vs Columbia Falls: Which Montana Town is Right for You?

Laurel and Columbia Falls are both mid-sized Montana communities with strong outdoor access, but they sit in completely different parts of the state and offer very different lifestyles. Laurel is the City Between the Yellowstone and the Rimrocks, a working-class Yellowstone Valley community of 7,215 in Yellowstone County at 3,307 feet, with Billings Logan International Airport 18 miles away, 60 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 21 fishing access sites, 10 museums, 6 state parks, 5 golf courses, 4 historic sites, 3 nature reserves, 2 disc golf courses, 1 river, and a 1,800-student school district. Columbia Falls is the Gateway to Glacier, a fast-growing Flathead Valley community of 5,308 in Flathead County at 3,077 feet, with Glacier Park International Airport in Kalispell 6 miles away, 214 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 48 campgrounds, 31 trailheads, 27 fishing access sites, 26 lakes, 19 ski areas, 9 state parks, 8 golf courses, 8 museums, 6 viewpoints, 6 nature reserves, 4 historic sites, 4 disc golf courses, 4 boat launches, 11 waterfalls, 1 national forest, 1 national park, and a 1,800-student school district.

## Who Should Move to Laurel?

*   Working families and professionals who want easy access to Billings, the largest city in Montana, just 18 miles away, with its hospitals, employers, shopping, and airport, while living in a more affordable community
*   Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want 21 fishing access sites on the Yellowstone River, 6 state parks, 10 museums, and 5 golf courses within 30 miles, with the Rimrocks and the Yellowstone Valley as a backdrop
*   Buyers who want an affordable Montana community close to a major city, with a typical home value of $369,826 and rent averaging $1,160 per month
*   Families who want a 1,800-student school district, 4 historic sites, and a genuine working-class Montana community with strong Yellowstone Valley character

## Who Should Move to Columbia Falls?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 48 campgrounds, 31 trailheads, 27 fishing access sites, 26 lakes, 19 ski areas, 11 waterfalls, 9 state parks, 6 nature reserves, and Glacier National Park all within 30 miles, making it one of the most recreation-rich communities in Montana
*   Skiers and snowboarders who want access to 19 ski areas within 30 miles, including Whitefish Mountain Resort, with 14.3 inches of annual snowfall and a Flathead Valley location that puts them in the heart of northwestern Montana's winter recreation scene
*   Buyers who want a fast-growing Flathead Valley community close to Glacier National Park at a higher price point, with a typical home value of $584,580 but rent averaging just $1,003 per month
*   Families who want a 1,800-student school district, Glacier Park International Airport 6 miles away, and the full range of Flathead Valley amenities in Kalispell just minutes away

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Columbia Falls is significantly more expensive to buy into than Laurel. The typical home value in Columbia Falls is $584,580, compared to $369,826 in Laurel, about $215,000 more. Rent is surprisingly close: Columbia Falls averages $1,003 per month versus $1,160 in Laurel, making Columbia Falls the better value on a monthly basis despite higher purchase prices.

Median household income is nearly identical: $65,313 in Columbia Falls versus $66,382 in Laurel. Both communities are solidly middle-income, but Columbia Falls buyers are paying a significant premium for proximity to Glacier National Park and the Flathead Valley's outdoor lifestyle.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Laurel is a working-class Yellowstone Valley community where the railroad, the refinery, the Rimrocks, and proximity to Billings define daily life. Columbia Falls is a fast-growing Flathead Valley gateway town where Glacier National Park, the Flathead River, and a booming outdoor tourism economy shape the community. The outdoor recreation gap is dramatic: Columbia Falls has 214 sites within 30 miles versus Laurel's 60, including 31 trailheads and 19 ski areas that Laurel simply cannot match. Laurel has 21 fishing access sites and 10 museums that reflect its deep Yellowstone Valley roots. If you want an affordable working-class community with easy access to Billings and strong Yellowstone Valley outdoor access, Laurel is a solid and practical choice. If you want to live at the gateway to Glacier National Park in one of the most outdoor-rich communities in Montana, Columbia Falls is one of the most compelling places to live in the state.

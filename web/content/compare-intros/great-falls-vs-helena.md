# Great Falls vs Helena: Which Montana City is Right for You?

Great Falls and Helena are both central Montana cities that often get overlooked in favor of Bozeman and Missoula, but they offer genuine and distinct Montana lifestyles at a fraction of the cost. Great Falls is the Electric City, a city of 60,000 on the Missouri River, anchored by Malmstrom Air Force Base, the C.M. Russell Museum, and a trail system that follows the river through the heart of town. Helena is the Queen City of the Rockies, the state capital at 33,100 people, a historically rich city with gold rush origins, a trail network that starts at the edge of downtown, and a community shaped by government, law, and a quiet but genuine love of the outdoors.

## Who Should Move to Great Falls?

*   Military families stationed at or connected to Malmstrom Air Force Base
*   Families and professionals who want a larger city with more job diversity, a regional airport, and lower housing costs
*   History and art lovers drawn to the C.M. Russell Museum and the Lewis and Clark National Historic Trail
*   People who prefer a flatter, more open setting with a warmer January climate and easy access to central Montana

## Who Should Move to Helena?

*   State government employees, attorneys, lobbyists, and anyone whose career is tied to the state capitol
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want a trail network, hot springs, mountain lakes, and wilderness within 30 miles of a smaller city
*   History lovers drawn to Helena's gold rush past, Victorian architecture, and the Cathedral of St. Helena
*   Families and retirees who want a more intimate community with a manageable pace of life and a higher median income

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Great Falls is the more affordable city to buy into. The median home price in Great Falls is around $328,554, compared to $466,393 in Helena, a difference of nearly $138,000. Rent also runs higher in Helena at about $1,489 per month versus $1,340 in Great Falls.

In a notable reversal, Helena's median household income of $69,341 is higher than Great Falls' $63,934, reflecting the stability and compensation levels of state government employment. Helena residents pay more for housing but also earn more, and the outdoor recreation inventory within 30 miles, 107 sites versus Great Falls' 45, is substantially richer.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Great Falls is a working river city, practical, unpretentious, and anchored by its military base and its extraordinary art museum. The Missouri River trail system is a genuine community treasure, and the series of waterfalls that gives the city its name is a reminder that this stretch of river was once one of the most significant geographic obstacles in the entire Lewis and Clark expedition. Helena is quieter and more self-contained, a city where the state capitol dome is visible from the hiking trails and the governor shops at the same farmers market as everyone else. Both cities are underrated Montana choices; the decision comes down to size, cost, and whether you want the river city or the capital city.

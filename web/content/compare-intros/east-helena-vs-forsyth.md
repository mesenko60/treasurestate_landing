# East Helena vs Forsyth, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

East Helena and Forsyth offer two very different Montana lifestyles. East Helena is a growing residential community in the Helena Valley with 2,264 people, its industrial heritage, its rapid recent growth as a suburb of the capital, and quick access to Canyon Ferry Lake and the Elkhorn Mountains. Forsyth, with 1,630 residents, is a historic railroad town on the Yellowstone River known for its beautiful, historic county courthouse, its position as a transportation and agricultural hub, and the Yellowstone River and the eastern plains.

## Who Should Move to East Helena?

* Families looking for a strong local school district and a tight-knit community feel.
* Professionals working in Helena who want a slightly quieter, more residential environment.
* People who appreciate a town with a strong working-class heritage that is actively reinventing itself.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want quick access to Canyon Ferry Lake and the surrounding national forest.

## Who Should Move to Forsyth?

* Workers in agriculture, transportation, or local services looking for a stable community.
* People seeking very affordable housing and a low overall cost of living.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle on the eastern plains.
* Hunters and anglers who want uncrowded access to the Yellowstone River.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in East Helena is around $430,196, while in Forsyth it is closer to $208,690, a difference of roughly $221,506, making Forsyth significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in East Helena averages about $1,020 per month, compared to roughly $743 in Forsyth.

East Helena's overall cost of living differs from Forsyth's. East Helena offers lower average salaries compared to Forsyth, with a median household income of $53,611 compared to Forsyth's $58,375.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

East Helena is transitioning from its historic roots as a smelter town into a rapidly growing, family-friendly suburb of Helena. It offers a quieter, slightly more affordable alternative to the capital city while maintaining its own distinct, blue-collar identity. Forsyth is a quiet, practical community in eastern Montana, defined by the railroad, agriculture, and the Yellowstone River. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle with a strong sense of local history.

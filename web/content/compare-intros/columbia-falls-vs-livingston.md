# Columbia Falls vs Livingston: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Columbia Falls and Livingston are both small Montana communities with strong outdoor identities and a genuine small-town character, but they sit in very different landscapes and offer very different kinds of outdoor access. Columbia Falls is the Gateway to Glacier, a small community of 5,308 in Flathead County at 3,077 feet elevation, with 214 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 48 campgrounds, 31 trailheads, 19 ski areas, and Glacier National Park just up the road. Livingston is the Windiest City in Montana, a community of 8,040 in Park County at 4,501 feet on the Yellowstone River, with 144 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 24 fishing access sites, 40 lakes, 12 trailheads, and the gateway to Paradise Valley and Yellowstone National Park.

## Who Should Move to Columbia Falls?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 48 campgrounds, 31 trailheads, 27 fishing access sites, 19 ski areas, and Glacier National Park all within 30 miles
*   Families and remote workers who want a small Flathead Valley community with lower rent, averaging about $1,003 per month versus Livingston's $1,783
*   People who want northwestern Montana's mountain and lake scenery, with 26 lakes within 30 miles, as their daily backdrop
*   Anyone who wants a small-town feel with extraordinary outdoor access and Kalispell's services just 6 miles away

## Who Should Move to Livingston?

*   Fly fishing enthusiasts who want the Yellowstone River at their doorstep, with 24 fishing access sites, 7 boat launches, and 2 rivers within 30 miles
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 40 lakes, 12 trailheads, 16 waterfalls, 2 hot springs, and a ski area all within 30 miles, plus Yellowstone National Park just 55 miles south
*   Artists, writers, and creative professionals drawn to Livingston's well-established arts community and its reputation as one of Montana's most culturally vibrant small towns
*   People who want a small Montana town with a strong sense of place, a historic downtown, and easy access to both Bozeman (35 miles) and Yellowstone

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Columbia Falls is more expensive on home prices but considerably cheaper on rent. The typical home value in Columbia Falls is around $584,580, compared to $509,477 in Livingston, a difference of about $75,000. Rent diverges more sharply: Columbia Falls averages about $1,003 per month, while Livingston comes in at roughly $1,783, about $780 more per month.

The median household incomes are nearly identical: $65,313 in Columbia Falls and $65,187 in Livingston. These two towns are genuinely comparable on earnings, making the rent and home price differences the primary financial consideration.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Columbia Falls is a small gateway community where Glacier National Park, the Flathead River, and the surrounding mountains define daily life. Livingston is a town with a different kind of energy, a place where the Yellowstone River runs through downtown, the wind is a constant companion, and the arts community has given the town a cultural identity that punches well above its size. Both towns are genuinely excellent choices for people who want a small Montana community with extraordinary outdoor access. The choice comes down to which landscape calls to you more: Glacier and the Flathead Valley, or the Yellowstone River and the gateway to Paradise Valley.

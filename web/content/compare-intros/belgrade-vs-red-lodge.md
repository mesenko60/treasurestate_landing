# Red Lodge vs Belgrade, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Red Lodge and Belgrade offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Red Lodge is a historic mountain town and gateway to Yellowstone with 2,336 people, its vibrant, historic downtown, its position at the base of the Beartooth Highway, and quick access to Red Lodge Mountain and the Beartooth Mountains. Belgrade, with just 12,509 residents, is a rapidly growing hub in the Gallatin Valley known for proximity to Bozeman, the Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport, and the Bridger Mountains and Gallatin River.

## Who Should Move to Red Lodge?

* Outdoor enthusiasts who want immediate access to skiing, hiking, and the Beartooth Wilderness.
* People seeking a lively, historic mountain town with a strong sense of community.
* Retirees and remote workers drawn to the spectacular scenery and small-town charm.
* Those who want to live in a gateway community to Yellowstone National Park.

## Who Should Move to Belgrade?

* Families and professionals who work in Bozeman but want slightly more affordable housing options.
* Frequent travelers who appreciate being minutes away from Montana's busiest airport.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want quick access to the Bridger Mountains, Big Sky, and blue-ribbon trout streams.
* People looking for a community with a strong local identity that still benefits from the economic engine of the Gallatin Valley.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Red Lodge is around $550,000, while in Belgrade it is closer to $544,626, a difference of roughly $5,374, making Belgrade significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Red Lodge averages about $1,200 per month, compared to roughly $2,206 in Belgrade.

Red Lodge's overall cost of living differs from Belgrade's. Red Lodge offers lower average salaries compared to Belgrade, with a median household income of $58,000 compared to Belgrade's $88,896.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Red Lodge is a vibrant, historic mountain town that balances its role as a tourist destination with a strong, year-round local community. It offers spectacular access to the Beartooth Mountains and a lively downtown filled with shops, restaurants, and local events. Belgrade feels like a busy, practical town that is absorbing much of the Gallatin Valley's growth. It has its own distinct community pride, centered around local schools and events, but its daily rhythm is closely tied to the economic hum of neighboring Bozeman and the constant activity at the airport.

# Libby vs Missoula, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Libby and Missoula offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Libby is a remote timber town in the Kootenai National Forest with 2,775 people, its deep roots in the logging industry, its stunning, rugged isolation, and quick access to the Kootenai River and Cabinet Mountains. Missoula, with just 77,757 residents, is a vibrant university town known for its craft breweries, its bustling downtown, and world-class hiking and skiing.

## Who Should Move to Libby?

* Outdoor enthusiasts seeking uncrowded access to some of Montana's most rugged and remote wilderness.
* Retirees and remote workers looking for affordable mountain living far from major tourist hubs.
* People who appreciate a tight-knit, resilient community with a strong working-class heritage.
* Hunters, anglers, and hikers who want to live in the heart of the Kootenai National Forest.

## Who Should Move to Missoula?

* Young professionals and families who want good job opportunities.
* People who love a lively downtown with restaurants, breweries, and events.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want to be close to the mountains for hiking, skiing, and rafting.
* Anyone who wants a university town vibe with younger energy.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Libby is around $285,000, while in Missoula it is closer to $554,389, a difference of roughly $269,389, making Libby significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Libby averages about $850 per month, compared to roughly $1,486 in Missoula.

Libby's overall cost of living differs from Missoula's. Libby offers lower average salaries compared to Missoula, with a median household income of $45,000 compared to Missoula's $65,329.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Libby is a resilient, isolated town tucked into the far northwest corner of the state. It is defined by its timber heritage and its incredible, uncrowded access to the Kootenai River and Cabinet Mountains, offering a quiet, rugged lifestyle. Missoula feels like a real city in the mountains, with busy sidewalks, weekly farmers markets, live music, and constant activity.

# Glendive vs Kalispell: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Glendive and Kalispell sit at opposite ends of Montana and offer two very different lifestyles. Glendive is the Paddlefish Capital of the World, a community of 4,873 in Dawson County in eastern Montana at 2,083 feet elevation, with 30 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles and Makoshika State Park at its edge. Kalispell is the Gateway to Glacier Country, a regional hub of 29,886 in Flathead County in western Montana at 2,959 feet, with 189 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 28 campgrounds, 28 fishing access sites, 27 lakes, 26 trailheads, 19 ski areas, 15 state parks, Glacier National Park 35 miles away, and Glacier Park International Airport 10 miles away.

## Who Should Move to Glendive?

*   Budget-conscious buyers who want Montana at a very low price, with a typical home value of $185,447 and median rent of just $832 per month, compared to Kalispell's $541,638 and $2,086
*   Outdoor enthusiasts and anglers who want Makoshika State Park, the Yellowstone River, and some of the best paddlefish snagging in the country right in their backyard
*   People who want a small, quiet eastern Montana community with a genuine small-town character and a slower pace of life
*   Remote workers or retirees who want affordable housing and a community where $71,063 in median household income goes a very long way

## Who Should Move to Kalispell?

*   Families and professionals who want a regional hub with a 4,200-student school district, Glacier Park International Airport 10 miles away, and the full range of services, retail, and employment that come with being the Flathead Valley's largest city
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 28 campgrounds, 28 fishing access sites, 27 lakes, 26 trailheads, 19 ski areas, 15 state parks, and Glacier National Park all within 30 miles
*   People who want a mid-sized Montana city with a walkable downtown, a growing arts scene, and easy access to both Whitefish and Bigfork
*   Anyone who wants western Montana's mountain and lake scenery as their daily backdrop

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Glendive is dramatically more affordable than Kalispell. The typical home value in Glendive is around $185,447, compared to $541,638 in Kalispell, nearly three times the price. Rent is even more dramatic: Glendive averages about $832 per month, while Kalispell comes in at roughly $2,086, more than double.

Median household income is higher in Glendive at $71,063 versus $61,590 in Kalispell. Glendive residents earn more on average and pay far less for housing, making it one of the most affordable lifestyles in Montana relative to income.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Glendive is a small eastern Montana town where the Yellowstone River, Makoshika State Park, and the paddlefish season define the calendar. Kalispell is the Flathead Valley's commercial and service hub, busier and more connected, with a downtown that has been reinvesting in itself and a community that serves the entire valley. If you want small-town eastern Montana at a very low cost of living, Glendive is a genuine option. If you want western Montana's mountain and lake scenery, Glacier National Park at your doorstep, and a regional hub with real services and employment, Kalispell is the practical answer.

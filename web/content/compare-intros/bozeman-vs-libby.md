# Libby vs Bozeman, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Libby and Bozeman offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Libby is a remote timber town in the Kootenai National Forest with 2,775 people, its deep roots in the logging industry, its stunning, rugged isolation, and quick access to the Kootenai River and Cabinet Mountains. Bozeman, with just 54,539 residents, is a booming tech and university hub known for Montana State University, its high-tech economy, and the Bridger Mountains and Big Sky.

## Who Should Move to Libby?

* Outdoor enthusiasts seeking uncrowded access to some of Montana's most rugged and remote wilderness.
* Retirees and remote workers looking for affordable mountain living far from major tourist hubs.
* People who appreciate a tight-knit, resilient community with a strong working-class heritage.
* Hunters, anglers, and hikers who want to live in the heart of the Kootenai National Forest.

## Who Should Move to Bozeman?

* Tech workers and entrepreneurs.
* Students and faculty at MSU.
* Affluent outdoor enthusiasts.
* People who want a highly active, progressive mountain town.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Libby is around $285,000, while in Bozeman it is closer to $750,000, a difference of roughly $465,000, making Libby significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Libby averages about $850 per month, compared to roughly $2,000 in Bozeman.

Libby's overall cost of living differs from Bozeman's. Libby offers lower average salaries compared to Bozeman, with a median household income of $45,000 compared to Bozeman's $70,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Libby is a resilient, isolated town tucked into the far northwest corner of the state. It is defined by its timber heritage and its incredible, uncrowded access to the Kootenai River and Cabinet Mountains, offering a quiet, rugged lifestyle. Bozeman is fast-paced, highly educated, and outdoor-obsessed, with a premium cost of living and a constant influx of new residents.

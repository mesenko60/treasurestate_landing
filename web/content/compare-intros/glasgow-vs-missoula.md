# Glasgow vs Missoula, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Glasgow and Missoula offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Glasgow is a historic railroad town on the Hi-Line with 3,224 people, its position as a regional hub for northeastern Montana, its proximity to Fort Peck Dam, and quick access to Fort Peck Lake and the Charles M. Russell National Wildlife Refuge. Missoula, with just 77,757 residents, is a vibrant university town known for its craft breweries, its bustling downtown, and world-class hiking and skiing.

## Who Should Move to Glasgow?

* Workers in agriculture, government, or the railroad industry looking for stable employment in northeastern Montana.
* Anglers and boaters who want to live near Fort Peck Lake, one of the premier walleye fisheries in the country.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, isolated community.
* Hunters and outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the vast, uncrowded public lands of the Missouri River Breaks.

## Who Should Move to Missoula?

* Young professionals and families who want good job opportunities.
* People who love a lively downtown with restaurants, breweries, and events.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want to be close to the mountains for hiking, skiing, and rafting.
* Anyone who wants a university town vibe with younger energy.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Glasgow is around $195,000, while in Missoula it is closer to $554,389, a difference of roughly $359,389, making Glasgow significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Glasgow averages about $750 per month, compared to roughly $1,486 in Missoula.

Glasgow's overall cost of living differs from Missoula's. Glasgow offers lower average salaries compared to Missoula, with a median household income of $55,000 compared to Missoula's $65,329.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Glasgow is a resilient, isolated community that serves as the commercial and social center for a massive swath of northeastern Montana. It offers a quiet, slow-paced lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture, the railroad, and the incredible recreational opportunities at nearby Fort Peck Lake. Missoula feels like a real city in the mountains, with busy sidewalks, weekly farmers markets, live music, and constant activity.

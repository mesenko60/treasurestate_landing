# Lewistown vs Dillon: Which Montana Town is Right for You?

Lewistown and Dillon are both small, traditional Montana communities with affordable housing and strong ties to Montana's ranching and agricultural heritage, but they sit in different parts of the state and offer different outdoor recreation profiles. Lewistown is the Geographic Center of Montana, a historic ranching and agricultural community of 6,204 in Fergus County at 3,934 feet, with the nearest airport in Great Falls 115 miles away, 37 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 17 campgrounds, 7 fishing access sites, 3 lakes, 3 nature reserves, 2 golf courses, 2 museums, 2 state parks, 1 hot spring, and a 1,200-student school district. Dillon is Montana's Hometown, a small university and ranching community of 3,880 in Beaverhead County at 5,095 feet in the Beaverhead Valley, with Bert Mooney Airport in Butte 62 miles away, 71 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 24 fishing access sites, 16 campgrounds, 8 lakes, 7 nature reserves, 5 state parks, 4 trailheads, 2 hot springs, 2 historic sites, 1 museum, 1 river, and a 650-student school district.

## Who Should Move to Lewistown?

*   Retirees and remote workers who want a quiet, self-contained central Montana community with a genuine small-town character, affordable housing, and wide-open central Montana skies
*   History buffs and people drawn to Montana's ranching and agricultural heritage, with the Central Montana Historical Society and Museum, the Lewistown Art Center, and the surrounding Judith Basin country
*   Budget-conscious buyers who want one of the most affordable housing markets in Montana, with a typical home value of $268,126 and rent averaging just $896 per month
*   Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want 17 campgrounds, 7 fishing access sites, 3 nature reserves, 2 state parks, and a hot spring within 30 miles in the heart of central Montana's ranch country

## Who Should Move to Dillon?

*   Fly fishers and anglers who want 24 fishing access sites on the Beaverhead River and its tributaries, along with 7 nature reserves, 5 state parks, and 2 hot springs within 30 miles in one of the most fish-rich corridors in southwestern Montana
*   University of Montana Western students, faculty, and staff who want to live in a small university town with a 650-student school district and the cultural and athletic amenities that come with a college campus
*   Budget-conscious buyers who want an affordable southwestern Montana community, with a typical home value of $375,585 and rent averaging just $821 per month, along with 71 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles
*   History enthusiasts who want to live near Clark's Lookout State Park, where William Clark of the Lewis and Clark Expedition surveyed the Beaverhead Valley in 1805, and the Beaverhead County Museum in downtown Dillon

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Dillon is more expensive than Lewistown in terms of home prices. The typical home value in Dillon is $375,585, compared to $268,126 in Lewistown, about $107,000 more. Rent is slightly lower in Dillon at $821 per month versus $896 in Lewistown.

Median household income is slightly higher in Dillon at $51,458 versus $44,195 in Lewistown. Both communities are genuinely affordable by Montana standards. Lewistown offers a lower entry price for buyers, while Dillon offers slightly lower rent and a stronger outdoor recreation profile with 71 sites within 30 miles versus Lewistown's 37.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Lewistown is a quiet, traditional central Montana community where ranching, agriculture, the Judith Basin, and a deep sense of place define daily life. Dillon is a small southwestern Montana university and ranching town where the Beaverhead River, the Beaverhead Mountains, the University of Montana Western, and the Lewis and Clark history of the valley define community character. Dillon has 24 fishing access sites, 5 state parks, and 2 hot springs within 30 miles that reflect its position in one of the most fish-rich and historically significant valleys in Montana. Lewistown has 17 campgrounds, a hot spring, and the wide-open central Montana landscape that defines its identity. If you want an affordable central Montana community with genuine small-town character and a deep connection to Montana's ranching heritage, Lewistown is a solid choice. If you want an affordable southwestern Montana community with world-class Beaverhead River fishing, Lewis and Clark history, and a small university town atmosphere, Dillon offers a genuinely distinctive Montana lifestyle.

# Malta vs Wolf Point, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Malta and Wolf Point offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Malta is a historic agricultural town on the Hi-Line with 2,100 people, its world-class dinosaur fossils, its deep ranching heritage, and quick access to the Bowdoin National Wildlife Refuge and the Missouri River Breaks. Wolf Point, with 2,517 residents, is a historic agricultural town on the Fort Peck Indian Reservation known for its position as the largest community on the reservation, its deep roots in farming and ranching, and the Missouri River and the vast eastern plains.

## Who Should Move to Malta?

* Workers in agriculture, government, or local services looking for a quiet, stable community.
* People seeking very affordable housing and a low overall cost of living.
* Hunters, birders, and outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the vast, uncrowded public lands of northeastern Montana.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle on the high plains.

## Who Should Move to Wolf Point?

* People seeking some of the most affordable housing in the state.
* Workers in agriculture, tribal government, or local services.
* Those who appreciate a quiet, isolated lifestyle on the high plains.
* Hunters and anglers who want uncrowded access to the Missouri River and surrounding lands.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Malta is around $244,540, while in Wolf Point it is closer to $145,000, a difference of roughly $99,540, making Wolf Point significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Malta averages about $693 per month, compared to roughly $600 in Wolf Point.

Malta's overall cost of living differs from Wolf Point's. Malta offers higher average salaries compared to Wolf Point, with a median household income of $59,449 compared to Wolf Point's $42,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Malta is a quiet, resilient community on the eastern Hi-Line, defined by agriculture, wide-open spaces, and its unique paleontology attractions. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle far from the state's urban centers. Wolf Point is a quiet, resilient community deeply tied to the Fort Peck Indian Reservation and the agricultural economy of northeastern Montana. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle far removed from the state's major urban centers.

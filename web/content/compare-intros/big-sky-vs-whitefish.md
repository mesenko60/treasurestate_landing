# Big Sky vs Whitefish: Which Montana Resort Town is Right for You?

Big Sky and Whitefish are Montana's two premier mountain resort communities, and choosing between them is one of the most interesting comparisons in the state. Big Sky is the Biggest Skiing in America, a resort community of 3,591 in Gallatin County at 6,319 feet, known for Big Sky Resort, 23 trailheads, 17 waterfalls, 18 lakes, 3 hot springs, 2 wilderness areas, and 140 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles. Whitefish is Stumptown, a resort town of 8,915 in Flathead County at 3,038 feet in northwestern Montana, with 202 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 46 campgrounds, 33 trailheads, 26 lakes, 22 fishing access sites, 19 ski areas, 8 state parks, 8 museums, 8 nature reserves, 7 waterfalls, 7 golf courses, 4 historic sites, 4 disc golf courses, 4 boat launches, 1 national park, Glacier Park International Airport 9 miles away, and a 1,800-student school district.

## Who Should Move to Big Sky?

*   Skiers and snowboarders who want the single largest ski resort in Montana, with over 5,800 acres of skiable terrain at Big Sky Resort, including some of the most challenging and uncrowded terrain in North America
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 23 trailheads, 17 waterfalls, 18 lakes, 19 fishing access sites, 3 hot springs, 2 wilderness areas, and a national forest all within 30 miles, in a quieter and more remote setting than Whitefish
*   High-income buyers and investors who want the most exclusive resort real estate in Montana, with a typical home value of $1,819,905 and a median household income of $103,625
*   People who want a small, intimate resort community where the mountain is the entire identity of the town and the pace of life is defined by the seasons

## Who Should Move to Whitefish?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 202 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, including 19 ski areas, 33 trailheads, 26 lakes, 22 fishing access sites, 8 state parks, 7 waterfalls, and Glacier National Park, all in one of the most scenically spectacular corners of Montana
*   Resort town residents who want a genuine community with a vibrant downtown, excellent restaurants, a strong arts scene, and a lively social culture alongside world-class outdoor access
*   Families and professionals who want Glacier Park International Airport 9 miles away, a 1,800-student school district, 8 museums, and a median household income of $71,110 in a resort town with real infrastructure
*   Buyers who want a premier Montana resort community at a significantly lower price than Big Sky, with a typical home value of $840,135 and access to both Whitefish Mountain Resort and Glacier National Park

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Big Sky is significantly more expensive than Whitefish. The typical home value in Big Sky is $1,819,905, compared to $840,135 in Whitefish, more than twice as high. Rent is higher in Whitefish: $2,639 per month versus $1,712 in Big Sky, reflecting Whitefish's extremely tight rental market driven by high demand and limited supply.

Median household income is higher in Big Sky at $103,625 versus $71,110 in Whitefish. Both communities are expensive by Montana standards, but Whitefish is far more accessible to a wider range of buyers. Big Sky's home prices are in a category that very few Montana communities can approach.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Big Sky and Whitefish are Montana's two most celebrated resort communities, and both are genuinely extraordinary places to live. Big Sky is more remote, more exclusive, and more focused on a single mountain experience. Whitefish is more connected, more diverse in its outdoor offerings, and has a richer community life with a real downtown, more restaurants, and a stronger arts scene. Whitefish has 202 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles compared to Big Sky's 140, and it has Glacier National Park right in its backyard. Big Sky has 17 waterfalls, 3 hot springs, and a ski resort that is simply larger than anything in the Whitefish area. If you want the most exclusive mountain resort lifestyle in Montana with the biggest ski mountain in the state, Big Sky is the answer. If you want a vibrant resort community with outstanding outdoor diversity, proximity to Glacier National Park, and a genuine small-town character alongside world-class amenities, Whitefish is one of the most desirable places to live in the entire Mountain West.

# Miles City vs Wolf Point, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Miles City and Wolf Point offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Miles City is a ranching community on the eastern plains with 8,412 people, the livestock industry, rodeo culture, and quick access to the wide-open Yellowstone River country. Wolf Point, with just 2,517 residents, is a historic agricultural town on the Fort Peck Indian Reservation known for its position as the largest community on the reservation, its deep roots in farming and ranching, and the Missouri River and the vast eastern plains.

## Who Should Move to Miles City?

* Ranchers, livestock producers, and people whose careers and lifestyle are rooted in Eastern Montana's agricultural economy.
* Those seeking very affordable housing in a community where the typical home costs less than half what it does in many western Montana towns.
* Remote workers or retirees who want quiet, uncrowded living without the cost pressures that come with proximity to major tourist hubs.
* People who appreciate the authentic cowboy heritage of the Yellowstone Valley and the Bucking Horse Sale rodeo tradition.

## Who Should Move to Wolf Point?

* People seeking some of the most affordable housing in the state.
* Workers in agriculture, tribal government, or local services.
* Those who appreciate a quiet, isolated lifestyle on the high plains.
* Hunters and anglers who want uncrowded access to the Missouri River and surrounding lands.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Miles City is around $233,769, while in Wolf Point it is closer to $145,000, a difference of roughly $88,769, making Wolf Point significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Miles City averages about $750 per month, compared to roughly $600 in Wolf Point.

Miles City's overall cost of living differs from Wolf Point's. Miles City offers higher average salaries compared to Wolf Point, with a median household income of $60,732 compared to Wolf Point's $42,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Miles City is quiet and agricultural in character, with a lifestyle shaped by ranching seasons and rodeo. It sits along the Yellowstone River, looking east toward the open plains. Wolf Point is a quiet, resilient community deeply tied to the Fort Peck Indian Reservation and the agricultural economy of northeastern Montana. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle far removed from the state's major urban centers.

# Red Lodge vs Livingston, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Red Lodge and Livingston offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Red Lodge is a historic mountain town and gateway to Yellowstone with 2,336 people, its vibrant, historic downtown, its position at the base of the Beartooth Highway, and quick access to Red Lodge Mountain and the Beartooth Mountains. Livingston, with just 8,386 residents, is a historic railroad and arts town known for its literary heritage, its vibrant arts scene, and the Yellowstone River and Paradise Valley.

## Who Should Move to Red Lodge?

* Outdoor enthusiasts who want immediate access to skiing, hiking, and the Beartooth Wilderness.
* People seeking a lively, historic mountain town with a strong sense of community.
* Retirees and remote workers drawn to the spectacular scenery and small-town charm.
* Those who want to live in a gateway community to Yellowstone National Park.

## Who Should Move to Livingston?

* Artists, writers, and creative professionals.
* Fly fishing enthusiasts who want to live on the Yellowstone River.
* People who want a small-town atmosphere with easy access to Bozeman.
* Those who appreciate historic downtown architecture.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Red Lodge is around $550,000, while in Livingston it is closer to $509,477, a difference of roughly $40,523, making Livingston significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Red Lodge averages about $1,200 per month, compared to roughly $1,783 in Livingston.

Red Lodge's overall cost of living differs from Livingston's. Red Lodge offers lower average salaries compared to Livingston, with a median household income of $58,000 compared to Livingston's $65,187.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Red Lodge is a vibrant, historic mountain town that balances its role as a tourist destination with a strong, year-round local community. It offers spectacular access to the Beartooth Mountains and a lively downtown filled with shops, restaurants, and local events. Livingston is a windy, eclectic town that blends its gritty railroad past with a sophisticated arts community, serving as the scenic gateway to Yellowstone.

# Lewistown vs Columbia Falls: Which Montana Town is Right for You?

Lewistown and Columbia Falls are both small Montana communities with similar populations and similar annual precipitation, but they sit in completely different parts of the state and offer dramatically different lifestyles and price points. Lewistown is the Geographic Center of Montana, a historic ranching and agricultural community of 6,204 in Fergus County at 3,934 feet, with the nearest airport in Great Falls 115 miles away, 37 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 17 campgrounds, 7 fishing access sites, 3 lakes, 3 nature reserves, 2 golf courses, 2 museums, 2 state parks, 1 hot spring, and a 1,200-student school district. Columbia Falls is the Gateway to Glacier, a fast-growing Flathead Valley community of 5,308 in Flathead County at 3,077 feet, with Glacier Park International Airport in Kalispell just 6 miles away, 214 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 48 campgrounds, 31 trailheads, 27 fishing access sites, 26 lakes, 19 ski areas, 9 state parks, 8 golf courses, 8 museums, 6 nature reserves, 6 viewpoints, 4 historic sites, 4 disc golf courses, 4 boat launches, 11 waterfalls, 1 national forest, 1 national park, 1 river, and a 1,800-student school district.

## Who Should Move to Lewistown?

*   Retirees and remote workers who want a quiet, self-contained central Montana community with a genuine small-town character, affordable housing, and wide-open central Montana skies
*   History buffs and people drawn to Montana's ranching and agricultural heritage, with the Central Montana Historical Society and Museum, the Lewistown Art Center, and the surrounding Judith Basin country offering a deep connection to the region's past
*   Budget-conscious buyers who want one of the most affordable housing markets in Montana, with a typical home value of $268,126 and rent averaging just $896 per month
*   Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want 17 campgrounds, 7 fishing access sites, 3 nature reserves, 2 state parks, and a hot spring within 30 miles in the heart of central Montana's ranch country

## Who Should Move to Columbia Falls?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 48 campgrounds, 31 trailheads, 27 fishing access sites, 26 lakes, 19 ski areas, 11 waterfalls, 9 state parks, 6 nature reserves, and Glacier National Park all within 30 miles, making it one of the most recreation-rich communities in Montana
*   Skiers and snowboarders who want access to 19 ski areas within 30 miles, including Whitefish Mountain Resort, with 14.3 inches of annual snowfall and a Flathead Valley location that puts them in the heart of northwestern Montana's winter recreation scene
*   Buyers who want a fast-growing Flathead Valley community close to Glacier National Park at a higher price point, with a typical home value of $584,580 and Glacier Park International Airport just 6 miles away
*   Families who want a 1,800-student school district, 8 museums, 4 historic sites, and the full range of Flathead Valley amenities in Kalispell just minutes away

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Columbia Falls is dramatically more expensive than Lewistown. The typical home value in Columbia Falls is $584,580, compared to $268,126 in Lewistown, more than double. Rent is also higher in Columbia Falls at $1,003 per month versus $896 in Lewistown.

Median household income is significantly higher in Columbia Falls at $65,313 versus $44,195 in Lewistown. Lewistown's lower income reflects its more rural, agricultural economy, but its dramatically lower home prices make it one of the most affordable communities in Montana for buyers who want to own outright. Columbia Falls buyers are paying a significant premium for proximity to Glacier National Park and the Flathead Valley's outdoor lifestyle.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Lewistown is a quiet, traditional central Montana community where ranching, agriculture, the Judith Basin, and a deep sense of place define daily life. Columbia Falls is a fast-growing Flathead Valley gateway town where Glacier National Park, the Flathead River, and a booming outdoor tourism economy shape the community. The outdoor recreation gap is enormous: Columbia Falls has 214 sites within 30 miles versus Lewistown's 37, including 31 trailheads, 19 ski areas, and Glacier National Park that Lewistown simply cannot match. Lewistown has 17 campgrounds, a hot spring, and the wide-open central Montana landscape that defines its identity. If you want an affordable, quiet central Montana community with genuine small-town character and a deep connection to Montana's ranching heritage, Lewistown is one of the most underrated places to live in the state. If you want to live at the gateway to Glacier National Park in one of the most outdoor-rich communities in Montana, Columbia Falls is a compelling and increasingly popular choice.

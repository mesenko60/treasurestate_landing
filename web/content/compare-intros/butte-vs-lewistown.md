# Lewistown vs Butte: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Lewistown and Butte are both affordable Montana communities with strong outdoor access and a working-class character, but they sit in very different landscapes and carry very different histories. Lewistown is the Geographic Center of Montana, a quiet ranching community of 6,204 in Fergus County with 17 campgrounds within 30 miles and a traditional agricultural identity. Butte is the Richest Hill on Earth, a city of 34,494 in Silver Bow County at 5,741 feet elevation, with 17 museums within 30 miles, 4 hot springs, 14 campgrounds, and one of the most dramatic industrial and cultural histories in the American West.

## Who Should Move to Lewistown?

*   Retirees and remote workers who want a quiet, affordable small-town lifestyle with a typical home value around $268,126 and rent averaging just $896 per month
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 17 campgrounds, 7 fishing access sites, and the Big Snowy Mountains within 30 miles
*   People who want a traditional, tight-knit ranching community with a genuine small-town character
*   Anyone who wants a warmer, drier climate, with July highs averaging 82°F versus Butte's 78°F and January highs of 37°F versus Butte's 28°F

## Who Should Move to Butte?

*   History enthusiasts drawn to Butte's National Historic Landmark district, the Berkeley Pit, and 17 museums within 30 miles
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 14 campgrounds, 4 hot springs, 13 lakes, and a national forest within 30 miles
*   Budget-conscious buyers who want western Montana at an accessible price, with a typical home value around $274,163
*   People who want a larger city with a strong community identity, a vibrant St. Patrick's Day tradition, and a cultural calendar that reflects Butte's Irish and immigrant heritage

## Cost of Living Breakdown

These two towns are remarkably close on home prices. The typical home value in Lewistown is around $268,126, compared to $274,163 in Butte, a difference of less than $6,000. Rent diverges more: Lewistown averages about $896 per month, while Butte comes in at roughly $1,318, about $420 more per month.

The income gap is notable. The median household income in Butte is $57,633, compared to $44,195 in Lewistown. Butte residents earn considerably more on average, which helps offset the higher rent costs.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Lewistown is a town where the ranching calendar shapes daily life, the pace is slow, and the wide-open central Montana landscape is the main attraction. Butte is a city with a story that is unlike any other in Montana, a place where copper built ornate Victorian neighborhoods, the bust left a Superfund site visible from the highway, and the community has responded with a stubborn pride and a cultural revival that makes it one of the most interesting small cities in the West. If you want quiet, affordable, traditional small-town Montana, Lewistown is the right fit. If you want an affordable western Montana city with a remarkable history and a strong community identity, Butte is one of the state's most underrated options.

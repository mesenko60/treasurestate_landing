# Billings vs Helena: Which Montana City is Right for You?

Billings and Helena are both important Montana cities, but they serve very different purposes in the state's landscape. Billings is the Magic City, Montana's largest city at 117,116 people, a commercial and medical hub on the eastern plains with a warm, dry climate and a job market that draws people from across the region. Helena is the Queen City of the Rockies, the state capital at 33,100 people, a historically rich city tucked into the mountains of central Montana, where government, outdoor recreation, and a surprisingly vibrant arts scene coexist.

## Who Should Move to Billings?

*   Professionals in healthcare, energy, agriculture, or retail who want Montana's largest and most diverse job market
*   Families seeking a full-service city with strong schools, major hospitals, and a lower cost of living than Bozeman
*   People who prefer a warmer, drier climate and easy access to Yellowstone Country and the Beartooth Highway
*   Anyone who wants big-city amenities, major retailers, a busy airport, professional sports, in a Montana setting

## Who Should Move to Helena?

*   State government employees, lobbyists, lawyers, and anyone whose career is tied to the state capitol
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want trailheads, hot springs, mountain lakes, and wilderness within 30 miles of downtown
*   History lovers drawn to Helena's gold rush origins, Victorian architecture, and the Cathedral of St. Helena
*   People who want a smaller, more intimate city with a genuine sense of community and a manageable pace of life

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Helena is actually slightly more expensive to buy into than Billings, which surprises many people. The median home price in Helena is around $466,393, compared to $390,654 in Billings, a gap of roughly $75,000. Rent is nearly identical: about $1,489 per month in Helena versus $1,383 in Billings.

Income levels are also close, with Billings edging ahead at $71,855 median household income versus Helena's $69,341. The difference is modest, but combined with the higher home prices, Helena's cost of entry is meaningfully higher than Billings'.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Billings has the energy and infrastructure of a regional capital, always busy, always building, with a commercial pulse that keeps the city humming year-round. Helena has a quieter confidence about it, the kind of place where the governor might be at the same coffee shop as the hiking guide and the retired schoolteacher. The Last Chance Gulch pedestrian mall, the trail network in the surrounding hills, and the proximity to the Missouri River make Helena feel like a city that was designed for people who actually want to be outside. Both cities are authentically Montana; they just express it differently.

# Sidney vs Great Falls, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Sidney and Great Falls offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Sidney is a bustling agricultural and energy hub with 6,173 people, its position in the Bakken oil field, its rich farming heritage in the Yellowstone Valley, and quick access to the Yellowstone River and eastern Montana badlands. Great Falls, with just 60,403 residents, is a historic river city known for its military base, its rich Lewis and Clark history, and the Missouri River and Giant Springs.

## Who Should Move to Sidney?

* Workers in the energy, agriculture, or transportation sectors looking for strong employment opportunities.
* People seeking a hardworking, practical community with a resilient local economy.
* Those who want affordable housing compared to western Montana, with higher average wages driven by the oil and gas industry.
* Hunters and anglers who appreciate the rugged, uncrowded access to the Yellowstone River and surrounding plains.

## Who Should Move to Great Falls?

* Military families stationed at Malmstrom AFB.
* People seeking affordable urban living in Montana.
* History buffs interested in the Lewis and Clark expedition.
* Those who enjoy river recreation right in town.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Sidney is around $241,500, while in Great Falls it is closer to $285,000, a difference of roughly $43,500, making Sidney significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Sidney averages about $850 per month, compared to roughly $950 in Great Falls.

Sidney's overall cost of living differs from Great Falls's. Sidney offers higher average salaries compared to Great Falls, with a median household income of $75,200 compared to Great Falls's $55,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Sidney is a dynamic, industrious town where the pace of life is closely tied to the energy markets and agricultural seasons. It has a practical, no-nonsense atmosphere, serving as a major economic engine for eastern Montana while maintaining its roots as a tight-knit farming community. Great Falls is a grounded, affordable city with a strong military presence and a deep connection to the Missouri River.

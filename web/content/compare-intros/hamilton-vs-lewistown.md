# Lewistown vs Hamilton: Which Montana Town is Right for You?

Lewistown and Hamilton are both small Montana communities with similar populations and similar elevations, but they sit in very different parts of the state and offer dramatically different lifestyles and price points. Lewistown is the Geographic Center of Montana, a historic ranching and agricultural community of 6,204 in Fergus County at 3,934 feet, with the nearest airport in Great Falls 115 miles away, 37 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 17 campgrounds, 7 fishing access sites, 3 lakes, 3 nature reserves, 2 golf courses, 2 museums, 2 state parks, 1 hot spring, and a 1,200-student school district. Hamilton is the Banana Belt of Montana, a Bitterroot Valley community of 4,659 in Ravalli County at 3,573 feet known for its mild climate and outdoor access, with Missoula Montana Airport 56 miles away, 194 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 47 trailheads, 55 lakes, 32 campgrounds, 25 fishing access sites, 6 museums, 5 nature reserves, 3 golf courses, 3 historic sites, 3 disc golf courses, 2 state parks, 2 hot springs, 1 national forest, 1 river, 1 wilderness area, 1 wildlife refuge, 5 viewpoints, 3 waterfalls, and a 1,800-student school district.

## Who Should Move to Lewistown?

*   Retirees and remote workers who want a quiet, self-contained central Montana community with a genuine small-town character, affordable housing, and wide-open central Montana skies
*   History buffs and people drawn to Montana's ranching and agricultural heritage, with the Central Montana Historical Society and Museum, the Lewistown Art Center, and the surrounding Judith Basin country
*   Budget-conscious buyers who want one of the most affordable housing markets in Montana, with a typical home value of $268,126 and rent averaging just $896 per month
*   Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want 17 campgrounds, 7 fishing access sites, 3 nature reserves, 2 state parks, and a hot spring within 30 miles in the heart of central Montana's ranch country

## Who Should Move to Hamilton?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 47 trailheads, 55 lakes, 32 campgrounds, 25 fishing access sites, 5 nature reserves, 3 waterfalls, 1 wilderness area, and a national forest all within 30 miles in the Bitterroot Valley, one of the most outdoor-rich corridors in western Montana
*   People who want a mild climate, with Hamilton's January average high of 40 degrees and its reputation as the Banana Belt of Montana making it one of the warmer small communities in the state during winter
*   Buyers who want a western Montana community with strong Bitterroot Valley character and proximity to Missoula, with a typical home value of $516,033 and a 1,800-student school district
*   Hikers, fly fishers, and Bitterroot River enthusiasts who want 47 trailheads, 25 fishing access sites on the Bitterroot River and its tributaries, and the Bitterroot National Forest at their doorstep

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Hamilton is significantly more expensive than Lewistown. The typical home value in Hamilton is $516,033, compared to $268,126 in Lewistown, nearly double. Rent is slightly higher in Hamilton at $958 per month versus $896 in Lewistown.

Median household income is higher in Hamilton at $52,917 versus $44,195 in Lewistown. Hamilton's higher home prices reflect the Bitterroot Valley's strong demand from Missoula-area buyers and remote workers who want western Montana's outdoor lifestyle at a lower price point than Missoula itself. Lewistown remains one of the most affordable communities in Montana for buyers who want to own outright.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Lewistown is a quiet, traditional central Montana community where ranching, agriculture, the Judith Basin, and a deep sense of place define daily life. Hamilton is a Bitterroot Valley community where the river, the mountains, the trails, and a growing population of remote workers and outdoor enthusiasts define the character of daily life. The outdoor recreation gap is enormous: Hamilton has 194 sites within 30 miles versus Lewistown's 37, including 47 trailheads, 55 lakes, and a national forest that Lewistown does not have. Lewistown has 17 campgrounds, a hot spring, and the wide-open central Montana landscape that defines its identity. If you want an affordable, quiet central Montana community with genuine small-town character and a deep connection to Montana's ranching heritage, Lewistown is one of the most underrated places to live in the state. If you want a mild-climate Bitterroot Valley community with extraordinary outdoor access and proximity to Missoula, Hamilton is a compelling and increasingly popular choice.

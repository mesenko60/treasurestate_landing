# Laurel vs Lewistown: Which Montana Town is Right for You?

Laurel and Lewistown are both mid-sized central Montana communities, but they serve very different residents and offer very different access to the rest of the state. Laurel is the City Between the Yellowstone and the Rimrocks, a working-class railroad and refinery town of 7,215 in Yellowstone County at 3,307 feet, with Billings Logan International Airport 18 miles away, 60 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 21 fishing access sites, 10 museums, 6 state parks, 5 golf courses, 4 historic sites, 3 nature reserves, 2 disc golf courses, 1 river, and a 1,800-student school district. Lewistown is the Geographic Center of Montana, a quiet ranching and agricultural hub of 6,204 in Fergus County at 3,934 feet, with the nearest airport in Great Falls 115 miles away, 37 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 17 campgrounds, 7 fishing access sites, 3 nature reserves, 2 state parks, 2 golf courses, 2 museums, 1 hot spring, and a 1,200-student school district.

## Who Should Move to Laurel?

*   Working families and professionals who want easy access to Billings, the largest city in Montana, just 18 miles away, with its hospitals, employers, shopping, and airport, while living in a more affordable community
*   Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want 21 fishing access sites on the Yellowstone River, 6 state parks, 10 museums, and 5 golf courses within 30 miles, with the Rimrocks and the Yellowstone Valley as a backdrop
*   Buyers who want an affordable Montana community close to a major city, with a typical home value of $369,826 and rent averaging $1,160 per month
*   Families who want a 1,800-student school district, 4 historic sites, and a genuine working-class Montana community with strong Yellowstone Valley character

## Who Should Move to Lewistown?

*   Retirees and remote workers who want a quiet, self-contained central Montana town at the geographic center of the state, with a low cost of living and a strong sense of community identity
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 17 campgrounds, 7 fishing access sites, 3 nature reserves, 2 state parks, and 1 hot spring in the Big Snowy Mountains and Judith Basin area, far from the crowds of western Montana
*   Budget-conscious buyers who want to own a home in a genuine Montana small town at a very affordable price, with a typical home value of $268,126 and rent averaging just $896 per month
*   People who value a traditional, agricultural Montana lifestyle where ranching, community events, and wide-open central Montana skies define daily life

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Laurel is more expensive than Lewistown, though both are affordable by Montana standards. The typical home value in Laurel is $369,826, compared to $268,126 in Lewistown, about $100,000 more. Rent is also higher in Laurel at $1,160 per month versus $896 in Lewistown.

Median household income is higher in Laurel at $66,382 versus $44,195 in Lewistown. Laurel's higher income reflects its proximity to Billings and its more diverse employment base. Lewistown offers some of the most affordable housing of any Montana community its size.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Laurel is a working-class Yellowstone Valley community where the railroad, the refinery, the Rimrocks, and proximity to Billings define daily life. Lewistown is a quieter, more isolated central Montana ranching town where the Big Snowy Mountains, the Judith Basin, and a strong agricultural identity shape the community. Laurel has 21 fishing access sites, 10 museums, 6 state parks, and 4 historic sites within 30 miles that reflect its position in the heart of the Yellowstone Valley. Lewistown has 17 campgrounds and 1 hot spring, and sits at the true geographic center of Montana, surrounded by some of the most unspoiled open country in the state. If you want a working-class Montana community with easy access to Billings and strong Yellowstone Valley outdoor access, Laurel is a solid and affordable choice. If you want a quiet, affordable central Montana ranching town where the pace is slow, the skies are wide, and the community is genuinely tight-knit, Lewistown is one of the most underrated places to live in Montana.

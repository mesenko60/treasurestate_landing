# Libby vs Lewistown, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Libby and Lewistown offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Libby is a remote timber town in the Kootenai National Forest with 2,775 people, its deep roots in the logging industry, its stunning, rugged isolation, and quick access to the Kootenai River and Cabinet Mountains. Lewistown, with just 6,204 residents, is a historic agricultural center known for its charming stone architecture, its position at the geographic center of Montana, and the surrounding island mountain ranges and Spring Creek.

## Who Should Move to Libby?

* Outdoor enthusiasts seeking uncrowded access to some of Montana's most rugged and remote wilderness.
* Retirees and remote workers looking for affordable mountain living far from major tourist hubs.
* People who appreciate a tight-knit, resilient community with a strong working-class heritage.
* Hunters, anglers, and hikers who want to live in the heart of the Kootenai National Forest.

## Who Should Move to Lewistown?

* Retirees or remote workers who want a quiet, peaceful lifestyle in a classic Montana setting.
* People looking for affordable housing and a lower overall cost of living.
* Those who prefer a tight-knit, traditional small-town community with a strong agricultural heritage.
* History buffs and outdoor enthusiasts who enjoy uncrowded access to the Snowy, Judith, and Moccasin mountains.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Libby is around $285,000, while in Lewistown it is closer to $268,126, a difference of roughly $16,874, making Lewistown significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Libby averages about $850 per month, compared to roughly $896 in Lewistown.

Libby's overall cost of living differs from Lewistown's. Libby offers higher average salaries compared to Lewistown, with a median household income of $45,000 compared to Lewistown's $44,195.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Libby is a resilient, isolated town tucked into the far northwest corner of the state. It is defined by its timber heritage and its incredible, uncrowded access to the Kootenai River and Cabinet Mountains, offering a quiet, rugged lifestyle. Lewistown is quiet, slow-paced, and friendly in that classic Montana way. People wave when they drive by, and life revolves around ranching, community events, and the pristine waters of Big Spring Creek that flow right through the center of town.

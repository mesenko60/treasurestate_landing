# Conrad vs Laurel, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Conrad and Laurel offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Conrad is a quiet agricultural town on the northern plains with 2,318 people, its position as the seat of Pondera County, its strong farming and ranching economy, and quick access to the Rocky Mountain Front and Lake Frances. Laurel, with 7,215 residents, is a railroad and refinery town known for its industrial economy, its position as a transportation hub, and the Yellowstone River and nearby Beartooth Mountains.

## Who Should Move to Conrad?

* Workers in agriculture or local services looking for a quiet, stable community.
* People seeking very affordable housing far removed from the state's busy tourist hubs.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle.
* Hunters and anglers who want uncrowded access to the plains and the nearby Rocky Mountain Front.

## Who Should Move to Laurel?

* Industrial workers, railroad employees, and tradespeople looking for solid employment opportunities.
* Families who want a smaller school district and community feel while remaining just 15 minutes from Billings.
* Homebuyers seeking more affordable real estate than what is typically found in the Billings city limits.
* People who want easy access to the Beartooth Highway and the recreational opportunities south of the Yellowstone River.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Conrad is around $244,817, while in Laurel it is closer to $369,826, a difference of roughly $125,009, making Conrad significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Conrad averages about $888 per month, compared to roughly $1,160 in Laurel.

Conrad's overall cost of living differs from Laurel's. Conrad offers lower average salaries compared to Laurel, with a median household income of $47,348 compared to Laurel's $66,382.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Conrad is a classic, quiet Montana farming community where life revolves around the agricultural seasons and local school sports. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle with spectacular views of the Rocky Mountain Front to the west. Laurel is a hardworking, blue-collar community where the local economy is driven by the CHS refinery and the Montana Rail Link yard. It offers a slower, more traditional small-town pace than Billings, but residents frequently commute to the larger city for shopping, dining, and entertainment.

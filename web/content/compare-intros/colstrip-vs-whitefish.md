# Colstrip vs Whitefish, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Colstrip and Whitefish offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Colstrip is a purpose-built energy town in eastern Montana with 2,055 people, its massive coal-fired power plant, its excellent, company-built community amenities, and quick access to Castle Rock Lake and the surrounding pine hills. Whitefish, with 8,492 residents, is a premier ski and summer resort town known for Whitefish Mountain Resort, its pristine lake, and Glacier National Park and Whitefish Lake.

## Who Should Move to Colstrip?

* Workers in the energy, mining, or industrial sectors looking for high-paying employment.
* Families seeking excellent, well-funded local schools and community parks.
* People looking for very affordable housing combined with high average salaries.
* Those who appreciate a practical, hardworking community with a strong industrial identity.

## Who Should Move to Whitefish?

* Affluent buyers looking for a premium mountain lifestyle.
* Dedicated skiers and snowboarders.
* People who want a lively, walkable downtown with upscale dining.
* Those who desire immediate access to a major ski resort and a beautiful lake.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Colstrip is around $219,986, while in Whitefish it is closer to $850,000, a difference of roughly $630,014, making Colstrip significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Colstrip averages about $632 per month, compared to roughly $1,800 in Whitefish.

Colstrip's overall cost of living differs from Whitefish's. Colstrip offers higher average salaries compared to Whitefish, with a median household income of $86,000 compared to Whitefish's $75,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Colstrip is a unique, industrious town built specifically to support the local energy industry. It boasts surprisingly excellent parks, schools, and recreational facilities for its size, offering a practical, high-income lifestyle for those working in the energy sector. Whitefish is a polished, high-end resort community with a vibrant downtown, drawing tourists and affluent residents year-round.

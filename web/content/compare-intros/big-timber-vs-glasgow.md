# Big Timber vs Glasgow, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Big Timber and Glasgow offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Big Timber is a historic ranching community in the Sweet Grass Valley with 1,715 people, its deep, multi-generational ranching heritage, its classic, historic downtown architecture, and quick access to the Boulder River and the Crazy Mountains. Glasgow, with 3,224 residents, is a historic railroad town on the Hi-Line known for its position as a regional hub for northeastern Montana, its proximity to Fort Peck Dam, and Fort Peck Lake and the Charles M. Russell National Wildlife Refuge.

## Who Should Move to Big Timber?

* People seeking an authentic, traditional western community with a strong agricultural identity.
* Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want access to the pristine waters of the Boulder River.
* Those looking for a scenic, quiet lifestyle with relatively easy access to Bozeman or Billings.
* Retirees and remote workers drawn to the town's historic charm and spectacular mountain views.

## Who Should Move to Glasgow?

* Workers in agriculture, government, or the railroad industry looking for stable employment in northeastern Montana.
* Anglers and boaters who want to live near Fort Peck Lake, one of the premier walleye fisheries in the country.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, isolated community.
* Hunters and outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the vast, uncrowded public lands of the Missouri River Breaks.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Big Timber is around $422,291, while in Glasgow it is closer to $195,000, a difference of roughly $227,291, making Glasgow significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Big Timber averages about $1,009 per month, compared to roughly $750 in Glasgow.

Big Timber's overall cost of living differs from Glasgow's. Big Timber offers higher average salaries compared to Glasgow, with a median household income of $63,514 compared to Glasgow's $55,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Big Timber is a quintessential Montana ranching town, fiercely protective of its agricultural roots and stunning natural surroundings. It offers a quiet, authentic western lifestyle set against the dramatic backdrop of the Crazy Mountains. Glasgow is a resilient, isolated community that serves as the commercial and social center for a massive swath of northeastern Montana. It offers a quiet, slow-paced lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture, the railroad, and the incredible recreational opportunities at nearby Fort Peck Lake.

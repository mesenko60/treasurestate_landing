# Anaconda vs Whitefish: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Anaconda and Whitefish are both small western Montana communities of similar size, 9,421 and 8,915 people respectively, but they represent perhaps the most dramatic contrast in lifestyle and cost of living of any two towns in the state. Anaconda is the Smelter City, a historically extraordinary community in Deer Lodge County, where the iconic smokestack of the former Washoe Smelter still towers over town, 48 lakes lie within 30 miles, and Fairmont Hot Springs and the Jack Nicklaus-designed Old Works Golf Course are practically in the backyard. Whitefish is Stumptown, a resort-oriented mountain town in the Flathead Valley, where Whitefish Mountain Resort, Glacier National Park, and Whitefish Lake converge to create one of the most sought-after small-town lifestyle destinations in the American West.

## Who Should Move to Anaconda?

*   Remote workers and retirees seeking some of Montana's most affordable housing, median rent of just $661 per month and a median home price under $285,000
*   Outdoor lovers who want 48 lakes within 30 miles, Fairmont Hot Springs, Georgetown Lake, and the Pintler Wilderness
*   History enthusiasts drawn to Anaconda's copper-boom heritage, the Anaconda Smelter Stack State Park, and 16 museums within 30 miles
*   People who want a quiet, tight-knit community with a fascinating industrial history and a surprisingly rich outdoor recreation inventory at a fraction of Whitefish's cost

## Who Should Move to Whitefish?

*   Skiers who want Whitefish Mountain Resort as a home mountain, with Glacier National Park as a backyard
*   Remote workers and high-income earners who want one of Montana's most beautiful and walkable small-town settings
*   People who love a vibrant, boutique downtown with excellent dining, galleries, and a strong arts and music scene
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 202 recreation sites within 30 miles, including 19 ski areas, 33 trailheads, and 7 waterfalls

## Cost of Living Breakdown

The housing gap between these two communities is among the most extreme in Montana. The median home price in Anaconda is around $283,380, while in Whitefish it reaches $840,135, nearly three times the cost. The rent gap is equally staggering: about $661 per month in Anaconda versus $2,639 in Whitefish.

Whitefish's median household income of $71,110 is higher than Anaconda's $49,533, but that $21,500 income advantage does nothing to bridge a housing gap of over $556,000. The premium in Whitefish is almost entirely a lifestyle and location premium; Anaconda's affordability is real and remarkable.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Anaconda and Whitefish are two of Montana's most distinctive small communities, and they could hardly be more different. Anaconda is quiet, historically fascinating, and extraordinarily affordable, a town that has reinvented itself around its industrial past and its remarkable natural surroundings. Whitefish is a destination that people also happen to live in, a town where the ski season is a community event, Whitefish Lake defines the summer, and the proximity to Glacier National Park gives everyday life a sense of grandeur that is hard to replicate. If budget is the primary consideration, Anaconda wins decisively. If lifestyle and outdoor access are paramount and budget is flexible, Whitefish is one of the finest small towns in the American West.

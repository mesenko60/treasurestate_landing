# Columbia Falls vs Billings: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Columbia Falls and Billings offer two very different Montana lifestyles, separated by geography, size, and what they put at your doorstep. Columbia Falls is the Gateway to Glacier, a small community of 5,308 in Flathead County at 3,077 feet elevation, with 214 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 48 campgrounds, 31 trailheads, 19 ski areas, and Glacier National Park just up the road. Billings is the Magic City, Montana's largest city at 117,116 people in Yellowstone County, with a full-service regional economy, a major airport, 16 fishing access sites, 9 museums, and the kind of urban infrastructure that no other Montana city can match.

## Who Should Move to Columbia Falls?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 48 campgrounds, 31 trailheads, 27 fishing access sites, 19 ski areas, and Glacier National Park all within 30 miles
*   Families and remote workers who want a small Flathead Valley community with a 1,800-student school district and easy access to Kalispell's services just 6 miles away
*   People who want northwestern Montana's lush mountain and lake scenery as their daily backdrop
*   Anyone who wants a small-town feel with the Flathead Valley's growing economy and outdoor recreation culture within easy reach

## Who Should Move to Billings?

*   Professionals and families who want Montana's strongest job market, with a median household income of $71,855 and a broad range of employers across healthcare, energy, agriculture, and retail
*   People who want a full-service city with a regional airport, major retailers, a 16,000-student school district, and the widest range of services in the state
*   Anyone who wants a warmer, sunnier climate, with July highs averaging 89°F versus Columbia Falls's 81°F and significantly less annual snowfall
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 16 fishing access sites, 4 state parks, and the Rimrocks within easy reach of a major city

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Columbia Falls is considerably more expensive on housing despite being a much smaller town. The typical home value in Columbia Falls is around $584,580, compared to $390,654 in Billings, nearly $200,000 more. Rent, however, is lower in Columbia Falls: Lewistown averages about $1,003 per month, while Billings comes in at roughly $1,383.

The income gap is modest. The median household income in Billings is $71,855, compared to $65,313 in Columbia Falls. Billings residents earn somewhat more on average, and the lower home prices make Billings a significantly more affordable purchase market.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Columbia Falls is a small gateway community where the mountains, the river, and Glacier National Park define daily life. Billings is a working city with a downtown that has been reinvesting in itself, a strong economy, and the energy of a place that is Montana's undisputed commercial center. If you want northwestern Montana's extraordinary outdoor recreation and the Flathead Valley's natural setting, Columbia Falls is a compelling base. If you want Montana's best job market, lower home prices, and full urban services in a city that is growing with purpose, Billings is the clear choice.

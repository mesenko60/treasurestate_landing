# Red Lodge vs Kalispell, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Red Lodge and Kalispell offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Red Lodge is a historic mountain town and gateway to Yellowstone with 2,336 people, its vibrant, historic downtown, its position at the base of the Beartooth Highway, and quick access to Red Lodge Mountain and the Beartooth Mountains. Kalispell, with just 26,110 residents, is a commercial center of the Flathead Valley known for its retail and medical facilities, its historic downtown, and Flathead Lake and Glacier National Park.

## Who Should Move to Red Lodge?

* Outdoor enthusiasts who want immediate access to skiing, hiking, and the Beartooth Wilderness.
* People seeking a lively, historic mountain town with a strong sense of community.
* Retirees and remote workers drawn to the spectacular scenery and small-town charm.
* Those who want to live in a gateway community to Yellowstone National Park.

## Who Should Move to Kalispell?

* Families who want access to Glacier National Park without the resort town prices.
* Professionals working in healthcare or retail.
* People who want a mix of historic charm and modern conveniences.
* Outdoor lovers who want to be near Flathead Lake.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Red Lodge is around $550,000, while in Kalispell it is closer to $550,000, a difference of roughly $0, making Red Lodge significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Red Lodge averages about $1,200 per month, compared to roughly $1,400 in Kalispell.

Red Lodge's overall cost of living differs from Kalispell's. Red Lodge offers lower average salaries compared to Kalispell, with a median household income of $58,000 compared to Kalispell's $60,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Red Lodge is a vibrant, historic mountain town that balances its role as a tourist destination with a strong, year-round local community. It offers spectacular access to the Beartooth Mountains and a lively downtown filled with shops, restaurants, and local events. Kalispell is the practical, bustling heart of the Flathead Valley, balancing its role as a commercial hub with incredible access to natural wonders.

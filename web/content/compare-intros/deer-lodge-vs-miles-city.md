# Deer Lodge vs Miles City, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Deer Lodge and Miles City offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Deer Lodge is a historic ranching and prison town with 2,938 people, the Old Montana Prison Museum, its deep ranching heritage in the Powell Valley, and quick access to the Clark Fork River and the Flint Creek Range. Miles City, with just 8,412 residents, is a ranching community on the eastern plains known for the livestock industry, rodeo culture, and the wide-open Yellowstone River country.

## Who Should Move to Deer Lodge?

* History buffs who appreciate the town's unique museums and historic architecture.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, traditional western town.
* Workers in agriculture, government, or local services looking for a slow-paced lifestyle.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want uncrowded access to the Clark Fork River and surrounding mountains.

## Who Should Move to Miles City?

* Ranchers, livestock producers, and people whose careers and lifestyle are rooted in Eastern Montana's agricultural economy.
* Those seeking very affordable housing in a community where the typical home costs less than half what it does in many western Montana towns.
* Remote workers or retirees who want quiet, uncrowded living without the cost pressures that come with proximity to major tourist hubs.
* People who appreciate the authentic cowboy heritage of the Yellowstone Valley and the Bucking Horse Sale rodeo tradition.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Deer Lodge is around $225,000, while in Miles City it is closer to $233,769, a difference of roughly $8,769, making Deer Lodge significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Deer Lodge averages about $800 per month, compared to roughly $750 in Miles City.

Deer Lodge's overall cost of living differs from Miles City's. Deer Lodge offers lower average salaries compared to Miles City, with a median household income of $48,000 compared to Miles City's $60,732.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Deer Lodge is a quiet, deeply historic town that feels like a step back in time. It is defined by its agricultural roots and its unique history as the site of the original territorial prison, offering a slow-paced, affordable lifestyle in a beautiful valley setting. Miles City is quiet and agricultural in character, with a lifestyle shaped by ranching seasons and rodeo. It sits along the Yellowstone River, looking east toward the open plains.

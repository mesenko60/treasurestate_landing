# Libby vs Helena, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Libby and Helena offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Libby is a remote timber town in the Kootenai National Forest with 2,775 people, its deep roots in the logging industry, its stunning, rugged isolation, and quick access to the Kootenai River and Cabinet Mountains. Helena, with just 33,885 residents, is a historic state capital known for its government sector, its gold rush architecture, and Mount Helena and the Missouri River lakes.

## Who Should Move to Libby?

* Outdoor enthusiasts seeking uncrowded access to some of Montana's most rugged and remote wilderness.
* Retirees and remote workers looking for affordable mountain living far from major tourist hubs.
* People who appreciate a tight-knit, resilient community with a strong working-class heritage.
* Hunters, anglers, and hikers who want to live in the heart of the Kootenai National Forest.

## Who Should Move to Helena?

* Government workers and professionals.
* Families seeking a stable, safe community.
* Mountain bikers and hikers who want trails right out their back door.
* People who appreciate historic architecture and a central location.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Libby is around $285,000, while in Helena it is closer to $425,000, a difference of roughly $140,000, making Libby significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Libby averages about $850 per month, compared to roughly $1,100 in Helena.

Libby's overall cost of living differs from Helena's. Libby offers lower average salaries compared to Helena, with a median household income of $45,000 compared to Helena's $68,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Libby is a resilient, isolated town tucked into the far northwest corner of the state. It is defined by its timber heritage and its incredible, uncrowded access to the Kootenai River and Cabinet Mountains, offering a quiet, rugged lifestyle. Helena is a stable, professional town driven by state government, with a quiet downtown and immediate access to mountain trails.

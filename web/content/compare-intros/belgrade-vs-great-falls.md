# Belgrade vs Great Falls: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Belgrade and Great Falls are both in central Montana, but they occupy completely different worlds. Belgrade is the Hub of the Gallatin Valley, a fast-growing community of 12,509 tucked into the Gallatin Valley near Bozeman, with 104 outdoor recreation sites, a booming housing market, and the energy of a town that is still figuring out what it wants to be. Great Falls is the Electric City, a working-class city of 60,000 on the Missouri River, home to Malmstrom Air Force Base, the world-class C.M. Russell Museum, and a series of historic waterfalls that once stopped the Lewis and Clark Expedition for nearly a month.

## Who Should Move to Belgrade?

*   Families and professionals who want Gallatin Valley access with newer housing and strong school enrollment of about 3,800 students
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want a ski area, 21 fishing access sites, hot springs, and 16 trailheads all within 30 miles
*   Remote workers who want a quiet, growing community with quick access to Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport
*   Anyone drawn to the energy of a booming small city with a strong household income base

## Who Should Move to Great Falls?

*   Military families and veterans connected to Malmstrom Air Force Base
*   People who want a full-service city at a significantly lower cost, with a median home price around $328,554
*   History and art enthusiasts drawn to the C.M. Russell Museum and the Missouri River's Lewis and Clark heritage
*   Anyone who wants a practical, mid-sized Montana city with a strong sense of community and lower cost of living

## Cost of Living Breakdown

The cost difference between these two towns is substantial. The typical home value in Belgrade is around $544,626, compared to just $328,554 in Great Falls, a gap of over $216,000. Rent follows the same pattern: Belgrade averages about $2,206 per month, while Great Falls comes in at roughly $1,340.

Belgrade's higher prices are supported by a stronger income base. The median household income in Belgrade is $88,896, compared to $63,934 in Great Falls. But in Great Falls, everyday costs are lower across the board, and the housing market remains accessible for first-time buyers.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Belgrade is a town in motion, growing fast with new subdivisions and a young, professional demographic that is spilling out of Bozeman. Great Falls moves at a different pace entirely, a city with deep roots in ranching, military service, and the Missouri River, where the C.M. Russell Museum anchors a genuine arts community and the waterfalls along the river give the city a natural grandeur that surprises first-time visitors. If you want the Gallatin Valley's outdoor access and economic energy, Belgrade is the right call. If you want a more affordable, historically rich Montana city with a strong community identity, Great Falls delivers.

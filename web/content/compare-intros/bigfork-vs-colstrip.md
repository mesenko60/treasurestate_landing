# Colstrip vs Bigfork, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Colstrip and Bigfork offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Colstrip is a purpose-built energy town in eastern Montana with 2,055 people, its massive coal-fired power plant, its excellent, company-built community amenities, and quick access to Castle Rock Lake and the surrounding pine hills. Bigfork, with 5,000 residents, is a picturesque village on Flathead Lake known for its thriving arts scene, its cherry orchards, and Flathead Lake and the Swan River.

## Who Should Move to Colstrip?

* Workers in the energy, mining, or industrial sectors looking for high-paying employment.
* Families seeking excellent, well-funded local schools and community parks.
* People looking for very affordable housing combined with high average salaries.
* Those who appreciate a practical, hardworking community with a strong industrial identity.

## Who Should Move to Bigfork?

* Retirees and affluent professionals looking for a scenic, upscale lakeside community.
* Boaters, sailors, and water sports enthusiasts who want to live on the largest natural freshwater lake in the West.
* Art lovers who appreciate the town's galleries, summer playhouse, and cultural events.
* People who desire a resort-like atmosphere with high-end dining and boutique shopping.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Colstrip is around $219,986, while in Bigfork it is closer to $758,435, a difference of roughly $538,449, making Colstrip significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Colstrip averages about $632 per month, compared to roughly $1,103 in Bigfork.

Colstrip's overall cost of living differs from Bigfork's. Colstrip offers higher average salaries compared to Bigfork, with a median household income of $86,000 compared to Bigfork's $82,648.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Colstrip is a unique, industrious town built specifically to support the local energy industry. It boasts surprisingly excellent parks, schools, and recreational facilities for its size, offering a practical, high-income lifestyle for those working in the energy sector. Bigfork feels like a curated resort village, where life centers around the water, the arts, and seasonal tourism. The downtown is polished and walkable, filled with galleries and cafes, offering a lifestyle that is significantly more upscale and leisure-focused than most Montana towns.

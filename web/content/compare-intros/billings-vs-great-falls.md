# Billings vs Great Falls: Which Montana City is Right for You?

Billings and Great Falls are both working-class Montana cities with deep roots in agriculture and industry, but they serve very different roles in the state. Billings is the Magic City, Montana's largest city at 117,116 people, a regional powerhouse with a diversified economy, a busy airport, and a commercial core that draws people from across eastern Montana and Wyoming. Great Falls is the Electric City, a smaller, more compact city of 60,000 on the Missouri River, known for its world-class Lewis and Clark history, spectacular waterfalls, and a strong military presence anchored by Malmstrom Air Force Base.

## Who Should Move to Billings?

*   Professionals seeking Montana's widest range of job opportunities in healthcare, energy, and retail
*   Families who want a full-service city with strong schools, major medical facilities, and plenty of amenities
*   People who want the most affordable housing among Montana's larger cities without sacrificing services
*   Anyone who values a warmer, drier climate and easy access to the Beartooth Highway and Yellowstone Country

## Who Should Move to Great Falls?

*   Military families stationed at or connected to Malmstrom Air Force Base
*   History enthusiasts drawn to the Lewis and Clark National Historic Trail and the C.M. Russell Museum
*   Retirees and remote workers who want a quiet, affordable city with a genuine small-town feel
*   Outdoor lovers who want easy access to the Missouri River, the Rocky Mountain Front, and Glacier Country

## Cost of Living Breakdown

These two cities are closer in cost than most Montana comparisons. The median home price in Billings is around $390,654, while Great Falls comes in at $328,554, a meaningful gap but not a dramatic one. Rent is nearly identical: about $1,383 per month in Billings versus $1,340 in Great Falls.

Where Billings pulls ahead is income. The median household income in Billings is $71,855 compared to $63,934 in Great Falls, reflecting the larger city's broader and higher-paying job market.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Billings has the energy of a regional capital, always something going on, always somewhere new to eat, and a constant flow of people passing through from across the region. Great Falls is quieter and more settled, a city where people know their neighbors and the pace of life follows the seasons. The Missouri River trail system is a genuine gem, and the art scene, anchored by the C.M. Russell Museum, punches well above the city's size. Both are honest, unpretentious Montana cities, but Billings is where you go to build a career, and Great Falls is where you go to build a life.

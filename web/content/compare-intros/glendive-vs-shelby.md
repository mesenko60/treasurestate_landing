# Shelby vs Glendive, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Shelby and Glendive offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Shelby is a historic railroad and agricultural town with 3,169 people, its position on the Hi-Line, its famous 1923 Dempsey-Gibbons heavyweight title fight, and quick access to the Sweet Grass Hills and Lake Elwell. Glendive, with just 4,873 residents, is a rugged eastern Montana town known for its rich dinosaur fossil beds, its position as an agricultural and energy hub, and Makoshika State Park and the Yellowstone River.

## Who Should Move to Shelby?

* Workers in the agriculture, railroad, or energy sectors looking for steady employment on the Hi-Line.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, rural setting.
* Those who appreciate the stark beauty of the northern plains and the close-knit community of a small agricultural town.
* History buffs interested in the unique legacy of the 1923 heavyweight championship fight.

## Who Should Move to Glendive?

* Workers in the agriculture, railroad, or energy sectors looking for steady employment in eastern Montana.
* Homebuyers seeking some of the most affordable real estate in the state.
* People who appreciate the stark beauty of the badlands and the quiet isolation of the high plains.
* Paleontology enthusiasts and rockhounds drawn to the fossil-rich terrain of Makoshika State Park.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Shelby is around $185,000, while in Glendive it is closer to $185,447, a difference of roughly $447, making Shelby significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Shelby averages about $700 per month, compared to roughly $832 in Glendive.

Shelby's overall cost of living differs from Glendive's. Shelby offers lower average salaries compared to Glendive, with a median household income of $58,000 compared to Glendive's $71,063.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Shelby is a resilient, hardworking town on the northern plains, where life is shaped by the railroad, agriculture, and the vast open spaces of the Hi-Line. It offers a quiet, practical lifestyle far removed from the state's major urban centers. Glendive is a practical, hardworking town set against the dramatic backdrop of the Montana badlands. It is far removed from the tourist crowds of the western mountains, offering a quiet, resilient community life where the local economy is tied to the land, the railroad, and the river.

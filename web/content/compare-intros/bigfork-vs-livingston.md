# Bigfork vs Livingston: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Bigfork and Livingston are both small Montana communities with strong outdoor identities and a disproportionate number of artists and writers for their size, but they sit in very different landscapes. Bigfork is a Village by the Bay, a community of 5,000 on the eastern shore of Flathead Lake in Flathead County at 2,940 feet elevation, with 176 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 32 lakes, 27 campgrounds, and Glacier National Park nearby. Livingston is the Windiest City in Montana, a community of 8,040 in Park County at 4,501 feet on the Yellowstone River, with 144 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 40 lakes, 24 fishing access sites, 16 waterfalls, 16 campgrounds, and Yellowstone National Park 53 miles to the south.

## Who Should Move to Bigfork?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 27 campgrounds, 25 trailheads, 28 fishing access sites, 32 lakes, 16 state parks, and Glacier National Park all within 30 miles
*   Affluent buyers and retirees who want a small, scenic lakeside community with a median household income of $82,648 and a milder climate, with January lows averaging 23°F versus Livingston's 17°F
*   People who want a genuine village atmosphere on Flathead Lake with a summer arts festival, a community theater, and one of the most beautiful natural settings in Montana
*   Anyone who wants western Montana's mountain and lake scenery as their daily backdrop

## Who Should Move to Livingston?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts and anglers who want the Yellowstone River, 24 fishing access sites, 40 lakes, 16 waterfalls, 2 hot springs, and Yellowstone National Park all within reach
*   Artists, writers, and creative professionals drawn to Livingston's literary and artistic heritage, its historic downtown, and a community that has attracted writers and painters for generations
*   Budget-conscious buyers who want a smaller Montana community at a lower price, with a typical home value of $509,477 versus Bigfork's $758,435
*   People who want a small Montana town with a genuine Western character, a historic railroad depot, and easy access to both Bozeman and Yellowstone National Park

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Bigfork is more expensive than Livingston. The typical home value in Bigfork is around $758,435, compared to $509,477 in Livingston, a difference of about $249,000. Rent is lower in Bigfork: Bigfork averages about $1,103 per month, while Livingston comes in at roughly $1,783.

The median household income in Bigfork is $82,648, compared to $65,187 in Livingston. Bigfork residents earn more on average, but the home price gap is significant enough that Livingston offers a more accessible path to homeownership for most buyers.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Bigfork is a small lakeside village where the arts, outdoor recreation, and Flathead Lake define daily life. Livingston is a small river town where the Yellowstone River, the Absaroka Mountains, the wind, and a long literary tradition define daily life. Both towns punch above their size in cultural identity. If you want the lake, the mountains of the Flathead Valley, and Glacier National Park at your doorstep, Bigfork is exceptional. If you want the Yellowstone River, a historic downtown, and a community that has drawn writers and artists for over a century, Livingston is one of Montana's most compelling small towns.

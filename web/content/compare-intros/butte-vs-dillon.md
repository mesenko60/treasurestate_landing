# Dillon vs Butte: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Dillon and Butte are both small-to-mid-sized western Montana communities with deep historical roots and strong outdoor access, but they differ significantly in size, character, and climate. Dillon is Montana's Hometown, a small Beaverhead County community of 3,880 at 5,095 feet, known for its quiet ranching character, the Beaverhead River, 24 fishing access sites, 16 campgrounds, 5 state parks, 2 hot springs, and the University of Montana Western. Butte is the Richest Hill on Earth, a community of 34,494 in Silver Bow County at 5,741 feet, with 94 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 17 museums, 14 campgrounds, 13 lakes, 12 fishing access sites, 9 nature reserves, 7 golf courses, 4 hot springs, 3 historic sites, 3 viewpoints, 2 disc golf courses, a national forest, and a wilderness area.

## Who Should Move to Dillon?

*   Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want 24 fishing access sites, 16 campgrounds, 5 state parks, 2 hot springs, 8 lakes, and the Beaverhead River all within 30 miles
*   Remote workers, retirees, and families who want a genuinely quiet, small-town Montana lifestyle at a very affordable price, with a typical home value of $375,585 and rent averaging just $821 per month
*   People who want a small university town with the University of Montana Western providing community events, sports, and cultural programming
*   Anyone drawn to southwestern Montana's wide-open spaces, ranching character, and the Big Hole Valley just to the west

## Who Should Move to Butte?

*   History enthusiasts and people drawn to Butte's extraordinary copper mining heritage, with 17 museums, 3 historic sites, the Berkeley Pit, and one of the most architecturally rich historic districts in the American West
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 14 campgrounds, 13 lakes, 12 fishing access sites, 9 nature reserves, 7 golf courses, 4 hot springs, a national forest, and a wilderness area all within 30 miles
*   Budget-conscious buyers who want a mid-sized Montana community at a low price, with a typical home value of $274,163, lower than both Dillon and most other Montana cities
*   People who want a mid-sized Montana community with a 4,200-student school district, Butte Airport 5 miles away, and the gritty, authentic character of a city shaped by a century of hard-rock mining

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Dillon has a higher typical home price than Butte. The typical home value in Dillon is around $375,585, compared to $274,163 in Butte, about 37% higher. Rent is lower in Dillon: Dillon averages about $821 per month, while Butte comes in at roughly $1,318, about 60% higher.

Median household income is slightly higher in Butte at $57,633 versus $51,458 in Dillon. Dillon's dramatically lower rent makes it the more affordable monthly lifestyle, while Butte offers a lower home purchase price.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Dillon is a small southwestern Montana town where the Beaverhead River, ranching culture, and the University of Montana Western define community life. Butte is a mid-sized mining city where copper history, a fierce civic pride, and a rugged mountain character define community identity. Butte has a density of history and culture that Dillon does not, with a remarkable historic district, a strong Irish-American heritage, and a community that is deeply proud of its past. If you want a quiet, affordable southwestern Montana lifestyle with excellent fishing and outdoor access, Dillon is a compelling choice. If you want a mid-sized Montana city with extraordinary history, affordable home prices, and a community with real character, Butte is one of Montana's most distinctive and underappreciated places to live.

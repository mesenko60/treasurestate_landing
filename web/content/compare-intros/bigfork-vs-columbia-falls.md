# Columbia Falls vs Bigfork: Which Montana Town Is Right for You?

Columbia Falls and Bigfork are neighbors in the Flathead Valley, both sitting in the shadow of Glacier National Park and Flathead Lake, yet they offer noticeably different lifestyles. Columbia Falls, known as the Gateway to Glacier, is a working-class community of 5,308 people with a blue-collar heart, a growing arts scene, and direct access to some of the most dramatic wilderness in North America. Bigfork, a Village by the Bay on the northeast shore of Flathead Lake, is a smaller, more polished resort community of about 5,000 people, known for its summer theater, galleries, and upscale dining along Electric Avenue.

## Who Should Move to Columbia Falls?

- Families and working professionals who want Glacier Country access without resort-town price tags
- Outdoor enthusiasts who want a massive range of recreation options, from skiing to paddling to hiking, right out the door
- People who prefer a genuine small-town community with local businesses and a strong sense of place
- Anyone who wants proximity to Kalispell's services while living in a quieter, more affordable setting

## Who Should Move to Bigfork?

- Retirees and remote workers drawn to a walkable, arts-forward village atmosphere on Flathead Lake
- People who enjoy summer festivals, live theater, and a curated dining and gallery scene
- Those willing to pay a premium for a scenic lakeside setting with a resort-town feel
- Boaters, kayakers, and lake lovers who want Flathead Lake as their backyard

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is the sharpest difference between these two towns. The typical home value in Bigfork is $758,435, compared to $584,580 in Columbia Falls, a gap of roughly $174,000. Rent follows a similar pattern, with Bigfork averaging $1,103 per month versus $1,003 in Columbia Falls. Bigfork also reports a higher median household income at $82,648, compared to $65,313 in Columbia Falls, which partially offsets the cost difference for those with strong remote incomes.

Both towns share the same Flathead County tax base and are served by Glacier Park International Airport in Kalispell, just 6 miles from Columbia Falls and 22 miles from Bigfork.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Columbia Falls feels like a town that works for a living. The streets are busy with tradespeople, families, and outdoor adventurers fueling up before heading into Glacier. There is genuine community here, the kind built around school sports, local diners, and neighbors who know each other. Bigfork, by contrast, slows down in a different way. In summer it buzzes with tourists and theater-goers along Electric Avenue, and in the off-season it settles into a quiet, intimate lakeside rhythm that longtime residents treasure. Both towns are beautiful, but they attract different kinds of people.

Columbia Falls has 214 recreational sites within 30 miles, including 48 campgrounds, 19 ski areas, 31 trailheads, and direct access to Glacier National Park. Bigfork has 176 sites, with strong lake access, 16 state parks, and 32 lakes within range. If mountain and backcountry recreation is the priority, Columbia Falls wins on volume. If lake life and a polished village setting matter more, Bigfork is the clear choice.

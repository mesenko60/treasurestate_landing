# Glendive vs Butte: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Glendive and Butte are both smaller Montana communities with strong identities and affordable housing, but they sit in very different landscapes and carry very different histories. Glendive is the Paddlefish Capital of the World, a community of 4,873 in Dawson County in eastern Montana at 2,083 feet elevation, with 30 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles and Makoshika State Park at its edge. Butte is the Richest Hill on Earth, a community of 34,494 in Silver Bow County in western Montana at 5,741 feet, with 94 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 17 museums, 14 campgrounds, 13 lakes, 12 fishing access sites, 9 nature reserves, 7 golf courses, 4 hot springs, 3 historic sites, a national forest, a ski area, and one of the most extraordinary industrial heritage landscapes in the American West.

## Who Should Move to Glendive?

*   Budget-conscious buyers who want Montana at a very low price, with a typical home value of $185,447 and median rent of just $832 per month, compared to Butte's $274,163 and $1,318
*   Outdoor enthusiasts and anglers who want Makoshika State Park, the Yellowstone River, and some of the best paddlefish snagging in the country right in their backyard
*   People who want a small, quiet eastern Montana community with a genuine small-town character and a slower pace of life
*   Remote workers or retirees who want affordable housing and a community where $71,063 in median household income goes a very long way

## Who Should Move to Butte?

*   History enthusiasts and people drawn to authentic, gritty Montana character, with the Butte Historic District, the Berkeley Pit, the World Museum of Mining, and one of the most architecturally rich Victorian-era downtowns in the West
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 14 campgrounds, 12 fishing access sites, 13 lakes, 9 nature reserves, 4 hot springs, a ski area at Discovery Mountain, and a national forest all within 30 miles
*   Budget-conscious buyers who want western Montana at a moderate price, with a typical home value of $274,163, well below Missoula or Bozeman
*   People who want a mid-sized Montana community with a strong sense of place, a labor history unlike anywhere else in the state, and easy access to both Helena and Anaconda

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Glendive is more affordable than Butte, though both are among the more reasonably priced communities in Montana. The typical home value in Glendive is around $185,447, compared to $274,163 in Butte, about 48% higher. Rent follows the same pattern: Glendive averages about $832 per month, while Butte comes in at roughly $1,318.

Median household income is higher in Glendive at $71,063 versus $57,633 in Butte. Glendive residents earn more on average and pay less for housing, giving it the better affordability ratio of the two.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Glendive is a small eastern Montana town where the Yellowstone River, Makoshika State Park, and the paddlefish season define the calendar. Butte is a western Montana city where copper mining history, Irish-American heritage, labor union tradition, and the Continental Divide define community identity. Butte has a roughness and authenticity that few Montana cities can match, along with serious outdoor access and some of the most affordable housing in western Montana. If you want small-town eastern Montana at the lowest possible price, Glendive is a solid choice. If you want a community with a deep, complex history and western Montana's outdoor access at a moderate price, Butte is one of Montana's most interesting and underrated cities.

# Malta vs Columbia Falls, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Malta and Columbia Falls offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Malta is a historic agricultural town on the Hi-Line with 2,100 people, its world-class dinosaur fossils, its deep ranching heritage, and quick access to the Bowdoin National Wildlife Refuge and the Missouri River Breaks. Columbia Falls, with 5,308 residents, is a gateway community to Glacier National Park known for its timber industry roots, its revitalized downtown, and the Flathead River and Glacier National Park.

## Who Should Move to Malta?

* Workers in agriculture, government, or local services looking for a quiet, stable community.
* People seeking very affordable housing and a low overall cost of living.
* Hunters, birders, and outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the vast, uncrowded public lands of northeastern Montana.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle on the high plains.

## Who Should Move to Columbia Falls?

* Outdoor lovers who want to live minutes from the entrance to Glacier National Park.
* Families looking for a community with a strong local spirit and a growing downtown scene.
* People who work in the Flathead Valley but prefer a town with a slightly more blue-collar, unpretentious history than Whitefish.
* Those who want immediate access to the Flathead River for fishing, floating, and recreation.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Malta is around $244,540, while in Columbia Falls it is closer to $584,580, a difference of roughly $340,040, making Malta significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Malta averages about $693 per month, compared to roughly $1,003 in Columbia Falls.

Malta's overall cost of living differs from Columbia Falls's. Malta offers lower average salaries compared to Columbia Falls, with a median household income of $59,449 compared to Columbia Falls's $65,313.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Malta is a quiet, resilient community on the eastern Hi-Line, defined by agriculture, wide-open spaces, and its unique paleontology attractions. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle far from the state's urban centers. Columbia Falls has transitioned from a traditional timber town into a vibrant gateway community, balancing its working-class roots with a new influx of breweries, markets, and tourism. It feels less resort-focused than neighboring Whitefish, maintaining a strong, independent local character while serving as the front porch to Glacier National Park.

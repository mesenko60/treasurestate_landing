# Columbia Falls vs Helena: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Columbia Falls and Helena are both western Montana communities, but they serve very different needs. Columbia Falls is the Gateway to Glacier, a small community of 5,308 in Flathead County at 3,077 feet elevation, with 214 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 48 campgrounds, 31 trailheads, 19 ski areas, and Glacier National Park just up the road. Helena is the Queen City of the Rockies, Montana's state capital with 33,100 residents in Lewis and Clark County at 4,039 feet, with 107 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 10 historic sites, 28 campgrounds, 3 hot springs, and a civic identity shaped by gold rush history and state government.

## Who Should Move to Columbia Falls?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 48 campgrounds, 31 trailheads, 27 fishing access sites, 19 ski areas, and Glacier National Park all within 30 miles
*   Families and remote workers who want a small Flathead Valley community with lower rent, averaging about $1,003 per month versus Helena's $1,489
*   People who want northwestern Montana's mountain and lake scenery, with 26 lakes within 30 miles, as their daily backdrop
*   Anyone who wants a small-town feel with extraordinary outdoor access and Kalispell's services just 6 miles away

## Who Should Move to Helena?

*   State government employees, lobbyists, and public sector professionals drawn to Montana's capital city and its stable employment base
*   History enthusiasts who want 10 historic sites, 9 museums, the Cathedral of Saint Helena, and the Last Chance Gulch pedestrian mall all within 30 miles
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 28 campgrounds, 11 trailheads, 10 fishing access sites, 3 hot springs, and a national forest within 30 miles
*   Families who want a mid-sized Montana city with a 7,800-student school district, a regional airport, and the full range of services that come with a state capital

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Columbia Falls is considerably more expensive on home prices. The typical home value in Columbia Falls is around $584,580, compared to $466,393 in Helena, a difference of over $118,000. Rent is lower in Columbia Falls: Columbia Falls averages about $1,003 per month, while Helena comes in at roughly $1,489.

The median household incomes are close: $65,313 in Columbia Falls and $69,341 in Helena. Helena residents earn somewhat more on average, reflecting the influence of state government salaries and a broader professional economy.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Columbia Falls is a small gateway community where Glacier National Park, the Flathead River, and the surrounding mountains define daily life. Helena is a capital city with a gold rush past, a Victorian downtown that has been carefully preserved, and a community that takes genuine pride in its role as the seat of Montana government. If you want northwestern Montana's extraordinary outdoor recreation and the Flathead Valley's natural setting, Columbia Falls is a compelling base. If you want a mid-sized Montana city with a rich history, a strong outdoor recreation footprint, and the stability of a government-anchored economy, Helena is a compelling choice.

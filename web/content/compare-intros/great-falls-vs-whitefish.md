# Great Falls vs Whitefish: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Great Falls and Whitefish represent two very different visions of Montana life, one practical and affordable, the other spectacular and expensive. Great Falls is the Electric City, a city of 60,000 on the Missouri River in central Montana, anchored by Malmstrom Air Force Base, the C.M. Russell Museum, and a river trail system that winds through the heart of town. Whitefish is Stumptown, a small, resort-oriented mountain town of 8,915 in the Flathead Valley, where Whitefish Mountain Resort, Glacier National Park, and Whitefish Lake converge to create one of the most sought-after outdoor lifestyle destinations in the country.

## Who Should Move to Great Falls?

*   Military families stationed at or connected to Malmstrom Air Force Base
*   Families and professionals who want a full-service city with a regional airport, larger job market, and dramatically lower housing costs
*   History and art lovers drawn to the C.M. Russell Museum and the Lewis and Clark National Historic Trail
*   People who want a stable, community-oriented Montana city without the resort-town price tag

## Who Should Move to Whitefish?

*   Skiers and outdoor enthusiasts who want Whitefish Mountain Resort and Glacier National Park essentially in their backyard
*   Remote workers and high-income earners who can afford the premium and want one of Montana's most beautiful settings
*   People who love a vibrant, walkable small-town downtown with excellent dining, boutiques, and a strong arts scene
*   Families and retirees who want a tight-knit, scenically spectacular community and are prepared for the cost of entry

## Cost of Living Breakdown

The housing gap between these two communities is among the most dramatic of any comparison in Montana. The median home price in Great Falls is around $328,554, while in Whitefish it climbs to $840,135, more than two and a half times the cost. Rent is equally stark: about $1,340 per month in Great Falls versus $2,639 in Whitefish.

Whitefish's median household income of $71,110 is higher than Great Falls' $63,934, but that $7,000 difference does nothing to close a housing gap of over $500,000. The premium in Whitefish is almost entirely a lifestyle and location premium, and it is one of the steepest in the state.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Great Falls is a steady, unpretentious city that does what it does without a lot of fanfare. The Missouri River trail system, the C.M. Russell Museum, and the military community give it a quiet but genuine sense of purpose. Whitefish is a destination that people also happen to live in, a town where the ski season defines the winter, the lake defines the summer, and the proximity to Glacier National Park gives everyday life a sense of grandeur that few places in the country can match. These two communities are not really competing for the same residents; they are simply offering very different answers to the question of what Montana life should look like.

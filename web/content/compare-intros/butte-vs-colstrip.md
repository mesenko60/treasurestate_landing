# Colstrip vs Butte, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Colstrip and Butte offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Colstrip is a purpose-built energy town in eastern Montana with 2,055 people, its massive coal-fired power plant, its excellent, company-built community amenities, and quick access to Castle Rock Lake and the surrounding pine hills. Butte, with 34,768 residents, is a historic mining city known for its rich labor history, its stunning historic architecture, and the Continental Divide and surrounding national forests.

## Who Should Move to Colstrip?

* Workers in the energy, mining, or industrial sectors looking for high-paying employment.
* Families seeking excellent, well-funded local schools and community parks.
* People looking for very affordable housing combined with high average salaries.
* Those who appreciate a practical, hardworking community with a strong industrial identity.

## Who Should Move to Butte?

* History lovers who appreciate historic homes and architecture.
* People looking for very affordable mountain living.
* Those who want a town with a strong, independent character.
* Engineering students attending Montana Tech.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Colstrip is around $219,986, while in Butte it is closer to $250,000, a difference of roughly $30,014, making Colstrip significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Colstrip averages about $632 per month, compared to roughly $800 in Butte.

Colstrip's overall cost of living differs from Butte's. Colstrip offers higher average salaries compared to Butte, with a median household income of $86,000 compared to Butte's $50,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Colstrip is a unique, industrious town built specifically to support the local energy industry. It boasts surprisingly excellent parks, schools, and recreational facilities for its size, offering a practical, high-income lifestyle for those working in the energy sector. Butte is fiercely proud of its working-class roots, offering a unique, historic atmosphere that is distinct from the rest of the state.

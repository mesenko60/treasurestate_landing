# Cut Bank vs Deer Lodge, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Cut Bank and Deer Lodge offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Cut Bank is a windy agricultural town on the Hi-Line with 3,056 people, its proximity to the Blackfeet Indian Reservation, its agricultural and energy economy, and quick access to the eastern edge of Glacier National Park. Deer Lodge, with just 2,938 residents, is a historic ranching and prison town known for the Old Montana Prison Museum, its deep ranching heritage in the Powell Valley, and the Clark Fork River and the Flint Creek Range.

## Who Should Move to Cut Bank?

* Workers in agriculture, energy, or government looking for affordable living on the Hi-Line.
* People seeking a quiet, resilient community with a low cost of living.
* Those who want to live near Glacier National Park without paying resort-town prices.
* Individuals who appreciate the stark, windy beauty of the northern plains.

## Who Should Move to Deer Lodge?

* History buffs who appreciate the town's unique museums and historic architecture.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, traditional western town.
* Workers in agriculture, government, or local services looking for a slow-paced lifestyle.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want uncrowded access to the Clark Fork River and surrounding mountains.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Cut Bank is around $175,000, while in Deer Lodge it is closer to $225,000, a difference of roughly $50,000, making Cut Bank significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Cut Bank averages about $650 per month, compared to roughly $800 in Deer Lodge.

Cut Bank's overall cost of living differs from Deer Lodge's. Cut Bank offers higher average salaries compared to Deer Lodge, with a median household income of $52,000 compared to Deer Lodge's $48,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Cut Bank is a tough, practical town known for its wind and its hardworking residents. It serves as a gateway between the vast agricultural plains of the Hi-Line and the dramatic peaks of the Rocky Mountain Front, offering a quiet, affordable lifestyle. Deer Lodge is a quiet, deeply historic town that feels like a step back in time. It is defined by its agricultural roots and its unique history as the site of the original territorial prison, offering a slow-paced, affordable lifestyle in a beautiful valley setting.

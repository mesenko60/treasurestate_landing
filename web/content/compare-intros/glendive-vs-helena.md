# Glendive vs Helena: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Glendive and Helena are both smaller Montana communities with similar median household incomes, but they sit in very different landscapes and offer very different lifestyles. Glendive is the Paddlefish Capital of the World, a community of 4,873 in Dawson County in eastern Montana at 2,083 feet elevation, with 30 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles and Makoshika State Park at its edge. Helena is the Queen City of the Rockies, Montana's state capital with 33,100 residents in Lewis and Clark County at 4,039 feet, with 107 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 28 campgrounds, 11 trailheads, 10 historic sites, 10 fishing access sites, 10 lakes, 9 museums, 3 hot springs, and a community shaped by government, gold rush history, and the Continental Divide.

## Who Should Move to Glendive?

*   Budget-conscious buyers who want Montana at a very low price, with a typical home value of $185,447 and median rent of just $832 per month, compared to Helena's $466,393 and $1,489
*   Outdoor enthusiasts and anglers who want Makoshika State Park, the Yellowstone River, and some of the best paddlefish snagging in the country right in their backyard
*   People who want a small, quiet eastern Montana community with a genuine small-town character and a slower pace of life
*   Remote workers or retirees who want affordable housing and a community where $71,063 in median household income goes a very long way

## Who Should Move to Helena?

*   Government employees, professionals, and families who want Montana's state capital, with a 7,800-student school district, Helena Regional Airport 5 miles away, and the stability that comes with a government-anchored economy
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 28 campgrounds, 11 trailheads, 10 fishing access sites, 3 hot springs, 10 historic sites, and the Mount Helena trail system all within 30 miles
*   History enthusiasts drawn to Helena's gold rush heritage, the Cathedral of Saint Helena, Last Chance Gulch, and one of the most architecturally rich downtowns in Montana
*   People who want a mid-sized Montana city with a walkable downtown, a strong arts scene, and easy access to the Helena National Forest and the Elkhorn Mountains

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Glendive is dramatically more affordable than Helena. The typical home value in Glendive is around $185,447, compared to $466,393 in Helena, more than double. Rent follows the same pattern: Glendive averages about $832 per month, while Helena comes in at roughly $1,489.

Median household incomes are nearly identical: $71,063 in Glendive versus $69,341 in Helena. That means Glendive residents get nearly the same purchasing power at a fraction of the housing cost.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Glendive is a small eastern Montana town where the Yellowstone River, Makoshika State Park, and the paddlefish season define the calendar. Helena is a mid-sized capital city with a gold rush history, a walkable downtown built along Last Chance Gulch, and a community that balances government work with serious outdoor recreation on the Continental Divide. If you want small-town eastern Montana at a very low cost of living, Glendive is a solid choice. If you want a mid-sized Montana city with a rich history, a stable economy, and easy access to the Helena National Forest, Helena is a strong and often underappreciated option.

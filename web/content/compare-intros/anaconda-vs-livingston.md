# Anaconda vs Livingston: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Anaconda and Livingston are two of Montana's most historically interesting small communities, similar in size, both deeply rooted in the state's industrial and cultural past, but offering very different daily lives. Anaconda is the Smelter City, a community of 9,421 in Deer Lodge County, where the iconic smokestack of the former Washoe Smelter still towers over town, 48 lakes lie within 30 miles, and Fairmont Hot Springs and the Jack Nicklaus-designed Old Works Golf Course are practically in the backyard. Livingston is the Windiest City in Montana, a small, storied town of 8,040 in the Paradise Valley, where the Yellowstone River flows cold and clear through downtown, the Absaroka Range rises to the south, and a long tradition of artists, writers, and fly fishers gives the place a character that is genuinely hard to find elsewhere.

## Who Should Move to Anaconda?

*   Remote workers and retirees seeking some of Montana's most affordable housing, median rent of just $661 per month and a median home price under $285,000
*   Outdoor lovers who want 48 lakes within 30 miles, Fairmont Hot Springs, Georgetown Lake, and the Pintler Wilderness
*   History enthusiasts drawn to Anaconda's copper-boom heritage, the Anaconda Smelter Stack State Park, and 16 museums within 30 miles
*   People who want a quiet, tight-knit community with a fascinating industrial history and extraordinary affordability

## Who Should Move to Livingston?

*   Artists, writers, and creative professionals drawn to Livingston's long-established arts and literary community
*   Fly fishing enthusiasts who want direct access to the Yellowstone River, one of Montana's finest, right in town, plus 24 fishing access points within 30 miles
*   Remote workers and retirees who want a small, character-rich community close to Yellowstone National Park and the Paradise Valley
*   People who want the feel of a genuine Montana small town with 16 waterfalls and 40 lakes within 30 miles

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Anaconda is dramatically more affordable. The median home price in Anaconda is around $283,380, compared to $509,477 in Livingston, a difference of about $226,000. The rent gap is even more striking: about $661 per month in Anaconda versus $1,783 in Livingston, less than half the cost.

Livingston's median household income of $65,187 is higher than Anaconda's $49,533, but that $15,600 income advantage does not come close to bridging a housing gap of over $225,000. Livingston's higher prices reflect its desirability among remote workers, second-home buyers, and people drawn to the Paradise Valley and Yellowstone access.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Anaconda and Livingston are both authentically Montana communities with deep historical roots, but they attract very different kinds of people. Anaconda is quiet, affordable, and historically extraordinary, a town that has found a way to turn its industrial past into an asset, with the Old Works Golf Course and the Smelter Stack State Park as its most visible symbols of reinvention. Livingston is more romantic and literary, a small town known for its wind, its writers, its fly fishing, and its proximity to Yellowstone. Both communities have remarkable outdoor access; Anaconda leads in lakes (48 vs 40), while Livingston leads in waterfalls (16 vs 3) and fishing access points (24 vs 8). The choice comes down to whether you want the Smelter City's affordability or the Windiest City's soul.

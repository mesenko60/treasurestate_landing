# Malta vs Big Timber, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Malta and Big Timber offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Malta is a historic agricultural town on the Hi-Line with 2,100 people, its world-class dinosaur fossils, its deep ranching heritage, and quick access to the Bowdoin National Wildlife Refuge and the Missouri River Breaks. Big Timber, with 1,715 residents, is a historic ranching community in the Sweet Grass Valley known for its deep, multi-generational ranching heritage, its classic, historic downtown architecture, and the Boulder River and the Crazy Mountains.

## Who Should Move to Malta?

* Workers in agriculture, government, or local services looking for a quiet, stable community.
* People seeking very affordable housing and a low overall cost of living.
* Hunters, birders, and outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the vast, uncrowded public lands of northeastern Montana.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle on the high plains.

## Who Should Move to Big Timber?

* People seeking an authentic, traditional western community with a strong agricultural identity.
* Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want access to the pristine waters of the Boulder River.
* Those looking for a scenic, quiet lifestyle with relatively easy access to Bozeman or Billings.
* Retirees and remote workers drawn to the town's historic charm and spectacular mountain views.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Malta is around $244,540, while in Big Timber it is closer to $422,291, a difference of roughly $177,751, making Malta significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Malta averages about $693 per month, compared to roughly $1,009 in Big Timber.

Malta's overall cost of living differs from Big Timber's. Malta offers lower average salaries compared to Big Timber, with a median household income of $59,449 compared to Big Timber's $63,514.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Malta is a quiet, resilient community on the eastern Hi-Line, defined by agriculture, wide-open spaces, and its unique paleontology attractions. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle far from the state's urban centers. Big Timber is a quintessential Montana ranching town, fiercely protective of its agricultural roots and stunning natural surroundings. It offers a quiet, authentic western lifestyle set against the dramatic backdrop of the Crazy Mountains.

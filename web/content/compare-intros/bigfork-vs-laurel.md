# Laurel vs Bigfork: Which Montana Town is Right for You?

Laurel and Bigfork are both mid-sized Montana communities, but they represent very different lifestyles and price points. Laurel is the City Between the Yellowstone and the Rimrocks, a working-class Yellowstone Valley community of 7,215 in Yellowstone County at 3,307 feet, with Billings Logan International Airport 18 miles away, 60 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 21 fishing access sites, 10 museums, 6 state parks, 5 golf courses, 4 historic sites, 3 nature reserves, 2 disc golf courses, 1 river, and a 1,800-student school district. Bigfork is A Village by the Bay, a resort and arts community of 5,000 in Flathead County at 2,940 feet on the eastern shore of Flathead Lake, with Glacier Park International Airport in Kalispell 22 miles away, 176 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 32 lakes, 28 fishing access sites, 27 campgrounds, 25 trailheads, 16 state parks, 10 museums, 9 golf courses, 8 nature reserves, 6 historic sites, 4 disc golf courses, 4 boat launches, 1 national forest, 1 national park, 1 river, 1 ski area, and a 900-student school district.

## Who Should Move to Laurel?

*   Working families and professionals who want easy access to Billings, the largest city in Montana, just 18 miles away, with its hospitals, employers, shopping, and airport, while living in a more affordable community
*   Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want 21 fishing access sites on the Yellowstone River, 6 state parks, 10 museums, and 5 golf courses within 30 miles, with the Rimrocks and the Yellowstone Valley as a backdrop
*   Buyers who want an affordable Montana community close to a major city, with a typical home value of $369,826 and rent averaging $1,160 per month
*   Families who want a 1,800-student school district, 4 historic sites, and a genuine working-class Montana community with strong Yellowstone Valley character

## Who Should Move to Bigfork?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts and lake lovers who want 32 lakes, 28 fishing access sites, 27 campgrounds, 25 trailheads, 16 state parks, 8 nature reserves, and Glacier National Park all within 30 miles, with Flathead Lake at their doorstep
*   Arts and culture enthusiasts who want a community known for its summer theater, galleries, and boutique shops along Electric Avenue, in one of the most scenic small towns in western Montana
*   Affluent buyers and retirees who want a resort-quality lifestyle on Flathead Lake, with a typical home value of $758,435 and a median household income of $82,648, in a community that draws visitors from across the country
*   Golfers and outdoor recreation seekers who want 9 golf courses, 25 trailheads, 4 boat launches, and a ski area all within 30 miles, with Flathead Lake as the centerpiece of daily life

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Bigfork is significantly more expensive than Laurel. The typical home value in Bigfork is $758,435, compared to $369,826 in Laurel, more than double. Rent is close: Bigfork averages $1,103 per month versus $1,160 in Laurel, making Bigfork the slightly better monthly value despite much higher purchase prices.

Median household income is higher in Bigfork at $82,648 versus $66,382 in Laurel. Bigfork's higher income reflects its resort community character and the affluent retiree and remote worker population that has settled on Flathead Lake. Laurel's more affordable purchase prices make homeownership accessible to a much broader range of buyers.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Laurel is a working-class Yellowstone Valley community where the railroad, the refinery, the Rimrocks, and proximity to Billings define daily life. Bigfork is a polished resort village on Flathead Lake where summer theater, gallery walks, boating, and a vibrant arts scene set the tone. The outdoor recreation gap is substantial: Bigfork has 176 sites within 30 miles versus Laurel's 60, including 25 trailheads, 32 lakes, and a ski area that Laurel does not have. Laurel has 21 fishing access sites, 10 museums, and 6 state parks that reflect its deep Yellowstone Valley roots. If you want an affordable working-class community with easy access to Billings and strong Yellowstone Valley outdoor access, Laurel is a practical and solid choice. If you want a resort-quality lakeside village with extraordinary outdoor access, an arts scene, and Flathead Lake as your backyard, Bigfork is one of the most distinctive small towns in Montana.

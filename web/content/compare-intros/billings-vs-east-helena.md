# East Helena vs Billings, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

East Helena and Billings offer two very different Montana lifestyles. East Helena is a growing residential community in the Helena Valley with 2,264 people, its industrial heritage, its rapid recent growth as a suburb of the capital, and quick access to Canyon Ferry Lake and the Elkhorn Mountains. Billings, with 117,445 residents, is a Montana's largest city known for its major medical centers, its sprawling retail sector, and the Rimrocks and Yellowstone River.

## Who Should Move to East Helena?

* Families looking for a strong local school district and a tight-knit community feel.
* Professionals working in Helena who want a slightly quieter, more residential environment.
* People who appreciate a town with a strong working-class heritage that is actively reinventing itself.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want quick access to Canyon Ferry Lake and the surrounding national forest.

## Who Should Move to Billings?

* Professionals seeking corporate or medical careers.
* Families wanting large school districts and diverse activities.
* People who prefer urban conveniences over small-town quiet.
* Those who want easy access to a major regional airport.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in East Helena is around $430,196, while in Billings it is closer to $375,000, a difference of roughly $55,196, making Billings significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in East Helena averages about $1,020 per month, compared to roughly $1,200 in Billings.

East Helena's overall cost of living differs from Billings's. East Helena offers lower average salaries compared to Billings, with a median household income of $53,611 compared to Billings's $65,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

East Helena is transitioning from its historic roots as a smelter town into a rapidly growing, family-friendly suburb of Helena. It offers a quieter, slightly more affordable alternative to the capital city while maintaining its own distinct, blue-collar identity. Billings is a bustling regional hub with a true city feel, offering extensive amenities and a fast-paced environment.

# Deer Lodge vs Laurel, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Deer Lodge and Laurel offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Deer Lodge is a historic ranching and prison town with 2,938 people, the Old Montana Prison Museum, its deep ranching heritage in the Powell Valley, and quick access to the Clark Fork River and the Flint Creek Range. Laurel, with just 7,215 residents, is a railroad and refinery town known for its industrial economy, its position as a transportation hub, and the Yellowstone River and nearby Beartooth Mountains.

## Who Should Move to Deer Lodge?

* History buffs who appreciate the town's unique museums and historic architecture.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, traditional western town.
* Workers in agriculture, government, or local services looking for a slow-paced lifestyle.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want uncrowded access to the Clark Fork River and surrounding mountains.

## Who Should Move to Laurel?

* Industrial workers, railroad employees, and tradespeople looking for solid employment opportunities.
* Families who want a smaller school district and community feel while remaining just 15 minutes from Billings.
* Homebuyers seeking more affordable real estate than what is typically found in the Billings city limits.
* People who want easy access to the Beartooth Highway and the recreational opportunities south of the Yellowstone River.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Deer Lodge is around $225,000, while in Laurel it is closer to $369,826, a difference of roughly $144,826, making Deer Lodge significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Deer Lodge averages about $800 per month, compared to roughly $1,160 in Laurel.

Deer Lodge's overall cost of living differs from Laurel's. Deer Lodge offers lower average salaries compared to Laurel, with a median household income of $48,000 compared to Laurel's $66,382.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Deer Lodge is a quiet, deeply historic town that feels like a step back in time. It is defined by its agricultural roots and its unique history as the site of the original territorial prison, offering a slow-paced, affordable lifestyle in a beautiful valley setting. Laurel is a hardworking, blue-collar community where the local economy is driven by the CHS refinery and the Montana Rail Link yard. It offers a slower, more traditional small-town pace than Billings, but residents frequently commute to the larger city for shopping, dining, and entertainment.

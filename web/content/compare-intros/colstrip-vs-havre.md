# Havre vs Colstrip, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Havre and Colstrip offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Havre is a historic railroad and agricultural hub on the Hi-Line with 9,362 people, its deep roots in the railroad industry, the Havre Beneath the Streets underground tour, and quick access to the nearby Bear Paw Mountains and Fresno Reservoir. Colstrip, with 2,055 residents, is a purpose-built energy town in eastern Montana known for its massive coal-fired power plant, its excellent, company-built community amenities, and Castle Rock Lake and the surrounding pine hills.

## Who Should Move to Havre?

* Workers in agriculture, education, or the railroad industry looking for steady employment.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a community with all essential amenities.
* Those who appreciate the stark beauty of the northern plains and the close-knit feel of a Hi-Line town.
* History buffs interested in the unique underground history and nearby frontier battlefields.

## Who Should Move to Colstrip?

* Workers in the energy, mining, or industrial sectors looking for high-paying employment.
* Families seeking excellent, well-funded local schools and community parks.
* People looking for very affordable housing combined with high average salaries.
* Those who appreciate a practical, hardworking community with a strong industrial identity.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Havre is around $224,252, while in Colstrip it is closer to $219,986, a difference of roughly $4,266, making Colstrip significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Havre averages about $925 per month, compared to roughly $632 in Colstrip.

Havre's overall cost of living differs from Colstrip's. Havre offers lower average salaries compared to Colstrip, with a median household income of $58,000 compared to Colstrip's $86,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Havre is a resilient, hardworking community that serves as the commercial center for a vast stretch of northern Montana. It offers a practical, unpretentious lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture and the railroad. Colstrip is a unique, industrious town built specifically to support the local energy industry. It boasts surprisingly excellent parks, schools, and recreational facilities for its size, offering a practical, high-income lifestyle for those working in the energy sector.

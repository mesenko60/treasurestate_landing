# Big Timber vs Dillon, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Big Timber and Dillon offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Big Timber is a historic ranching community in the Sweet Grass Valley with 1,715 people, its deep, multi-generational ranching heritage, its classic, historic downtown architecture, and quick access to the Boulder River and the Crazy Mountains. Dillon, with 3,880 residents, is a historic ranching and college town known for the University of Montana Western, its deep agricultural roots, and the Beaverhead River and surrounding Pioneer Mountains.

## Who Should Move to Big Timber?

* People seeking an authentic, traditional western community with a strong agricultural identity.
* Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want access to the pristine waters of the Boulder River.
* Those looking for a scenic, quiet lifestyle with relatively easy access to Bozeman or Billings.
* Retirees and remote workers drawn to the town's historic charm and spectacular mountain views.

## Who Should Move to Dillon?

* Students, faculty, and staff associated with the University of Montana Western.
* Ranchers and agricultural workers who want to live in a community that still deeply values its livestock heritage.
* Anglers seeking world-class trout fishing on the Beaverhead and Big Hole rivers.
* People looking for a genuine, unpretentious western town with a stable economy and access to vast public lands.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Big Timber is around $422,291, while in Dillon it is closer to $375,585, a difference of roughly $46,706, making Dillon significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Big Timber averages about $1,009 per month, compared to roughly $821 in Dillon.

Big Timber's overall cost of living differs from Dillon's. Big Timber offers higher average salaries compared to Dillon, with a median household income of $63,514 compared to Dillon's $51,458.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Big Timber is a quintessential Montana ranching town, fiercely protective of its agricultural roots and stunning natural surroundings. It offers a quiet, authentic western lifestyle set against the dramatic backdrop of the Crazy Mountains. Dillon strikes a unique balance between a traditional cowboy town and a small college community. It remains deeply tied to the ranching industry, but the presence of the university adds a layer of cultural and educational activity, creating a grounded, authentic Montana atmosphere.

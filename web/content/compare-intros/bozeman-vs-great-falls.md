# Great Falls vs Bozeman: Which Montana City is Right for You?

Great Falls and Bozeman are nearly the same size, 60,000 and 58,000 people respectively, but they could hardly be more different in character, cost, and trajectory. Great Falls is the Electric City, a traditional, working-class city on the Missouri River in central Montana, anchored by Malmstrom Air Force Base, the world-class C.M. Russell Museum, and a spectacular series of waterfalls that once stopped Lewis and Clark for nearly a month. Bozeman is the Gallatin Gateway, a fast-growing, tech-friendly university city at the foot of the Bridger Range, where Montana State University, Big Sky ski country, and one of the most in-demand real estate markets in the American West all converge.

## Who Should Move to Great Falls?

*   Military families stationed at or connected to Malmstrom Air Force Base
*   Families and professionals who want a full-service city at a fraction of Bozeman's cost
*   History and art lovers drawn to the C.M. Russell Museum and the Lewis and Clark National Historic Trail
*   Retirees and remote workers who want a quieter, more affordable Montana city with a genuine community feel

## Who Should Move to Bozeman?

*   Tech workers, entrepreneurs, and remote professionals drawn to Bozeman's booming economy and startup culture
*   Skiers and outdoor enthusiasts who want Bridger Bowl and Big Sky resort within easy reach
*   Montana State University students, faculty, and the broader academic and research community
*   People who want a cosmopolitan small city with upscale dining, a vibrant downtown, and a younger, ambitious energy

## Cost of Living Breakdown

The housing gap between these two cities is one of the most dramatic in Montana. The median home price in Great Falls is around $328,554, while in Bozeman it climbs to $715,781, more than double. Rent follows the same pattern: about $1,340 per month in Great Falls versus $2,113 in Bozeman.

Bozeman does offer higher salaries, with a median household income of $79,903 compared to Great Falls' $63,934. But even accounting for that $16,000 income gap, Great Falls residents enjoy substantially more financial breathing room. The premium in Bozeman is real, and it's growing.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Great Falls is a steady, unpretentious city that takes its Missouri River trail system, its C.M. Russell Museum, and its military community seriously. It doesn't have Bozeman's buzz, but it also doesn't have Bozeman's traffic, its housing pressure, or its sense of constant change. Bozeman is exciting and ambitious, a city that is remaking itself in real time and attracting people from across the country who want Montana with a cosmopolitan edge. Both cities are authentic Montana; they just represent very different chapters of the state's story.

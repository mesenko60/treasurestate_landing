# Malta vs Cut Bank, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Malta and Cut Bank offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Malta is a historic agricultural town on the Hi-Line with 2,100 people, its world-class dinosaur fossils, its deep ranching heritage, and quick access to the Bowdoin National Wildlife Refuge and the Missouri River Breaks. Cut Bank, with 3,056 residents, is a windy agricultural town on the Hi-Line known for its proximity to the Blackfeet Indian Reservation, its agricultural and energy economy, and the eastern edge of Glacier National Park.

## Who Should Move to Malta?

* Workers in agriculture, government, or local services looking for a quiet, stable community.
* People seeking very affordable housing and a low overall cost of living.
* Hunters, birders, and outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the vast, uncrowded public lands of northeastern Montana.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle on the high plains.

## Who Should Move to Cut Bank?

* Workers in agriculture, energy, or government looking for affordable living on the Hi-Line.
* People seeking a quiet, resilient community with a low cost of living.
* Those who want to live near Glacier National Park without paying resort-town prices.
* Individuals who appreciate the stark, windy beauty of the northern plains.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Malta is around $244,540, while in Cut Bank it is closer to $175,000, a difference of roughly $69,540, making Cut Bank significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Malta averages about $693 per month, compared to roughly $650 in Cut Bank.

Malta's overall cost of living differs from Cut Bank's. Malta offers higher average salaries compared to Cut Bank, with a median household income of $59,449 compared to Cut Bank's $52,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Malta is a quiet, resilient community on the eastern Hi-Line, defined by agriculture, wide-open spaces, and its unique paleontology attractions. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle far from the state's urban centers. Cut Bank is a tough, practical town known for its wind and its hardworking residents. It serves as a gateway between the vast agricultural plains of the Hi-Line and the dramatic peaks of the Rocky Mountain Front, offering a quiet, affordable lifestyle.

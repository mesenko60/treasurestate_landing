# Malta vs Shelby, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Malta and Shelby offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Malta is a historic agricultural town on the Hi-Line with 2,100 people, its world-class dinosaur fossils, its deep ranching heritage, and quick access to the Bowdoin National Wildlife Refuge and the Missouri River Breaks. Shelby, with 3,169 residents, is a historic railroad and agricultural town known for its position on the Hi-Line, its famous 1923 Dempsey-Gibbons heavyweight title fight, and the Sweet Grass Hills and Lake Elwell.

## Who Should Move to Malta?

* Workers in agriculture, government, or local services looking for a quiet, stable community.
* People seeking very affordable housing and a low overall cost of living.
* Hunters, birders, and outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the vast, uncrowded public lands of northeastern Montana.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle on the high plains.

## Who Should Move to Shelby?

* Workers in the agriculture, railroad, or energy sectors looking for steady employment on the Hi-Line.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, rural setting.
* Those who appreciate the stark beauty of the northern plains and the close-knit community of a small agricultural town.
* History buffs interested in the unique legacy of the 1923 heavyweight championship fight.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Malta is around $244,540, while in Shelby it is closer to $185,000, a difference of roughly $59,540, making Shelby significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Malta averages about $693 per month, compared to roughly $700 in Shelby.

Malta's overall cost of living differs from Shelby's. Malta offers higher average salaries compared to Shelby, with a median household income of $59,449 compared to Shelby's $58,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Malta is a quiet, resilient community on the eastern Hi-Line, defined by agriculture, wide-open spaces, and its unique paleontology attractions. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle far from the state's urban centers. Shelby is a resilient, hardworking town on the northern plains, where life is shaped by the railroad, agriculture, and the vast open spaces of the Hi-Line. It offers a quiet, practical lifestyle far removed from the state's major urban centers.

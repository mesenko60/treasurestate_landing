# Cut Bank vs Lewistown, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Cut Bank and Lewistown offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Cut Bank is a windy agricultural town on the Hi-Line with 3,056 people, its proximity to the Blackfeet Indian Reservation, its agricultural and energy economy, and quick access to the eastern edge of Glacier National Park. Lewistown, with just 6,204 residents, is a historic agricultural center known for its charming stone architecture, its position at the geographic center of Montana, and the surrounding island mountain ranges and Spring Creek.

## Who Should Move to Cut Bank?

* Workers in agriculture, energy, or government looking for affordable living on the Hi-Line.
* People seeking a quiet, resilient community with a low cost of living.
* Those who want to live near Glacier National Park without paying resort-town prices.
* Individuals who appreciate the stark, windy beauty of the northern plains.

## Who Should Move to Lewistown?

* Retirees or remote workers who want a quiet, peaceful lifestyle in a classic Montana setting.
* People looking for affordable housing and a lower overall cost of living.
* Those who prefer a tight-knit, traditional small-town community with a strong agricultural heritage.
* History buffs and outdoor enthusiasts who enjoy uncrowded access to the Snowy, Judith, and Moccasin mountains.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Cut Bank is around $175,000, while in Lewistown it is closer to $268,126, a difference of roughly $93,126, making Cut Bank significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Cut Bank averages about $650 per month, compared to roughly $896 in Lewistown.

Cut Bank's overall cost of living differs from Lewistown's. Cut Bank offers higher average salaries compared to Lewistown, with a median household income of $52,000 compared to Lewistown's $44,195.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Cut Bank is a tough, practical town known for its wind and its hardworking residents. It serves as a gateway between the vast agricultural plains of the Hi-Line and the dramatic peaks of the Rocky Mountain Front, offering a quiet, affordable lifestyle. Lewistown is quiet, slow-paced, and friendly in that classic Montana way. People wave when they drive by, and life revolves around ranching, community events, and the pristine waters of Big Spring Creek that flow right through the center of town.

# Polson vs Great Falls, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Polson and Great Falls offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Polson is a scenic lakeside community with 5,335 people, its location on the southern shore of Flathead Lake, its cherry orchards, and quick access to Flathead Lake and the Mission Mountains. Great Falls, with just 60,403 residents, is a historic river city known for its military base, its rich Lewis and Clark history, and the Missouri River and Giant Springs.

## Who Should Move to Polson?

* Retirees and remote workers seeking a beautiful lakeside setting with a slower pace of life.
* Boaters, anglers, and water sports enthusiasts who want immediate access to the largest natural freshwater lake in the West.
* People looking for a more affordable alternative to the resort towns of the northern Flathead Valley.
* Those who appreciate a community with a strong agricultural heritage, particularly its famous cherry harvest.

## Who Should Move to Great Falls?

* Military families stationed at Malmstrom AFB.
* People seeking affordable urban living in Montana.
* History buffs interested in the Lewis and Clark expedition.
* Those who enjoy river recreation right in town.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Polson is around $415,000, while in Great Falls it is closer to $285,000, a difference of roughly $130,000, making Great Falls significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Polson averages about $1,050 per month, compared to roughly $950 in Great Falls.

Polson's overall cost of living differs from Great Falls's. Polson offers lower average salaries compared to Great Falls, with a median household income of $52,500 compared to Great Falls's $55,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Polson offers a relaxed, scenic lifestyle defined by its position on Flathead Lake. It lacks the polished resort feel of towns further north, maintaining a more grounded, agricultural character while still providing spectacular water and mountain access. Great Falls is a grounded, affordable city with a strong military presence and a deep connection to the Missouri River.

# Bigfork vs Billings: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Bigfork and Billings represent two very different versions of Montana life. Bigfork is a Village by the Bay, a small community of 5,000 on the eastern shore of Flathead Lake in Flathead County at 2,940 feet elevation, with 176 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 32 lakes, 27 campgrounds, 25 trailheads, and Glacier National Park nearby. Billings is the Magic City, Montana's largest city with 117,116 residents in Yellowstone County at 3,123 feet, with 53 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 9 museums, a regional medical center, and the full range of services that come with being the state's commercial hub.

## Who Should Move to Bigfork?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 27 campgrounds, 25 trailheads, 28 fishing access sites, 32 lakes, 16 state parks, and Glacier National Park all within 30 miles
*   Affluent buyers and retirees who want a small, scenic lakeside community with a median household income of $82,648 and a strong arts and cultural scene
*   People who want a small-town feel on Flathead Lake with Kalispell's services 22 miles away and the Flathead Valley's extraordinary natural setting as their daily backdrop
*   Anyone who wants a genuine village atmosphere with a summer arts festival, a community theater, and one of the most beautiful lake settings in the American West

## Who Should Move to Billings?

*   Professionals and families who want Montana's largest city, with a 16,000-student school district, a regional medical center, Billings Logan International Airport, and the widest range of employment and services in the state
*   Budget-conscious buyers who want a Montana city at a much lower price, with a typical home value of $390,654 versus Bigfork's $758,435
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 16 fishing access sites, 9 golf courses, 5 state parks, and the Rimrocks all within 30 miles, with Beartooth Highway and Red Lodge Mountain a short drive away
*   People who want a warmer, sunnier Montana climate, with July highs averaging 89°F and only 13.2 inches of annual snowfall

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Bigfork is one of the most expensive communities in Montana. The typical home value in Bigfork is around $758,435, compared to $390,654 in Billings, nearly double the price. Rent is lower in Bigfork: Bigfork averages about $1,103 per month, while Billings comes in at roughly $1,383.

The median household income in Bigfork is $82,648, compared to $71,855 in Billings. Bigfork residents earn more on average, but the home price gap is large enough that Billings offers a far more accessible path to homeownership.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Bigfork is a small lakeside village where the arts, outdoor recreation, and Flathead Lake define daily life. Billings is a genuine city, busy and practical, with a downtown that has been reinvesting in itself and a community that serves as the economic engine for a large swath of eastern Montana and northern Wyoming. If you want a small, scenic community on one of the most beautiful lakes in the American West and are prepared to pay for it, Bigfork is exceptional. If you want Montana's most complete city, with the broadest range of employment, services, and urban amenities, Billings is the clear choice.

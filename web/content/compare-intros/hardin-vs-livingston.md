# Hardin vs Livingston: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Hardin and Livingston are both small Montana communities with strong connections to Montana's history and outdoor heritage, but they are located in very different parts of the state and offer very different lifestyles. Hardin is Where the Rivers Meet, a small Big Horn County community of 3,742 at 2,910 feet in eastern Montana, known for its proximity to the Little Bighorn Battlefield, the Bighorn River, 10 fishing access sites, 4 historic sites, and a quiet agricultural lifestyle. Livingston is the Windiest City in Montana, a south-central Montana community of 8,040 in Park County at 4,501 feet, with 144 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 40 lakes, 24 fishing access sites, 16 waterfalls, 16 campgrounds, 12 trailheads, 8 museums, 7 boat launches, 6 golf courses, 2 disc golf courses, 2 hot springs, 2 nature reserves, 2 rivers, 2 state parks, 2 viewpoints, a national forest, a ski area, Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport 35 miles away, and a 1,400-student school district.

## Who Should Move to Hardin?

*   History enthusiasts drawn to the Little Bighorn Battlefield National Monument 14 miles away, one of the most significant and evocative historic sites in the American West
*   Anglers who want 10 fishing access sites and the Bighorn River, one of Montana's premier blue-ribbon trout streams, within easy reach
*   Remote workers, retirees, and families who want a very affordable small-town lifestyle in eastern Montana, with a typical home value of just $204,573 and rent averaging $726 per month
*   People who want a quiet, traditional eastern Montana community with a strong Native American cultural heritage and a slower pace of life

## Who Should Move to Livingston?

*   Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want 24 fishing access sites, 40 lakes, 16 waterfalls, 16 campgrounds, 12 trailheads, 2 hot springs, a national forest, and a ski area all within 30 miles, plus the Yellowstone River running right through town
*   Artists, writers, and creative professionals drawn to Livingston's well-established arts community, its literary history, and its reputation as one of Montana's most culturally rich small towns
*   People who want a gateway community to Yellowstone National Park about 55 miles south, with Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport 35 miles away and easy access to Paradise Valley
*   Buyers who want a south-central Montana community with a strong sense of identity, a 1,400-student school district, 8 museums, and a median household income of $65,187

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Livingston is considerably more expensive than Hardin. The typical home value in Hardin is around $204,573, compared to $509,477 in Livingston, about 2.5 times higher. Rent is also substantially higher in Livingston: Hardin averages about $726 per month, while Livingston comes in at roughly $1,783, about 2.5 times more.

Median household income is slightly higher in Livingston at $65,187 versus $60,463 in Hardin. Even with higher incomes, Livingston's housing costs are substantially greater. Hardin is dramatically more affordable for both buyers and renters.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Hardin is a small eastern Montana town where the Bighorn River, the Little Bighorn Battlefield, and a quiet agricultural and ranching community define daily life. Livingston is a small south-central Montana town where the Yellowstone River, Paradise Valley, and a vibrant arts and literary community define community identity. Livingston has a creative energy, a density of outdoor access, and a proximity to Yellowstone National Park that gives it a draw well beyond its 8,000 residents. If you want a very affordable small-town lifestyle with access to one of America's great historic battlefields and excellent trout fishing, Hardin is a compelling choice. If you want a small Montana town with a strong arts scene, 24 fishing access sites, 40 lakes, 16 waterfalls, and the Yellowstone River as your backyard, Livingston is one of Montana's most distinctive and beloved communities.

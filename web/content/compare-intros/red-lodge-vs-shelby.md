# Red Lodge vs Shelby, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Red Lodge and Shelby offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Red Lodge is a historic mountain town and gateway to Yellowstone with 2,336 people, its vibrant, historic downtown, its position at the base of the Beartooth Highway, and quick access to Red Lodge Mountain and the Beartooth Mountains. Shelby, with just 3,169 residents, is a historic railroad and agricultural town known for its position on the Hi-Line, its famous 1923 Dempsey-Gibbons heavyweight title fight, and the Sweet Grass Hills and Lake Elwell.

## Who Should Move to Red Lodge?

* Outdoor enthusiasts who want immediate access to skiing, hiking, and the Beartooth Wilderness.
* People seeking a lively, historic mountain town with a strong sense of community.
* Retirees and remote workers drawn to the spectacular scenery and small-town charm.
* Those who want to live in a gateway community to Yellowstone National Park.

## Who Should Move to Shelby?

* Workers in the agriculture, railroad, or energy sectors looking for steady employment on the Hi-Line.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, rural setting.
* Those who appreciate the stark beauty of the northern plains and the close-knit community of a small agricultural town.
* History buffs interested in the unique legacy of the 1923 heavyweight championship fight.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Red Lodge is around $550,000, while in Shelby it is closer to $185,000, a difference of roughly $365,000, making Shelby significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Red Lodge averages about $1,200 per month, compared to roughly $700 in Shelby.

Red Lodge's overall cost of living differs from Shelby's. Red Lodge offers lower average salaries compared to Shelby, with a median household income of $58,000 compared to Shelby's $58,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Red Lodge is a vibrant, historic mountain town that balances its role as a tourist destination with a strong, year-round local community. It offers spectacular access to the Beartooth Mountains and a lively downtown filled with shops, restaurants, and local events. Shelby is a resilient, hardworking town on the northern plains, where life is shaped by the railroad, agriculture, and the vast open spaces of the Hi-Line. It offers a quiet, practical lifestyle far removed from the state's major urban centers.

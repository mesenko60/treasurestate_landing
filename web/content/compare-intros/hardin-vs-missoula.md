# Hardin vs Missoula: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Hardin and Missoula are about as different as two Montana communities can be. Hardin is Where the Rivers Meet, a small Big Horn County community of 3,742 at 2,910 feet in eastern Montana, known for its proximity to the Little Bighorn Battlefield, the Bighorn River, 10 fishing access sites, 4 historic sites, and a quiet agricultural lifestyle. Missoula is the Garden City, a vibrant western Montana university city of 77,757 in Missoula County at 3,215 feet, with 211 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 65 fishing access sites, 49 trailheads, 22 campgrounds, 13 lakes, 10 museums, 9 golf courses, 9 historic sites, 8 state parks, 4 disc golf courses, 3 rivers, 2 hot springs, 2 viewpoints, 2 waterfalls, 2 wilderness areas, a national forest, a ski area, a wildlife refuge, and Missoula Montana Airport 7 miles away.

## Who Should Move to Hardin?

*   History enthusiasts drawn to the Little Bighorn Battlefield National Monument 14 miles away, one of the most significant and evocative historic sites in the American West
*   Anglers who want 10 fishing access sites and the Bighorn River, one of Montana's premier blue-ribbon trout streams, within easy reach
*   Remote workers, retirees, and families who want a very affordable small-town lifestyle in eastern Montana, with a typical home value of just $204,573 and rent averaging $726 per month
*   People who want a quiet, traditional eastern Montana community with a strong Native American cultural heritage and a slower pace of life

## Who Should Move to Missoula?

*   Young professionals, students, and families who want a vibrant university city with the University of Montana, a lively downtown, craft breweries, live music, farmers markets, and a strong arts and culture scene
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 65 fishing access sites, 49 trailheads, 22 campgrounds, 13 lakes, 8 state parks, 2 hot springs, 2 wilderness areas, a national forest, and a ski area all within 30 miles
*   People who want a progressive, active community with a strong nonprofit and healthcare sector, a 9,000-student school district, and Missoula Montana Airport 7 miles away
*   Anyone who wants western Montana's most dynamic mid-sized city, with a median household income of $65,329 and a community that punches well above its weight in culture, recreation, and livability

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Missoula is dramatically more expensive than Hardin. The typical home value in Hardin is around $204,573, compared to $554,389 in Missoula, nearly three times higher. Rent is also dramatically higher in Missoula: Hardin averages about $726 per month, while Missoula comes in at roughly $1,486, more than double.

Median household income is higher in Missoula at $65,329 versus $60,463 in Hardin. Even with higher incomes, Missoula's housing costs are substantially greater. Hardin is dramatically more affordable for both buyers and renters.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Hardin is a small eastern Montana town where the Bighorn River, the Little Bighorn Battlefield, and a quiet agricultural and ranching community define daily life. Missoula is a western Montana university city where the Clark Fork River, the University of Montana, and a thriving outdoor and arts culture define daily life. Missoula has an energy, a diversity of people, and a density of outdoor access that Hardin simply cannot match, with 49 trailheads, 65 fishing access sites, and a downtown that buzzes year-round. If you want a very affordable small-town lifestyle with access to one of America's great historic sites and excellent trout fishing, Hardin is a compelling choice. If you want Montana's most culturally vibrant mid-sized city with world-class outdoor recreation at your doorstep, Missoula is one of the most livable communities in the American West.

# Laurel vs Missoula: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Laurel and Missoula represent two very different versions of Montana life. Laurel is the City Between the Yellowstone and the Rimrocks, a quiet, working-class community of 7,215 in Yellowstone County with affordable housing, 21 fishing access sites within 30 miles, and the practical, unpretentious character of a town that has never tried to be anything other than what it is. Missoula is the Garden City, a university-anchored city of 77,757 on the Clark Fork River in western Montana, with 211 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, a vibrant arts and music scene, and a cultural energy that is genuinely hard to find in a city this size.

## Who Should Move to Laurel?

*   Budget-conscious families and first-time buyers who want eastern Montana living at a lower price, with a typical home value around $369,826
*   People who want a quiet, small-town community with easy access to Billings just 18 miles away
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 21 fishing access sites and 6 state parks along the Yellowstone River corridor
*   Anyone who prefers a practical, working-class Montana community without the urban energy of a university town

## Who Should Move to Missoula?

*   Young professionals, students, and academics drawn to the University of Montana's energy and cultural calendar
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 49 trailheads, 65 fishing access sites, and the Clark Fork River all within 30 miles
*   People who value a walkable, arts-rich downtown with independent restaurants, live music, and farmers markets
*   Anyone who wants a progressive, culturally active community in a dramatic mountain setting

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Laurel is significantly more affordable than Missoula. The typical home value in Laurel is around $369,826, compared to $554,389 in Missoula, a gap of nearly $185,000. Rent, however, is closer than you might expect: Laurel averages about $1,160 per month, while Missoula comes in at roughly $1,486, a difference of about $326 per month.

The income picture is nearly identical. The median household income in Laurel is $66,382, compared to $65,329 in Missoula. Both towns have similar earning profiles, but Laurel's lower housing costs give buyers a meaningful advantage in building equity.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Laurel is a quiet, unpretentious river town where fishing the Yellowstone on a weekday morning is a perfectly normal thing to do. Missoula is the opposite: a city with a strong identity, a river running through downtown, and a general sense that culture, outdoor adventure, and community are all happening at once. If you want affordable, low-key Montana living near Billings, Laurel delivers. If you want a university city with a genuine arts scene and one of the best outdoor recreation footprints in the state, Missoula is in a class of its own.

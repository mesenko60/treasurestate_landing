# Wolf Point vs Anaconda, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Wolf Point and Anaconda offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Wolf Point is a historic agricultural town on the Fort Peck Indian Reservation with 2,517 people, its position as the largest community on the reservation, its deep roots in farming and ranching, and quick access to the Missouri River and the vast eastern plains. Anaconda, with just 9,491 residents, is a historic smelting town known for its towering smokestack, its working-class heritage, and Georgetown Lake and the Anaconda-Pintler Wilderness.

## Who Should Move to Wolf Point?

* People seeking some of the most affordable housing in the state.
* Workers in agriculture, tribal government, or local services.
* Those who appreciate a quiet, isolated lifestyle on the high plains.
* Hunters and anglers who want uncrowded access to the Missouri River and surrounding lands.

## Who Should Move to Anaconda?

* Retirees and remote workers seeking very low housing costs.
* History buffs fascinated by Montana's copper era.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want uncrowded access to the Pintlers.
* People looking for a quiet, slow-paced mountain community.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Wolf Point is around $145,000, while in Anaconda it is closer to $210,000, a difference of roughly $65,000, making Wolf Point significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Wolf Point averages about $600 per month, compared to roughly $750 in Anaconda.

Wolf Point's overall cost of living differs from Anaconda's. Wolf Point offers lower average salaries compared to Anaconda, with a median household income of $42,000 compared to Anaconda's $48,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Wolf Point is a quiet, resilient community deeply tied to the Fort Peck Indian Reservation and the agricultural economy of northeastern Montana. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle far removed from the state's major urban centers. Anaconda is a quiet, resilient town with a deep industrial history, offering incredibly affordable access to some of Montana's best mountain recreation.

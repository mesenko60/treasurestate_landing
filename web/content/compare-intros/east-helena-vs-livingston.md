# East Helena vs Livingston, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

East Helena and Livingston offer two very different Montana lifestyles. East Helena is a growing residential community in the Helena Valley with 2,264 people, its industrial heritage, its rapid recent growth as a suburb of the capital, and quick access to Canyon Ferry Lake and the Elkhorn Mountains. Livingston, with 8,386 residents, is a historic railroad and arts town known for its literary heritage, its vibrant arts scene, and the Yellowstone River and Paradise Valley.

## Who Should Move to East Helena?

* Families looking for a strong local school district and a tight-knit community feel.
* Professionals working in Helena who want a slightly quieter, more residential environment.
* People who appreciate a town with a strong working-class heritage that is actively reinventing itself.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want quick access to Canyon Ferry Lake and the surrounding national forest.

## Who Should Move to Livingston?

* Artists, writers, and creative professionals.
* Fly fishing enthusiasts who want to live on the Yellowstone River.
* People who want a small-town atmosphere with easy access to Bozeman.
* Those who appreciate historic downtown architecture.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in East Helena is around $430,196, while in Livingston it is closer to $509,477, a difference of roughly $79,281, making East Helena significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in East Helena averages about $1,020 per month, compared to roughly $1,783 in Livingston.

East Helena's overall cost of living differs from Livingston's. East Helena offers lower average salaries compared to Livingston, with a median household income of $53,611 compared to Livingston's $65,187.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

East Helena is transitioning from its historic roots as a smelter town into a rapidly growing, family-friendly suburb of Helena. It offers a quieter, slightly more affordable alternative to the capital city while maintaining its own distinct, blue-collar identity. Livingston is a windy, eclectic town that blends its gritty railroad past with a sophisticated arts community, serving as the scenic gateway to Yellowstone.

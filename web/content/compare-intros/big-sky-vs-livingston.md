# Big Sky vs Livingston: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Big Sky and Livingston are neighbors in the Gallatin and Paradise Valley corridor, separated by about 60 miles of some of the most spectacular mountain scenery in Montana. Big Sky is the Biggest Skiing in America, a resort community of 3,591 in Gallatin County at 6,319 feet, known for Big Sky Resort, 23 trailheads, 17 waterfalls, 18 lakes, 3 hot springs, 2 wilderness areas, and 140 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles. Livingston is the Windiest City in Montana, a historic railroad and ranching town of 8,040 in Park County at 4,501 feet, with 144 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 40 lakes, 24 fishing access sites, 16 waterfalls, 16 campgrounds, 12 trailheads, 8 museums, 7 boat launches, 6 golf courses, 2 hot springs, 2 rivers, 2 state parks, 2 nature reserves, 1 ski area, Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport 35 miles away, and a 1,400-student school district.

## Who Should Move to Big Sky?

*   Skiers, snowboarders, and mountain sports enthusiasts who want Big Sky Resort, one of the largest ski areas in North America with over 5,800 acres of skiable terrain, right in their backyard
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 23 trailheads, 17 waterfalls, 18 lakes, 19 fishing access sites, 3 hot springs, 2 wilderness areas, and a national forest all within 30 miles
*   High-income remote workers and affluent buyers who can afford Big Sky's extraordinary real estate market, with a typical home value of $1,819,905 and a median household income of $103,625
*   People who want a small, exclusive resort community lifestyle where outdoor recreation is the primary focus and the mountains define every aspect of daily life

## Who Should Move to Livingston?

*   Anglers, fly fishing enthusiasts, and river lovers who want 24 fishing access sites, 7 boat launches, 2 rivers including the legendary Yellowstone River, and 16 waterfalls all within 30 miles, in one of the premier fly fishing destinations in North America
*   Artists, writers, and creative professionals drawn to Livingston's well-established arts community, its historic downtown with galleries and independent businesses, and its long tradition as a gathering place for writers and painters
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 40 lakes, 16 campgrounds, 12 trailheads, 2 hot springs, a ski area, and Yellowstone National Park nearby, at a fraction of Big Sky's cost
*   Buyers who want an authentic Montana small town with a genuine historic character, Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport 35 miles away, a 1,400-student school district, and a typical home value of $509,477

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Big Sky is dramatically more expensive than Livingston. The typical home value in Big Sky is $1,819,905, compared to $509,477 in Livingston, more than three and a half times higher. Rent is comparable: Livingston averages $1,783 per month versus $1,712 in Big Sky, a negligible difference.

Median household income is significantly higher in Big Sky at $103,625 versus $65,187 in Livingston. Livingston is moderately priced by Montana standards, particularly given its proximity to Bozeman and its strong outdoor recreation access. Big Sky's home prices are in a completely different category.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Big Sky is a world-class mountain resort community where the ski mountain, the wilderness, and a high-income outdoor lifestyle define daily life. Livingston is a historic railroad town where the Yellowstone River, a strong arts and literary tradition, the wind, and a genuine Montana small-town character define community identity. Both towns have outstanding outdoor access, with Livingston actually edging out Big Sky in fishing access sites and boat launches. Big Sky has 17 waterfalls, 3 hot springs, and a ski resort that Livingston cannot match, but Livingston has the Yellowstone River, 40 lakes, and a community culture that feels deeply rooted in place. If you want a resort lifestyle in the mountains with world-class skiing and wilderness access, Big Sky is extraordinary. If you want an authentic Montana river town with a strong arts scene, legendary fly fishing, and a community that has welcomed artists and adventurers for generations, Livingston is one of the most characterful small towns in the Mountain West.

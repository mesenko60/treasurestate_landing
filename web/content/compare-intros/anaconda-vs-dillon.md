# Dillon vs Anaconda: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Dillon and Anaconda are two of western Montana's most affordable small cities, both sitting above 5,000 feet and sharing a similar climate, but they have very different characters and histories. Dillon is Montana's Hometown, a small Beaverhead County community of 3,880 at 5,095 feet, known for its quiet ranching character, the Beaverhead River, 24 fishing access sites, 16 campgrounds, 5 state parks, 2 hot springs, and the University of Montana Western. Anaconda is the Smelter City, a community of 9,421 in Deer Lodge County at 5,279 feet, shaped by a century of copper smelting, with 134 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 48 lakes, 19 campgrounds, 16 museums, 9 trailheads, 8 fishing access sites, 8 nature reserves, 6 golf courses, 3 historic sites, 3 viewpoints, 3 waterfalls, 2 disc golf courses, a national forest, a ski area, and a scenic drive.

## Who Should Move to Dillon?

*   Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want 24 fishing access sites, 16 campgrounds, 5 state parks, 2 hot springs, 8 lakes, and the Beaverhead River all within 30 miles
*   Remote workers, retirees, and families who want a genuinely quiet, small-town Montana lifestyle at a very affordable price, with a typical home value of $375,585 and rent averaging just $821 per month
*   People who want a small university town with the University of Montana Western providing community events, sports, and cultural programming
*   Anyone drawn to southwestern Montana's wide-open spaces, ranching culture, and the Big Hole Valley just to the west

## Who Should Move to Anaconda?

*   History enthusiasts and people drawn to Anaconda's extraordinary copper smelting heritage, with 16 museums, 3 historic sites, the iconic Anaconda Smelter Stack State Park, and one of the most distinctive industrial histories in the American West
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 48 lakes, 19 campgrounds, 9 trailheads, 8 fishing access sites, 8 nature reserves, 6 golf courses, a national forest, a ski area, and 3 waterfalls all within 30 miles
*   Budget-conscious buyers who want one of Montana's most affordable communities, with a typical home value of $283,380 and median rent of just $661 per month, the lowest of any town in this comparison set
*   People who want a small western Montana community with a strong sense of identity, the famous Old Works Golf Course built on a former smelter site, and easy access to Butte 30 miles away

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Anaconda is considerably more affordable than Dillon for home buyers. The typical home value in Dillon is around $375,585, compared to $283,380 in Anaconda, about 25% lower. Rent is also lower in Anaconda: Dillon averages about $821 per month, while Anaconda comes in at roughly $661, about 20% lower.

Median household income is similar in both towns, at $51,458 in Dillon and $49,533 in Anaconda. Both communities are affordable by Montana standards, but Anaconda offers lower home prices and lower rent, making it the more budget-friendly choice for both buyers and renters.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Dillon is a small southwestern Montana town where the Beaverhead River, ranching culture, and the University of Montana Western define community life. Anaconda is a small western Montana city where copper history, the Anaconda Smelter Stack, and a rugged mountain character define community identity. Both towns are quiet, affordable, and deeply rooted in Montana's working-class heritage, but Anaconda has a more complex industrial history and a denser concentration of museums and historic sites. If you want excellent fishing access, a university town atmosphere, and a ranching community feel, Dillon is the better fit. If you want Montana's most affordable small city with extraordinary history, 48 nearby lakes, and a community shaped by one of the great industrial stories of the American West, Anaconda is a genuinely compelling choice.

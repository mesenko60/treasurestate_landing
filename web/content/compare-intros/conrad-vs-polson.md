# Conrad vs Polson, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Conrad and Polson offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Conrad is a quiet agricultural town on the northern plains with 2,318 people, its position as the seat of Pondera County, its strong farming and ranching economy, and quick access to the Rocky Mountain Front and Lake Frances. Polson, with 5,335 residents, is a scenic lakeside community known for its location on the southern shore of Flathead Lake, its cherry orchards, and Flathead Lake and the Mission Mountains.

## Who Should Move to Conrad?

* Workers in agriculture or local services looking for a quiet, stable community.
* People seeking very affordable housing far removed from the state's busy tourist hubs.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle.
* Hunters and anglers who want uncrowded access to the plains and the nearby Rocky Mountain Front.

## Who Should Move to Polson?

* Retirees and remote workers seeking a beautiful lakeside setting with a slower pace of life.
* Boaters, anglers, and water sports enthusiasts who want immediate access to the largest natural freshwater lake in the West.
* People looking for a more affordable alternative to the resort towns of the northern Flathead Valley.
* Those who appreciate a community with a strong agricultural heritage, particularly its famous cherry harvest.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Conrad is around $244,817, while in Polson it is closer to $415,000, a difference of roughly $170,183, making Conrad significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Conrad averages about $888 per month, compared to roughly $1,050 in Polson.

Conrad's overall cost of living differs from Polson's. Conrad offers lower average salaries compared to Polson, with a median household income of $47,348 compared to Polson's $52,500.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Conrad is a classic, quiet Montana farming community where life revolves around the agricultural seasons and local school sports. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle with spectacular views of the Rocky Mountain Front to the west. Polson offers a relaxed, scenic lifestyle defined by its position on Flathead Lake. It lacks the polished resort feel of towns further north, maintaining a more grounded, agricultural character while still providing spectacular water and mountain access.

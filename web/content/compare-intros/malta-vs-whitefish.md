# Malta vs Whitefish, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Malta and Whitefish offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Malta is a historic agricultural town on the Hi-Line with 2,100 people, its world-class dinosaur fossils, its deep ranching heritage, and quick access to the Bowdoin National Wildlife Refuge and the Missouri River Breaks. Whitefish, with 8,492 residents, is a premier ski and summer resort town known for Whitefish Mountain Resort, its pristine lake, and Glacier National Park and Whitefish Lake.

## Who Should Move to Malta?

* Workers in agriculture, government, or local services looking for a quiet, stable community.
* People seeking very affordable housing and a low overall cost of living.
* Hunters, birders, and outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the vast, uncrowded public lands of northeastern Montana.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle on the high plains.

## Who Should Move to Whitefish?

* Affluent buyers looking for a premium mountain lifestyle.
* Dedicated skiers and snowboarders.
* People who want a lively, walkable downtown with upscale dining.
* Those who desire immediate access to a major ski resort and a beautiful lake.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Malta is around $244,540, while in Whitefish it is closer to $850,000, a difference of roughly $605,460, making Malta significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Malta averages about $693 per month, compared to roughly $1,800 in Whitefish.

Malta's overall cost of living differs from Whitefish's. Malta offers lower average salaries compared to Whitefish, with a median household income of $59,449 compared to Whitefish's $75,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Malta is a quiet, resilient community on the eastern Hi-Line, defined by agriculture, wide-open spaces, and its unique paleontology attractions. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle far from the state's urban centers. Whitefish is a polished, high-end resort community with a vibrant downtown, drawing tourists and affluent residents year-round.

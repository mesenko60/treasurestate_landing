# Glendive vs Hardin: Which Montana Town Is Right for You?

Glendive and Hardin are both small eastern Montana towns with deep roots in the land and the river, but they offer distinctly different identities. Glendive, the Paddlefish Capital of the World in Dawson County, is a prairie town of 4,873 people on the Yellowstone River known for Makoshika State Park and the annual paddlefish snagging season. Hardin, Where the Rivers Meet in Big Horn County, is a town of 3,742 people near the confluence of the Bighorn and Little Bighorn rivers, known for the Little Bighorn Battlefield, the cultural heritage of the Crow Nation, and the Bighorn River's world-class tailwater trout fishery.

## Who Should Move to Glendive?

- People looking for affordable housing and a low cost of living in a quiet, self-contained prairie community
- Anglers who want the Yellowstone River and the unique experience of paddlefish snagging season each spring
- Fossil and geology enthusiasts who want Makoshika State Park, Montana's largest state park, as a backyard
- Those who prefer the wide-open eastern Montana landscape and a traditional, community-centered way of life

## Who Should Move to Hardin?

- History enthusiasts drawn to Little Bighorn Battlefield and the rich cultural heritage of the Crow Nation
- Anglers who want the Bighorn River, one of Montana's premier tailwater trout fisheries, close at hand
- People looking for very affordable housing and a low cost of living in a quiet, rural setting
- Those who want to experience the unique culture of southeastern Montana and the annual Crow Fair, one of the largest Native American gatherings in the country

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Both towns are among the most affordable in Montana. The typical home value in Hardin is $204,573 compared to $185,447 in Glendive, a difference of less than $20,000. Rent is slightly lower in Hardin at $726 per month versus $832 in Glendive. Glendive's median household income of $71,063 is higher than Hardin's $60,463. Both towns offer genuinely affordable housing by any Montana standard. Glendive has a significant advantage in airport access, with its own small regional airport just 6 miles from town. Hardin's nearest airport is in Billings, 54 miles to the west.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Glendive and Hardin are both quiet, community-centered towns where the river and the surrounding landscape define daily life. Glendive's identity is shaped by the Yellowstone River, the badlands of Makoshika, and the paddlefish season. Hardin's is shaped by the Bighorn River, the Little Bighorn Battlefield, and the Crow Nation's living cultural presence. The Crow Fair each August brings thousands of visitors to Hardin and gives the town a cultural richness that is unique in Montana. These are two towns that share a region and a price point but offer very different experiences.

Glendive has 30 recreational sites within 30 miles, including Makoshika State Park, 4 fishing access points, and 7 scenic viewpoints. Hardin has 20 sites, anchored by the Bighorn River, 10 fishing access points, and the Little Bighorn Battlefield historic site. Glendive leads in geological and natural attractions. Hardin leads in river fishing access and historical significance.

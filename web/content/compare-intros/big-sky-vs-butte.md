# Big Sky vs Butte: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Big Sky and Butte are both mountain communities in western Montana, but they could not be more different in character, history, or price. Big Sky is the Biggest Skiing in America, a resort community of 3,591 in Gallatin County at 6,319 feet, known for Big Sky Resort, 23 trailheads, 17 waterfalls, 18 lakes, 3 hot springs, 2 wilderness areas, and 140 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles. Butte is the Richest Hill on Earth, a historic mining city of 34,494 in Silver Bow County at 5,741 feet in western Montana, with 94 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 17 museums, 14 campgrounds, 13 lakes, 12 fishing access sites, 9 nature reserves, 7 golf courses, 4 hot springs, 3 historic sites, 3 viewpoints, 2 state parks, a national forest, and Butte Bert Mooney Airport 5 miles away.

## Who Should Move to Big Sky?

*   Skiers, snowboarders, and mountain sports enthusiasts who want Big Sky Resort, one of the largest ski areas in North America with over 5,800 acres of skiable terrain, right in their backyard
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 23 trailheads, 17 waterfalls, 18 lakes, 19 fishing access sites, 3 hot springs, 2 wilderness areas, and a national forest all within 30 miles
*   High-income remote workers and affluent buyers who can afford Big Sky's extraordinary real estate market, with a typical home value of $1,819,905 and a median household income of $103,625
*   People who want a small, exclusive resort community lifestyle where outdoor recreation is the primary focus and the mountains define every aspect of daily life

## Who Should Move to Butte?

*   History enthusiasts drawn to Butte's extraordinary copper mining heritage, with 17 museums, 3 historic sites, the famous Berkeley Pit, and one of the most architecturally rich and historically significant downtowns in the American West
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 14 campgrounds, 13 lakes, 12 fishing access sites, 9 nature reserves, 4 hot springs, a national forest, and 2 state parks all within 30 miles, at a fraction of Big Sky's cost
*   Budget-conscious buyers who want a western Montana mountain community at a very affordable price, with a typical home value of $274,163 and rent averaging $1,318 per month
*   People who want a real Montana city with a strong sense of identity, a 4,200-student school district, Butte Bert Mooney Airport 5 miles away, and a community that wears its mining heritage with genuine pride

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Big Sky is dramatically more expensive than Butte. The typical home value in Big Sky is $1,819,905, compared to $274,163 in Butte, more than six and a half times higher. Rent is also higher in Big Sky: $1,712 per month versus $1,318 in Butte, though the gap is much more modest.

Median household income is significantly higher in Big Sky at $103,625 versus $57,633 in Butte. Butte is one of the more affordable mountain communities in Montana, making it accessible to a wide range of buyers and renters. Big Sky's extraordinary home prices are in a completely different category from any other Montana community.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Big Sky is a world-class mountain resort community where the ski mountain, the wilderness, and a high-income outdoor lifestyle define daily life. Butte is a historic mining city where copper smelting history, a gritty Irish-Catholic working-class heritage, and a surprisingly vibrant arts and nightlife scene define community identity. Butte has 17 museums and one of the most distinctive historic downtowns in Montana, while Big Sky has 17 waterfalls and one of the great ski resorts in North America. Both sit at high elevation in western Montana, but they serve completely different needs. If you want a resort lifestyle in the mountains with world-class skiing and wilderness access, Big Sky is extraordinary. If you want an affordable western Montana mountain city with extraordinary history, 4 hot springs, and a community with more character per square mile than almost anywhere in the state, Butte is genuinely one of a kind.

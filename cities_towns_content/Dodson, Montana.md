### Quick Facts

- **Population:** ~128 (Source: U.S. Census Bureau estimates)
- **County:** Phillips County
- **Elevation:** 2,293 ft (699 m)
- **Known For:** Historic agricultural roots and serving as a quiet Hi-Line outpost along the Milk River.

### History & Heritage

Dodson is a small, quiet community located in north-central Montana, nestled along the meandering Milk River just off U.S. Highway 2 (the Hi-Line). Like many towns in this region, Dodson's history is closely tied to the arrival of the Great Northern Railway in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. The town developed as an agricultural supply and shipping center for the surrounding cattle ranches and homesteads. The nearby Milk River provided crucial water for irrigation, allowing the dry prairie to support hardy farming operations. Over the decades, as agriculture consolidated and required less manual labor, the population slowly declined, leaving Dodson as a peaceful, tight-knit rural hamlet.

### Top Things to Do in Dodson

- **Explore the Hi-Line:** Dodson represents the quintessential, stark beauty of Montana's Hi-Line. Driving through the area offers incredible views of endless skies, rolling plains, and massive grain elevators silhouetted against the horizon.
- **Wildlife Viewing and Hunting:** The vast plains and the riparian habitat along the Milk River provide excellent opportunities for observing waterfowl, mule deer, and antelope. It is a popular area for fall hunting.
- **Visit Nearby Malta:** For more structured activities, visitors often drive just east to Malta to explore the Great Plains Dinosaur Museum and the Phillips County Museum.

### Local Industry & Economy

The economy of Dodson is entirely reliant on agriculture. The surrounding lands are utilized for extensive cattle ranching, sheep grazing, and farming (primarily wheat and hay). Due to the town's small size, there are very few commercial businesses, and residents often travel to the nearby county seat of Malta for major services, medical care, and retail shopping. The demographic makeup reflects a strong blend of pioneer descendants and Native American heritage.

### Getting There & Nearby Destinations

- **Getting There:** Dodson is located directly on U.S. Highway 2. It sits roughly 18 miles west of Malta and 50 miles east of Havre.
- **Nearby Destinations:**
    - **Malta, Montana:** The closest major town (to the east), offering dinosaur museums, dining, and lodging.
    - **Bowdoin National Wildlife Refuge:** Located northeast of Malta, this massive wetland complex is an incredible destination for birdwatching.
    - **Fort Belknap Indian Reservation:** Located just to the west of Dodson, home to the Gros Ventre and Assiniboine Tribes.
    - **The Little Rocky Mountains:** Visible to the south, offering off-the-beaten-path hiking and gold mining history (near Zortman).

### Where to Stay in Dodson

Because Dodson is an exceptionally small, rural community, there are no commercial hotels or motels located within the town itself.
- **Malta (18 miles East):** The vast majority of travelers stay in nearby Malta, which offers several reliable motels (like the Maltana Motel and Edgewater Inn), RV parks, and dining options.
- **Havre (50 miles West):** For travelers heading west, Havre provides comprehensive accommodations, including major hotel chains and larger RV parks.

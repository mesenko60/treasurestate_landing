### Quick Facts

- **Population:** ~107
- **County:** Carbon County
- **Elevation:** 4,826 ft (1,471 m)
- **Known For:** Historic coal mining, the Smith Mine disaster, Bear Creek Saloon & Steakhouse, and the famous Bear Creek Downs Pig Races.

### History & Heritage

Bearcreek is deeply tied to the history of coal mining in Montana. Founded between 1905 and 1906, the town boomed thanks to the abundant coal seams in the area, which provided fuel for the Northern Pacific Railway and regional smelters. At its peak in the 1920s, Bearcreek and the surrounding mining camps were home to thousands of residents. However, the town's history is marked by tragedy; on February 27, 1943, an explosion at the nearby Smith Mine killed 74 miners. It remains the worst coal mine disaster in Montana history. Following the disaster and the declining demand for coal, the population sharply decreased, turning Bearcreek into a quiet, historic community.

### Top Things to Do in Bearcreek

- **Attend the Bear Creek Downs Pig Races:** This is the primary modern attraction in Bearcreek. Hosted at the Bear Creek Saloon & Steakhouse during the summer months (typically Thursday through Sunday), visitors can watch, cheer, and place friendly wagers on racing pigs in a lively, family-friendly atmosphere.
- **Dine at the Bear Creek Saloon & Steakhouse:** Enjoy authentic Montana dining in a historic setting, famous for its excellent steaks and local charm.
- **Visit the Smith Mine Disaster Memorial:** Pay respects at the memorial site dedicated to the 74 miners who lost their lives in the 1943 tragedy. The ruins of the mine buildings are still visible from the highway.
- **Drive the Scenic Highway 308:** The drive between Red Lodge, through Bearcreek, and on to Belfry offers beautiful views of the Beartooth foothills and historical mining ruins.

### Local Industry & Economy

Today, Bearcreek's economy is very small and heavily reliant on regional tourism and hospitality, driven primarily by the Bear Creek Saloon and its seasonal events. The town serves as a bedroom community for nearby Red Lodge, with many residents commuting for employment in tourism, construction, or regional services in Carbon County. 

### Getting There & Nearby Destinations

- **Getting There:** Bearcreek is located in south-central Montana along Montana Highway 308. It is just 7 miles east of the popular resort town of Red Lodge.
- **Nearby Destinations:**
    - **Red Lodge, Montana:** A bustling mountain town just 10 minutes west, offering extensive dining, shopping, and access to the Beartooth Mountains.
    - **The Beartooth Highway:** One of the most scenic drives in America, starting just south of Red Lodge and leading into Yellowstone National Park.
    - **Red Lodge Mountain:** A popular ski resort offering winter sports and summer activities, located a short drive away.
    - **Yellowstone National Park:** The northeast entrance to the park is accessible via the Beartooth Highway (in summer) or through the Chief Joseph Scenic Byway.

### Where to Stay in Bearcreek

Because Bearcreek is a very small community, there are generally no major hotels within the town limits. 
- **Red Lodge (7 miles West):** The vast majority of visitors to the Bearcreek area stay in Red Lodge, which boasts a wide array of accommodations including historic hotels (like The Pollard), modern motels, bed and breakfasts, and numerous vacation rentals.
- **Camping:** There are several U.S. Forest Service campgrounds and private RV parks in the surrounding Beartooth Mountains near Red Lodge.

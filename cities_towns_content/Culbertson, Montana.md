- **Known For:** Agricultural heritage, proximity to the Missouri River, and nearby access to the Medicine Lake National Wildlife Refuge.

### History & Heritage

Culbertson is a historic agricultural community located in the far northeastern corner of Montana, situated in the rolling prairie just north of the Missouri River. The town was established in the late 19th century and named in honor of Major Alexander Culbertson, a prominent fur trader and diplomat who built several historic trading posts (including Fort Union and Fort Benton) across the Upper Missouri region. The town grew steadily with the arrival of the Great Northern Railway, which transformed the vast surrounding grasslands into highly productive wheat farms and cattle ranches. Today, Culbertson remains deeply connected to this pioneering, agricultural legacy.

### Top Things to Do in Culbertson

- **Visit Medicine Lake National Wildlife Refuge:** Located about 25 miles north of Culbertson, this massive refuge is a spectacular destination for wildlife viewing. During the summer, it hosts over 100,000 migrating waterfowl, including great blue herons, white pelicans, sandhill cranes, and numerous duck species.
- **Explore the Missouri River:** Culbertson sits just a few miles north of the mighty Missouri River, offering nearby public access sites for fishing, boating, and exploring the cottonwood river bottoms.
- **Attend Frontier Days:** If you visit during the summer, catch the local Frontier Days NRA Rodeo, a classic community event that celebrates the town's western and ranching roots.
- **Visit the Culbertson Museum:** Located in the local visitor center, it offers a glimpse into the homesteading era and the early days of Roosevelt County.

### Local Industry & Economy

Culbertson’s economy is historically and fundamentally rooted in agriculture. The sprawling plains surrounding the town are dominated by dryland grain farming (particularly spring and winter wheat) and expansive cattle and sheep ranches. In more recent decades, the local economy has also experienced fluctuations tied to the oil and gas industry, as the town sits on the western edge of the massive Bakken oil formation. Oil production and related services provide a supplementary economic boost to the traditional agricultural base.

### Getting There & Nearby Destinations

- **Getting There:** Culbertson is located in northeastern Montana at the junction of U.S. Highway 2 (the Hi-Line) and Montana Highway 16. It is situated about 25 miles west of the North Dakota state line.
- **Nearby Destinations:**
    - **Medicine Lake National Wildlife Refuge:** Just a half-hour drive north, offering world-class birdwatching.
    - **Fort Union Trading Post National Historic Site:** Located near the North Dakota border, this reconstructed 19th-century fur trading post is a fascinating dive into the history of Major Alexander Culbertson and the fur trade era.
    - **Sidney, Montana:** Located to the south along Highway 16, Sidney is a larger regional hub offering more extensive dining and shopping.
    - **Williston, North Dakota:** A major regional city across the state line, providing a commercial airport and significant retail services.

### Where to Stay in Culbertson

Despite its small size, Culbertson is an important waypoint on U.S. Highway 2 and offers reliable lodging for travelers and workers:
- **Local Motels & Hotels:** The town features accommodations like The Kings Inn Motel and the Hi Line Inn & Suites, which provide comfortable, modern amenities for highway travelers and hunters.
- **Culbertson RV Park:** Provides convenient hookups and spaces for those traveling via motorhome or camper.
- **Regional Hubs:** For a wider variety of options, travelers sometimes stay in Sidney (to the south) or Williston, ND (to the east).

### Quick Facts

- **Population:** ~572
- **County:** Cascade County
- **Elevation:** 3,392 ft (1,034 m)
- **Known For:** World-class fly fishing and floating on the Missouri River, the stunning Crown Butte Preserve, and being a quintessential western ranching community.

### History & Heritage

Cascade is a peaceful, historic town situated along the banks of the Missouri River in central Montana. The town began to take shape in the late 19th century, developing as an agricultural hub for the surrounding cattle ranches and wheat farms that stretched across the plains towards the Rocky Mountain Front. The arrival of the Montana Central Railway in the 1880s solidified Cascade as a vital shipping point for regional agriculture. Interestingly, the town also claims a connection to the famous cowboy artist Charles M. Russell, who spent significant time in the area and found inspiration for many of his iconic Western paintings in the sweeping landscapes and ranching culture surrounding Cascade.

### Top Things to Do in Cascade

- **Fish and Float the Missouri River:** Cascade is world-renowned among anglers. The stretch of the Missouri River flowing near the town is a premier blue-ribbon trout fishery. There are numerous public access sites where visitors can launch drift boats or cast from the banks.
- **Hike Crown Butte Preserve:** Located roughly halfway between Cascade and Simms, this impressive natural preserve is managed by The Nature Conservancy. A challenging day hike takes you to the top of the flat-topped butte—rising 1,000 feet above the plains—where you'll find one of the few remaining examples of pristine, unplowed native grassland ecosystems in Montana.
- **Experience Small-Town Dining:** Grab a bite at local institutions like the Missouri Inn Restaurant and Bar, where anglers and ranchers mix over hearty western meals after a long day on the river.
- **Enjoy Scenic Drives:** The roads winding out of Cascade offer spectacular views of the Rocky Mountain Front and the winding, cottonwood-lined banks of the Missouri River.

### Local Industry & Economy

Cascade’s economy remains deeply tied to agriculture and ranching, reflecting its historical roots. The surrounding lands are primarily utilized for cattle grazing and farming. However, the town has developed a significant secondary economy based on outdoor recreation and tourism. Outfitting services, fly shops, local motels, and restaurants rely heavily on the influx of anglers and hunters who visit the Missouri River corridor during the spring, summer, and fall seasons. 

### Getting There & Nearby Destinations

- **Getting There:** Cascade is conveniently located just off Interstate 15 in central Montana. It is situated about 25 miles southwest of Great Falls and roughly 65 miles north of Helena.
- **Nearby Destinations:**
    - **Great Falls, Montana:** A major city just 30 minutes to the northeast, offering extensive dining, the C.M. Russell Museum, and the Lewis and Clark Interpretive Center.
    - **Helena, Montana:** The state capital is about an hour's drive to the south, offering rich gold-rush history, the State Capitol building, and the stunning Gates of the Mountains on the Missouri River.
    - **Craig, Montana:** Located further south along the Missouri River, Craig is a tiny hamlet known internationally as an epicenter for fly fishing guides and river culture.

### Where to Stay in Cascade

For visitors exploring the Missouri River, Cascade offers excellent, specialized lodging tailored primarily to outdoor enthusiasts:
- **Local Motels & Lodges:** Accommodations like the Bobkat Mountain Motel and On DeMark Lodge provide comfortable, rustic stays directly in town.
- **Riverfront Cabins and Rentals:** There are numerous private cabins, vacation homes, and specialized fishing lodges available for rent along the Missouri River corridor surrounding Cascade.
- **Camping:** There are several state fishing access sites and RV parks in the immediate area that offer tent camping and full RV hookups for visitors wanting to sleep under the Big Sky.

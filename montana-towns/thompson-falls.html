Thompson Falls, Montana

Quick Facts

  • Population: 1,336 (2020 census)
  • County: Sanders County
  • Founded: 1910 (established as a town)
  • Elevation: 2,419 ft
  • Known For: Natural waterfalls on the Clark Fork River, Thompson Falls Dam, explorer David Thompson
  • Nearby Landmarks: Cabinet Mountains, Thompson Falls State Park, Clark Fork River
  • Fun Fact: The Thompson Falls Dam features a fish ladder with 48 pools, completed in 2010, allowing native fish like bull trout to pass the dam for the first time in nearly a century!

Notable People & Pop Culture

  • Marc Racicot: Former Governor of Montana and lobbyist
  • Jennifer Fielder: Former member of the Montana Senate

Top Things to Do in Thompson Falls

  • Thompson Falls High Bridge: Stunning views of the Clark Fork River and the dam
  • Thompson Falls State Park: Camping, fishing, nature trails, and a boat launch
  • Old Jail Museum: Glimpse into local history and early Sanders County
  • Thompson Falls Community Trails: Hiking and scenic exploration

Local Industry & Economy

  • Historically: forestry, mining, and the railroad
  • Today: tourism, small businesses, services, and the Thompson Falls Dam
  • Sanders County Chamber of Commerce supports local economic and community development

History & Heritage

  • Named for explorer David Thompson, who established a trading post near the falls in 1809
  • Railroad arrival in 1881 and gold rush in 1883 spurred growth
  • Townsite platted in 1885, officially established in 1910
  • Thompson Falls Dam constructed atop the original falls (in operation since 1915)

Seasonal Activities & Local Events

  • Spring/Summer: Hiking, fishing, boating, camping, wildlife viewing, family fishing pond
  • Fall/Winter: Autumn scenery, late-season hiking, Old Jail Museum, snowshoeing, cross-country skiing
  • Annual Events: Cornhole Festival, "Sanders Saleing" county-wide yard sale, and more (see Chamber calendar)

Getting There & Nearby Destinations

  • Located in NW Montana on MT Highway 200, county seat of Sanders County
  • Nearby: Trout Creek, Noxon (west), Plains (east), Cabinet Mountains, Lolo National Forest

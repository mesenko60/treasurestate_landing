Gardiner, Montana

Population: 791 (2020 Census)

County: Park County

Founded: 1880

Elevation: 5,259 ft

Known For: The original northern entrance to Yellowstone National Park, the historic Roosevelt Arch, abundant wildlife, and year-round access to Yellowstone's Northern Range.

Nearby Landmarks: Yellowstone National Park, Roosevelt Arch, Mammoth Hot Springs, Absaroka-Beartooth Wilderness, Gallatin National Forest.

Top Things to Do in Gardiner

  • Explore Yellowstone's Northern Range: Year-round wildlife viewing, including bison, elk, wolves, and bears.
  • Visit the Historic Roosevelt Arch: Iconic stone archway dedicated in 1903, marking the original grand entrance to Yellowstone.
  • Whitewater Raft the Yellowstone River: Experience thrilling rapids and stunning Montana scenery.
  • Soak in Natural Hot Springs: Relax in Yellowstone Hot Springs or Chico Hot Springs, surrounded by mountain views.

Local Industry & Economy

Tourism is the lifeblood of Gardiner, with the town serving as a gateway for visitors to Yellowstone National Park. Outfitters, guides, lodging, and restaurants cater to park-goers, nature enthusiasts, and adventure seekers. The community also supports local ranching and small businesses.

Seasonal Activities & Local Events

  • Spring/Summer: Whitewater rafting, hiking, fly-fishing, wildlife viewing, and local festivals.
  • Fall/Winter: Elk rut viewing, wolf watching in Lamar Valley, cross-country skiing, snowshoeing, and snowmobile tours into Yellowstone.
  • Annual Events: Seasonal celebrations, Fourth of July festivities, and community events. Check the Gardiner Chamber of Commerce for current schedules.

Getting There & Nearby Destinations

Located on US Highway 89, Gardiner is about 55 miles south of Livingston, MT, and is the only Yellowstone entrance open year-round to wheeled vehicles. Nearby towns include Livingston and Bozeman. Paradise Valley offers scenic drives and outdoor recreation.

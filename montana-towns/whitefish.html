Whitefish, Montana

A Montana Community

Quick Facts

  • Population: Approximately 8,915 (2022/2023 est.; 7,751 in 2020 Census)
  • County: Flathead County
  • Founded: Incorporated 1905 (settlement began earlier, railroad c. 1904)
  • Elevation: 3,033 ft (924 m)
  • Known For: World-class skiing at Whitefish Mountain Resort, gateway to Glacier National Park, year-round outdoor recreation, vibrant downtown.
  • Nearby Landmarks: Glacier National Park, Flathead Lake, Whitefish Lake, Big Mountain.
  • Fun Fact: Originally known as "Stumptown" for the cleared timber during town and railroad construction.

Notable People & Pop Culture

  • Constance Towers: Actress and singer born in Whitefish.
  • Maggie Voisin: Olympic freestyle skier from Whitefish.
  • Brian Schweitzer: Former Montana Governor, resident of Whitefish.
  • David Graham: Pro golfer, major winner, associated with Whitefish.
  • Deric McCabe: Actor born in Whitefish ("A Wrinkle in Time").
  • Dorothy M. Johnson: Author of Western stories, connections to Whitefish.
  • Whitefish Mountain Resort has featured in adventure media and film.

Top Things to Do in Whitefish

  • Ski or Snowboard at Whitefish Mountain Resort: Over 3,000 acres of legendary terrain and views.
  • Explore Glacier National Park: Hike, drive Going-to-the-Sun Road, spot wildlife.
  • Enjoy Whitefish Lake: Boating, swimming, paddleboarding, City Beach access.
  • Discover Downtown Whitefish: Central Avenue, boutiques, galleries, dining, nightlife.

Local Industry & Economy

  • Tourism, outdoor recreation, hospitality, arts, retail, and services.
  • Historic roots in timber, railroad, and agriculture.

History & Heritage

  • Founded with railroad expansion in early 1900s; incorporated 1905.
  • Originally "Stumptown" for timber clearing.
  • Growth fueled by Whitefish Lake, railroad, and later ski resort.

Seasonal Activities & Local Events

  • Spring/Summer: Hiking, biking, water sports, golf, Glacier National Park access.
  • Fall/Winter: Skiing, snowboarding, cross-country, snowshoeing, dog sledding.
  • Annual Events: Whitefish Winter Carnival, Huckleberry Days Arts Festival, Whitefish Arts Festival, Taste of Whitefish, farmers markets, holiday celebrations.

Getting There & Nearby Destinations

  • Glacier Park International Airport (FCA) 11 miles SE; Amtrak Empire Builder stops downtown.
  • On U.S. Highway 93, 15 miles north of Kalispell.
  • West Entrance to Glacier National Park ~25 miles east; Flathead Lake ~30-45 min south.

East Glacier, Montana

Gateway to Glacier National Park & Blackfeet Country

Location: On the southeast edge of Glacier National Park, East Glacier is a classic railroad and park gateway town, serving as a hub for visitors to the park and the Blackfeet Indian Reservation.

Quick Facts

  • Population: ~400 (seasonal influx in summer)
  • County: Glacier County
  • Elevation: 4,823 ft (1,470 m)
  • Known For: Glacier Park Lodge, Amtrak Empire Builder stop, Blackfeet culture, hiking, and scenic drives
  • Nearby Landmarks: Glacier National Park, Two Medicine Lake, Looking Glass Highway, Blackfeet Reservation

History & Heritage

East Glacier was established in the early 1900s as the main eastern gateway for visitors arriving by rail to Glacier National Park. The historic Glacier Park Lodge, built by the Great Northern Railway in 1913, remains an iconic landmark. The town is deeply connected to the Blackfeet Nation, whose ancestral lands border the park. Today, East Glacier blends railroad, park, and Native American history into a unique cultural experience.

Top Things to Do in East Glacier

  • Glacier Park Lodge: Tour or stay at the historic lodge, known for its massive Douglas fir columns.
  • Two Medicine Valley: Hike, boat, or picnic in this less-crowded, stunning section of Glacier National Park.
  • Blackfeet Cultural Experiences: Attend powwows, visit the Museum of the Plains Indian, and explore Blackfeet art galleries.
  • Scenic Drives: Take the Looking Glass Highway or drive to Many Glacier and St. Mary for world-class views.
  • Amtrak: Arrive by train on the Empire Builder line, which stops at the historic depot in town.

Seasonal Activities & Events

  • Spring/Summer: Hiking, horseback riding, wildflower viewing, fishing, and park tours.
  • Fall: Autumn foliage, photography, and quieter park experiences.
  • Winter: Cross-country skiing, snowshoeing (limited services, but the area is beautiful and peaceful).
  • Annual Events: Blackfeet Nation Powwow, Glacier Park Lodge events, and art festivals.

Local Industry & Economy

Twin Bridges is a tourism and recreation hub, especially for fly fishing and cycling. The economy also includes agriculture, and the town is known for its cyclist-friendly infrastructure and fishing lodges. The Ruby Valley Chamber of Commerce supports local business and agriculture.

History & Heritage

Originally a convergence of Indian trails, Twin Bridges was settled in 1864 by Judge M.H. Lott. The town’s name comes from two bridges built across the Beaverhead River and Point of Rocks. The Doncaster Round Barn and Historical Association Museum celebrate its rich past, including producing Kentucky Derby winner Spokane.

Seasonal Activities & Local Events

  • Spring/Summer: Fly fishing, cycling, hiking in the Tobacco Root Mountains
  • Fall/Winter: Fall Festival, winter fishing, cross-country skiing, snowshoeing
  • Annual Events: Floating Flotillas Fish Fantasies, Fall Festival, Christmas Stroll, Madison County Fairgrounds events

Getting There & Nearby Destinations

Twin Bridges is at the intersection of MT-287 and MT-41, near Sheridan, Whitehall, and Dillon. Accessible by car and a popular stop for cyclists on the TransAmerica and Lewis & Clark Bicycle Trails. Nearest airports: Bozeman and Butte.

Helena, Montana

The Capital City

Quick Facts

  • Population: Approximately 33,100 (2023)
  • County: Lewis and Clark County
  • Founded: October 30, 1864
  • Elevation: 3,996 ft
  • Known For: Its rich Gold Rush history, status as Montana's state capital, vibrant arts scene, and access to stunning outdoor recreation including the Continental Divide Trail.
  • Nearby Landmarks: Mount Helena, Gates of the Mountains Wilderness, Last Chance Gulch, Montana State Capitol, Cathedral of St. Helena
  • Fun Fact: The city's main street, Last Chance Gulch, winds through the historic downtown, following the path of the original gold-rich creek that sparked Helena's founding in 1864.

Notable People & Pop Culture

  • Gary Cooper: Legendary American actor, born in Helena, known for his authentic acting style and two Academy Awards for Best Actor.
  • Myrna Loy: Acclaimed actress of the Golden Age of Hollywood, also born in Helena, celebrated for her roles in "The Thin Man" series.
  • The Untouchables (1987 Film): Parts of this classic film were shot in Helena, using its historic architecture to represent 1930s Chicago.

Top Things to Do in Helena

  • Gates of the Mountains Boat Tour: Take a breathtaking boat tour through a dramatic canyon carved by the Missouri River, following the journey of Lewis and Clark.
  • Montana State Capitol Building: Explore the seat of Montana's government, featuring impressive murals and a majestic copper dome.
  • Mount Helena City Park: Hike or bike through over 620 acres of parkland with panoramic city and valley views.
  • Last Chance Gulch Walking Mall: Stroll along the historic gold strike path, now a vibrant pedestrian mall with unique shops and eateries.

Local Industry & Economy

Helena's economy is a blend of government, healthcare, small business, and a growing tourism sector. As the state capital, government services and employment are major pillars. Healthcare is vital, with St. Peter's Health as a major regional center. The city supports many locally-owned businesses and has a growing tourism industry, thanks to its history, natural surroundings, and vibrant culture.

History & Heritage

Founded in 1864 during the Gold Rush, Helena quickly became a hub for wealth, commerce, and culture. Its historic downtown, Last Chance Gulch, traces the original gold strike. The city’s rich architectural legacy, including the Montana State Capitol and Cathedral of St. Helena, reflects its storied past and enduring significance as Montana’s capital.

Seasonal Activities & Local Events

  • Spring/Summer: Hike or bike Mount Helena, fish the Missouri River, explore Canyon Ferry Lake, enjoy outdoor concerts, farmers markets, and the Don't Fence Me In Trail Run.
  • Fall/Winter: Enjoy fall colors, ski at Great Divide, ice fish, and experience Helena’s breweries and holiday festivities.
  • Annual Events: Last Chance Stampede and Fair, Symphony Under the Stars, Ales for Trails, Spring and Fall Art Walks, and more.

Getting There & Nearby Destinations

  • By Road: Located on Interstate 15, easily reached from Bozeman, Butte, and beyond.
  • By Air: Helena Regional Airport (HLN) offers direct flights from Denver, Salt Lake City, and Seattle.
  • Nearby: Yellowstone and Glacier National Parks (about 180 miles), Virginia City, Nevada City, Bozeman, and Big Sky.

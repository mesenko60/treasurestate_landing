West Glacier, Montana

A Montana Community

Quick Facts

  • Population: 221 (2020 census)
  • County: Flathead
  • Founded: Originally Belton (post office 1900), renamed West Glacier in 1949
  • Elevation: 3,169 ft
  • Known For: Western gateway to Glacier National Park, Going-to-the-Sun Road, outdoor recreation
  • Nearby Landmarks: Glacier National Park, Lake McDonald, Flathead River, Apgar Village, Belton Chalets
  • Fun Fact: The Belton Chalets, built in 1910, were the first Great Northern Railway hotel for Glacier National Park visitors and remain a National Historic Landmark.

Notable People & Pop Culture

  • George Bird Grinnell – Anthropologist and writer who championed the creation of Glacier National Park.
  • John Fery & Winold Reiss – Artists whose depictions of Glacier's landscapes and Native cultures inspired generations of visitors.
  • Glacier National Park in Media – The park, accessed through West Glacier, has featured in documentaries, travel shows, and films, showcasing its wild beauty to the world.

Top Things to Do in West Glacier

  • Conquer the Going-to-the-Sun Road – One of North America’s most spectacular alpine drives.
  • Hike to Hidden Lake – Stunning alpine scenery and wildlife spotting.
  • Discover Apgar Village & Lake McDonald – Kayak, relax, or explore historic lakeside charm.
  • Brave the Flathead River – Whitewater rafting or scenic float trips.

Local Industry & Economy

West Glacier’s economy is fueled by Glacier National Park tourism. Local businesses provide lodging, dining, guiding, and gear for visitors. The National Park Service is a major presence, and many services are seasonal.

Seasonal Activities & Events

  • Spring/Summer: Hike, raft, kayak, golf, horseback ride, or join ranger-led programs. Don’t miss the Crown of the Continent Discovery Center.
  • Fall/Winter: Enjoy fall foliage, wildlife viewing, snowshoeing, or cross-country skiing in Apgar Village or McDonald Creek Valley.
  • Annual Events: The Flathead Valley hosts events like the Whitefish Winter Carnival and summer arts festivals. Glacier National Park offers ranger programs and astronomy events.

History & Heritage

Originally known as Belton, West Glacier’s significance grew with the Great Northern Railway’s arrival in 1890. The Belton Chalets (1910) welcomed early park visitors. In 1949, the town was renamed West Glacier to reflect its role as the park’s gateway. The region is historically important to the Blackfeet, Salish, Pend d’Oreille, and Kootenai tribes.

Getting There & Nearby Destinations

Arrive by car via U.S. Highway 2, by Amtrak’s Empire Builder, or fly into Glacier Park International Airport (FCA) in Kalispell. Nearby: Apgar Village, Lake McDonald, Polebridge, Whitefish, Kalispell, and Flathead Lake.

Options range from the historic Belton Chalet and Lake McDonald Lodge to motels, cabins, and campgrounds. Book early for summer stays!

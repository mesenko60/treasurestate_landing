Local Industry & Economy

Big Sandy is a trade center for farmers and ranchers in the "Golden Triangle" wheat region. Grain elevators, wheat and barley production, and a long-standing agricultural tradition define the local economy. The town's farming heritage dates to the early 1900s homesteaders and is celebrated at the local museum.

History & Heritage

Big Sandy's story begins with its namesake creek and the arrival of the railroad in 1887. It quickly became a supply junction for routes from the Missouri River to Fort Assiniboine. The town's frontier past is preserved at the Big Sandy Historical Society Museum, where visitors can explore its evolution from railway stop to agricultural hub.

Seasonal Activities & Local Events

  • Spring/Summer: Outdoor swimming, wildlife viewing at Lonesome Lake, hiking in the Bear Paw Mountains, fishing along the Missouri River
  • Fall/Winter: Hunting, cross-country skiing, and community gatherings
  • Annual Events: Christmas Stroll, Fire Department Chili Cook-off, Rotary Banquet, Big Sandy Homecoming (every five years)

Getting There & Nearby Destinations

Big Sandy is on U.S. Route 87 between Great Falls and Havre. Montana Highway 432 intersects in town. The Big Sandy Airport is one mile south. Nearby: MSU Northern (30 min north), Missouri River recreation, Bear Paw Mountains.

Where to Stay in Big Sandy

History & Heritage

Butte, once known as the "Richest Hill on Earth," was one of the most prosperous cities in the American West during the late 19th and early 20th centuries due to its rich mineral deposits. The city's history is deeply intertwined with copper mining, labor movements, and the growth of the American West. The unique geological formations of the area created one of the world's richest mineral deposits, making Butte a global mining capital. Today, Butte preserves its colorful past through well-preserved historic buildings, museums, and the hauntingly beautiful Berkeley Pit.

Top Things to Do in Butte

  • World Museum of Mining: Delve into Butte's mining legacy with an underground tour and a recreated 1890s mining town.
  • Our Lady of the Rockies: Visit this 90-foot statue atop the Continental Divide for panoramic views and a story of faith.
  • The Berkeley Pit: Witness the immense scale of this former open-pit copper mine, now a Superfund site, offering a starkly beautiful and thought-provoking view into mining's environmental impact and reclamation efforts.
  • Montana Tech Mineral Museum: Explore a vast collection of minerals and learn about the region's geological history that created Butte's rich mineral deposits.
  • Uptown Butte Historic District: Stroll through one of the largest National Historic Landmark Districts in the U.S., featuring Victorian architecture and historic buildings.
  • Copper King Mansion: Tour the former home of William A. Clark, one of Butte's legendary Copper Kings.

Local Industry & Economy

While mining remains part of Butte's identity, the economy has diversified to include healthcare, education (Montana Tech), tourism, and small businesses. The city's rich history and cultural heritage continue to attract visitors from around the world.

Seasonal Activities & Events

  • Spring/Summer: Explore historic sites, attend the Montana Folk Festival, or hike the trails in the surrounding mountains.
  • Fall: Enjoy the changing colors of autumn and visit the Berkeley Pit for stunning reflections.
  • Winter: Experience holiday festivities, including the Christmas Stroll and the Berkeley Pit Lights display.
  • Annual Events: Montana Folk Festival, An Ri Ra Montana Irish Festival, Butte 100 Mountain Bike Race, and the Evel Knievel Days celebration.

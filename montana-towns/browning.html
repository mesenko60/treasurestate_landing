Browning, Montana

Heart of Blackfeet Nation

Quick Facts

  • Population: Approximately 1,000
  • County: Glacier County
  • Founded: Late 19th/early 20th century (ancestral Blackfeet homeland)
  • Elevation: 4,377 ft
  • Known For: Headquarters of the Blackfeet Nation, proximity to Glacier National Park, rich Native American culture, North American Indian Days celebration
  • Nearby Landmarks: Glacier National Park (East Entrance), Chief Mountain, Museum of the Plains Indian, Blackfeet Heritage Center
  • Fun Fact: Browning is a crucial gateway to the eastern entrances of Glacier National Park.

Notable People & Pop Culture

  • James Welch (writer): Renowned Native American author, "Fools Crow," "Winter in the Blood"
  • Zahn McClarnon (actor): "Fargo," "Westworld," "Reservation Dogs"
  • Joe Hipp (boxer): Former heavyweight boxer, Blackfeet Nation
  • Steve Reevis (actor): "Fargo," "Dances with Wolves," "The Last of the Mohicans"

Top Things to Do in Browning

  • Museum of the Plains Indian: History, art, and culture of the Northern Plains Indians
  • Blackfeet Heritage Center & Art Gallery: Authentic Blackfeet art, crafts, and historical displays
  • North American Indian Days (Annual Event): One of the largest gatherings of US/Canadian tribes, dancing, drumming, rodeo (July)
  • Gateway to Glacier National Park: Use Browning as your basecamp for the eastern side of Glacier

Local Industry & Economy

  • Tribal administration and services, tourism and hospitality, ranching, local businesses, guiding, lodging, and retail
  • Blackfeet Nation economic development and cultural heritage initiatives

History & Heritage

  • Ancestral home of the Blackfeet people for centuries
  • Developed with the Blackfeet Indian Reservation and the Great Northern Railway
  • Governmental and cultural center for the Blackfeet Nation
  • Rich history of resilience, treaties, language, and cultural preservation

Seasonal Activities & Local Events

  • Spring/Summer: Hiking, camping, fishing, boating, wildlife viewing, North American Indian Days (July)
  • Fall/Winter: Hunting (with tribal licenses), cross-country skiing, snowshoeing, winter wildlife viewing
  • Annual Events: North American Indian Days (July), Heart Butte Indian Days (August), local rodeos

Getting There & Nearby Destinations

  • Located in NW Montana on US Highway 2, near Glacier National Park (East Entrance)
  • Major nearby towns: Cut Bank (east), East Glacier Park Village (west)
  • Closest major airports: Glacier Park International (FCA) and Great Falls International (GTF)

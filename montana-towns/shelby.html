Shelby, Montana

Gateway to Adventure on the Northern Frontier

Quick Facts

  • Population: 3,169 (2020 Census)
  • County: Toole County (County Seat)
  • Founded: 1892
  • Elevation: 3,297 ft (1,005 m)
  • Known For: 1923 Dempsey-Gibbons boxing match, railroad junction, transportation hub
  • Nearby Landmarks: Sweet Grass Hills, Glacier National Park (80 mi), Marias River, Lake Shel-oole
  • Fun Fact: The 1923 heavyweight championship boxing match between Jack Dempsey and Tommy Gibbons nearly bankrupted the town but cemented Shelby's place in sports history.

Notable People & Pop Culture

  • Jack Horner – World-renowned paleontologist, Jurassic Park advisor, born in Shelby
  • James Grady – Author of "Six Days of the Condor," born in Shelby
  • Welker Farms – Social media-famous agricultural operation near Shelby

Top Things to Do in Shelby

  • ✧ Marias Museum of History and Art – Local history and art collections
  • ✧ Shelby Carousel and Champions Park – Historic carousel and boxing park
  • ✧ Lake Shel-oole Park – Scenic views, fishing, and outdoor recreation
  • ✧ Historic Downtown – Unique shops and local businesses

Local Industry & Economy

Shelby is a transportation and agricultural hub, with major rail and trucking operations, wheat and cattle ranching, and a growing tourism sector. The city supports new business growth with tax incentives and has a developing industrial park.

History & Heritage

Shelby’s history is shaped by its railroad roots, the legendary 1923 Dempsey-Gibbons boxing match, and its role as a northern Montana crossroads. The town honors its past with museums, parks, and annual celebrations.

Seasonal Activities & Local Events

  • Spring/Summer: Fishing, hiking, golf, swimming, and the Roadrunner Trail
  • Fall/Winter: Bow hunting, cross-country skiing, snowshoeing, winter wildlife viewing, indoor recreation at the Civic Center
  • Annual Events: Champions Park Celebration, Booster Club events, Farmers Markets, community festivals

Getting There & Nearby Destinations

Shelby is on I-15 and Highway 2, with Amtrak service and a regional airport. Nearby: Glacier National Park, Sweet Grass Hills, Canadian border, Great Falls, Kalispell, Helena.

Sidney, Montana

Montana's Sunrise City

Quick Facts

  • Population: 6,346 (2020 Census)
  • County: Richland
  • Founded: 1911 (incorporated)
  • Elevation: 1,949 ft
  • Known For: Agriculture, oil production, wildlife abundance, MonDak Heritage Center
  • Nearby Landmarks: Fort Union Trading Post National Historic Site, Fort Buford, Yellowstone River
  • Fun Fact: Sidney was named after a six-year-old boy, Sidney Walters, who was staying with the local justice of the peace when the town was established in 1888.

Notable People & Pop Culture

  • Donald Grant Nutter – 15th Governor of Montana (1961-1962); born in Lambert and raised in Sidney
  • Barry Petersen – Award-winning CBS News correspondent who grew up in Sidney
  • J.K. Ralston – Famed western artist and Richland County native who painted a Federal Arts Project mural at the Richland County Courthouse
  • Chuck Stevenson – Race car driver who competed in the Indianapolis 500 multiple times in the 1950s

Top Things to Do in Sidney

  • ✧ MonDak Heritage Center – Explore Eastern Montana's premier museum and cultural hub showcasing regional art, history, and heritage
  • ✧ Agate Hunting – Search for unique Montana agates along the Yellowstone River banks
  • ✧ Sidney Country Club – Play a round at the only 18-hole golf course in eastern Montana
  • ✧ Wildlife Viewing – Witness the abundant wildlife of the MonDak region

Local Industry & Economy

Sidney's economy has historically been anchored by agriculture, with the Lower Yellowstone Irrigation Project transforming the region into productive farmland since 1909. Oil production and healthcare are also major economic pillars, with the Sidney Health Center serving as a key employer. The city is a regional hub for commerce and services in northeastern Montana.

History & Heritage

Sidney's story begins in the 1870s with early settlers in the Yellowstone Valley. The town was officially established in 1888 and incorporated in 1911. The Lower Yellowstone Irrigation Project and the arrival of the Holly Sugar Company in 1924 were transformative. Sidney has weathered the cycles of agriculture and oil, and today the MonDak Heritage Center preserves the region's cultural legacy.

Seasonal Activities & Local Events

  • Spring/Summer: River floating, fishing, hiking, wildlife photography
  • Fall/Winter: Hunting, cross-country skiing, snowshoeing, ice fishing
  • Annual Events: Richland County Fair and Rodeo, MonDak Ag Days, Sunrise Festival of the Arts, Christmas Stroll and Parade of Lights

Getting There & Nearby Destinations

Sidney is in northeastern Montana, about 270 miles northeast of Billings and less than 10 miles from the North Dakota border. Accessible via Highway 16 and 200, with Williston, ND about 45 miles east and Glendive, MT about 50 miles southwest. The nearest airports are in Williston and Billings.

Where to Stay in Sidney

Sidney offers a variety of accommodations, including the Richland Inn and Suites, Holiday Inn Express, Microtel Inn & Suites, Lone Tree Inn, and Sidney Motor Lodge. Camping is available at nearby state parks and along the Yellowstone River.

Troy, Montana

Yaak Valley's Forest Haven

Quick Facts

  • Population: 985
  • County: Lincoln
  • Founded: 1892
  • Elevation: 1,892 ft (lowest elevation in Montana)
  • Known For: Kootenai Falls, ancient cedar groves, outdoor recreation, mining heritage
  • Nearby Landmarks: Kootenai Falls & Swinging Bridge, Cabinet Mountains Wilderness, Ross Creek Cedar Grove
  • Fun Fact: Teddy Roosevelt's initials are carved on city hall

Notable People & Pop Culture

  • E.L. Preston – Civil engineer who named Troy
  • Les Balsiger – Notable religious figure
  • The River Wild – Movie filmed at the swinging bridge

Top Things to Do in Troy

  • ✧ Kootenai Falls & Swinging Bridge – Montana's largest undammed falls and the iconic bridge
  • ✧ Ross Creek Cedar Grove – Walk among 500-year-old cedars
  • ✧ Troy Museum & Visitors' Center – Local history and FOLF course
  • ✧ Roosevelt Park – Riverside recreation on the Kootenai River

Local Industry & Economy

Troy's economy has shifted from mining and logging to a mix of education, retail, and tourism. Major draws include Kootenai Falls, Cabinet Mountains Wilderness, and summer events at Roosevelt Park.

History & Heritage

Founded in 1892, Troy grew from a mining and railroad camp to a vibrant frontier town. Surviving the great forest fires of 1910, it became Lincoln County's richest town by the 1920s. The Troy Museum preserves this colorful past.

Seasonal Activities & Local Events

  • Spring/Summer: Hiking, fishing, rafting, mountain biking, summer concerts
  • Fall/Winter: Snowmobiling, skiing, hunting, ice fishing, mountain views
  • Annual Events: 4th of July, Bluegrass Festival, Arts on the Grass, Rock & Blues Festival

Getting There & Nearby Destinations

Troy is on Highway 2, 20 miles west of Libby and near the Idaho border. Kalispell is the nearest major airport (100 miles). Explore Libby, Cabinet Mountains Wilderness, Kootenai National Forest, and Bonners Ferry, Idaho.

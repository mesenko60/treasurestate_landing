Hot Springs, Montana, is a town in Sanders County, located on the Flathead Indian Reservation. It is known for its natural hot springs.

## History

The area was originally inhabited by Native American tribes who valued the hot springs for their healing properties. European-American settlement began in the late 19th century. The town was founded as Pineville and Camas in the 1890s. It was officially incorporated as Hot Springs in 1929. The hot springs have been a central part of the town's identity and economy throughout its history, attracting visitors for health and recreation.

## Geography

Hot Springs is situated in northwestern Montana, in the Little Bitterroot River Valley. It is surrounded by mountains and forests. The town's geography is defined by the presence of natural geothermal hot springs. According to the U.S. Census Bureau, the town has a total area of 0.42 square miles (1.09 km2), all land.

## Weather and Climate

Hot Springs has a climate influenced by its mountainous location and proximity to the Flathead Valley. Winters can be cold with snowfall, while summers are generally warm and dry. The presence of geothermal activity can locally influence microclimates.

## Contemporary Information

As of the 2020 census, the population of Hot Springs was 559. The town remains a destination for those seeking the therapeutic benefits of its hot springs. It has several soaking facilities and related businesses. The community also hosts local events and has a public school system.

## Industry and Economy

The primary industry in Hot Springs is tourism, centered around its natural hot springs and recreational opportunities. Agriculture and small local businesses also contribute to the economy. The town's location on the Flathead Indian Reservation also means that tribal enterprises and governance play a role in the local economy.

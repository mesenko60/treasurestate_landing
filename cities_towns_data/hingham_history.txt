Hingham, Montana, is a small agricultural town in Hill County, located on U.S. Route 2. It developed as a grain storage and shipping center along the Great Northern Railway.

## History

Settlement in the Hingham area began around 1909, with homesteaders purchasing land for the town site. By early 1910, the town was established, planned with a central town square. Hingham incorporated in 1917. In its early years, the town prospered with a school, hospital, several churches, and numerous businesses. The Hingham School operated from 1910 until the early 1980s when it consolidated with Rudyard School to form Blue Sky Schools, which later consolidated again in 2005 to become North Star Schools. Hingham celebrated its centennial in 2010.

## Geography

Hingham is situated in northern Montana, in Hill County. It is located on U.S. Route 2, approximately 35 miles west of Havre and near the Fresno Reservoir. According to the U.S. Census Bureau, the town has a total area of 0.17 square miles, all of which is land.

## Contemporary Information

As of the 2020 census, the population of Hingham was 131. The town is a small agricultural community. A notable landmark is the town's water tower. The town square, developed largely through volunteer efforts, is considered one of the best parks on the Hi-Line.

## Industry and Economy

Hingham's economy is primarily based on agriculture. Like many towns in the Hi-Line region, it historically served as a center for grain storage and shipping, facilitated by its location along the Great Northern Railway and U.S. Route 2. In recent decades, the town has seen a decline in population due to limited economic opportunities.

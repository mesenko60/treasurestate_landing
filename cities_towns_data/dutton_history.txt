Dutton, Montana, a town in Teton County, owes its name to Charles E. Dutton, who was a general agent for the Great Northern Railway. The town's origins are linked to the railway's expansion, as it was initially established as a station on the branch line connecting Great Falls and Conrad. The area is characterized by rolling hills and grassy meadows and is part of a region known as the "Golden Triangle," renowned for its production of wheat and barley, highlighting the agricultural significance of the locality.

# Dillon vs Helena: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Dillon and Helena are both western Montana communities with strong outdoor access and deep historical roots, but they differ significantly in size, character, and role. Dillon is Montana's Hometown, a small Beaverhead County community of 3,880 at 5,095 feet, known for its quiet ranching character, the Beaverhead River, 24 fishing access sites, 16 campgrounds, 5 state parks, 2 hot springs, and the University of Montana Western. Helena is the Queen City of the Rockies, Montana's state capital with 33,100 residents in Lewis and Clark County at 4,039 feet, with 107 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 28 campgrounds, 11 trailheads, 10 historic sites, 10 fishing access sites, 10 lakes, 9 museums, 4 golf courses, 3 hot springs, a national forest, and Helena Regional Airport 5 miles away.

## Who Should Move to Dillon?

*   Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want 24 fishing access sites, 16 campgrounds, 5 state parks, 2 hot springs, 8 lakes, and the Beaverhead River all within 30 miles
*   Remote workers, retirees, and families who want a genuinely quiet, small-town Montana lifestyle at a very affordable price, with a typical home value of $375,585 and rent averaging just $821 per month
*   People who want a small university town with the University of Montana Western providing community events, sports, and cultural programming
*   Anyone drawn to southwestern Montana's wide-open spaces, ranching culture, and the Big Hole Valley just to the west

## Who Should Move to Helena?

*   Government employees, professionals, and families who want Montana's state capital, with state government as a major employer, Helena Regional Airport 5 miles away, and a 7,800-student school district
*   History enthusiasts who want 10 historic sites, 9 museums, and one of the most architecturally rich Victorian-era downtowns in the American West, shaped by Montana's gold rush and copper smelting heritage
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 28 campgrounds, 11 trailheads, 10 fishing access sites, 10 lakes, 3 hot springs, and a national forest all within 30 miles
*   Anyone who wants a mid-sized Montana capital city with a strong job market, a median household income of $69,341, and a community that balances government, history, and outdoor recreation

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Helena is considerably more expensive than Dillon. The typical home value in Dillon is around $375,585, compared to $466,393 in Helena, about 24% higher. Rent is dramatically higher in Helena: Dillon averages about $821 per month, while Helena comes in at roughly $1,489, nearly double.

Median household income is higher in Helena at $69,341 versus $51,458 in Dillon. Helena residents earn considerably more, which helps offset the higher housing costs. Dillon remains dramatically more affordable for both buyers and renters.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Dillon is a small southwestern Montana town where the Beaverhead River, ranching culture, and the University of Montana Western define community life. Helena is Montana's state capital, where government, history, and a strong outdoor culture define daily life. Helena has a density of history and civic activity that Dillon does not, with a remarkable historic district, the Montana State Capitol, and a community that is deeply engaged in state politics and culture. If you want a quiet, affordable southwestern Montana lifestyle with excellent fishing and outdoor access, Dillon is a compelling choice. If you want Montana's capital city with a strong job market, rich history, and a mid-sized community that punches above its weight, Helena is one of Montana's most livable and underappreciated cities.

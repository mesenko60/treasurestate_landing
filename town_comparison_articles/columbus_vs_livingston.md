# Columbus vs Livingston, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Columbus and Livingston offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Columbus is a scenic river town in the Stillwater Valley with 2,015 people, its position at the confluence of the Yellowstone and Stillwater rivers, its proximity to the Stillwater Mine, and quick access to the Absaroka-Beartooth Wilderness. Livingston, with 8,386 residents, is a historic railroad and arts town known for its literary heritage, its vibrant arts scene, and the Yellowstone River and Paradise Valley.

## Who Should Move to Columbus?

* Workers in the mining or industrial sectors looking for steady, high-paying employment.
* Anglers and boaters who want immediate access to two of Montana's premier rivers.
* People seeking a scenic, small-town lifestyle with easy highway access to the amenities of Billings.
* Outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the rugged beauty of the Absaroka-Beartooth Wilderness.

## Who Should Move to Livingston?

* Artists, writers, and creative professionals.
* Fly fishing enthusiasts who want to live on the Yellowstone River.
* People who want a small-town atmosphere with easy access to Bozeman.
* Those who appreciate historic downtown architecture.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Columbus is around $428,574, while in Livingston it is closer to $509,477, a difference of roughly $80,903, making Columbus significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Columbus averages about $926 per month, compared to roughly $1,783 in Livingston.

Columbus's overall cost of living differs from Livingston's. Columbus offers lower average salaries compared to Livingston, with a median household income of $62,500 compared to Livingston's $65,187.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Columbus is a beautiful, thriving town that balances a strong industrial economy driven by the nearby palladium mine with spectacular outdoor recreation. It offers a relaxed, scenic lifestyle with excellent access to both the mountains and the city of Billings. Livingston is a windy, eclectic town that blends its gritty railroad past with a sophisticated arts community, serving as the scenic gateway to Yellowstone.

# Dillon vs Kalispell: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Dillon and Kalispell are both western Montana communities, but they sit at opposite ends of the state and offer very different lifestyles. Dillon is Montana's Hometown, a small Beaverhead County community of 3,880 at 5,095 feet in the southwest, known for its quiet ranching character, the Beaverhead River, 24 fishing access sites, 16 campgrounds, 5 state parks, 2 hot springs, and the University of Montana Western. Kalispell is the Gateway to Glacier Country, a growing community of 29,886 in Flathead County at 2,959 feet in the Flathead Valley of northwestern Montana, with 189 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 28 campgrounds, 28 fishing access sites, 27 lakes, 26 trailheads, 19 ski areas, 15 state parks, 10 golf courses, 8 museums, 8 nature reserves, 6 historic sites, 4 boat launches, 4 disc golf courses, a national forest, and Glacier Park International Airport 10 miles away.

## Who Should Move to Dillon?

*   Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want 24 fishing access sites, 16 campgrounds, 5 state parks, 2 hot springs, 8 lakes, and the Beaverhead River all within 30 miles
*   Remote workers, retirees, and families who want a genuinely quiet, small-town Montana lifestyle at a dramatically lower cost than Kalispell, with a typical home value of $375,585 and rent averaging just $821 per month
*   People who want a small university town with the University of Montana Western providing community events, sports, and cultural programming
*   Anyone drawn to southwestern Montana's wide-open spaces, ranching culture, and the Big Hole Valley just to the west

## Who Should Move to Kalispell?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 28 campgrounds, 28 fishing access sites, 27 lakes, 26 trailheads, 19 ski areas, 15 state parks, a national forest, and Glacier National Park all within 30 miles, making it one of Montana's premier outdoor destinations
*   Families and professionals who want a growing mid-sized city with a 4,200-student school district, Glacier Park International Airport 10 miles away, and a strong healthcare and retail economy
*   People who want the full Flathead Valley lifestyle, with Whitefish Mountain Resort, Flathead Lake, and Glacier National Park all within easy reach
*   Anyone who wants northwestern Montana's most complete city, with 10 golf courses, 8 museums, and a community that is growing rapidly while retaining its Montana character

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Kalispell is significantly more expensive than Dillon. The typical home value in Dillon is around $375,585, compared to $541,638 in Kalispell, about 44% higher. Rent is dramatically higher in Kalispell: Dillon averages about $821 per month, while Kalispell comes in at roughly $2,086, more than double.

Median household income is higher in Kalispell at $61,590 versus $51,458 in Dillon. Even with higher incomes, Kalispell's housing costs are substantially greater. Dillon is dramatically more affordable for both buyers and renters.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Dillon is a small southwestern Montana town where the Beaverhead River, ranching culture, and the University of Montana Western define community life. Kalispell is a growing northwestern Montana city where the Flathead Valley, Glacier National Park, and a booming outdoor recreation economy define daily life. Kalispell has a density of outdoor access and a pace of growth that Dillon does not, with Glacier National Park, Flathead Lake, and Whitefish Mountain Resort all within easy reach. If you want a quiet, affordable southwestern Montana lifestyle with excellent fishing and outdoor access, Dillon is a compelling choice. If you want northwestern Montana's most complete city with world-class outdoor recreation at your doorstep, Kalispell is one of the most desirable places to live in the American West.

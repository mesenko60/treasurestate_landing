# Big Timber vs Bigfork, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Big Timber and Bigfork offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Big Timber is a historic ranching community in the Sweet Grass Valley with 1,715 people, its deep, multi-generational ranching heritage, its classic, historic downtown architecture, and quick access to the Boulder River and the Crazy Mountains. Bigfork, with 5,000 residents, is a picturesque village on Flathead Lake known for its thriving arts scene, its cherry orchards, and Flathead Lake and the Swan River.

## Who Should Move to Big Timber?

* People seeking an authentic, traditional western community with a strong agricultural identity.
* Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want access to the pristine waters of the Boulder River.
* Those looking for a scenic, quiet lifestyle with relatively easy access to Bozeman or Billings.
* Retirees and remote workers drawn to the town's historic charm and spectacular mountain views.

## Who Should Move to Bigfork?

* Retirees and affluent professionals looking for a scenic, upscale lakeside community.
* Boaters, sailors, and water sports enthusiasts who want to live on the largest natural freshwater lake in the West.
* Art lovers who appreciate the town's galleries, summer playhouse, and cultural events.
* People who desire a resort-like atmosphere with high-end dining and boutique shopping.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Big Timber is around $422,291, while in Bigfork it is closer to $758,435, a difference of roughly $336,144, making Big Timber significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Big Timber averages about $1,009 per month, compared to roughly $1,103 in Bigfork.

Big Timber's overall cost of living differs from Bigfork's. Big Timber offers lower average salaries compared to Bigfork, with a median household income of $63,514 compared to Bigfork's $82,648.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Big Timber is a quintessential Montana ranching town, fiercely protective of its agricultural roots and stunning natural surroundings. It offers a quiet, authentic western lifestyle set against the dramatic backdrop of the Crazy Mountains. Bigfork feels like a curated resort village, where life centers around the water, the arts, and seasonal tourism. The downtown is polished and walkable, filled with galleries and cafes, offering a lifestyle that is significantly more upscale and leisure-focused than most Montana towns.

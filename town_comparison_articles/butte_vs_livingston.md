# Butte vs Livingston: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Butte and Livingston are both small Montana cities with outsized personalities and histories that run deeper than their populations suggest. Butte is the Richest Hill on Earth, a gritty, historically extraordinary mining city of 34,494 perched at 5,741 feet in Silver Bow County, with a Victorian-era Uptown district, 17 museums within 30 miles, and a civic identity forged in the fires of the copper industry. Livingston is the Windiest City in Montana, a small, storied town of 8,040 in the Paradise Valley, where the Yellowstone River flows cold and clear through downtown, the Absaroka Range rises to the south, and a long tradition of artists, writers, and fly fishers gives the place a character that is genuinely hard to find elsewhere.

## Who Should Move to Butte?

*   History lovers and architecture enthusiasts drawn to Butte's extraordinary Victorian-era Uptown district and its copper-boom heritage
*   Remote workers and retirees seeking affordable housing in a mountain city, Butte's median home price is significantly lower than Livingston's
*   People who want hot springs, mountain lakes, and wilderness within easy reach, Butte has 4 hot springs within 30 miles
*   Anyone who appreciates a working-class Montana city that has survived hard times with its identity fiercely intact

## Who Should Move to Livingston?

*   Artists, writers, and creative professionals drawn to Livingston's long-established arts and literary community
*   Fly fishing enthusiasts who want direct access to the Yellowstone River, one of Montana's finest, right in town
*   Remote workers and retirees who want a small, character-rich community close to Yellowstone National Park and the Paradise Valley
*   People who want the feel of a genuine Montana small town with a surprisingly rich cultural life and stunning mountain scenery

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Butte is the more affordable city to buy into. The median home price in Butte is around $274,163, compared to $509,477 in Livingston, a difference of about $235,000. Rent also runs higher in Livingston at about $1,783 per month versus $1,318 in Butte.

Livingston's median household income of $65,187 is higher than Butte's $57,633, but that $7,500 income advantage does not come close to bridging a housing gap of nearly a quarter million dollars. Livingston's higher prices reflect its desirability among remote workers, second-home buyers, and people drawn to the Paradise Valley and Yellowstone access.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Butte and Livingston are both authentically Montana cities with deep historical roots and strong community identities, but they attract very different kinds of people. Butte is defiant and proud, a city that wears its industrial scars as a badge of honor and has a mythology that no amount of growth can manufacture. Livingston is more romantic and literary, a small town known for its wind, its writers, its fly fishing, and its proximity to Yellowstone. The historic downtown, the railroad heritage, and the backdrop of the Absarokas give Livingston a sense of place that is genuinely irreplaceable. Both are excellent choices for people who want a real Montana community rather than a resort town; the question is whether you want the copper city or the river town.

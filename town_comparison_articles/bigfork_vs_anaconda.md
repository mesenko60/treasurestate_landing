# Bigfork vs Anaconda: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Bigfork and Anaconda are both western Montana communities, but they sit in very different landscapes and carry very different price tags. Bigfork is a Village by the Bay, a small community of 5,000 on the eastern shore of Flathead Lake in Flathead County at 2,940 feet elevation, with 176 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 32 lakes, 27 campgrounds, and Glacier National Park nearby. Anaconda is the Smelter City, a community of 9,421 in Deer Lodge County at 5,279 feet, with 134 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 48 lakes, 19 campgrounds, 16 museums, 9 trailheads, a ski area, and a copper smelting heritage that left behind one of the most distinctive skylines in Montana.

## Who Should Move to Bigfork?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 27 campgrounds, 25 trailheads, 28 fishing access sites, 32 lakes, 16 state parks, and Glacier National Park all within 30 miles
*   Affluent buyers and retirees who want a small, scenic lakeside community with a median household income of $82,648 and a mild climate, with January lows averaging 23°F versus Anaconda's 12°F
*   People who want a genuine village atmosphere on Flathead Lake with a summer arts festival, a community theater, and one of the most beautiful natural settings in Montana
*   Anyone who wants western Montana's mountain and lake scenery as their daily backdrop

## Who Should Move to Anaconda?

*   Budget-conscious buyers who want western Montana at a dramatically lower price, with a typical home value of $283,380 versus Bigfork's $758,435, less than half the cost, and median rent of just $661 per month
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 48 lakes, 19 campgrounds, 9 trailheads, 8 fishing access sites, a ski area, a hot spring at Fairmont Hot Springs, and Lost Creek Falls all within 30 miles
*   History enthusiasts drawn to Anaconda's extraordinary copper smelting heritage, the Anaconda Smelter Stack State Park, the Old Works Golf Course built on the historic smelter site, and 16 museums within 30 miles
*   People who want a small Montana community with a strong sense of place, easy access to Butte and Deer Lodge, and some of the most affordable housing in western Montana

## Cost of Living Breakdown

The gap between these two towns is very large. The typical home value in Bigfork is around $758,435, compared to $283,380 in Anaconda, nearly three times the price. Rent is dramatically lower in Anaconda: Bigfork averages about $1,103 per month, while Anaconda comes in at roughly $661, the lowest of any community in this comparison set.

The median household income in Bigfork is $82,648, compared to $49,533 in Anaconda. Bigfork residents earn considerably more on average, but the cost difference is so large that Anaconda offers one of the most affordable lifestyles in western Montana.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Bigfork is a small lakeside village where the arts, outdoor recreation, and Flathead Lake define daily life. Anaconda is a community shaped by copper, labor history, and the Pintler Mountains, with a gritty authenticity and a landscape that includes 48 lakes within 30 miles and one of the most distinctive industrial heritage sites in the American West. If you want a quiet, scenic village on one of Montana's most beautiful lakes and are prepared to pay for it, Bigfork is exceptional. If you want western Montana at a fraction of the price, with a fascinating industrial history and serious outdoor access, Anaconda is one of Montana's most underrated communities.

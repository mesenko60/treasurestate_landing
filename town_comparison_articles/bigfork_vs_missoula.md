# Bigfork vs Missoula: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Bigfork and Missoula are both western Montana communities with strong outdoor identities, but they offer very different scales of living. Bigfork is a Village by the Bay, a small community of 5,000 on the eastern shore of Flathead Lake in Flathead County at 2,940 feet elevation, with 176 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 32 lakes, 28 fishing access sites, and Glacier National Park nearby. Missoula is the Garden City, a university town of 77,757 in Missoula County at 3,215 feet on the Clark Fork River, with 211 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 65 fishing access sites, 49 trailheads, 10 museums, and the energy of a city built around the University of Montana.

## Who Should Move to Bigfork?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 27 campgrounds, 25 trailheads, 28 fishing access sites, 32 lakes, 16 state parks, and Glacier National Park all within 30 miles
*   Affluent buyers and retirees who want a small, scenic lakeside community with a median household income of $82,648 and a quieter pace of life than Missoula
*   People who want a genuine village atmosphere on Flathead Lake with a summer arts festival, a community theater, and one of the most beautiful natural settings in Montana
*   Anyone who wants a small-town feel with Kalispell's services 22 miles away and the Flathead Valley's extraordinary outdoor access

## Who Should Move to Missoula?

*   Young professionals, students, and families who want a vibrant university town with a lively downtown, craft breweries, live music, farmers markets, and a strong job market
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 65 fishing access sites, 49 trailheads, 22 campgrounds, 2 hot springs, and 3 rivers all within 30 miles, with the Rattlesnake Wilderness and Lolo National Forest at the city's edge
*   People who want a mid-sized Montana city with a 9,000-student school district, a regional airport, and the cultural amenities that come with a major university
*   Anyone who wants western Montana's mountain scenery and outdoor access at a lower price than Bigfork, with a typical home value of $554,389 versus $758,435

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Bigfork is considerably more expensive than Missoula. The typical home value in Bigfork is around $758,435, compared to $554,389 in Missoula, a difference of over $200,000. Rent is lower in Bigfork: Bigfork averages about $1,103 per month, while Missoula comes in at roughly $1,486.

The median household income in Bigfork is $82,648, compared to $65,329 in Missoula. Bigfork residents earn significantly more on average, but the home price premium is large enough that Missoula offers a more accessible path to homeownership for most buyers.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Bigfork is a small lakeside village where the arts, outdoor recreation, and Flathead Lake define daily life. Missoula is a university city with a progressive, outdoor-oriented culture, a downtown that hums year-round, and a community that has built a genuine identity around the Clark Fork River, the surrounding mountains, and the University of Montana. If you want a quiet, scenic village on one of Montana's most beautiful lakes, Bigfork is hard to beat. If you want western Montana's outdoor access with the energy and amenities of a real city, Missoula is the right choice.

# Hamilton vs Whitefish: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Hamilton and Whitefish are both small western Montana communities with exceptional outdoor access, but they differ dramatically in cost and character. Hamilton is the Banana Belt of Montana, a small Bitterroot Valley community of 4,659 in Ravalli County at 3,573 feet, known for its mild winters, 194 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 55 lakes, 47 trailheads, 32 campgrounds, 25 fishing access sites, and the Bitterroot National Forest at its doorstep. Whitefish is Stumptown, a resort and outdoor recreation community of 8,915 in Flathead County at 3,038 feet, with 202 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 46 campgrounds, 33 trailheads, 26 lakes, 22 fishing access sites, 19 ski areas, 8 state parks, 7 waterfalls, 4 boat launches, a national park, and Glacier Park International Airport 9 miles away.

## Who Should Move to Hamilton?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 47 trailheads, 55 lakes, 32 campgrounds, 25 fishing access sites, a hot spring, a national forest, and a wilderness area all within 30 miles
*   People who want a small, quiet Bitterroot Valley community with a genuinely mild winter climate, where January average highs reach 40 degrees, compared to Whitefish's 33 degrees
*   Remote workers, retirees, and families who want a peaceful western Montana lifestyle at dramatically lower costs than Whitefish, with Missoula's full range of services 56 miles north
*   Anyone drawn to the Bitterroot Valley's combination of mountain scenery, river access, and the laid-back character of a small agricultural community

## Who Should Move to Whitefish?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 19 ski areas, 46 campgrounds, 33 trailheads, 26 lakes, 22 fishing access sites, 8 state parks, 7 waterfalls, 4 boat launches, and Glacier National Park all within 30 miles
*   Skiers, mountain bikers, and people drawn to Whitefish Mountain Resort's year-round recreation economy, with some of the best skiing in Montana and a vibrant resort town atmosphere
*   Families and professionals who want a small resort community with Glacier Park International Airport 9 miles away, a 1,800-student school district, and Glacier National Park as a regular destination
*   Anyone who wants a Montana mountain resort town with a lively downtown, excellent restaurants, and a community that attracts people from across the country

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Whitefish is dramatically more expensive than Hamilton. The typical home value in Hamilton is around $516,033, compared to $840,135 in Whitefish, about 63% higher. Rent is dramatically higher in Whitefish: Hamilton averages about $958 per month, while Whitefish comes in at roughly $2,639, nearly three times higher.

Median household income is higher in Whitefish at $71,110 versus $52,917 in Hamilton. Even with higher incomes, Whitefish's housing costs are extraordinary. Hamilton is dramatically more affordable for both buyers and renters.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Hamilton is a small Bitterroot Valley town where the river, the mountains, and the mild climate define daily life. It is quiet, agricultural, and genuinely affordable. Whitefish is a Montana resort town where Whitefish Mountain Resort, Glacier National Park, and a year-round outdoor recreation economy define daily life. Whitefish has a resort energy that Hamilton does not, with a lively downtown, high-end restaurants, and a community that draws visitors and new residents from across the country. If you want the Bitterroot Valley's mild climate, quiet, and outdoor access at a dramatically lower cost, Hamilton is a compelling choice. If you want Montana's premier resort town with Glacier access and a year-round ski and outdoor culture, Whitefish is one of the most desirable small towns in the American West.

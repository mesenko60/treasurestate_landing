# Columbia Falls vs Hamilton: Which Montana Town Is Right for You?

Columbia Falls and Hamilton are both western Montana towns with strong outdoor identities, but they sit in very different valleys and offer distinct lifestyles. Columbia Falls, the Gateway to Glacier in the Flathead Valley, is a community of 5,308 people with direct access to Glacier National Park and some of the most dramatic mountain terrain in the lower 48. Hamilton, the Banana Belt of Montana in the Bitterroot Valley, is a town of 4,659 people known for its unusually mild climate, the Bitterroot River, and a growing reputation as a destination for outdoor lovers and remote workers seeking affordability.

## Who Should Move to Columbia Falls?

- Outdoor enthusiasts who want Glacier National Park, world-class skiing, and alpine wilderness close at hand
- Families who want a strong school district and a genuine community feel in a fast-growing part of Montana
- People drawn to the dramatic scenery of the northern Rockies and the Flathead Valley
- Those who want easy airport access via Glacier Park International in Kalispell, just 6 miles away

## Who Should Move to Hamilton?

- People who want a mild, sunny climate by Montana standards, with more annual sunshine than most of the state
- Fly fishers and river enthusiasts who want the legendary Bitterroot River as a daily resource
- Remote workers and retirees seeking affordability in a beautiful valley with a relaxed, welcoming pace
- Hikers and backpackers who want direct access to the Bitterroot National Forest and dozens of trailheads

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing in Columbia Falls is moderately more expensive. The typical home value is $584,580 compared to $516,033 in Hamilton, a difference of about $68,000. Rent is similarly close, with Columbia Falls at $1,003 per month and Hamilton at $958. Columbia Falls has a higher median household income at $65,313 versus Hamilton's $52,917, which means the relative affordability advantage in Hamilton is somewhat offset by lower local wages.

Both towns are in western Montana but are served by different airports. Columbia Falls uses Glacier Park International in Kalispell, 6 miles away. Hamilton's nearest airport is Missoula, 56 miles north.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Columbia Falls has the energy of a gateway town, busy in summer with Glacier-bound visitors and active year-round with locals who take their outdoor recreation seriously. Hamilton has a slower, sunnier rhythm. The Bitterroot Valley draws people who want space, river access, and a community that feels unhurried. Both towns have strong local identities and are genuinely welcoming to newcomers. The choice often comes down to whether you want the dramatic north or the sunny, river-threaded south.

Columbia Falls has 214 recreational sites within 30 miles, including 48 campgrounds, 19 ski areas, and Glacier National Park. Hamilton has 194 sites, including 47 trailheads, 55 lakes, 32 campgrounds, and a hot spring, with the Bitterroot National Forest serving as its primary outdoor anchor. Both towns are exceptionally well-positioned for outdoor living.

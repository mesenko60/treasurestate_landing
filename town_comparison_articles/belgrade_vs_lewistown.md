# Belgrade vs Lewistown: Which Montana Town is Right for You?

Belgrade and Lewistown sit in the same broad region of central Montana but represent very different ways of life. Belgrade is the Hub of the Gallatin Valley, a fast-growing suburb of 12,509 in Gallatin County at 4,462 feet, with Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport 5 miles away, 104 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 16 trailheads, 14 waterfalls, 15 lakes, 21 fishing access sites, 2 hot springs, a national forest, a ski area, and a 3,800-student school district. Lewistown is the Geographic Center of Montana, a quiet ranching and agricultural hub of 6,204 in Fergus County at 3,934 feet, with the nearest airport in Great Falls 115 miles away, 37 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 17 campgrounds, 7 fishing access sites, 3 nature reserves, 2 state parks, 2 museums, 1 hot spring, and a 1,200-student school district.

## Who Should Move to Belgrade?

*   Families and professionals who want Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport 5 miles away, a 3,800-student school district, and all the amenities of the Bozeman metro area at a slightly lower price than Bozeman itself
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 16 trailheads, 14 waterfalls, 15 lakes, 21 fishing access sites, 2 hot springs, a ski area, and a national forest all within 30 miles, with quick access to the Bridger Mountains and Gallatin Canyon
*   Buyers who want a rapidly growing community with strong job access in the Gallatin Valley, with a typical home value of $544,626 and a median household income of $88,896
*   Remote workers and young professionals who want a suburban Gallatin Valley lifestyle with proximity to Bozeman's restaurants, university, and cultural amenities without paying Bozeman prices

## Who Should Move to Lewistown?

*   Retirees and remote workers who want a quiet, self-contained central Montana town at the geographic center of the state, with a low cost of living and a strong sense of community identity
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 17 campgrounds, 7 fishing access sites, 3 nature reserves, 2 state parks, and 1 hot spring in the Big Snowy Mountains and Judith Basin area, far from the crowds of western Montana
*   Budget-conscious buyers who want to own a home in a genuine Montana small town at a very affordable price, with a typical home value of $268,126 and rent averaging just $896 per month
*   People who value a traditional, agricultural Montana lifestyle where ranching, community events, and wide-open central Montana skies define daily life

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Belgrade is significantly more expensive than Lewistown. The typical home value in Belgrade is $544,626, compared to $268,126 in Lewistown, more than double. Rent reflects the same gap: Belgrade averages $2,206 per month versus $896 in Lewistown, nearly two and a half times higher.

Median household income is considerably higher in Belgrade at $88,896 versus $44,195 in Lewistown. Belgrade's higher costs reflect its position in one of the fastest-growing real estate markets in Montana. Lewistown offers some of the most affordable housing of any Montana community its size.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Belgrade feels like a prosperous, growing suburb of Bozeman, with new subdivisions, strong outdoor access, and a community identity tied closely to the Gallatin Valley's outdoor and university culture. Lewistown is the complete opposite: quiet, slow-paced, and rooted in the rhythms of ranching and agriculture in central Montana. Belgrade has 16 trailheads, 14 waterfalls, and a ski area within 30 miles that Lewistown cannot match. Lewistown has 17 campgrounds and sits at the geographic center of Montana, surrounded by the Judith Basin, the Big Snowy Mountains, and some of the most unspoiled open country in the state. If you want a fast-growing Gallatin Valley community with outstanding mountain access and a suburban feel, Belgrade is one of the best values in the Bozeman area. If you want an affordable, peaceful central Montana town where the pace is slow, the skies are wide, and the community is genuinely tight-knit, Lewistown is one of the most underrated places to live in Montana.

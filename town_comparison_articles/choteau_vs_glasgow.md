# Choteau vs Glasgow, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Choteau and Glasgow offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Choteau is a scenic gateway town to the Rocky Mountain Front with 1,721 people, its spectacular views of the Front, its rich dinosaur paleontology history, and quick access to the Bob Marshall Wilderness and Freezout Lake. Glasgow, with 3,224 residents, is a historic railroad town on the Hi-Line known for its position as a regional hub for northeastern Montana, its proximity to Fort Peck Dam, and Fort Peck Lake and the Charles M. Russell National Wildlife Refuge.

## Who Should Move to Choteau?

* Outdoor enthusiasts who want uncrowded access to the Bob Marshall Wilderness.
* Birders and wildlife watchers drawn to the spectacular migrations at nearby Freezout Lake.
* People seeking a quiet, affordable community with some of the best mountain views in the state.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle.

## Who Should Move to Glasgow?

* Workers in agriculture, government, or the railroad industry looking for stable employment in northeastern Montana.
* Anglers and boaters who want to live near Fort Peck Lake, one of the premier walleye fisheries in the country.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, isolated community.
* Hunters and outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the vast, uncrowded public lands of the Missouri River Breaks.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Choteau is around $288,323, while in Glasgow it is closer to $195,000, a difference of roughly $93,323, making Glasgow significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Choteau averages about $713 per month, compared to roughly $750 in Glasgow.

Choteau's overall cost of living differs from Glasgow's. Choteau offers lower average salaries compared to Glasgow, with a median household income of $53,929 compared to Glasgow's $55,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Choteau is a quiet, stunningly located town that serves as the Front Porch of the Rockies. It offers a slow-paced, affordable lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture, paleontology, and the incredible wilderness right in its backyard. Glasgow is a resilient, isolated community that serves as the commercial and social center for a massive swath of northeastern Montana. It offers a quiet, slow-paced lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture, the railroad, and the incredible recreational opportunities at nearby Fort Peck Lake.

# Kalispell vs Livingston: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Kalispell and Livingston are both desirable Montana communities with strong outdoor access and distinct personalities, but they are separated by more than just geography, they represent two very different Montana lifestyles. Kalispell is the Gateway to Glacier Country, a growing, family-oriented city of 29,886 in the Flathead Valley, where Glacier National Park is less than an hour away, Flathead Lake is just to the south, and 189 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles make it one of the most recreation-rich communities in Montana. Livingston is the Windiest City in Montana, a small, storied town of 8,040 in the Paradise Valley, where the Yellowstone River flows cold and clear through downtown, the Absaroka Range rises to the south, and a long tradition of artists, writers, and fly fishers gives the place a character that is genuinely hard to find elsewhere.

## Who Should Move to Kalispell?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want Glacier National Park, Flathead Lake, and 19 ski areas within 30 miles
*   Families and remote workers drawn to the Flathead Valley's extraordinary natural beauty and expanding amenities
*   People who want a growing, family-friendly community with a broad range of services and strong school enrollment
*   Anyone who wants one of the highest concentrations of outdoor recreation in Montana, 189 sites, 27 lakes, 26 trailheads, and 28 fishing access points within 30 miles

## Who Should Move to Livingston?

*   Artists, writers, and creative professionals drawn to Livingston's long-established arts and literary community
*   Fly fishing enthusiasts who want direct access to the Yellowstone River, one of Montana's finest, right in town
*   Remote workers and retirees who want a small, character-rich community close to Yellowstone National Park and the Paradise Valley
*   People who want the feel of a genuine Montana small town with 16 waterfalls and 40 lakes within 30 miles, and 2 hot springs nearby

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Kalispell is the more expensive community. The median home price in Kalispell is around $541,638, compared to $509,477 in Livingston, a difference of about $32,000. The rent gap is more significant: about $2,086 per month in Kalispell versus $1,783 in Livingston.

Livingston's median household income of $65,187 is slightly higher than Kalispell's $61,590, which, combined with lower rent, makes Livingston the stronger financial proposition for renters. For buyers, the home price difference is modest enough that other lifestyle factors, Glacier vs Yellowstone, resort town vs river town, should drive the decision.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Kalispell is a gateway city in the most literal sense, a practical, family-friendly hub that serves as the base camp for Glacier National Park and the Flathead Valley. Livingston is more intimate and literary, a small town known for its wind, its writers, its fly fishing, and its proximity to Yellowstone. Kalispell has more outdoor recreation by the numbers; Livingston has 16 waterfalls within 30 miles and the Yellowstone River running through downtown. Both are genuinely great Montana communities; the choice comes down to whether you want the gateway to Glacier or the gateway to Yellowstone.

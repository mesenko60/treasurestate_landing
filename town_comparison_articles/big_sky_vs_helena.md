# Big Sky vs Helena: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Big Sky and Helena are both central Montana communities, but they serve very different needs. Big Sky is the Biggest Skiing in America, a resort community of 3,591 in Gallatin County at 6,319 feet, known for Big Sky Resort, 23 trailheads, 17 waterfalls, 18 lakes, 3 hot springs, 2 wilderness areas, and 140 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles. Helena is the Queen City of the Rockies, Montana's state capital with 33,100 residents in Lewis and Clark County at 4,039 feet, with 107 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 10 historic sites, 10 lakes, 9 museums, 4 state parks, 3 hot springs, 28 campgrounds, 11 trailheads, 8 nature reserves, 4 golf courses, Helena Regional Airport 5 miles away, and a 7,800-student school district.

## Who Should Move to Big Sky?

*   Skiers, snowboarders, and mountain sports enthusiasts who want Big Sky Resort, one of the largest ski areas in North America with over 5,800 acres of skiable terrain, right in their backyard
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 23 trailheads, 17 waterfalls, 18 lakes, 19 fishing access sites, 3 hot springs, 2 wilderness areas, and a national forest all within 30 miles
*   High-income remote workers and affluent buyers who can afford Big Sky's extraordinary real estate market, with a typical home value of $1,819,905 and a median household income of $103,625
*   People who want a small, exclusive resort community lifestyle where outdoor recreation is the primary focus and the mountains define every aspect of daily life

## Who Should Move to Helena?

*   State government employees, attorneys, lobbyists, and professionals whose careers are tied to Montana's state capital and its unique political and regulatory ecosystem
*   History enthusiasts drawn to Helena's extraordinary past as a gold rush boomtown, with 10 historic sites, 9 museums, the Montana State Capitol, and one of the most architecturally distinctive downtowns in the Mountain West
*   Families and professionals who want Helena Regional Airport 5 miles away, a 7,800-student school district, 4 state parks, 3 hot springs, and a median household income of $69,341 in a genuine Montana city
*   Anyone who wants an affordable, livable Montana capital city with outstanding outdoor access at a fraction of Big Sky's cost, with a typical home value of $466,393 and rent averaging $1,489 per month

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Big Sky is dramatically more expensive than Helena. The typical home value in Big Sky is $1,819,905, compared to $466,393 in Helena, nearly four times higher. Rent is comparable: Big Sky averages $1,712 per month versus $1,489 in Helena, a modest difference.

Median household income is higher in Big Sky at $103,625 versus $69,341 in Helena. Helena is moderately priced by Montana standards, making it accessible to a wide range of buyers and renters. Big Sky's home prices are in a completely different category from any other Montana community.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Big Sky is a world-class mountain resort community where the ski mountain, the wilderness, and a high-income outdoor lifestyle define daily life. Helena is Montana's state capital, where government, history, the arts, and a genuine small-city character define community identity. Helena has 10 historic sites and 9 museums, including the Holter Museum of Art, and sits at the base of the Rocky Mountain Front with outstanding outdoor access. Big Sky has 17 waterfalls and one of the great ski resorts in North America, but virtually no urban amenities. If you want a resort lifestyle in the mountains with world-class skiing and wilderness access, Big Sky is extraordinary. If you want a livable Montana capital city with deep history, strong outdoor access, and a community that punches well above its size in culture and character, Helena is one of the most underrated cities in the Mountain West.

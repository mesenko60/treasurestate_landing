# Forsyth vs Missoula, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Forsyth and Missoula offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Forsyth is a historic railroad town on the Yellowstone River with 1,630 people, its beautiful, historic county courthouse, its position as a transportation and agricultural hub, and quick access to the Yellowstone River and the eastern plains. Missoula, with 77,757 residents, is a vibrant university town known for its craft breweries, its bustling downtown, and world-class hiking and skiing.

## Who Should Move to Forsyth?

* Workers in agriculture, transportation, or local services looking for a stable community.
* People seeking very affordable housing and a low overall cost of living.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle on the eastern plains.
* Hunters and anglers who want uncrowded access to the Yellowstone River.

## Who Should Move to Missoula?

* Young professionals and families who want good job opportunities.
* People who love a lively downtown with restaurants, breweries, and events.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want to be close to the mountains for hiking, skiing, and rafting.
* Anyone who wants a university town vibe with younger energy.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Forsyth is around $208,690, while in Missoula it is closer to $554,389, a difference of roughly $345,699, making Forsyth significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Forsyth averages about $743 per month, compared to roughly $1,486 in Missoula.

Forsyth's overall cost of living differs from Missoula's. Forsyth offers lower average salaries compared to Missoula, with a median household income of $58,375 compared to Missoula's $65,329.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Forsyth is a quiet, practical community in eastern Montana, defined by the railroad, agriculture, and the Yellowstone River. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle with a strong sense of local history. Missoula feels like a real city in the mountains, with busy sidewalks, weekly farmers markets, live music, and constant activity.

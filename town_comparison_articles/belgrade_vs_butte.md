# Belgrade vs Butte: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Belgrade and Butte sit on opposite ends of Montana's character spectrum. Belgrade is the Hub of the Gallatin Valley, a fast-growing community of 12,509 in the Gallatin Valley with a booming real estate market, a strong professional economy, and 104 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles. Butte is the Richest Hill on Earth, a city of 34,494 with one of the most distinctive histories in the American West, a National Historic Landmark district, 17 museums within 30 miles, and a gritty, proud identity that no amount of gentrification has ever fully smoothed over.

## Who Should Move to Belgrade?

*   Families and remote workers who want Gallatin Valley access with newer housing, strong schools, and a median household income of $88,896
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want a ski area, 21 fishing access sites, hot springs, and 16 trailheads all within 30 miles
*   People who want a fast-growing community with a young, professional demographic and quick airport access
*   Anyone drawn to the energy of a booming small city with a suburban feel and mountain proximity

## Who Should Move to Butte?

*   History enthusiasts and people who want to live inside one of Montana's most architecturally and culturally significant cities
*   Budget-conscious buyers who want a home in western Montana at a typical value of around $274,163, well below the state's resort-corridor prices
*   People who value a strong sense of community identity, local pride, and a city that has been through hard times and come out with character
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 14 campgrounds, 4 hot springs, and 17 museums all within 30 miles

## Cost of Living Breakdown

The housing gap between these two towns is dramatic. The typical home value in Belgrade is around $544,626, compared to just $274,163 in Butte, making Butte one of the most affordable cities in western Montana. Rent follows the same pattern: Belgrade averages about $2,206 per month, while Butte comes in at roughly $1,318.

Belgrade's income advantage is significant. The median household income is $88,896 in Belgrade versus $57,633 in Butte. But in Butte, the lower cost of living means that income stretches considerably further on everyday expenses.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Belgrade is a town in motion, building out fast with new subdivisions and a demographic that skews young and professional. Butte is the opposite: a city that has already lived several full lives, from copper boom to Superfund site to cultural revival, and carries that history in its bones. The uptown historic district, the Berkeley Pit, and the annual St. Patrick's Day celebration that draws tens of thousands of visitors give Butte a personality that is entirely its own. If you want the Gallatin Valley's economic energy and outdoor access, Belgrade is the right call. If you want a Montana city with genuine historical depth and some of the most affordable housing in the western part of the state, Butte is worth a serious look.

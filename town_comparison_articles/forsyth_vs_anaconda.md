# Forsyth vs Anaconda, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Forsyth and Anaconda offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Forsyth is a historic railroad town on the Yellowstone River with 1,630 people, its beautiful, historic county courthouse, its position as a transportation and agricultural hub, and quick access to the Yellowstone River and the eastern plains. Anaconda, with 9,491 residents, is a historic smelting town known for its towering smokestack, its working-class heritage, and Georgetown Lake and the Anaconda-Pintler Wilderness.

## Who Should Move to Forsyth?

* Workers in agriculture, transportation, or local services looking for a stable community.
* People seeking very affordable housing and a low overall cost of living.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle on the eastern plains.
* Hunters and anglers who want uncrowded access to the Yellowstone River.

## Who Should Move to Anaconda?

* Retirees and remote workers seeking very low housing costs.
* History buffs fascinated by Montana's copper era.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want uncrowded access to the Pintlers.
* People looking for a quiet, slow-paced mountain community.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Forsyth is around $208,690, while in Anaconda it is closer to $210,000, a difference of roughly $1,310, making Forsyth significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Forsyth averages about $743 per month, compared to roughly $750 in Anaconda.

Forsyth's overall cost of living differs from Anaconda's. Forsyth offers higher average salaries compared to Anaconda, with a median household income of $58,375 compared to Anaconda's $48,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Forsyth is a quiet, practical community in eastern Montana, defined by the railroad, agriculture, and the Yellowstone River. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle with a strong sense of local history. Anaconda is a quiet, resilient town with a deep industrial history, offering incredibly affordable access to some of Montana's best mountain recreation.

# Laurel vs Dillon: Which Montana Town is Right for You?

Laurel and Dillon are both mid-sized Montana communities with similar home prices but very different characters and locations. Laurel is the City Between the Yellowstone and the Rimrocks, a working-class Yellowstone Valley community of 7,215 in Yellowstone County at 3,307 feet, with Billings Logan International Airport 18 miles away, 60 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 21 fishing access sites, 10 museums, 6 state parks, 5 golf courses, 4 historic sites, 3 nature reserves, 2 disc golf courses, 1 river, and a 1,800-student school district. Dillon is Montana's Hometown, a small university and ranching community of 3,880 in Beaverhead County at 5,095 feet, with the nearest airport in Butte 62 miles away, 71 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 24 fishing access sites, 16 campgrounds, 8 lakes, 7 nature reserves, 5 state parks, 2 hot springs, 2 historic sites, 1 museum, 1 golf course, 1 river, and a 650-student school district.

## Who Should Move to Laurel?

*   Working families and professionals who want easy access to Billings, the largest city in Montana, just 18 miles away, with its hospitals, employers, shopping, and airport, while living in a more affordable community
*   Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want 21 fishing access sites on the Yellowstone River, 6 state parks, 10 museums, and 5 golf courses within 30 miles, with the Rimrocks and the Yellowstone Valley as a backdrop
*   Buyers who want an affordable Montana community close to a major city, with a typical home value of $369,826 and rent averaging $1,160 per month
*   Families who want a 1,800-student school district, 4 historic sites, and a genuine working-class Montana community with strong Yellowstone Valley character

## Who Should Move to Dillon?

*   Anglers and fly fishing enthusiasts who want 24 fishing access sites on the Beaverhead River, one of the most celebrated blue-ribbon trout streams in Montana, along with 16 campgrounds, 7 nature reserves, and 5 state parks within 30 miles
*   University of Montana Western students, faculty, and staff who want to live in a tight-knit college town with a 650-student school district and a genuine small-town Montana character
*   Budget-conscious buyers who want to own a home in a real western Montana community at a price nearly identical to Laurel, with a typical home value of $375,585 but rent averaging just $821 per month, significantly lower than Laurel
*   Retirees and remote workers who want a quiet, self-contained southwestern Montana community at 5,095 feet with 2 hot springs, 5 state parks, and wide-open Beaverhead County skies

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Laurel and Dillon are remarkably close in home purchase prices. The typical home value in Laurel is $369,826, compared to $375,585 in Dillon, nearly identical. Rent is where the difference shows up: Laurel averages $1,160 per month versus $821 in Dillon, about $340 less per month in Dillon.

Median household income is higher in Laurel at $66,382 versus $51,458 in Dillon. Dillon's lower income reflects its smaller, more rural economy centered on agriculture, ranching, and the university. For buyers who want similar purchase prices but lower monthly costs, Dillon has a clear advantage on rent.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Laurel is a working-class Yellowstone Valley community where the railroad, the refinery, the Rimrocks, and proximity to Billings define daily life. Dillon is a quieter, more traditional Montana community where the Beaverhead River, the surrounding ranch country, and the University of Montana Western define daily life. Dillon has 24 fishing access sites and 5 state parks within 30 miles that reflect its position in one of Montana's premier fly fishing corridors. Laurel has 21 fishing access sites, 10 museums, and 6 state parks that reflect its deep Yellowstone Valley roots. Both communities are genuinely affordable by Montana standards, but they serve very different kinds of residents. If you want a working-class community with easy access to Billings and strong Yellowstone Valley outdoor access, Laurel is a practical and solid choice. If you want an affordable, self-contained southwestern Montana community with world-class fly fishing, genuine small-town character, and a university presence, Dillon is one of the most underrated places to live in Montana.

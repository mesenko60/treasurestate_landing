# Dillon vs Big Sky: Which Montana Town Is Right for You?

Dillon and Big Sky represent two very different visions of Montana life. Dillon, Montana's Hometown in the Beaverhead Valley, is a quiet, affordable ranching and university town of 3,880 people, home to the University of Montana Western and surrounded by some of the finest fly fishing rivers in the state. Big Sky, The Biggest Skiing in America in the Gallatin Canyon, is a resort community of 3,591 people built around world-class skiing and outdoor recreation, with home prices that place it among the most expensive communities in Montana.

## Who Should Move to Dillon?

- Fly fishers who want the Beaverhead, Big Hole, and Ruby rivers, among the most storied trout streams in Montana, close at hand
- Students or families connected to the University of Montana Western, which gives Dillon a youthful energy unusual for a town of its size
- People looking for very affordable housing and a quiet, traditional small-town lifestyle in a wide-open high-desert setting
- History enthusiasts interested in Lewis and Clark country and the ranching heritage of the Beaverhead Valley

## Who Should Move to Big Sky?

- Skiers and snowboarders who want one of the largest ski resorts in North America as a literal backyard
- High-income remote workers, retirees, or second-home buyers who want a luxury mountain lifestyle in the Gallatin Canyon
- Outdoor enthusiasts who want dramatic canyon scenery, world-class hiking, hot springs, and proximity to Yellowstone National Park
- People who want a rapidly growing resort community with excellent restaurants and a sophisticated year-round energy

## Cost of Living Breakdown

The housing gap between these two towns is dramatic. The typical home value in Big Sky is $1,819,905 compared to $375,585 in Dillon, a difference of over $1.4 million. Rent is also dramatically higher in Big Sky at $1,712 per month versus $821 in Dillon. Big Sky's median household income of $103,625 is double Dillon's $51,458, reflecting the resort economy and the high-earning remote workers it attracts. Even accounting for higher incomes, Big Sky's cost of living is in a category of its own. Dillon's nearest airport is in Butte, 62 miles north. Big Sky's is in Bozeman, 44 miles away.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Dillon and Big Sky are both outstanding places to live for people who love the outdoors, but they serve entirely different markets. Dillon is a quiet, self-contained university and ranching town where the Beaverhead River and the wide-open Beaverhead Valley define daily life. Big Sky is a resort town that has grown dramatically in recent years, drawing wealthy transplants and remote workers who want a high-end mountain lifestyle in the Gallatin Canyon. Dillon has 71 recreational sites within 30 miles, anchored by 24 fishing access points, 5 state parks, and 2 hot springs. Big Sky has 140 sites, led by Big Sky Resort, 23 trailheads, 17 waterfalls, and 3 hot springs. For river fishing and affordability, Dillon is the clear choice. For skiing and mountain resort living, Big Sky has no equal in Montana.

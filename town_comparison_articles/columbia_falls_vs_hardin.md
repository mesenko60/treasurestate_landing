# Columbia Falls vs Hardin: Which Montana Town Is Right for You?

Columbia Falls and Hardin sit at opposite ends of Montana, both in size and character. Columbia Falls, the Gateway to Glacier in the northwest, is a mountain community of 5,308 people surrounded by wilderness, ski areas, and the wild country of Glacier National Park. Hardin, known as Where the Rivers Meet in southeastern Montana, is a town of 3,742 people near the confluence of the Bighorn and Little Bighorn rivers, best known for its proximity to Little Bighorn Battlefield and its strong ties to the Crow Nation and ranching culture.

## Who Should Move to Columbia Falls?

- Outdoor enthusiasts who want Glacier National Park, alpine skiing, and mountain wilderness as daily amenities
- Families seeking a growing community with strong schools and a vibrant outdoor culture in western Montana
- People who want the lush, green scenery of the Flathead Valley and the northern Rockies
- Those who want easy airport access via Glacier Park International in Kalispell, just 6 miles away

## Who Should Move to Hardin?

- History enthusiasts drawn to Little Bighorn Battlefield and the rich cultural heritage of the Crow Nation
- People who want very affordable housing and a low cost of living in a quiet, rural setting
- Anglers who want access to the Bighorn River, one of Montana's premier tailwater trout fisheries
- Those who prefer eastern Montana's wide-open prairie landscapes and a traditional, community-centered way of life

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is dramatically more affordable in Hardin. The typical home value is $204,573 compared to $584,580 in Columbia Falls, a difference of roughly $380,000. Rent reflects the same divide, with Columbia Falls at $1,003 per month and Hardin at $726. Despite the large housing gap, the two towns have closer median household incomes than expected, with Columbia Falls at $65,313 and Hardin at $60,463.

Columbia Falls is served by Glacier Park International Airport in Kalispell, 6 miles away. Hardin's nearest airport is in Billings, 54 miles to the west.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Columbia Falls is an active, forward-moving gateway town with Glacier National Park drawing visitors and energy year-round. The community is growing, and the outdoor recreation culture is central to daily life. Hardin is quieter and more rooted in history and tradition. The Crow Fair each August is one of the largest Native American gatherings in the country and gives Hardin a cultural richness that is unique in Montana. These are two towns that share a state but almost nothing else in terms of landscape, culture, or daily rhythm.

Columbia Falls has 214 recreational sites within 30 miles, including 48 campgrounds, 19 ski areas, 31 trailheads, and Glacier National Park. Hardin has 20 sites within range, anchored by the Bighorn River, several fishing access points, and the Little Bighorn Battlefield historic site. For sheer outdoor recreation volume, Columbia Falls is in a different league. For history, river fishing, and cultural depth, Hardin offers something genuinely distinctive.

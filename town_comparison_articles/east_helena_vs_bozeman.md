# East Helena vs Bozeman, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

East Helena and Bozeman offer two very different Montana lifestyles. East Helena is a growing residential community in the Helena Valley with 2,264 people, its industrial heritage, its rapid recent growth as a suburb of the capital, and quick access to Canyon Ferry Lake and the Elkhorn Mountains. Bozeman, with 54,539 residents, is a booming tech and university hub known for Montana State University, its high-tech economy, and the Bridger Mountains and Big Sky.

## Who Should Move to East Helena?

* Families looking for a strong local school district and a tight-knit community feel.
* Professionals working in Helena who want a slightly quieter, more residential environment.
* People who appreciate a town with a strong working-class heritage that is actively reinventing itself.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want quick access to Canyon Ferry Lake and the surrounding national forest.

## Who Should Move to Bozeman?

* Tech workers and entrepreneurs.
* Students and faculty at MSU.
* Affluent outdoor enthusiasts.
* People who want a highly active, progressive mountain town.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in East Helena is around $430,196, while in Bozeman it is closer to $750,000, a difference of roughly $319,804, making East Helena significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in East Helena averages about $1,020 per month, compared to roughly $2,000 in Bozeman.

East Helena's overall cost of living differs from Bozeman's. East Helena offers lower average salaries compared to Bozeman, with a median household income of $53,611 compared to Bozeman's $70,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

East Helena is transitioning from its historic roots as a smelter town into a rapidly growing, family-friendly suburb of Helena. It offers a quieter, slightly more affordable alternative to the capital city while maintaining its own distinct, blue-collar identity. Bozeman is fast-paced, highly educated, and outdoor-obsessed, with a premium cost of living and a constant influx of new residents.

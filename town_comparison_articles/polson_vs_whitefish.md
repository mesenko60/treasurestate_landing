# Polson vs Whitefish, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Polson and Whitefish offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Polson is a scenic lakeside community with 5,335 people, its location on the southern shore of Flathead Lake, its cherry orchards, and quick access to Flathead Lake and the Mission Mountains. Whitefish, with just 8,492 residents, is a premier ski and summer resort town known for Whitefish Mountain Resort, its pristine lake, and Glacier National Park and Whitefish Lake.

## Who Should Move to Polson?

* Retirees and remote workers seeking a beautiful lakeside setting with a slower pace of life.
* Boaters, anglers, and water sports enthusiasts who want immediate access to the largest natural freshwater lake in the West.
* People looking for a more affordable alternative to the resort towns of the northern Flathead Valley.
* Those who appreciate a community with a strong agricultural heritage, particularly its famous cherry harvest.

## Who Should Move to Whitefish?

* Affluent buyers looking for a premium mountain lifestyle.
* Dedicated skiers and snowboarders.
* People who want a lively, walkable downtown with upscale dining.
* Those who desire immediate access to a major ski resort and a beautiful lake.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Polson is around $415,000, while in Whitefish it is closer to $850,000, a difference of roughly $435,000, making Polson significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Polson averages about $1,050 per month, compared to roughly $1,800 in Whitefish.

Polson's overall cost of living differs from Whitefish's. Polson offers lower average salaries compared to Whitefish, with a median household income of $52,500 compared to Whitefish's $75,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Polson offers a relaxed, scenic lifestyle defined by its position on Flathead Lake. It lacks the polished resort feel of towns further north, maintaining a more grounded, agricultural character while still providing spectacular water and mountain access. Whitefish is a polished, high-end resort community with a vibrant downtown, drawing tourists and affluent residents year-round.

# Dillon vs Great Falls: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Dillon and Great Falls are both affordable Montana communities with strong outdoor access, but they differ significantly in size, character, and geography. Dillon is Montana's Hometown, a small Beaverhead County community of 3,880 at 5,095 feet in southwestern Montana, known for its quiet ranching character, the Beaverhead River, 24 fishing access sites, 16 campgrounds, 5 state parks, 2 hot springs, and the University of Montana Western. Great Falls is the Electric City, a community of 60,000 in Cascade County at 3,340 feet along the Missouri River in central Montana, with 45 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 10 museums, 8 fishing access sites, 6 waterfalls, 5 state parks, 4 golf courses, 2 rivers, a wildlife refuge, and Great Falls International Airport 5 miles away.

## Who Should Move to Dillon?

*   Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want 24 fishing access sites, 16 campgrounds, 5 state parks, 2 hot springs, 8 lakes, and the Beaverhead River all within 30 miles, considerably more than Great Falls
*   Remote workers, retirees, and families who want a genuinely quiet, small-town Montana lifestyle at a very affordable price, with a typical home value of $375,585 and rent averaging just $821 per month
*   People who want a small university town with the University of Montana Western providing community events, sports, and cultural programming
*   Anyone drawn to southwestern Montana's wide-open spaces, ranching culture, and the Big Hole Valley just to the west

## Who Should Move to Great Falls?

*   Professionals, families, and military personnel who want a mid-sized central Montana city with a 10,000-student school district, Great Falls International Airport 5 miles away, and Malmstrom Air Force Base as a major employer
*   People who want a city with 10 museums, 6 waterfalls, 5 state parks, the Missouri River, and the Lewis and Clark National Historic Trail all within 30 miles
*   Budget-conscious buyers who want a mid-sized Montana city at a lower home price, with a typical home value of $328,554, lower than both Dillon and most other Montana cities
*   Anyone who wants a central Montana location with easy access to both the Rocky Mountain Front and the Hi-Line

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Great Falls has a lower typical home price than Dillon. The typical home value in Dillon is around $375,585, compared to $328,554 in Great Falls, about 14% lower. Rent is dramatically lower in Dillon: Dillon averages about $821 per month, while Great Falls comes in at roughly $1,340, about 63% higher.

Median household income is higher in Great Falls at $63,934 versus $51,458 in Dillon. Great Falls residents earn more on average, which helps offset the higher rent. Dillon's rent is dramatically lower, making it the more affordable monthly lifestyle despite a slightly higher home price.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Dillon is a small southwestern Montana town where the Beaverhead River, ranching culture, and the University of Montana Western define community life. Great Falls is a mid-sized central Montana city where the Missouri River, Malmstrom Air Force Base, and a strong arts and museum culture define daily life. Great Falls has considerably more services, more employment options, and a more complex urban character than Dillon, but Dillon has dramatically more fishing access, campgrounds, and outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles. If you want a quiet, affordable southwestern Montana lifestyle with excellent fishing and outdoor access, Dillon is a compelling choice. If you want a mid-sized central Montana city with more services, more museums, and a stronger job market, Great Falls is one of Montana's most affordable and most underrated cities.

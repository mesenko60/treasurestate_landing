# Glasgow vs Whitefish, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Glasgow and Whitefish offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Glasgow is a historic railroad town on the Hi-Line with 3,224 people, its position as a regional hub for northeastern Montana, its proximity to Fort Peck Dam, and quick access to Fort Peck Lake and the Charles M. Russell National Wildlife Refuge. Whitefish, with just 8,492 residents, is a premier ski and summer resort town known for Whitefish Mountain Resort, its pristine lake, and Glacier National Park and Whitefish Lake.

## Who Should Move to Glasgow?

* Workers in agriculture, government, or the railroad industry looking for stable employment in northeastern Montana.
* Anglers and boaters who want to live near Fort Peck Lake, one of the premier walleye fisheries in the country.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, isolated community.
* Hunters and outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the vast, uncrowded public lands of the Missouri River Breaks.

## Who Should Move to Whitefish?

* Affluent buyers looking for a premium mountain lifestyle.
* Dedicated skiers and snowboarders.
* People who want a lively, walkable downtown with upscale dining.
* Those who desire immediate access to a major ski resort and a beautiful lake.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Glasgow is around $195,000, while in Whitefish it is closer to $850,000, a difference of roughly $655,000, making Glasgow significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Glasgow averages about $750 per month, compared to roughly $1,800 in Whitefish.

Glasgow's overall cost of living differs from Whitefish's. Glasgow offers lower average salaries compared to Whitefish, with a median household income of $55,000 compared to Whitefish's $75,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Glasgow is a resilient, isolated community that serves as the commercial and social center for a massive swath of northeastern Montana. It offers a quiet, slow-paced lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture, the railroad, and the incredible recreational opportunities at nearby Fort Peck Lake. Whitefish is a polished, high-end resort community with a vibrant downtown, drawing tourists and affluent residents year-round.

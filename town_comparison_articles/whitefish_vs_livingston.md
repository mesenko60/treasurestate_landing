# Whitefish vs Livingston: Which Montana Small Town is Right for You?

Whitefish and Livingston are two of Montana's most beloved and distinctive small towns, similar in population, both deeply desirable, and both with strong identities that attract people from across the country. But they are very different places. Whitefish is Stumptown, a resort-oriented mountain town of 8,915 in the Flathead Valley, where Whitefish Mountain Resort, Glacier National Park, and Whitefish Lake converge to create one of the most sought-after small-town lifestyle destinations in the American West. Livingston is the Windiest City in Montana, a small, storied town of 8,040 in the Paradise Valley, where the Yellowstone River flows cold and clear through downtown, the Absaroka Range rises to the south, and a long tradition of artists, writers, and fly fishers gives the place a character that is genuinely irreplaceable.

## Who Should Move to Whitefish?

*   Skiers who want Whitefish Mountain Resort as a home mountain, with Glacier National Park as a backyard
*   Remote workers and high-income earners who want one of Montana's most walkable and scenically spectacular small-town settings
*   People who love a vibrant, boutique downtown with excellent dining, galleries, and a strong arts and music scene
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 202 recreation sites within 30 miles, including 19 ski areas, 33 trailheads, 46 campgrounds, and 26 lakes

## Who Should Move to Livingston?

*   Artists, writers, and creative professionals drawn to Livingston's long-established arts and literary community
*   Fly fishing enthusiasts who want direct access to the Yellowstone River, one of Montana's finest, right in town, plus 24 fishing access points within 30 miles
*   Remote workers and retirees who want a small, character-rich community close to Yellowstone National Park and the Paradise Valley
*   People who want the feel of a genuine Montana small town with 16 waterfalls, 40 lakes, and 2 hot springs within 30 miles, at a significantly lower price than Whitefish

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Whitefish is significantly more expensive. The median home price in Whitefish is around $840,135, compared to $509,477 in Livingston, a difference of about $330,000. Rent follows the same pattern: about $2,639 per month in Whitefish versus $1,783 in Livingston.

Whitefish's median household income of $71,110 is modestly higher than Livingston's $65,187, but that $5,900 income advantage does nothing to bridge a housing gap of $330,000. The premium in Whitefish is a pure lifestyle and location premium; Livingston offers a comparable small-town Montana experience at a meaningfully lower cost.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Whitefish and Livingston are both extraordinary Montana small towns, and choosing between them is genuinely difficult. Whitefish is a destination that people also happen to live in, a town where the ski season is a community event, Whitefish Lake defines the summer, and Glacier National Park is always on the horizon. Livingston is more literary and grounded, a town known for its wind, its writers, its fly fishing, and its proximity to Yellowstone. Whitefish has more outdoor recreation by the numbers; Livingston has the Yellowstone River running through downtown and 16 waterfalls within 30 miles. Both are among the finest small towns in Montana; the choice comes down to whether you want the resort-town energy of Stumptown or the river-town soul of the Windiest City in Montana.

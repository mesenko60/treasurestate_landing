# Big Sky vs Anaconda: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Big Sky and Anaconda are both mountain communities in western Montana, but they represent opposite ends of the economic and lifestyle spectrum. Big Sky is the Biggest Skiing in America, a resort community of 3,591 in Gallatin County at 6,319 feet, known for Big Sky Resort, 23 trailheads, 17 waterfalls, 18 lakes, 3 hot springs, 2 wilderness areas, and 140 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles. Anaconda is the Smelter City, a historic copper smelting town of 9,421 in Deer Lodge County at 5,279 feet in western Montana, with 134 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 48 lakes, 19 campgrounds, 16 museums, 9 trailheads, 8 nature reserves, 6 golf courses, 3 historic sites, 3 state parks, 3 viewpoints, 1 hot spring, 1 ski area, and the nearest airport in Butte 30 miles away.

## Who Should Move to Big Sky?

*   Skiers, snowboarders, and mountain sports enthusiasts who want Big Sky Resort, one of the largest ski areas in North America with over 5,800 acres of skiable terrain, right in their backyard
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 23 trailheads, 17 waterfalls, 18 lakes, 19 fishing access sites, 3 hot springs, 2 wilderness areas, and a national forest all within 30 miles
*   High-income remote workers and affluent buyers who can afford Big Sky's extraordinary real estate market, with a typical home value of $1,819,905 and a median household income of $103,625
*   People who want a small, exclusive resort community lifestyle where outdoor recreation is the primary focus and the mountains define every aspect of daily life

## Who Should Move to Anaconda?

*   History enthusiasts drawn to Anaconda's remarkable copper smelting heritage, with 16 museums, 3 historic sites, the famous Anaconda Smelter Stack State Park, and the Old Works Golf Course built on the former smelter site
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 48 lakes, 19 campgrounds, 9 trailheads, 8 nature reserves, 1 ski area, 1 hot spring, and a national forest all within 30 miles at a fraction of Big Sky's cost
*   Budget-conscious buyers looking for one of the most affordable mountain communities in Montana, with a typical home value of $283,380 and rent averaging just $661 per month
*   Retirees and remote workers who want a quiet, historic western Montana town with outstanding outdoor access, low costs, and a strong sense of community identity rooted in its industrial past

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Big Sky is dramatically more expensive than Anaconda. The typical home value in Big Sky is $1,819,905, compared to $283,380 in Anaconda, more than six times higher. Rent reflects the same gap: Big Sky averages $1,712 per month versus just $661 in Anaconda, one of the lowest rents of any Montana community.

Median household income is significantly higher in Big Sky at $103,625 versus $49,533 in Anaconda. Anaconda is one of the most affordable mountain communities in Montana, making it accessible to buyers and renters on almost any budget. Big Sky's extraordinary home prices are in a completely different category.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Big Sky is a world-class mountain resort community where the ski mountain, the wilderness, and a high-income outdoor lifestyle define daily life. Anaconda is a historic smelter town where copper mining heritage, 48 nearby lakes, the Pintler Scenic Route, and a tight-knit working-class community define local identity. Anaconda has 16 museums and 48 lakes within 30 miles, both figures that dwarf Big Sky's totals, but Big Sky has 17 waterfalls, 3 hot springs, and a ski resort that Anaconda cannot match. Both communities sit at high elevation in western Montana, but they attract very different residents. If you want a resort lifestyle in the mountains with world-class skiing and wilderness access, Big Sky is extraordinary. If you want an affordable western Montana mountain town with remarkable history, outstanding lake access, and a community that has genuine character, Anaconda is one of the most undervalued places to live in Montana.

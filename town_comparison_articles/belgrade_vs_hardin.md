# Belgrade vs Hardin: Which Montana Town is Right for You?

Belgrade and Hardin sit at opposite ends of Montana's character spectrum. Belgrade is the Hub of the Gallatin Valley, a fast-growing suburb of 12,509 in Gallatin County at 4,462 feet, with Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport 5 miles away, 104 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 16 trailheads, 14 waterfalls, 15 lakes, 21 fishing access sites, 2 hot springs, a national forest, a ski area, and a 3,800-student school district. Hardin is Where the Rivers Meet, a small eastern Montana community of 3,742 in Big Horn County at 2,910 feet at the confluence of the Bighorn and Yellowstone rivers, with Billings Logan International Airport 54 miles away, 20 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 10 fishing access sites, 4 historic sites, 2 museums, 2 nature reserves, 1 river, 1 viewpoint, and a 900-student school district.

## Who Should Move to Belgrade?

*   Families and professionals who want Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport 5 miles away, a 3,800-student school district, and all the amenities of the Bozeman metro area at a slightly lower price than Bozeman itself
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 16 trailheads, 14 waterfalls, 15 lakes, 21 fishing access sites, 2 hot springs, a ski area, and a national forest all within 30 miles, with quick access to the Bridger Mountains and Gallatin Canyon
*   Buyers who want a rapidly growing community with strong job access in the Gallatin Valley, with a typical home value of $544,626 and a median household income of $88,896
*   Remote workers and young professionals who want a suburban Gallatin Valley lifestyle with proximity to Bozeman's restaurants, university, and cultural amenities without paying Bozeman prices

## Who Should Move to Hardin?

*   History enthusiasts and students of American history drawn to the Little Bighorn Battlefield National Monument 14 miles away, one of the most significant and moving historic sites in the American West, along with 4 historic sites and 2 museums within 30 miles
*   Anglers who want 10 fishing access sites on the Bighorn River, one of Montana's most celebrated tailwater trout fisheries, in a community where fishing is a central part of local identity
*   Budget-conscious buyers who want one of the most affordable housing markets in Montana, with a typical home value of $204,573 and rent averaging just $726 per month
*   Agricultural workers, energy industry employees, and anyone who wants a quiet, affordable eastern Montana community with a strong sense of local heritage and a 900-student school district

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Belgrade is dramatically more expensive than Hardin. The typical home value in Belgrade is $544,626, compared to $204,573 in Hardin, nearly two and a half times higher. Rent reflects the same gap: Belgrade averages $2,206 per month versus $726 in Hardin, more than three times higher.

Median household income is higher in Belgrade at $88,896 versus $60,463 in Hardin. Hardin offers some of the most affordable housing in Montana, making homeownership accessible to buyers on almost any budget. Belgrade's higher costs reflect its position in the booming Gallatin Valley market.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Belgrade feels like a prosperous, growing suburb of Bozeman, with new subdivisions, strong outdoor access, and a community identity tied closely to the Gallatin Valley's outdoor and university culture. Hardin is a quiet eastern Montana river town where the Bighorn River, the Little Bighorn Battlefield, and a strong agricultural and Native American heritage define community life. Belgrade has 16 trailheads, 14 waterfalls, 15 lakes, and a ski area within 30 miles that Hardin cannot match. Hardin has 4 historic sites and the Little Bighorn Battlefield nearby, giving it a historical depth and cultural significance that few Montana communities can claim. If you want a fast-growing Gallatin Valley community with outstanding mountain access and a suburban feel, Belgrade is one of the best values in the Bozeman area. If you want an affordable eastern Montana river town with world-class Bighorn River fishing, deep historical significance, and a community that is genuinely off the beaten path, Hardin is a distinctive and underrated place to live.

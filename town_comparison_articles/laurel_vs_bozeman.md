# Laurel vs Bozeman: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Laurel and Bozeman are both in central Montana, but they could not be more different in price, pace, or personality. Laurel is the City Between the Yellowstone and the Rimrocks, a quiet, working-class community of 7,215 in Yellowstone County with affordable housing, 21 fishing access sites within 30 miles, and a practical, unpretentious character. Bozeman is the Gallatin Gateway, one of the fastest-growing cities in the country at 58,000 people, with 139 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, a booming tech and professional economy, Montana State University, and home prices that reflect all of it.

## Who Should Move to Laurel?

*   Budget-conscious families and buyers who want central Montana living at a fraction of Bozeman's price, with a typical home value around $369,826 versus Bozeman's $715,781
*   People who want a quiet, small-town community with Billings' full-service amenities just 18 miles away
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 21 fishing access sites and 6 state parks along the Yellowstone River corridor
*   Anyone who wants a low-key, affordable Montana lifestyle without the resort-town premium

## Who Should Move to Bozeman?

*   Young professionals in tech, healthcare, or research drawn to Bozeman's booming economy and Montana State University
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 16 trailheads, 32 fishing access sites, 13 campgrounds, and a ski area all within 30 miles
*   Families who want a full-service city with a strong school district and a broad range of cultural and recreational amenities
*   Anyone who wants the energy of a fast-growing, cosmopolitan Montana city with a mountain backdrop

## Cost of Living Breakdown

The housing gap between these two towns is among the widest in Montana. The typical home value in Laurel is around $369,826, compared to $715,781 in Bozeman, nearly double the price. Rent follows the same pattern: Laurel averages about $1,160 per month, while Bozeman comes in at roughly $2,113, nearly twice as much.

The income gap is real but does not close the housing divide. The median household income in Bozeman is $79,903, compared to $66,382 in Laurel. Bozeman residents earn more, but the cost premium is so large that Laurel remains dramatically more affordable for buyers and renters alike.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Laurel is a no-frills river town where the Yellowstone is close and the pace is slow. Bozeman is one of Montana's most dynamic cities, with a downtown that hums with activity, a university that brings constant energy, and a mountain setting that draws outdoor enthusiasts from across the country. If you want affordable Montana living with Billings nearby, Laurel is a smart, practical choice. If you want to be in the middle of Montana's fastest-growing city and can absorb the cost, Bozeman is genuinely exceptional.

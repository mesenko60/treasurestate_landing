# Hardin vs Big Sky: Which Montana Town Is Right for You?

Hardin and Big Sky are two of the most distinctive communities in Montana, but they could hardly be more different. Hardin, Where the Rivers Meet in Big Horn County, is a town of 3,742 people near the confluence of the Bighorn and Little Bighorn rivers, known for the Little Bighorn Battlefield, the Bighorn River's world-class tailwater trout fishery, and the living cultural heritage of the Crow Nation. Big Sky, The Biggest Skiing in America in the Gallatin Canyon, is a resort community of 3,591 people built around one of the largest ski mountains in North America, with home prices that make it one of the most expensive places to live in Montana.

## Who Should Move to Hardin?

- History enthusiasts drawn to Little Bighorn Battlefield and the rich cultural heritage of the Crow Nation
- Anglers who want the Bighorn River, one of Montana's premier tailwater trout fisheries, close at hand
- People looking for very affordable housing and a low cost of living in a quiet, rural community
- Those who want to experience the unique culture of southeastern Montana, including the annual Crow Fair

## Who Should Move to Big Sky?

- Skiers and snowboarders who want one of the largest ski resorts in North America as a literal backyard
- High-income remote workers, retirees, or second-home buyers who want a luxury mountain lifestyle in the Gallatin Canyon
- Outdoor enthusiasts who want dramatic canyon scenery, world-class hiking, hot springs, and proximity to Yellowstone National Park
- People who want a rapidly growing resort community with excellent restaurants and a sophisticated year-round energy

## Cost of Living Breakdown

The housing gap between these two towns is among the largest of any pairing in Montana. The typical home value in Big Sky is $1,819,905 compared to $204,573 in Hardin, a difference of over $1.6 million. Rent is also dramatically higher in Big Sky at $1,712 per month versus $726 in Hardin. Big Sky's median household income of $103,625 is nearly double Hardin's $60,463. Even accounting for higher incomes, Big Sky's cost of living is in a category of its own. Hardin's nearest airport is in Billings, 54 miles to the west. Big Sky's is in Bozeman, 44 miles away.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Hardin and Big Sky are separated by the full width of Montana's character, not just its geography. Hardin is a quiet, community-centered town shaped by the Bighorn River, the Little Bighorn Battlefield, and the Crow Nation's living cultural presence. Life here is unhurried, grounded in history, and tied to the land and the river. Big Sky is a resort town that has grown dramatically in recent years, drawing wealthy transplants and remote workers who want a high-end mountain lifestyle in the Gallatin Canyon. Hardin has 20 recreational sites within 30 miles, anchored by the Bighorn River and the Little Bighorn Battlefield. Big Sky has 140, led by Big Sky Resort, 23 trailheads, 17 waterfalls, and 3 hot springs. For history, culture, and affordability, Hardin is in a class of its own. For skiing and mountain resort living, Big Sky is unmatched in Montana.

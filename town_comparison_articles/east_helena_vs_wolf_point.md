# East Helena vs Wolf Point, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

East Helena and Wolf Point offer two very different Montana lifestyles. East Helena is a growing residential community in the Helena Valley with 2,264 people, its industrial heritage, its rapid recent growth as a suburb of the capital, and quick access to Canyon Ferry Lake and the Elkhorn Mountains. Wolf Point, with 2,517 residents, is a historic agricultural town on the Fort Peck Indian Reservation known for its position as the largest community on the reservation, its deep roots in farming and ranching, and the Missouri River and the vast eastern plains.

## Who Should Move to East Helena?

* Families looking for a strong local school district and a tight-knit community feel.
* Professionals working in Helena who want a slightly quieter, more residential environment.
* People who appreciate a town with a strong working-class heritage that is actively reinventing itself.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want quick access to Canyon Ferry Lake and the surrounding national forest.

## Who Should Move to Wolf Point?

* People seeking some of the most affordable housing in the state.
* Workers in agriculture, tribal government, or local services.
* Those who appreciate a quiet, isolated lifestyle on the high plains.
* Hunters and anglers who want uncrowded access to the Missouri River and surrounding lands.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in East Helena is around $430,196, while in Wolf Point it is closer to $145,000, a difference of roughly $285,196, making Wolf Point significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in East Helena averages about $1,020 per month, compared to roughly $600 in Wolf Point.

East Helena's overall cost of living differs from Wolf Point's. East Helena offers higher average salaries compared to Wolf Point, with a median household income of $53,611 compared to Wolf Point's $42,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

East Helena is transitioning from its historic roots as a smelter town into a rapidly growing, family-friendly suburb of Helena. It offers a quieter, slightly more affordable alternative to the capital city while maintaining its own distinct, blue-collar identity. Wolf Point is a quiet, resilient community deeply tied to the Fort Peck Indian Reservation and the agricultural economy of northeastern Montana. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle far removed from the state's major urban centers.

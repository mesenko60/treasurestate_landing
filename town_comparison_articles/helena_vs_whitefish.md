# Helena vs Whitefish: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Helena and Whitefish are both desirable Montana communities, but they attract very different kinds of people and represent very different price points. Helena is the Queen City of the Rockies, the state capital at 33,100 people, a city born in a gold rush and shaped by government and law, with a walkable downtown, Broadwater Hot Springs minutes from the city center, and a trail network that climbs into the mountains behind it. Whitefish is Stumptown, a small, resort-oriented mountain town of 8,915 in the Flathead Valley, where Whitefish Mountain Resort, Glacier National Park, and Whitefish Lake converge to create one of the most sought-after outdoor lifestyle destinations in the country.

## Who Should Move to Helena?

*   State government employees, attorneys, and professionals whose careers are tied to the state capitol
*   Families who want a larger community with strong schools, Helena Public Schools enrolls about 7,800 students
*   History lovers drawn to Helena's gold rush past, Victorian architecture, and 10 historic sites within 30 miles
*   People who want a full-service Montana capital city with hot springs, trails, and mountain access at a significantly lower cost than Whitefish

## Who Should Move to Whitefish?

*   Skiers who want Whitefish Mountain Resort as a home mountain, with Glacier National Park as a backyard
*   Remote workers and high-income earners who want one of Montana's most beautiful and walkable small-town settings
*   People who love a vibrant, boutique downtown with excellent dining, galleries, and a strong arts and music scene
*   Families and retirees who want a tight-knit, scenically spectacular community and are prepared for the highest housing costs in the state

## Cost of Living Breakdown

The housing gap between these two communities is substantial. The median home price in Helena is around $466,393, while in Whitefish it climbs to $840,135, nearly double the cost. Rent follows the same pattern: about $1,489 per month in Helena versus $2,639 in Whitefish.

Median household incomes are remarkably similar, $69,341 in Helena versus $71,110 in Whitefish, which makes the housing gap all the more significant. The premium in Whitefish is almost entirely a lifestyle and location premium: you are paying for Glacier National Park, Whitefish Mountain Resort, and one of the most scenically spectacular small-town settings in the American West.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Helena is a capital city with a surprisingly outdoorsy soul, a place where legislators and lobbyists share the trails with mountain bikers and the hot springs with families. Whitefish is a destination that people also happen to live in, a town where the ski season defines the winter, Whitefish Lake defines the summer, and the proximity to Glacier National Park gives everyday life a sense of grandeur that is hard to replicate. Both are excellent Montana communities; the question is whether you want the Queen City's history and value, or Stumptown's scenery and resort-town energy.

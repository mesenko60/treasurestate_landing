# Havre vs Hardin, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Havre and Hardin offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Havre is a historic railroad and agricultural hub on the Hi-Line with 9,362 people, its deep roots in the railroad industry, the Havre Beneath the Streets underground tour, and quick access to the nearby Bear Paw Mountains and Fresno Reservoir. Hardin, with 3,742 residents, is a agricultural community near the Crow Reservation known for its proximity to the Little Bighorn Battlefield, its farming and ranching economy, and the Bighorn River and Yellowtail Dam.

## Who Should Move to Havre?

* Workers in agriculture, education, or the railroad industry looking for steady employment.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a community with all essential amenities.
* Those who appreciate the stark beauty of the northern plains and the close-knit feel of a Hi-Line town.
* History buffs interested in the unique underground history and nearby frontier battlefields.

## Who Should Move to Hardin?

* Agricultural workers and farmers involved in the sugar beet and wheat industries of the Bighorn Valley.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, rural setting.
* History enthusiasts interested in the rich Native American culture and the legacy of the Little Bighorn.
* Anglers who want to be near the world-famous trout fishing below Yellowtail Dam.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Havre is around $224,252, while in Hardin it is closer to $204,573, a difference of roughly $19,679, making Hardin significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Havre averages about $925 per month, compared to roughly $726 in Hardin.

Havre's overall cost of living differs from Hardin's. Havre offers lower average salaries compared to Hardin, with a median household income of $58,000 compared to Hardin's $60,463.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Havre is a resilient, hardworking community that serves as the commercial center for a vast stretch of northern Montana. It offers a practical, unpretentious lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture and the railroad. Hardin is a quiet, culturally diverse agricultural center where the plains meet the Bighorn River. It is a town defined by farming, ranching, and its location bordering the Crow Indian Reservation, offering a slow-paced, rural lifestyle far from the state's major urban centers.

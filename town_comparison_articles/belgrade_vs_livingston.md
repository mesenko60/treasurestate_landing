# Belgrade vs Livingston: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Belgrade and Livingston are both central Montana towns within the Gallatin and Yellowstone corridors, but they attract very different residents. Belgrade is the Hub of the Gallatin Valley, a fast-growing community of 12,509 with newer housing, a strong professional economy, and 104 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles. Livingston is the Windiest City in Montana, a community of 8,040 on the Yellowstone River, known for its historic railroad depot, a thriving arts community, 144 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, and a literary and creative culture that has drawn writers and artists for decades.

## Who Should Move to Belgrade?

*   Families and professionals who want Gallatin Valley access with newer housing, strong schools, and a median household income of $88,896
*   Remote workers who want a fast-growing community with five-minute access to Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport
*   People who want a practical, suburban community with a young demographic and strong school enrollment of about 3,800 students
*   Anyone drawn to the Gallatin Valley's economic energy and outdoor access

## Who Should Move to Livingston?

*   Artists, writers, and creative professionals drawn to Livingston's well-established arts community and literary heritage
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 24 fishing access sites on the Yellowstone River, 16 campgrounds, 12 trailheads, and 40 lakes within 30 miles
*   People who want a historic, character-rich small town with a lower cost of living, with a typical home value around $509,477 and rent averaging about $1,783 per month
*   Anyone who wants a small Montana city with a genuine sense of place and easy access to Yellowstone country

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Belgrade is the more expensive market. The typical home value in Belgrade is around $544,626, compared to $509,477 in Livingston, a difference of about $35,000. Rent diverges more noticeably: Belgrade averages about $2,206 per month, while Livingston comes in at roughly $1,783, making Livingston a more affordable rental market.

The income gap is significant. The median household income in Belgrade is $88,896, compared to $65,187 in Livingston. Belgrade's Gallatin Valley economy drives higher wages, but Livingston's lower costs and smaller-town character make it a compelling option for people whose work is not tied to a specific employer.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Belgrade is a town in motion, growing fast with new subdivisions and a demographic that skews young and professional. Livingston moves at a more deliberate pace, a town where the wind comes howling down from the Absarokas, the Yellowstone River runs cold and clear through the valley, and the historic downtown has a creative energy that feels earned rather than manufactured. If you want the Gallatin Valley's economic strength and a fast-growing community, Belgrade is the right fit. If you want a smaller, more characterful Montana town with a river, an arts scene, and a lower price of entry, Livingston is one of the most underrated communities in the state.

# Laurel vs Livingston: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Laurel and Livingston are two small Montana communities on the Yellowstone River corridor, similar in population but quite different in character and cost. Laurel is the City Between the Yellowstone and the Rimrocks, a community of 7,215 in Yellowstone County with affordable housing, 21 fishing access sites within 30 miles, and a quiet, working-class character just outside Billings. Livingston is the Windiest City in Montana, a community of 8,040 in Park County on the Yellowstone River, with 144 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, a well-established arts and literary community, and a historic downtown that has drawn writers, artists, and fly fishers for decades.

## Who Should Move to Laurel?

*   Budget-conscious families and buyers who want Yellowstone County living with a typical home value around $369,826, well below Livingston's $509,477
*   People who want a quiet, practical small-town community with Billings' full-service amenities just 18 miles away
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 21 fishing access sites and 6 state parks along the Yellowstone River corridor
*   Anyone who wants a warmer, drier climate, with July highs averaging 88°F versus Livingston's 82°F and annual snowfall of 15 inches versus Livingston's 25.7 inches

## Who Should Move to Livingston?

*   Artists, writers, and creative professionals drawn to Livingston's well-established arts community and literary heritage
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 24 fishing access sites on the Yellowstone River, 16 campgrounds, 12 trailheads, and 40 lakes within 30 miles
*   People who want a historic, character-rich small town with a strong sense of place and easy access to Yellowstone country
*   Remote workers and retirees who want a smaller, more characterful Montana community than Billings but with more personality than a purely agricultural town

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Laurel is the more affordable option. The typical home value in Laurel is around $369,826, compared to $509,477 in Livingston, a gap of nearly $140,000. Rent diverges similarly: Laurel averages about $1,160 per month, while Livingston comes in at roughly $1,783, a difference of over $600 per month.

The income figures are nearly identical. The median household income in Laurel is $66,382, compared to $65,187 in Livingston. Both towns have similar earning profiles, but Laurel's lower housing costs give buyers a meaningful advantage.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Laurel is a practical, unpretentious river town where the Yellowstone is close and Billings is always nearby. Livingston moves at a more deliberate pace, a town where the wind howls down from the Absarokas, the Yellowstone runs cold and clear through the valley, and the historic downtown has a creative energy that feels earned rather than manufactured. Both towns sit on the same river, but they attract very different people. If you want affordable, practical small-town life near Billings, Laurel is the right fit. If you want a smaller, more characterful Montana community with a river, an arts scene, and a gateway to Yellowstone country, Livingston is one of the most underrated towns in the state.

# Glasgow vs Sidney, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Glasgow and Sidney offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Glasgow is a historic railroad town on the Hi-Line with 3,224 people, its position as a regional hub for northeastern Montana, its proximity to Fort Peck Dam, and quick access to Fort Peck Lake and the Charles M. Russell National Wildlife Refuge. Sidney, with just 6,173 residents, is a bustling agricultural and energy hub known for its position in the Bakken oil field, its rich farming heritage in the Yellowstone Valley, and the Yellowstone River and eastern Montana badlands.

## Who Should Move to Glasgow?

* Workers in agriculture, government, or the railroad industry looking for stable employment in northeastern Montana.
* Anglers and boaters who want to live near Fort Peck Lake, one of the premier walleye fisheries in the country.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, isolated community.
* Hunters and outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the vast, uncrowded public lands of the Missouri River Breaks.

## Who Should Move to Sidney?

* Workers in the energy, agriculture, or transportation sectors looking for strong employment opportunities.
* People seeking a hardworking, practical community with a resilient local economy.
* Those who want affordable housing compared to western Montana, with higher average wages driven by the oil and gas industry.
* Hunters and anglers who appreciate the rugged, uncrowded access to the Yellowstone River and surrounding plains.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Glasgow is around $195,000, while in Sidney it is closer to $241,500, a difference of roughly $46,500, making Glasgow significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Glasgow averages about $750 per month, compared to roughly $850 in Sidney.

Glasgow's overall cost of living differs from Sidney's. Glasgow offers lower average salaries compared to Sidney, with a median household income of $55,000 compared to Sidney's $75,200.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Glasgow is a resilient, isolated community that serves as the commercial and social center for a massive swath of northeastern Montana. It offers a quiet, slow-paced lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture, the railroad, and the incredible recreational opportunities at nearby Fort Peck Lake. Sidney is a dynamic, industrious town where the pace of life is closely tied to the energy markets and agricultural seasons. It has a practical, no-nonsense atmosphere, serving as a major economic engine for eastern Montana while maintaining its roots as a tight-knit farming community.

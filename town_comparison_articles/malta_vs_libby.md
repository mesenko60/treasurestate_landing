# Malta vs Libby, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Malta and Libby offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Malta is a historic agricultural town on the Hi-Line with 2,100 people, its world-class dinosaur fossils, its deep ranching heritage, and quick access to the Bowdoin National Wildlife Refuge and the Missouri River Breaks. Libby, with 2,775 residents, is a remote timber town in the Kootenai National Forest known for its deep roots in the logging industry, its stunning, rugged isolation, and the Kootenai River and Cabinet Mountains.

## Who Should Move to Malta?

* Workers in agriculture, government, or local services looking for a quiet, stable community.
* People seeking very affordable housing and a low overall cost of living.
* Hunters, birders, and outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the vast, uncrowded public lands of northeastern Montana.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle on the high plains.

## Who Should Move to Libby?

* Outdoor enthusiasts seeking uncrowded access to some of Montana's most rugged and remote wilderness.
* Retirees and remote workers looking for affordable mountain living far from major tourist hubs.
* People who appreciate a tight-knit, resilient community with a strong working-class heritage.
* Hunters, anglers, and hikers who want to live in the heart of the Kootenai National Forest.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Malta is around $244,540, while in Libby it is closer to $285,000, a difference of roughly $40,460, making Malta significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Malta averages about $693 per month, compared to roughly $850 in Libby.

Malta's overall cost of living differs from Libby's. Malta offers higher average salaries compared to Libby, with a median household income of $59,449 compared to Libby's $45,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Malta is a quiet, resilient community on the eastern Hi-Line, defined by agriculture, wide-open spaces, and its unique paleontology attractions. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle far from the state's urban centers. Libby is a resilient, isolated town tucked into the far northwest corner of the state. It is defined by its timber heritage and its incredible, uncrowded access to the Kootenai River and Cabinet Mountains, offering a quiet, rugged lifestyle.

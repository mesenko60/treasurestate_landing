# Choteau vs Bigfork, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Choteau and Bigfork offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Choteau is a scenic gateway town to the Rocky Mountain Front with 1,721 people, its spectacular views of the Front, its rich dinosaur paleontology history, and quick access to the Bob Marshall Wilderness and Freezout Lake. Bigfork, with 5,000 residents, is a picturesque village on Flathead Lake known for its thriving arts scene, its cherry orchards, and Flathead Lake and the Swan River.

## Who Should Move to Choteau?

* Outdoor enthusiasts who want uncrowded access to the Bob Marshall Wilderness.
* Birders and wildlife watchers drawn to the spectacular migrations at nearby Freezout Lake.
* People seeking a quiet, affordable community with some of the best mountain views in the state.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle.

## Who Should Move to Bigfork?

* Retirees and affluent professionals looking for a scenic, upscale lakeside community.
* Boaters, sailors, and water sports enthusiasts who want to live on the largest natural freshwater lake in the West.
* Art lovers who appreciate the town's galleries, summer playhouse, and cultural events.
* People who desire a resort-like atmosphere with high-end dining and boutique shopping.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Choteau is around $288,323, while in Bigfork it is closer to $758,435, a difference of roughly $470,112, making Choteau significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Choteau averages about $713 per month, compared to roughly $1,103 in Bigfork.

Choteau's overall cost of living differs from Bigfork's. Choteau offers lower average salaries compared to Bigfork, with a median household income of $53,929 compared to Bigfork's $82,648.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Choteau is a quiet, stunningly located town that serves as the Front Porch of the Rockies. It offers a slow-paced, affordable lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture, paleontology, and the incredible wilderness right in its backyard. Bigfork feels like a curated resort village, where life centers around the water, the arts, and seasonal tourism. The downtown is polished and walkable, filled with galleries and cafes, offering a lifestyle that is significantly more upscale and leisure-focused than most Montana towns.

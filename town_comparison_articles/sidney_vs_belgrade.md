# Sidney vs Belgrade, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Sidney and Belgrade offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Sidney is a bustling agricultural and energy hub with 6,173 people, its position in the Bakken oil field, its rich farming heritage in the Yellowstone Valley, and quick access to the Yellowstone River and eastern Montana badlands. Belgrade, with just 12,509 residents, is a rapidly growing hub in the Gallatin Valley known for proximity to Bozeman, the Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport, and the Bridger Mountains and Gallatin River.

## Who Should Move to Sidney?

* Workers in the energy, agriculture, or transportation sectors looking for strong employment opportunities.
* People seeking a hardworking, practical community with a resilient local economy.
* Those who want affordable housing compared to western Montana, with higher average wages driven by the oil and gas industry.
* Hunters and anglers who appreciate the rugged, uncrowded access to the Yellowstone River and surrounding plains.

## Who Should Move to Belgrade?

* Families and professionals who work in Bozeman but want slightly more affordable housing options.
* Frequent travelers who appreciate being minutes away from Montana's busiest airport.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want quick access to the Bridger Mountains, Big Sky, and blue-ribbon trout streams.
* People looking for a community with a strong local identity that still benefits from the economic engine of the Gallatin Valley.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Sidney is around $241,500, while in Belgrade it is closer to $544,626, a difference of roughly $303,126, making Sidney significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Sidney averages about $850 per month, compared to roughly $2,206 in Belgrade.

Sidney's overall cost of living differs from Belgrade's. Sidney offers lower average salaries compared to Belgrade, with a median household income of $75,200 compared to Belgrade's $88,896.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Sidney is a dynamic, industrious town where the pace of life is closely tied to the energy markets and agricultural seasons. It has a practical, no-nonsense atmosphere, serving as a major economic engine for eastern Montana while maintaining its roots as a tight-knit farming community. Belgrade feels like a busy, practical town that is absorbing much of the Gallatin Valley's growth. It has its own distinct community pride, centered around local schools and events, but its daily rhythm is closely tied to the economic hum of neighboring Bozeman and the constant activity at the airport.

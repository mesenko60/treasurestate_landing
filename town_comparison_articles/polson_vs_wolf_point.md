# Polson vs Wolf Point, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Polson and Wolf Point offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Polson is a scenic lakeside community with 5,335 people, its location on the southern shore of Flathead Lake, its cherry orchards, and quick access to Flathead Lake and the Mission Mountains. Wolf Point, with just 2,517 residents, is a historic agricultural town on the Fort Peck Indian Reservation known for its position as the largest community on the reservation, its deep roots in farming and ranching, and the Missouri River and the vast eastern plains.

## Who Should Move to Polson?

* Retirees and remote workers seeking a beautiful lakeside setting with a slower pace of life.
* Boaters, anglers, and water sports enthusiasts who want immediate access to the largest natural freshwater lake in the West.
* People looking for a more affordable alternative to the resort towns of the northern Flathead Valley.
* Those who appreciate a community with a strong agricultural heritage, particularly its famous cherry harvest.

## Who Should Move to Wolf Point?

* People seeking some of the most affordable housing in the state.
* Workers in agriculture, tribal government, or local services.
* Those who appreciate a quiet, isolated lifestyle on the high plains.
* Hunters and anglers who want uncrowded access to the Missouri River and surrounding lands.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Polson is around $415,000, while in Wolf Point it is closer to $145,000, a difference of roughly $270,000, making Wolf Point significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Polson averages about $1,050 per month, compared to roughly $600 in Wolf Point.

Polson's overall cost of living differs from Wolf Point's. Polson offers higher average salaries compared to Wolf Point, with a median household income of $52,500 compared to Wolf Point's $42,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Polson offers a relaxed, scenic lifestyle defined by its position on Flathead Lake. It lacks the polished resort feel of towns further north, maintaining a more grounded, agricultural character while still providing spectacular water and mountain access. Wolf Point is a quiet, resilient community deeply tied to the Fort Peck Indian Reservation and the agricultural economy of northeastern Montana. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle far removed from the state's major urban centers.

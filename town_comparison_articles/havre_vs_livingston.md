# Havre vs Livingston, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Havre and Livingston offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Havre is a historic railroad and agricultural hub on the Hi-Line with 9,362 people, its deep roots in the railroad industry, the Havre Beneath the Streets underground tour, and quick access to the nearby Bear Paw Mountains and Fresno Reservoir. Livingston, with 8,386 residents, is a historic railroad and arts town known for its literary heritage, its vibrant arts scene, and the Yellowstone River and Paradise Valley.

## Who Should Move to Havre?

* Workers in agriculture, education, or the railroad industry looking for steady employment.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a community with all essential amenities.
* Those who appreciate the stark beauty of the northern plains and the close-knit feel of a Hi-Line town.
* History buffs interested in the unique underground history and nearby frontier battlefields.

## Who Should Move to Livingston?

* Artists, writers, and creative professionals.
* Fly fishing enthusiasts who want to live on the Yellowstone River.
* People who want a small-town atmosphere with easy access to Bozeman.
* Those who appreciate historic downtown architecture.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Havre is around $224,252, while in Livingston it is closer to $509,477, a difference of roughly $285,225, making Havre significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Havre averages about $925 per month, compared to roughly $1,783 in Livingston.

Havre's overall cost of living differs from Livingston's. Havre offers lower average salaries compared to Livingston, with a median household income of $58,000 compared to Livingston's $65,187.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Havre is a resilient, hardworking community that serves as the commercial center for a vast stretch of northern Montana. It offers a practical, unpretentious lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture and the railroad. Livingston is a windy, eclectic town that blends its gritty railroad past with a sophisticated arts community, serving as the scenic gateway to Yellowstone.

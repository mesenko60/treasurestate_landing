# Big Sky vs Great Falls: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Big Sky and Great Falls represent two very different visions of Montana life. Big Sky is the Biggest Skiing in America, a resort community of 3,591 in Gallatin County at 6,319 feet, known for Big Sky Resort, 23 trailheads, 17 waterfalls, 18 lakes, 3 hot springs, 2 wilderness areas, and 140 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles. Great Falls is the Electric City, a central Montana city of 60,000 in Cascade County at 3,340 feet, with 45 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 10 museums, 8 fishing access sites, 6 waterfalls, 5 state parks, 4 golf courses, 2 rivers, 2 viewpoints, a wildlife refuge, Great Falls International Airport 5 miles away, and a 10,000-student school district.

## Who Should Move to Big Sky?

*   Skiers, snowboarders, and mountain sports enthusiasts who want Big Sky Resort, one of the largest ski areas in North America with over 5,800 acres of skiable terrain, right in their backyard
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 23 trailheads, 17 waterfalls, 18 lakes, 19 fishing access sites, 3 hot springs, 2 wilderness areas, and a national forest all within 30 miles
*   High-income remote workers and affluent buyers who can afford Big Sky's extraordinary real estate market, with a typical home value of $1,819,905 and a median household income of $103,625
*   People who want a small, exclusive resort community lifestyle where outdoor recreation is the primary focus and the mountains define every aspect of daily life

## Who Should Move to Great Falls?

*   Military families and professionals connected to Malmstrom Air Force Base, one of the primary economic drivers of Great Falls and a major employer in the region
*   History and culture enthusiasts who want 10 museums, including the C.M. Russell Museum dedicated to one of Montana's most celebrated Western artists, and 6 waterfalls along the Missouri River
*   Families and professionals who want an affordable central Montana city with Great Falls International Airport 5 miles away, a 10,000-student school district, 5 state parks, and a median household income of $63,934
*   Anyone who wants a genuine Montana city lifestyle at a very accessible price, with a typical home value of $328,554 and rent averaging $1,340 per month

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Big Sky is dramatically more expensive than Great Falls. The typical home value in Big Sky is $1,819,905, compared to $328,554 in Great Falls, more than five times higher. Rent is closer: Big Sky averages $1,712 per month, while Great Falls comes in at $1,340, a more modest difference.

Median household income is significantly higher in Big Sky at $103,625 versus $63,934 in Great Falls. Great Falls is one of the more affordable cities in Montana, making it accessible to a wide range of buyers and renters. Big Sky's extraordinary home prices reflect its status as one of the premier ski resort communities in North America.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Big Sky is a world-class mountain resort community where the ski mountain, the wilderness, and a high-income outdoor lifestyle define daily life. Great Falls is a central Montana city where the Missouri River, Malmstrom Air Force Base, the C.M. Russell Museum, and a strong agricultural and military economy define community identity. Great Falls has a genuine city character with museums, parks, and a walkable downtown along the Missouri River, while Big Sky is a mountain enclave with extraordinary outdoor access but almost no urban amenities. If you want a resort lifestyle in the mountains with world-class skiing and wilderness access, Big Sky is extraordinary. If you want an affordable central Montana city with a strong cultural identity, excellent museum access, and a full-service community, Great Falls is a solid and underappreciated choice.

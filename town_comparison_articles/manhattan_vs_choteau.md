# Manhattan vs Choteau, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Manhattan and Choteau offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Manhattan is a rapidly growing agricultural town in the Gallatin Valley with 2,288 people, its historic role as the Seed Potato Capital of the World, its booming residential growth, and quick access to the Gallatin River and the Bridger Mountains. Choteau, with 1,721 residents, is a scenic gateway town to the Rocky Mountain Front known for its spectacular views of the Front, its rich dinosaur paleontology history, and the Bob Marshall Wilderness and Freezout Lake.

## Who Should Move to Manhattan?

* Professionals working in Bozeman who want a smaller-town feel and are willing to commute.
* Families looking for excellent local schools and a safe, tight-knit community.
* People who appreciate a town that still honors its agricultural heritage despite rapid modernization.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want immediate access to the world-class fishing of the Gallatin River.

## Who Should Move to Choteau?

* Outdoor enthusiasts who want uncrowded access to the Bob Marshall Wilderness.
* Birders and wildlife watchers drawn to the spectacular migrations at nearby Freezout Lake.
* People seeking a quiet, affordable community with some of the best mountain views in the state.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Manhattan is around $652,619, while in Choteau it is closer to $288,323, a difference of roughly $364,296, making Choteau significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Manhattan averages about $1,700 per month, compared to roughly $713 in Choteau.

Manhattan's overall cost of living differs from Choteau's. Manhattan offers higher average salaries compared to Choteau, with a median household income of $69,671 compared to Choteau's $53,929.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Manhattan blends its deep agricultural roots with its new reality as a highly desirable, expensive suburb of Bozeman. It maintains a charming, historic downtown and a strong community identity, even as it experiences significant growth and rising costs. Choteau is a quiet, stunningly located town that serves as the Front Porch of the Rockies. It offers a slow-paced, affordable lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture, paleontology, and the incredible wilderness right in its backyard.

# Lewistown vs Missoula: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Lewistown and Missoula offer two of the most contrasting lifestyles in Montana. Lewistown is the Geographic Center of Montana, a quiet ranching community of 6,204 in Fergus County with affordable housing, 17 campgrounds within 30 miles, and wide-open central Montana skies. Missoula is the Garden City, a university city of 77,757 on the Clark Fork River in western Montana, with 211 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 49 trailheads, 65 fishing access sites, a nationally recognized arts and music scene, and the energy of a place anchored by the University of Montana.

## Who Should Move to Lewistown?

*   Retirees and remote workers who want a quiet, affordable small-town lifestyle with a typical home value around $268,126 and rent averaging just $896 per month
*   People who want a traditional, tight-knit ranching community with a genuine small-town character
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 17 campgrounds, 7 fishing access sites, and the Big Snowy Mountains within 30 miles
*   Anyone who wants to live at the geographic heart of Montana, far from resort-corridor prices and crowds

## Who Should Move to Missoula?

*   Young professionals, students, and families who want a vibrant university city with a strong job market and a median household income of $65,329
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 49 trailheads, 65 fishing access sites, 22 campgrounds, and 2 wilderness areas all within 30 miles
*   People who want a lively downtown with craft breweries, live music, a farmers market, and a cultural calendar driven by the University of Montana
*   Anyone who wants western Montana's mountain scenery with the services and energy of a real city

## Cost of Living Breakdown

The gap between these two towns is substantial. The typical home value in Lewistown is around $268,126, compared to $554,389 in Missoula, more than double the price. Rent follows the same pattern: Lewistown averages about $896 per month, while Missoula comes in at roughly $1,486, nearly 66% more.

The income gap is significant. The median household income in Missoula is $65,329, compared to $44,195 in Lewistown. Missoula residents earn considerably more, driven in part by the university and a broader professional economy, but the housing premium is real and meaningful.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Lewistown is a town where the ranching calendar still shapes community life, the downtown has a quiet, historic character, and the nearest major city is over 100 miles away. Missoula is one of Montana's most dynamic communities, a place where the Clark Fork runs through downtown, the university brings a constant influx of ideas and energy, and the outdoor recreation is as good as anywhere in the state. If you want affordable, quiet, traditional Montana, Lewistown is a genuine option. If you want a university city with a mountain backdrop and one of the best outdoor recreation footprints in the country, Missoula is hard to beat.

# Havre vs Wolf Point, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Havre and Wolf Point offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Havre is a historic railroad and agricultural hub on the Hi-Line with 9,362 people, its deep roots in the railroad industry, the Havre Beneath the Streets underground tour, and quick access to the nearby Bear Paw Mountains and Fresno Reservoir. Wolf Point, with 2,517 residents, is a historic agricultural town on the Fort Peck Indian Reservation known for its position as the largest community on the reservation, its deep roots in farming and ranching, and the Missouri River and the vast eastern plains.

## Who Should Move to Havre?

* Workers in agriculture, education, or the railroad industry looking for steady employment.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a community with all essential amenities.
* Those who appreciate the stark beauty of the northern plains and the close-knit feel of a Hi-Line town.
* History buffs interested in the unique underground history and nearby frontier battlefields.

## Who Should Move to Wolf Point?

* People seeking some of the most affordable housing in the state.
* Workers in agriculture, tribal government, or local services.
* Those who appreciate a quiet, isolated lifestyle on the high plains.
* Hunters and anglers who want uncrowded access to the Missouri River and surrounding lands.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Havre is around $224,252, while in Wolf Point it is closer to $145,000, a difference of roughly $79,252, making Wolf Point significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Havre averages about $925 per month, compared to roughly $600 in Wolf Point.

Havre's overall cost of living differs from Wolf Point's. Havre offers higher average salaries compared to Wolf Point, with a median household income of $58,000 compared to Wolf Point's $42,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Havre is a resilient, hardworking community that serves as the commercial center for a vast stretch of northern Montana. It offers a practical, unpretentious lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture and the railroad. Wolf Point is a quiet, resilient community deeply tied to the Fort Peck Indian Reservation and the agricultural economy of northeastern Montana. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle far removed from the state's major urban centers.

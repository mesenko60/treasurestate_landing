# Hardin vs Great Falls: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Hardin and Great Falls are both eastern and central Montana communities with a similar climate and a strong connection to Montana's agricultural heritage, but they differ significantly in size and what they offer. Hardin is Where the Rivers Meet, a small Big Horn County community of 3,742 at 2,910 feet in eastern Montana, known for its proximity to the Little Bighorn Battlefield, the Bighorn River, 10 fishing access sites, 4 historic sites, and a quiet agricultural lifestyle. Great Falls is the Electric City, a central Montana community of 60,000 in Cascade County at 3,340 feet along the Missouri River, with 45 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 10 museums, 8 fishing access sites, 6 waterfalls, 5 state parks, 4 golf courses, 2 rivers, 2 viewpoints, a boat launch, a disc golf course, a national forest, a nature reserve, a wildlife refuge, Great Falls International Airport 5 miles away, and a 10,000-student school district.

## Who Should Move to Hardin?

*   History enthusiasts drawn to the Little Bighorn Battlefield National Monument 14 miles away, one of the most significant and evocative historic sites in the American West
*   Anglers who want 10 fishing access sites and the Bighorn River, one of Montana's premier blue-ribbon trout streams, within easy reach
*   Remote workers, retirees, and families who want a very affordable small-town lifestyle in eastern Montana, with a typical home value of just $204,573 and rent averaging $726 per month
*   People who want a quiet, traditional eastern Montana community with a strong Native American cultural heritage and a slower pace of life

## Who Should Move to Great Falls?

*   Professionals and families who want a mid-sized Montana city with Great Falls International Airport 5 miles away, a 10,000-student school district, and a strong military and healthcare economy anchored by Malmstrom Air Force Base
*   History and culture enthusiasts who want 10 museums, including the C.M. Russell Museum, one of the finest Western art museums in the country, plus 6 waterfalls along the Missouri River, 5 state parks, and 2 historic sites
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 8 fishing access sites, 5 state parks, 4 golf courses, 2 rivers, a national forest, and a wildlife refuge all within 30 miles
*   Budget-conscious buyers who want a mid-sized Montana city at a reasonable price, with a typical home value of $328,554 and a median household income of $63,934

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Great Falls is considerably more expensive than Hardin. The typical home value in Hardin is around $204,573, compared to $328,554 in Great Falls, about 61% higher. Rent is also substantially higher in Great Falls: Hardin averages about $726 per month, while Great Falls comes in at roughly $1,340, about 85% more.

Median household income is slightly higher in Great Falls at $63,934 versus $60,463 in Hardin. Great Falls offers more services and a larger job market, but Hardin remains significantly more affordable for both buyers and renters.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Hardin is a small eastern Montana town where the Bighorn River, the Little Bighorn Battlefield, and a quiet agricultural and ranching community define daily life. Great Falls is a central Montana city where the Missouri River, the C.M. Russell Museum, and a strong military and healthcare economy define daily life. Great Falls has a density of services, culture, and outdoor access that Hardin does not, with a real airport, 10 museums, and a community that is large enough to offer genuine urban amenities while remaining deeply rooted in Montana's agricultural and military heritage. If you want a very affordable small-town lifestyle with access to one of America's great historic sites and excellent trout fishing, Hardin is a compelling choice. If you want a mid-sized Montana city with a strong economy, a world-class Western art museum, and the Missouri River running through town, Great Falls is one of Montana's most underappreciated communities.

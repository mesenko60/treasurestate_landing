# Miles City vs Great Falls, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Miles City and Great Falls are both working Montana cities, but they serve very different roles in the state's landscape. Great Falls, the Electric City, is a regional hub of 60,000 on the Missouri River in central Montana, home to Malmstrom Air Force Base, a strong arts scene anchored by the C.M. Russell Museum, and a broad range of services. Miles City, the Cow Capital of Montana, is a proud ranching town of 8,412 on the eastern plains, where the livestock industry and frontier heritage shape daily life.

## Who Should Move to Great Falls?

Military families and veterans connected to Malmstrom Air Force Base. People who want a mid-sized city with a full range of services, hospitals, and retail options. Art lovers and history enthusiasts drawn to the C.M. Russell Museum and Great Falls' rich Lewis and Clark heritage. Those who want central Montana's wide-open feel with more urban infrastructure than a small town can offer.

## Who Should Move to Miles City?

Ranchers, rodeo culture enthusiasts, and people deeply connected to the working-livestock world of Eastern Montana. Those seeking very affordable housing and a genuinely low cost of living. Remote workers or retirees who want a quiet, uncrowded community with strong small-town character. History buffs drawn to the Range Riders Museum and the frontier cowboy legacy of the Yellowstone Valley.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Both towns are affordable by Montana standards, but Miles City runs lower. The typical home in Great Falls costs $328,554 compared to $233,769 in Miles City, a gap of roughly $95,000. Monthly rent averages $1,340 in Great Falls versus $750 in Miles City. Median household incomes are close: $63,934 in Great Falls and $60,732 in Miles City. For buyers on a tight budget, Miles City offers some of the most affordable housing in the state.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Great Falls has the feel of a working regional city: it has traffic, a hospital system, a performing arts center, and the steady hum of a military-influenced community. Miles City is quieter and more rural in character, with a downtown that retains genuine historic charm and a social calendar anchored by the legendary Bucking Horse Sale each May. Great Falls wins on services and amenities. Miles City wins on affordability, authenticity, and the kind of wide-open Eastern Montana atmosphere that is increasingly hard to find.

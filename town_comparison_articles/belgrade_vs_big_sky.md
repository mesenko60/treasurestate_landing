# Belgrade vs Big Sky: Which Montana Town is Right for You?

Belgrade and Big Sky are both in Gallatin County, but they represent two completely different Montana lifestyles at two completely different price points. Belgrade is the Hub of the Gallatin Valley, a fast-growing suburb of 12,509 at 4,462 feet, with Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport 5 miles away, 104 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 16 trailheads, 14 waterfalls, 15 lakes, 21 fishing access sites, 2 hot springs, a national forest, a ski area, and a 3,800-student school district. Big Sky is the Biggest Skiing in America, a resort community of 3,591 at 6,319 feet in the Gallatin Canyon, with Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport 44 miles away, 140 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 28 campgrounds, 23 trailheads, 19 fishing access sites, 18 lakes, 17 waterfalls, 7 golf courses, 7 nature reserves, 3 hot springs, 3 boat launches, 2 rivers, 2 wilderness areas, 1 ski area, and a 300-student school district.

## Who Should Move to Belgrade?

*   Families and professionals who want Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport 5 miles away, a 3,800-student school district, and all the amenities of the Bozeman metro area at a fraction of Big Sky prices
*   Buyers who want strong job access in the Gallatin Valley with a typical home value of $544,626 and a median household income of $88,896, without the extreme real estate costs of a resort community
*   Remote workers and young professionals who want a suburban Gallatin Valley lifestyle with proximity to Bozeman's restaurants, university, and cultural amenities
*   Families who need a full-service school district with 3,800 students, robust community infrastructure, and easy access to Bozeman's hospitals, shopping, and services

## Who Should Move to Big Sky?

*   Skiers and snowboarders who want Big Sky Resort, one of the largest ski areas in North America with over 5,800 acres of terrain, literally at their doorstep, along with 56.9 inches of annual snowfall
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 28 campgrounds, 23 trailheads, 19 fishing access sites, 18 lakes, 17 waterfalls, 7 nature reserves, 3 hot springs, and 2 wilderness areas all within 30 miles in the Gallatin Canyon
*   Affluent buyers and resort investors who want a high-end mountain community with a typical home value of $1,819,905 and a median household income of $103,625, in one of the most spectacular mountain settings in the American West
*   Remote workers and retirees who want a small, exclusive resort community with a 300-student school district, 7 golf courses, and Yellowstone National Park just 50 miles south

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Big Sky is by far the most expensive community in Montana. The typical home value in Big Sky is $1,819,905, compared to $544,626 in Belgrade, more than three times higher. Rent is actually lower in Big Sky at $1,712 per month versus $2,206 in Belgrade, reflecting Big Sky's resort-dominated rental market where many properties are short-term vacation rentals rather than long-term housing.

Median household income is higher in Big Sky at $103,625 versus $88,896 in Belgrade. Both communities are relatively affluent, but Big Sky's extreme home prices put ownership out of reach for most buyers who are not already wealthy or investing in resort real estate.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Belgrade feels like a prosperous, growing suburb of Bozeman, with new subdivisions, strong outdoor access, and a community identity tied closely to the Gallatin Valley's outdoor and university culture. Big Sky is a world-class mountain resort community where skiing, the Gallatin River, and a high-end outdoor lifestyle define everything. Big Sky has 23 trailheads, 17 waterfalls, 3 hot springs, and 2 wilderness areas within 30 miles that exceed Belgrade's counts, plus Big Sky Resort itself. Belgrade has 4 state parks and 8 museums that Big Sky does not, along with a full-service suburban community infrastructure that Big Sky cannot match. If you want an affordable, well-connected Gallatin Valley community with outstanding mountain access and a suburban feel, Belgrade is one of the best values in the Bozeman area. If you want a world-class ski resort community in one of the most dramatic mountain settings in North America and price is not the primary concern, Big Sky is in a category of its own.

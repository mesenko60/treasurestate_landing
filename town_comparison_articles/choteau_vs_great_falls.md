# Choteau vs Great Falls, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Choteau and Great Falls offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Choteau is a scenic gateway town to the Rocky Mountain Front with 1,721 people, its spectacular views of the Front, its rich dinosaur paleontology history, and quick access to the Bob Marshall Wilderness and Freezout Lake. Great Falls, with 60,403 residents, is a historic river city known for its military base, its rich Lewis and Clark history, and the Missouri River and Giant Springs.

## Who Should Move to Choteau?

* Outdoor enthusiasts who want uncrowded access to the Bob Marshall Wilderness.
* Birders and wildlife watchers drawn to the spectacular migrations at nearby Freezout Lake.
* People seeking a quiet, affordable community with some of the best mountain views in the state.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle.

## Who Should Move to Great Falls?

* Military families stationed at Malmstrom AFB.
* People seeking affordable urban living in Montana.
* History buffs interested in the Lewis and Clark expedition.
* Those who enjoy river recreation right in town.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Choteau is around $288,323, while in Great Falls it is closer to $285,000, a difference of roughly $3,323, making Great Falls significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Choteau averages about $713 per month, compared to roughly $950 in Great Falls.

Choteau's overall cost of living differs from Great Falls's. Choteau offers lower average salaries compared to Great Falls, with a median household income of $53,929 compared to Great Falls's $55,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Choteau is a quiet, stunningly located town that serves as the Front Porch of the Rockies. It offers a slow-paced, affordable lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture, paleontology, and the incredible wilderness right in its backyard. Great Falls is a grounded, affordable city with a strong military presence and a deep connection to the Missouri River.

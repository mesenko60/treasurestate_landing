# Malta vs Hamilton, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Malta and Hamilton offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Malta is a historic agricultural town on the Hi-Line with 2,100 people, its world-class dinosaur fossils, its deep ranching heritage, and quick access to the Bowdoin National Wildlife Refuge and the Missouri River Breaks. Hamilton, with 4,659 residents, is a scenic hub of the Bitterroot Valley known for its historic downtown, its relatively mild climate, and the Bitterroot Mountains and the Bitterroot River.

## Who Should Move to Malta?

* Workers in agriculture, government, or local services looking for a quiet, stable community.
* People seeking very affordable housing and a low overall cost of living.
* Hunters, birders, and outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the vast, uncrowded public lands of northeastern Montana.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle on the high plains.

## Who Should Move to Hamilton?

* Retirees and remote workers drawn to the stunning scenery and milder weather of the 'Banana Belt'.
* Fly fishing enthusiasts who want to live near the renowned waters of the Bitterroot River.
* People seeking a vibrant small-town community with a historic main street, local breweries, and community events.
* Outdoor adventurers who want immediate access to the rugged canyons and trails of the Bitterroot Selway Wilderness.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Malta is around $244,540, while in Hamilton it is closer to $516,033, a difference of roughly $271,493, making Malta significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Malta averages about $693 per month, compared to roughly $958 in Hamilton.

Malta's overall cost of living differs from Hamilton's. Malta offers higher average salaries compared to Hamilton, with a median household income of $59,449 compared to Hamilton's $52,917.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Malta is a quiet, resilient community on the eastern Hi-Line, defined by agriculture, wide-open spaces, and its unique paleontology attractions. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle far from the state's urban centers. Hamilton offers a highly desirable blend of spectacular mountain scenery and a charming, active community. The pace is relaxed but the town is lively, with a downtown that serves as a gathering place for locals and visitors alike, all set against the towering peaks of the Bitterroot Range.

# Cut Bank vs Miles City, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Cut Bank and Miles City offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Cut Bank is a windy agricultural town on the Hi-Line with 3,056 people, its proximity to the Blackfeet Indian Reservation, its agricultural and energy economy, and quick access to the eastern edge of Glacier National Park. Miles City, with just 8,412 residents, is a ranching community on the eastern plains known for the livestock industry, rodeo culture, and the wide-open Yellowstone River country.

## Who Should Move to Cut Bank?

* Workers in agriculture, energy, or government looking for affordable living on the Hi-Line.
* People seeking a quiet, resilient community with a low cost of living.
* Those who want to live near Glacier National Park without paying resort-town prices.
* Individuals who appreciate the stark, windy beauty of the northern plains.

## Who Should Move to Miles City?

* Ranchers, livestock producers, and people whose careers and lifestyle are rooted in Eastern Montana's agricultural economy.
* Those seeking very affordable housing in a community where the typical home costs less than half what it does in many western Montana towns.
* Remote workers or retirees who want quiet, uncrowded living without the cost pressures that come with proximity to major tourist hubs.
* People who appreciate the authentic cowboy heritage of the Yellowstone Valley and the Bucking Horse Sale rodeo tradition.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Cut Bank is around $175,000, while in Miles City it is closer to $233,769, a difference of roughly $58,769, making Cut Bank significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Cut Bank averages about $650 per month, compared to roughly $750 in Miles City.

Cut Bank's overall cost of living differs from Miles City's. Cut Bank offers lower average salaries compared to Miles City, with a median household income of $52,000 compared to Miles City's $60,732.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Cut Bank is a tough, practical town known for its wind and its hardworking residents. It serves as a gateway between the vast agricultural plains of the Hi-Line and the dramatic peaks of the Rocky Mountain Front, offering a quiet, affordable lifestyle. Miles City is quiet and agricultural in character, with a lifestyle shaped by ranching seasons and rodeo. It sits along the Yellowstone River, looking east toward the open plains.

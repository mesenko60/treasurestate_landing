# Libby vs Hardin, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Libby and Hardin offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Libby is a remote timber town in the Kootenai National Forest with 2,775 people, its deep roots in the logging industry, its stunning, rugged isolation, and quick access to the Kootenai River and Cabinet Mountains. Hardin, with just 3,742 residents, is a agricultural community near the Crow Reservation known for its proximity to the Little Bighorn Battlefield, its farming and ranching economy, and the Bighorn River and Yellowtail Dam.

## Who Should Move to Libby?

* Outdoor enthusiasts seeking uncrowded access to some of Montana's most rugged and remote wilderness.
* Retirees and remote workers looking for affordable mountain living far from major tourist hubs.
* People who appreciate a tight-knit, resilient community with a strong working-class heritage.
* Hunters, anglers, and hikers who want to live in the heart of the Kootenai National Forest.

## Who Should Move to Hardin?

* Agricultural workers and farmers involved in the sugar beet and wheat industries of the Bighorn Valley.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, rural setting.
* History enthusiasts interested in the rich Native American culture and the legacy of the Little Bighorn.
* Anglers who want to be near the world-famous trout fishing below Yellowtail Dam.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Libby is around $285,000, while in Hardin it is closer to $204,573, a difference of roughly $80,427, making Hardin significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Libby averages about $850 per month, compared to roughly $726 in Hardin.

Libby's overall cost of living differs from Hardin's. Libby offers lower average salaries compared to Hardin, with a median household income of $45,000 compared to Hardin's $60,463.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Libby is a resilient, isolated town tucked into the far northwest corner of the state. It is defined by its timber heritage and its incredible, uncrowded access to the Kootenai River and Cabinet Mountains, offering a quiet, rugged lifestyle. Hardin is a quiet, culturally diverse agricultural center where the plains meet the Bighorn River. It is a town defined by farming, ranching, and its location bordering the Crow Indian Reservation, offering a slow-paced, rural lifestyle far from the state's major urban centers.

# Hamilton vs Missoula: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Hamilton and Missoula are both western Montana communities in the same river corridor, connected by Highway 93 through the Bitterroot Valley. Hamilton is the Banana Belt of Montana, a small community of 4,659 in Ravalli County at 3,573 feet, known for its mild winters, 194 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 55 lakes, 47 trailheads, 32 campgrounds, 25 fishing access sites, and the Bitterroot National Forest at its doorstep. Missoula is the Garden City, a university city of 77,757 in Missoula County at 3,215 feet, with 211 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 65 fishing access sites, 49 trailheads, 22 campgrounds, 10 museums, 9 historic sites, a ski area, and Missoula Montana Airport 7 miles away.

## Who Should Move to Hamilton?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 47 trailheads, 55 lakes, 32 campgrounds, 25 fishing access sites, a hot spring, a national forest, and a wilderness area all within 30 miles
*   People who want a small, quiet Bitterroot Valley community with a genuinely mild winter climate, where January average highs reach 40 degrees, and Missoula's full range of services just 56 miles north
*   Remote workers, retirees, and families who want a peaceful western Montana lifestyle with lower rent than Missoula and a smaller school district
*   Anyone drawn to the Bitterroot Valley's combination of mountain scenery, river access, and the laid-back character of a small agricultural community

## Who Should Move to Missoula?

*   Young professionals, students, and families who want a university city with good job opportunities, a 9,000-student school district, and Missoula Montana Airport 7 miles away
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 65 fishing access sites, 49 trailheads, 22 campgrounds, 13 lakes, 2 hot springs, a ski area, and the Clark Fork River running through downtown
*   People who love a lively downtown with craft breweries, live music, farmers markets, and a cultural scene that punches well above Missoula's size
*   Anyone who wants western Montana's mountains, rivers, and forests as their daily backdrop with the convenience of a real city

## Cost of Living Breakdown

These two towns are surprisingly close in home prices. The typical home value in Hamilton is around $516,033, compared to $554,389 in Missoula, only about 7% more. Rent is considerably lower in Hamilton: Hamilton averages about $958 per month, while Missoula comes in at roughly $1,486, more than 50% higher.

Median household income is higher in Missoula at $65,329 versus $52,917 in Hamilton. Missoula residents earn more on average, which helps offset the higher rent. Hamilton offers a lower monthly cost of living despite similar home prices.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Hamilton is a small Bitterroot Valley town where the river, the mountains, and the mild climate define daily life. It has the feel of a community that chose quiet over growth, and the outdoor access is exceptional. Missoula is a vibrant university city where the Clark Fork River, the Rattlesnake Wilderness, and a thriving arts and food scene define daily life. Both towns are in the same valley and share the same mountain backdrop, but Missoula has the energy, the jobs, and the amenities of a real city. If you want the Bitterroot Valley's quiet and outdoor access at a lower monthly cost, Hamilton is a compelling choice. If you want a lively, connected western Montana city with a university energy, Missoula is one of the best places to live in the state.

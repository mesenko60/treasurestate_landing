# Belgrade vs Kalispell: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Belgrade and Kalispell are two of Montana's fastest-growing smaller cities, but they anchor opposite ends of the state. Belgrade is the Hub of the Gallatin Valley, a community of 12,509 in the Gallatin Valley near Bozeman, with strong incomes, a booming housing market, and 104 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles. Kalispell is the Gateway to Glacier Country, a city of 29,886 in the Flathead Valley, with 189 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, Glacier National Park just up the road, and one of the most spectacular natural settings in North America.

## Who Should Move to Belgrade?

*   Families and professionals who want Gallatin Valley access with newer housing, strong schools, and a median household income of $88,896
*   Remote workers who want a quieter, more suburban base with five-minute access to Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want hot springs, a ski area, 21 fishing access sites, and 16 trailheads all within 30 miles
*   Anyone drawn to the Gallatin Valley's economic energy and professional community

## Who Should Move to Kalispell?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 19 ski areas, 28 fishing access sites, 26 trailheads, and Glacier National Park all within 30 miles
*   Families who want a full-service city in northwestern Montana with a school district of about 4,200 students
*   People who want a lower cost of living than Belgrade, with a typical home value around $541,638 and rent averaging about $2,086 per month
*   Anyone drawn to the Flathead Valley's lakes, forests, and proximity to one of America's most iconic national parks

## Cost of Living Breakdown

These two towns are remarkably close on housing. The typical home value in Belgrade is around $544,626, compared to $541,638 in Kalispell, a difference of less than $3,000. Rent is also similar: Belgrade averages about $2,206 per month, while Kalispell comes in at roughly $2,086.

The income gap is where they diverge. The median household income in Belgrade is $88,896, compared to $61,590 in Kalispell. Belgrade's Gallatin Valley economy drives significantly higher wages, which helps offset the similar housing costs.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Belgrade is a practical, growing community with a family-first character, close to Bozeman's amenities but quieter and more suburban. Kalispell is a regional hub for northwestern Montana, with a downtown that has been steadily revitalizing, a massive outdoor recreation footprint, and the kind of natural beauty that makes people drive 10 hours to visit. If you want the Gallatin Valley's economic strength and outdoor access in a growing community, Belgrade is a smart choice. If you want to live in the shadow of Glacier National Park with 189 recreation sites at your doorstep, Kalispell is hard to argue with.

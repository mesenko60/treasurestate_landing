# Miles City vs Livingston, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Miles City and Livingston are two small Montana towns of nearly identical size, but they attract very different kinds of people. Livingston, the Windiest City in Montana, sits at the northern edge of Paradise Valley along the Yellowstone River, with a nationally recognized arts scene, a historic railroad downtown, and proximity to Yellowstone National Park and Bozeman. Miles City, the Cow Capital of Montana, is a ranching community on the eastern plains, where the livestock industry, rodeo culture, and the wide-open Yellowstone River country define the character of the town.

## Who Should Move to Livingston?

Artists, writers, and creative professionals drawn to Livingston's well-established arts community and its literary heritage. Fly fishing enthusiasts who want to live on one of the premier trout rivers in the country. People who want a small-town atmosphere with easy access to Bozeman's job market and amenities, just 35 miles away. Those who appreciate Livingston's historic downtown architecture and its position as a gateway to Yellowstone National Park.

## Who Should Move to Miles City?

Ranchers, livestock producers, and people whose careers and lifestyle are rooted in Eastern Montana's agricultural economy. Those seeking very affordable housing in a community where the typical home costs less than half what it does in Livingston. Remote workers or retirees who want quiet, uncrowded living without the cost pressures that come with proximity to Bozeman and Yellowstone. People who appreciate the authentic cowboy heritage of the Yellowstone Valley and the Bucking Horse Sale rodeo tradition.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Despite their similar populations, these two towns have a significant housing gap. The typical home in Livingston costs $509,477 compared to $233,769 in Miles City, a difference of roughly $276,000. Monthly rent averages $1,783 in Livingston versus $750 in Miles City. Median household income is $65,187 in Livingston and $60,732 in Miles City. Livingston residents earn modestly more, but Miles City offers dramatically lower housing costs. The overall cost of living in Miles City is substantially more affordable.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Livingston has a distinctive character shaped by its railroad history, its river, and its long-standing reputation as a haven for artists and writers. It is a small town with a cultural life that punches well above its weight. Miles City is quieter and more agricultural in character, with a lifestyle shaped by ranching seasons and rodeo rather than galleries and literary festivals. Both towns sit along the Yellowstone River, but they represent very different chapters of Montana's story. Livingston looks west toward the mountains and Yellowstone; Miles City looks east toward the open plains.

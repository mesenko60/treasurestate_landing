# Deer Lodge vs Livingston, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Deer Lodge and Livingston offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Deer Lodge is a historic ranching and prison town with 2,938 people, the Old Montana Prison Museum, its deep ranching heritage in the Powell Valley, and quick access to the Clark Fork River and the Flint Creek Range. Livingston, with just 8,386 residents, is a historic railroad and arts town known for its literary heritage, its vibrant arts scene, and the Yellowstone River and Paradise Valley.

## Who Should Move to Deer Lodge?

* History buffs who appreciate the town's unique museums and historic architecture.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, traditional western town.
* Workers in agriculture, government, or local services looking for a slow-paced lifestyle.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want uncrowded access to the Clark Fork River and surrounding mountains.

## Who Should Move to Livingston?

* Artists, writers, and creative professionals.
* Fly fishing enthusiasts who want to live on the Yellowstone River.
* People who want a small-town atmosphere with easy access to Bozeman.
* Those who appreciate historic downtown architecture.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Deer Lodge is around $225,000, while in Livingston it is closer to $509,477, a difference of roughly $284,477, making Deer Lodge significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Deer Lodge averages about $800 per month, compared to roughly $1,783 in Livingston.

Deer Lodge's overall cost of living differs from Livingston's. Deer Lodge offers lower average salaries compared to Livingston, with a median household income of $48,000 compared to Livingston's $65,187.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Deer Lodge is a quiet, deeply historic town that feels like a step back in time. It is defined by its agricultural roots and its unique history as the site of the original territorial prison, offering a slow-paced, affordable lifestyle in a beautiful valley setting. Livingston is a windy, eclectic town that blends its gritty railroad past with a sophisticated arts community, serving as the scenic gateway to Yellowstone.

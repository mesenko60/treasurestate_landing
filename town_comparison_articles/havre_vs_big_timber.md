# Havre vs Big Timber, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Havre and Big Timber offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Havre is a historic railroad and agricultural hub on the Hi-Line with 9,362 people, its deep roots in the railroad industry, the Havre Beneath the Streets underground tour, and quick access to the nearby Bear Paw Mountains and Fresno Reservoir. Big Timber, with 1,715 residents, is a historic ranching community in the Sweet Grass Valley known for its deep, multi-generational ranching heritage, its classic, historic downtown architecture, and the Boulder River and the Crazy Mountains.

## Who Should Move to Havre?

* Workers in agriculture, education, or the railroad industry looking for steady employment.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a community with all essential amenities.
* Those who appreciate the stark beauty of the northern plains and the close-knit feel of a Hi-Line town.
* History buffs interested in the unique underground history and nearby frontier battlefields.

## Who Should Move to Big Timber?

* People seeking an authentic, traditional western community with a strong agricultural identity.
* Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want access to the pristine waters of the Boulder River.
* Those looking for a scenic, quiet lifestyle with relatively easy access to Bozeman or Billings.
* Retirees and remote workers drawn to the town's historic charm and spectacular mountain views.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Havre is around $224,252, while in Big Timber it is closer to $422,291, a difference of roughly $198,039, making Havre significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Havre averages about $925 per month, compared to roughly $1,009 in Big Timber.

Havre's overall cost of living differs from Big Timber's. Havre offers lower average salaries compared to Big Timber, with a median household income of $58,000 compared to Big Timber's $63,514.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Havre is a resilient, hardworking community that serves as the commercial center for a vast stretch of northern Montana. It offers a practical, unpretentious lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture and the railroad. Big Timber is a quintessential Montana ranching town, fiercely protective of its agricultural roots and stunning natural surroundings. It offers a quiet, authentic western lifestyle set against the dramatic backdrop of the Crazy Mountains.

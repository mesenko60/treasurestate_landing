# Sidney vs Missoula, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Sidney and Missoula offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Sidney is a bustling agricultural and energy hub with 6,173 people, its position in the Bakken oil field, its rich farming heritage in the Yellowstone Valley, and quick access to the Yellowstone River and eastern Montana badlands. Missoula, with just 77,757 residents, is a vibrant university town known for its craft breweries, its bustling downtown, and world-class hiking and skiing.

## Who Should Move to Sidney?

* Workers in the energy, agriculture, or transportation sectors looking for strong employment opportunities.
* People seeking a hardworking, practical community with a resilient local economy.
* Those who want affordable housing compared to western Montana, with higher average wages driven by the oil and gas industry.
* Hunters and anglers who appreciate the rugged, uncrowded access to the Yellowstone River and surrounding plains.

## Who Should Move to Missoula?

* Young professionals and families who want good job opportunities.
* People who love a lively downtown with restaurants, breweries, and events.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want to be close to the mountains for hiking, skiing, and rafting.
* Anyone who wants a university town vibe with younger energy.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Sidney is around $241,500, while in Missoula it is closer to $554,389, a difference of roughly $312,889, making Sidney significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Sidney averages about $850 per month, compared to roughly $1,486 in Missoula.

Sidney's overall cost of living differs from Missoula's. Sidney offers higher average salaries compared to Missoula, with a median household income of $75,200 compared to Missoula's $65,329.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Sidney is a dynamic, industrious town where the pace of life is closely tied to the energy markets and agricultural seasons. It has a practical, no-nonsense atmosphere, serving as a major economic engine for eastern Montana while maintaining its roots as a tight-knit farming community. Missoula feels like a real city in the mountains, with busy sidewalks, weekly farmers markets, live music, and constant activity.

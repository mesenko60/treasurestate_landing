# Great Falls vs Butte: Which Montana City is Right for You?

Great Falls and Butte are two of Montana's most historically significant cities, and they share more in common than many people realize. Both are working-class, proud, and deeply rooted in Montana's industrial and military heritage. Great Falls is the Electric City, a city of 60,000 on the Missouri River in central Montana, anchored by Malmstrom Air Force Base and the C.M. Russell Museum. Butte is the Richest Hill on Earth, a gritty, storied mining city of 34,494 perched at 5,741 feet in the mountains of southwestern Montana, with one of the most extraordinary architectural and industrial histories of any city in the American West.

## Who Should Move to Great Falls?

*   Military families stationed at or connected to Malmstrom Air Force Base
*   Families and professionals who want a larger city with more job opportunities and a lower elevation, milder climate
*   History and art lovers drawn to the C.M. Russell Museum and the Lewis and Clark National Historic Trail
*   People who want a full-service city with a regional airport and a broader range of amenities

## Who Should Move to Butte?

*   History enthusiasts and architecture lovers drawn to Butte's extraordinary Victorian-era Uptown district
*   Remote workers and retirees seeking some of Montana's most affordable housing in a mountain city with real character
*   Outdoor lovers who want hot springs, mountain lakes, wilderness, and 94 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles
*   People who want a tight-knit, working-class Montana community with a mythology and a story unlike any other

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Both cities are among Montana's most affordable, but Butte edges ahead on housing. The median home price in Great Falls is around $328,554, compared to $274,163 in Butte, a difference of about $54,000. Rent is nearly identical: about $1,340 per month in Great Falls versus $1,318 in Butte.

Income levels favor Great Falls, with a median household income of $63,934 compared to Butte's $57,633. The combination of a larger job market and a military base gives Great Falls a more stable economic foundation, while Butte's lower home prices make it attractive for anyone with remote or retirement income.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Great Falls is a practical, steady city that does what it does without a lot of fanfare, it's a place where people raise families, serve their country, and appreciate the Missouri River trail system and the Russell Museum. Butte is something more complicated and more fascinating. It's a city that has survived the collapse of its copper industry, the creation of one of the country's largest Superfund sites, and decades of population decline, and it has come out the other side with its identity not just intact but fiercely celebrated. The St. Patrick's Day parade, the Uptown historic district, and the sheer audacity of a city built on a mountain of copper, Butte is unlike anywhere else in Montana.

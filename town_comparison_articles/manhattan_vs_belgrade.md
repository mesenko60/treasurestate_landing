# Manhattan vs Belgrade, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Manhattan and Belgrade offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Manhattan is a rapidly growing agricultural town in the Gallatin Valley with 2,288 people, its historic role as the Seed Potato Capital of the World, its booming residential growth, and quick access to the Gallatin River and the Bridger Mountains. Belgrade, with 12,509 residents, is a rapidly growing hub in the Gallatin Valley known for proximity to Bozeman, the Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport, and the Bridger Mountains and Gallatin River.

## Who Should Move to Manhattan?

* Professionals working in Bozeman who want a smaller-town feel and are willing to commute.
* Families looking for excellent local schools and a safe, tight-knit community.
* People who appreciate a town that still honors its agricultural heritage despite rapid modernization.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want immediate access to the world-class fishing of the Gallatin River.

## Who Should Move to Belgrade?

* Families and professionals who work in Bozeman but want slightly more affordable housing options.
* Frequent travelers who appreciate being minutes away from Montana's busiest airport.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want quick access to the Bridger Mountains, Big Sky, and blue-ribbon trout streams.
* People looking for a community with a strong local identity that still benefits from the economic engine of the Gallatin Valley.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Manhattan is around $652,619, while in Belgrade it is closer to $544,626, a difference of roughly $107,993, making Belgrade significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Manhattan averages about $1,700 per month, compared to roughly $2,206 in Belgrade.

Manhattan's overall cost of living differs from Belgrade's. Manhattan offers lower average salaries compared to Belgrade, with a median household income of $69,671 compared to Belgrade's $88,896.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Manhattan blends its deep agricultural roots with its new reality as a highly desirable, expensive suburb of Bozeman. It maintains a charming, historic downtown and a strong community identity, even as it experiences significant growth and rising costs. Belgrade feels like a busy, practical town that is absorbing much of the Gallatin Valley's growth. It has its own distinct community pride, centered around local schools and events, but its daily rhythm is closely tied to the economic hum of neighboring Bozeman and the constant activity at the airport.

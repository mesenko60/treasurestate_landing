# Havre vs Manhattan, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Havre and Manhattan offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Havre is a historic railroad and agricultural hub on the Hi-Line with 9,362 people, its deep roots in the railroad industry, the Havre Beneath the Streets underground tour, and quick access to the nearby Bear Paw Mountains and Fresno Reservoir. Manhattan, with 2,288 residents, is a rapidly growing agricultural town in the Gallatin Valley known for its historic role as the Seed Potato Capital of the World, its booming residential growth, and the Gallatin River and the Bridger Mountains.

## Who Should Move to Havre?

* Workers in agriculture, education, or the railroad industry looking for steady employment.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a community with all essential amenities.
* Those who appreciate the stark beauty of the northern plains and the close-knit feel of a Hi-Line town.
* History buffs interested in the unique underground history and nearby frontier battlefields.

## Who Should Move to Manhattan?

* Professionals working in Bozeman who want a smaller-town feel and are willing to commute.
* Families looking for excellent local schools and a safe, tight-knit community.
* People who appreciate a town that still honors its agricultural heritage despite rapid modernization.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want immediate access to the world-class fishing of the Gallatin River.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Havre is around $224,252, while in Manhattan it is closer to $652,619, a difference of roughly $428,367, making Havre significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Havre averages about $925 per month, compared to roughly $1,700 in Manhattan.

Havre's overall cost of living differs from Manhattan's. Havre offers lower average salaries compared to Manhattan, with a median household income of $58,000 compared to Manhattan's $69,671.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Havre is a resilient, hardworking community that serves as the commercial center for a vast stretch of northern Montana. It offers a practical, unpretentious lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture and the railroad. Manhattan blends its deep agricultural roots with its new reality as a highly desirable, expensive suburb of Bozeman. It maintains a charming, historic downtown and a strong community identity, even as it experiences significant growth and rising costs.

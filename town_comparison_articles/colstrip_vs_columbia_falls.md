# Colstrip vs Columbia Falls, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Colstrip and Columbia Falls offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Colstrip is a purpose-built energy town in eastern Montana with 2,055 people, its massive coal-fired power plant, its excellent, company-built community amenities, and quick access to Castle Rock Lake and the surrounding pine hills. Columbia Falls, with 5,308 residents, is a gateway community to Glacier National Park known for its timber industry roots, its revitalized downtown, and the Flathead River and Glacier National Park.

## Who Should Move to Colstrip?

* Workers in the energy, mining, or industrial sectors looking for high-paying employment.
* Families seeking excellent, well-funded local schools and community parks.
* People looking for very affordable housing combined with high average salaries.
* Those who appreciate a practical, hardworking community with a strong industrial identity.

## Who Should Move to Columbia Falls?

* Outdoor lovers who want to live minutes from the entrance to Glacier National Park.
* Families looking for a community with a strong local spirit and a growing downtown scene.
* People who work in the Flathead Valley but prefer a town with a slightly more blue-collar, unpretentious history than Whitefish.
* Those who want immediate access to the Flathead River for fishing, floating, and recreation.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Colstrip is around $219,986, while in Columbia Falls it is closer to $584,580, a difference of roughly $364,594, making Colstrip significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Colstrip averages about $632 per month, compared to roughly $1,003 in Columbia Falls.

Colstrip's overall cost of living differs from Columbia Falls's. Colstrip offers higher average salaries compared to Columbia Falls, with a median household income of $86,000 compared to Columbia Falls's $65,313.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Colstrip is a unique, industrious town built specifically to support the local energy industry. It boasts surprisingly excellent parks, schools, and recreational facilities for its size, offering a practical, high-income lifestyle for those working in the energy sector. Columbia Falls has transitioned from a traditional timber town into a vibrant gateway community, balancing its working-class roots with a new influx of breweries, markets, and tourism. It feels less resort-focused than neighboring Whitefish, maintaining a strong, independent local character while serving as the front porch to Glacier National Park.

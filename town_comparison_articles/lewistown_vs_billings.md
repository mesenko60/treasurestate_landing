# Lewistown vs Billings: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Lewistown and Billings represent two very different scales of Montana life. Lewistown is the Geographic Center of Montana, a quiet ranching and agricultural community of 6,204 in Fergus County at 3,934 feet elevation, with 17 campgrounds within 30 miles, a warm spring, and a historic brick downtown that has changed little in decades. Billings is the Magic City, Montana's largest city at 117,116 people in Yellowstone County, with a full-service regional economy, a major airport, 16 fishing access sites, 9 museums, and the kind of urban amenities that no other Montana city can match.

## Who Should Move to Lewistown?

*   Retirees and remote workers who want a quiet, affordable small-town lifestyle at the geographic heart of Montana
*   Budget-conscious buyers who want a typical home value around $268,126 and rent averaging just $896 per month
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 17 campgrounds, 7 fishing access sites, and the Big Snowy Mountains Wilderness Study Area within 30 miles
*   People who want a traditional, tight-knit ranching community with a genuine small-town character

## Who Should Move to Billings?

*   Professionals and families who want Montana's strongest job market, with a median household income of $71,855 and a broad range of employers
*   People who want a full-service city with a regional airport, major retailers, a strong healthcare sector, and a 16,000-student school district
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 16 fishing access sites, 4 state parks, and the Rimrocks within easy reach
*   Anyone who wants urban Montana amenities, including 9 museums, a vibrant arts scene, and a growing downtown

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Lewistown is dramatically more affordable. The typical home value in Lewistown is around $268,126, compared to $390,654 in Billings, a gap of over $120,000. Rent diverges similarly: Lewistown averages about $896 per month, while Billings comes in at roughly $1,383, more than $480 more per month.

The income gap is equally significant. The median household income in Billings is $71,855, compared to $44,195 in Lewistown. Billings residents earn considerably more, which helps offset the higher cost of living, but Lewistown's low prices mean that even modest incomes go a long way.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Lewistown is a town where the pace is slow, the skies are wide, and the community revolves around agriculture, ranching, and the outdoors. Billings is a city that works, a regional hub with a downtown that has been reinvesting in itself, a strong economy, and the energy of a place that is growing. If you want affordable, quiet, small-town Montana at the geographic center of the state, Lewistown is one of the most authentic choices available. If you want Montana's best job market, full urban services, and a city that punches above its weight, Billings is the clear answer.

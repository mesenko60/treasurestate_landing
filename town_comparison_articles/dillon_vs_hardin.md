# Dillon vs Hardin: Which Montana Town Is Right for You?

Dillon and Hardin are two of Montana's most characterful small towns, each shaped by a great river and a deep sense of place. Dillon, Montana's Hometown in the Beaverhead Valley of southwest Montana, is a high-desert ranching and university town of 3,880 people, home to the University of Montana Western and surrounded by the Beaverhead, Big Hole, and Ruby rivers. Hardin, Where the Rivers Meet in Big Horn County, is a town of 3,742 people near the confluence of the Bighorn and Little Bighorn rivers, known for the Little Bighorn Battlefield, the Bighorn River's world-class tailwater trout fishery, and the cultural heritage of the Crow Nation.

## Who Should Move to Dillon?

- Fly fishers who want access to the Beaverhead, Big Hole, and Ruby rivers, among the most storied trout streams in Montana
- Students or families connected to the University of Montana Western, which gives Dillon a youthful energy unusual for a town of its size
- People looking for affordable housing and a quiet, traditional small-town lifestyle in a wide-open high-desert setting
- History enthusiasts interested in Lewis and Clark country and the ranching heritage of the Beaverhead Valley

## Who Should Move to Hardin?

- History enthusiasts drawn to Little Bighorn Battlefield and the rich cultural heritage of the Crow Nation
- Anglers who want the Bighorn River, one of Montana's premier tailwater trout fisheries, close at hand
- People looking for very affordable housing and a low cost of living in a quiet, rural community
- Those who want to experience the unique culture of southeastern Montana, including the annual Crow Fair

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Dillon is more expensive. The typical home value is $375,585 compared to $204,573 in Hardin, a difference of about $171,000. Rent is slightly higher in Dillon at $821 per month versus $726 in Hardin. Hardin's median household income of $60,463 is higher than Dillon's $51,458, making Hardin the more affordable option in real terms. Both towns are among the most affordable in Montana. Dillon's nearest airport is in Butte, 62 miles north. Hardin's is in Billings, 54 miles to the west.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Dillon and Hardin share a quiet, community-centered pace and a deep connection to the land and the river, but they offer very different landscapes and histories. Dillon's identity is shaped by the Beaverhead River, the University of Montana Western, and the vast ranching landscape of the Big Hole and Beaverhead valleys. Hardin's is shaped by the Bighorn River, the Little Bighorn Battlefield, and the Crow Nation's living cultural presence. Dillon has 71 recreational sites within 30 miles to Hardin's 20, with a particular advantage in fishing access, campgrounds, and state parks. Hardin leads in historical significance and cultural richness. Both towns are genuinely distinctive in Montana and offer an authentic, unpretentious way of life.

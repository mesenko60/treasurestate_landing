# Havre vs Choteau, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Havre and Choteau offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Havre is a historic railroad and agricultural hub on the Hi-Line with 9,362 people, its deep roots in the railroad industry, the Havre Beneath the Streets underground tour, and quick access to the nearby Bear Paw Mountains and Fresno Reservoir. Choteau, with 1,721 residents, is a scenic gateway town to the Rocky Mountain Front known for its spectacular views of the Front, its rich dinosaur paleontology history, and the Bob Marshall Wilderness and Freezout Lake.

## Who Should Move to Havre?

* Workers in agriculture, education, or the railroad industry looking for steady employment.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a community with all essential amenities.
* Those who appreciate the stark beauty of the northern plains and the close-knit feel of a Hi-Line town.
* History buffs interested in the unique underground history and nearby frontier battlefields.

## Who Should Move to Choteau?

* Outdoor enthusiasts who want uncrowded access to the Bob Marshall Wilderness.
* Birders and wildlife watchers drawn to the spectacular migrations at nearby Freezout Lake.
* People seeking a quiet, affordable community with some of the best mountain views in the state.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Havre is around $224,252, while in Choteau it is closer to $288,323, a difference of roughly $64,071, making Havre significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Havre averages about $925 per month, compared to roughly $713 in Choteau.

Havre's overall cost of living differs from Choteau's. Havre offers higher average salaries compared to Choteau, with a median household income of $58,000 compared to Choteau's $53,929.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Havre is a resilient, hardworking community that serves as the commercial center for a vast stretch of northern Montana. It offers a practical, unpretentious lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture and the railroad. Choteau is a quiet, stunningly located town that serves as the Front Porch of the Rockies. It offers a slow-paced, affordable lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture, paleontology, and the incredible wilderness right in its backyard.

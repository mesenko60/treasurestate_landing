# Belgrade vs Dillon: Which Montana Town is Right for You?

Belgrade and Dillon are both western Montana communities, but they represent very different lifestyles and price points. Belgrade is the Hub of the Gallatin Valley, a fast-growing suburb of 12,509 in Gallatin County at 4,462 feet, with Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport 5 miles away, 104 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 16 trailheads, 14 waterfalls, 15 lakes, 21 fishing access sites, 2 hot springs, a national forest, a ski area, and a 3,800-student school district. Dillon is Montana's Hometown, a small university and ranching community of 3,880 in Beaverhead County at 5,095 feet, with the nearest airport in Butte 62 miles away, 71 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 24 fishing access sites, 16 campgrounds, 8 lakes, 7 nature reserves, 5 state parks, 2 hot springs, 2 historic sites, 1 museum, 1 golf course, and a 650-student school district.

## Who Should Move to Belgrade?

*   Families and professionals who want Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport 5 miles away, a 3,800-student school district, and all the amenities of the Bozeman metro area at a slightly lower price than Bozeman itself
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 16 trailheads, 14 waterfalls, 15 lakes, 21 fishing access sites, 2 hot springs, a ski area, and a national forest all within 30 miles, with quick access to the Bridger Mountains and Gallatin Canyon
*   Buyers who want a rapidly growing community with strong job access in the Gallatin Valley, with a typical home value of $544,626 and a median household income of $88,896
*   Remote workers and young professionals who want a suburban Gallatin Valley lifestyle with proximity to Bozeman's restaurants, university, and cultural amenities without paying Bozeman prices

## Who Should Move to Dillon?

*   Anglers and fly fishing enthusiasts who want 24 fishing access sites on the Beaverhead River, one of the most celebrated blue-ribbon trout streams in Montana, along with 16 campgrounds, 7 nature reserves, and 5 state parks within 30 miles
*   University of Montana Western students, faculty, and staff who want to live in a tight-knit college town with a 650-student school district and a genuine small-town Montana character
*   Budget-conscious buyers who want to own a home in a real western Montana community at an affordable price, with a typical home value of $375,585 and rent averaging just $821 per month
*   Retirees and remote workers who want a quiet, self-contained southwestern Montana community at 5,095 feet with 2 hot springs, 5 state parks, and wide-open Beaverhead County skies

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Belgrade is significantly more expensive than Dillon. The typical home value in Belgrade is $544,626, compared to $375,585 in Dillon, about $169,000 more. Rent reflects an even larger gap: Belgrade averages $2,206 per month versus $821 in Dillon, nearly three times higher.

Median household income is considerably higher in Belgrade at $88,896 versus $51,458 in Dillon. Dillon's lower income reflects its smaller, more rural economy centered on agriculture, ranching, and the university. For buyers and renters who want southwestern Montana outdoor access at a very affordable price, Dillon offers genuine value.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Belgrade feels like a prosperous, growing suburb of Bozeman, with new subdivisions, strong outdoor access, and a community identity tied closely to the Gallatin Valley's outdoor and university culture. Dillon is a quieter, more traditional Montana community where the Beaverhead River, the surrounding ranch country, and the University of Montana Western define daily life. Dillon has 24 fishing access sites and 5 state parks within 30 miles that reflect its position in one of Montana's premier fly fishing corridors. Belgrade has 14 waterfalls, a ski area, and a national forest that Dillon does not. If you want a fast-growing Gallatin Valley community with outstanding mountain access and a suburban feel, Belgrade is one of the best values in the Bozeman area. If you want an affordable, self-contained southwestern Montana community with world-class fly fishing, genuine small-town character, and a university presence, Dillon is one of the most underrated places to live in Montana.

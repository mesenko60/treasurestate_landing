# Big Sky vs Kalispell: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Big Sky and Kalispell are both mountain communities in Montana, but they sit in different corners of the state and offer very different lifestyles. Big Sky is the Biggest Skiing in America, a resort community of 3,591 in Gallatin County at 6,319 feet, known for Big Sky Resort, 23 trailheads, 17 waterfalls, 18 lakes, 3 hot springs, 2 wilderness areas, and 140 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles. Kalispell is the Gateway to Glacier Country, a western Montana city of 29,886 in Flathead County at 2,959 feet, with 189 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 28 fishing access sites, 27 lakes, 26 trailheads, 28 campgrounds, 19 ski areas, 15 state parks, 10 golf courses, 8 museums, 8 nature reserves, 6 historic sites, 4 disc golf courses, 4 boat launches, 1 national park, Glacier Park International Airport 10 miles away, and a 4,200-student school district.

## Who Should Move to Big Sky?

*   Skiers, snowboarders, and mountain sports enthusiasts who want Big Sky Resort, one of the largest ski areas in North America with over 5,800 acres of skiable terrain, right in their backyard
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 23 trailheads, 17 waterfalls, 18 lakes, 19 fishing access sites, 3 hot springs, 2 wilderness areas, and a national forest all within 30 miles
*   High-income remote workers and affluent buyers who can afford Big Sky's extraordinary real estate market, with a typical home value of $1,819,905 and a median household income of $103,625
*   People who want a small, exclusive resort community lifestyle where outdoor recreation is the primary focus and the mountains define every aspect of daily life

## Who Should Move to Kalispell?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 28 fishing access sites, 27 lakes, 26 trailheads, 19 ski areas, 15 state parks, 28 campgrounds, and Glacier National Park all within 30 miles, in a full-service western Montana city
*   Families and professionals who want Glacier Park International Airport 10 miles away, a 4,200-student school district, 10 golf courses, 8 museums, and a median household income of $61,590 in a genuine Montana community
*   Buyers looking for a western Montana mountain city at a more accessible price than Whitefish, with a typical home value of $541,638 and strong access to the Flathead Valley's outdoor amenities
*   Anyone who wants to live near Glacier National Park and Flathead Lake, two of the most spectacular natural features in the American West, with all the services and infrastructure of a real city

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Big Sky is dramatically more expensive than Kalispell. The typical home value in Big Sky is $1,819,905, compared to $541,638 in Kalispell, more than three times higher. Rent is actually higher in Kalispell: $2,086 per month versus $1,712 in Big Sky, reflecting Kalispell's tight rental market driven by strong demand in the Flathead Valley.

Median household income is significantly higher in Big Sky at $103,625 versus $61,590 in Kalispell. Kalispell is moderately expensive by Montana standards but offers far more urban amenities and services than Big Sky. Big Sky's home prices reflect its status as one of the premier ski resort communities in North America.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Big Sky is a world-class mountain resort community where the ski mountain, the wilderness, and a high-income outdoor lifestyle define daily life. Kalispell is a growing western Montana city where Glacier National Park, Flathead Lake, the Whitefish Mountain Resort area, and a strong outdoor recreation economy define community identity. Kalispell has 189 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles compared to Big Sky's 140, including 19 ski areas and 1 national park that Big Sky cannot match. Big Sky has 17 waterfalls, 3 hot springs, and a single ski resort that is larger than all 19 of Kalispell's combined. If you want the most exclusive mountain resort lifestyle in Montana with world-class skiing and wilderness access, Big Sky is extraordinary. If you want a full-service western Montana city with outstanding outdoor access, proximity to Glacier National Park, and a community that is genuinely livable at a fraction of Big Sky's cost, Kalispell is one of the most compelling choices in the Mountain West.

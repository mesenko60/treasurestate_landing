# Malta vs Glasgow, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Malta and Glasgow offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Malta is a historic agricultural town on the Hi-Line with 2,100 people, its world-class dinosaur fossils, its deep ranching heritage, and quick access to the Bowdoin National Wildlife Refuge and the Missouri River Breaks. Glasgow, with 3,224 residents, is a historic railroad town on the Hi-Line known for its position as a regional hub for northeastern Montana, its proximity to Fort Peck Dam, and Fort Peck Lake and the Charles M. Russell National Wildlife Refuge.

## Who Should Move to Malta?

* Workers in agriculture, government, or local services looking for a quiet, stable community.
* People seeking very affordable housing and a low overall cost of living.
* Hunters, birders, and outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the vast, uncrowded public lands of northeastern Montana.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle on the high plains.

## Who Should Move to Glasgow?

* Workers in agriculture, government, or the railroad industry looking for stable employment in northeastern Montana.
* Anglers and boaters who want to live near Fort Peck Lake, one of the premier walleye fisheries in the country.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, isolated community.
* Hunters and outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the vast, uncrowded public lands of the Missouri River Breaks.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Malta is around $244,540, while in Glasgow it is closer to $195,000, a difference of roughly $49,540, making Glasgow significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Malta averages about $693 per month, compared to roughly $750 in Glasgow.

Malta's overall cost of living differs from Glasgow's. Malta offers higher average salaries compared to Glasgow, with a median household income of $59,449 compared to Glasgow's $55,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Malta is a quiet, resilient community on the eastern Hi-Line, defined by agriculture, wide-open spaces, and its unique paleontology attractions. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle far from the state's urban centers. Glasgow is a resilient, isolated community that serves as the commercial and social center for a massive swath of northeastern Montana. It offers a quiet, slow-paced lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture, the railroad, and the incredible recreational opportunities at nearby Fort Peck Lake.

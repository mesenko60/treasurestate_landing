# Polson vs Livingston, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Polson and Livingston offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Polson is a scenic lakeside community with 5,335 people, its location on the southern shore of Flathead Lake, its cherry orchards, and quick access to Flathead Lake and the Mission Mountains. Livingston, with just 8,386 residents, is a historic railroad and arts town known for its literary heritage, its vibrant arts scene, and the Yellowstone River and Paradise Valley.

## Who Should Move to Polson?

* Retirees and remote workers seeking a beautiful lakeside setting with a slower pace of life.
* Boaters, anglers, and water sports enthusiasts who want immediate access to the largest natural freshwater lake in the West.
* People looking for a more affordable alternative to the resort towns of the northern Flathead Valley.
* Those who appreciate a community with a strong agricultural heritage, particularly its famous cherry harvest.

## Who Should Move to Livingston?

* Artists, writers, and creative professionals.
* Fly fishing enthusiasts who want to live on the Yellowstone River.
* People who want a small-town atmosphere with easy access to Bozeman.
* Those who appreciate historic downtown architecture.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Polson is around $415,000, while in Livingston it is closer to $509,477, a difference of roughly $94,477, making Polson significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Polson averages about $1,050 per month, compared to roughly $1,783 in Livingston.

Polson's overall cost of living differs from Livingston's. Polson offers lower average salaries compared to Livingston, with a median household income of $52,500 compared to Livingston's $65,187.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Polson offers a relaxed, scenic lifestyle defined by its position on Flathead Lake. It lacks the polished resort feel of towns further north, maintaining a more grounded, agricultural character while still providing spectacular water and mountain access. Livingston is a windy, eclectic town that blends its gritty railroad past with a sophisticated arts community, serving as the scenic gateway to Yellowstone.

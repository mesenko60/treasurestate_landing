# Conrad vs Forsyth, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Conrad and Forsyth offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Conrad is a quiet agricultural town on the northern plains with 2,318 people, its position as the seat of Pondera County, its strong farming and ranching economy, and quick access to the Rocky Mountain Front and Lake Frances. Forsyth, with 1,630 residents, is a historic railroad town on the Yellowstone River known for its beautiful, historic county courthouse, its position as a transportation and agricultural hub, and the Yellowstone River and the eastern plains.

## Who Should Move to Conrad?

* Workers in agriculture or local services looking for a quiet, stable community.
* People seeking very affordable housing far removed from the state's busy tourist hubs.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle.
* Hunters and anglers who want uncrowded access to the plains and the nearby Rocky Mountain Front.

## Who Should Move to Forsyth?

* Workers in agriculture, transportation, or local services looking for a stable community.
* People seeking very affordable housing and a low overall cost of living.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle on the eastern plains.
* Hunters and anglers who want uncrowded access to the Yellowstone River.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Conrad is around $244,817, while in Forsyth it is closer to $208,690, a difference of roughly $36,127, making Forsyth significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Conrad averages about $888 per month, compared to roughly $743 in Forsyth.

Conrad's overall cost of living differs from Forsyth's. Conrad offers lower average salaries compared to Forsyth, with a median household income of $47,348 compared to Forsyth's $58,375.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Conrad is a classic, quiet Montana farming community where life revolves around the agricultural seasons and local school sports. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle with spectacular views of the Rocky Mountain Front to the west. Forsyth is a quiet, practical community in eastern Montana, defined by the railroad, agriculture, and the Yellowstone River. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle with a strong sense of local history.

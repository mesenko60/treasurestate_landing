# Glasgow vs Kalispell, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Glasgow and Kalispell offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Glasgow is a historic railroad town on the Hi-Line with 3,224 people, its position as a regional hub for northeastern Montana, its proximity to Fort Peck Dam, and quick access to Fort Peck Lake and the Charles M. Russell National Wildlife Refuge. Kalispell, with just 26,110 residents, is a commercial center of the Flathead Valley known for its retail and medical facilities, its historic downtown, and Flathead Lake and Glacier National Park.

## Who Should Move to Glasgow?

* Workers in agriculture, government, or the railroad industry looking for stable employment in northeastern Montana.
* Anglers and boaters who want to live near Fort Peck Lake, one of the premier walleye fisheries in the country.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, isolated community.
* Hunters and outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the vast, uncrowded public lands of the Missouri River Breaks.

## Who Should Move to Kalispell?

* Families who want access to Glacier National Park without the resort town prices.
* Professionals working in healthcare or retail.
* People who want a mix of historic charm and modern conveniences.
* Outdoor lovers who want to be near Flathead Lake.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Glasgow is around $195,000, while in Kalispell it is closer to $550,000, a difference of roughly $355,000, making Glasgow significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Glasgow averages about $750 per month, compared to roughly $1,400 in Kalispell.

Glasgow's overall cost of living differs from Kalispell's. Glasgow offers lower average salaries compared to Kalispell, with a median household income of $55,000 compared to Kalispell's $60,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Glasgow is a resilient, isolated community that serves as the commercial and social center for a massive swath of northeastern Montana. It offers a quiet, slow-paced lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture, the railroad, and the incredible recreational opportunities at nearby Fort Peck Lake. Kalispell is the practical, bustling heart of the Flathead Valley, balancing its role as a commercial hub with incredible access to natural wonders.

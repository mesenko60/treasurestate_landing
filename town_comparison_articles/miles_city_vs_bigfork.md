# Miles City vs Bigfork, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Miles City and Bigfork offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Miles City is a ranching community on the eastern plains with 8,412 people, the livestock industry, rodeo culture, and quick access to the wide-open Yellowstone River country. Bigfork, with just 5,000 residents, is a picturesque village on Flathead Lake known for its thriving arts scene, its cherry orchards, and Flathead Lake and the Swan River.

## Who Should Move to Miles City?

* Ranchers, livestock producers, and people whose careers and lifestyle are rooted in Eastern Montana's agricultural economy.
* Those seeking very affordable housing in a community where the typical home costs less than half what it does in many western Montana towns.
* Remote workers or retirees who want quiet, uncrowded living without the cost pressures that come with proximity to major tourist hubs.
* People who appreciate the authentic cowboy heritage of the Yellowstone Valley and the Bucking Horse Sale rodeo tradition.

## Who Should Move to Bigfork?

* Retirees and affluent professionals looking for a scenic, upscale lakeside community.
* Boaters, sailors, and water sports enthusiasts who want to live on the largest natural freshwater lake in the West.
* Art lovers who appreciate the town's galleries, summer playhouse, and cultural events.
* People who desire a resort-like atmosphere with high-end dining and boutique shopping.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Miles City is around $233,769, while in Bigfork it is closer to $758,435, a difference of roughly $524,666, making Miles City significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Miles City averages about $750 per month, compared to roughly $1,103 in Bigfork.

Miles City's overall cost of living differs from Bigfork's. Miles City offers lower average salaries compared to Bigfork, with a median household income of $60,732 compared to Bigfork's $82,648.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Miles City is quiet and agricultural in character, with a lifestyle shaped by ranching seasons and rodeo. It sits along the Yellowstone River, looking east toward the open plains. Bigfork feels like a curated resort village, where life centers around the water, the arts, and seasonal tourism. The downtown is polished and walkable, filled with galleries and cafes, offering a lifestyle that is significantly more upscale and leisure-focused than most Montana towns.

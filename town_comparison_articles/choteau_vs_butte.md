# Choteau vs Butte, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Choteau and Butte offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Choteau is a scenic gateway town to the Rocky Mountain Front with 1,721 people, its spectacular views of the Front, its rich dinosaur paleontology history, and quick access to the Bob Marshall Wilderness and Freezout Lake. Butte, with 34,768 residents, is a historic mining city known for its rich labor history, its stunning historic architecture, and the Continental Divide and surrounding national forests.

## Who Should Move to Choteau?

* Outdoor enthusiasts who want uncrowded access to the Bob Marshall Wilderness.
* Birders and wildlife watchers drawn to the spectacular migrations at nearby Freezout Lake.
* People seeking a quiet, affordable community with some of the best mountain views in the state.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle.

## Who Should Move to Butte?

* History lovers who appreciate historic homes and architecture.
* People looking for very affordable mountain living.
* Those who want a town with a strong, independent character.
* Engineering students attending Montana Tech.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Choteau is around $288,323, while in Butte it is closer to $250,000, a difference of roughly $38,323, making Butte significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Choteau averages about $713 per month, compared to roughly $800 in Butte.

Choteau's overall cost of living differs from Butte's. Choteau offers higher average salaries compared to Butte, with a median household income of $53,929 compared to Butte's $50,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Choteau is a quiet, stunningly located town that serves as the Front Porch of the Rockies. It offers a slow-paced, affordable lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture, paleontology, and the incredible wilderness right in its backyard. Butte is fiercely proud of its working-class roots, offering a unique, historic atmosphere that is distinct from the rest of the state.

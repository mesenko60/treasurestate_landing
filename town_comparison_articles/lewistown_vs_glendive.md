# Lewistown vs Glendive: Which Montana Town is Right for You?

Lewistown and Glendive are both small, traditional Montana communities with affordable housing and a strong sense of place, but they sit in very different parts of the state and offer different outdoor recreation profiles. Lewistown is the Geographic Center of Montana, a historic ranching and agricultural community of 6,204 in Fergus County at 3,934 feet, with the nearest airport in Great Falls 115 miles away, 37 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 17 campgrounds, 7 fishing access sites, 3 lakes, 3 nature reserves, 2 golf courses, 2 museums, 2 state parks, 1 hot spring, and a 1,200-student school district. Glendive is the Paddlefish Capital of the World, a small eastern Montana community of 4,873 in Dawson County at 2,083 feet on the Yellowstone River near the North Dakota border, with its own Dawson Community Airport just 6 miles away, 30 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 7 viewpoints, 6 museums, 4 fishing access sites, 3 campgrounds, 3 lakes, 2 state parks, 1 historic site, 1 nature reserve, 1 trailhead, 1 disc golf course, 1 golf course, and a 1,000-student school district.

## Who Should Move to Lewistown?

*   Retirees and remote workers who want a quiet, self-contained central Montana community with a genuine small-town character, affordable housing, and wide-open central Montana skies
*   Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want 17 campgrounds, 7 fishing access sites, 3 nature reserves, 2 state parks, and a hot spring within 30 miles in the heart of central Montana's ranch country
*   History buffs and people drawn to Montana's ranching and agricultural heritage, with the Central Montana Historical Society and Museum, the Lewistown Art Center, and the surrounding Judith Basin country
*   Budget-conscious buyers who want one of the most affordable housing markets in Montana, with a typical home value of $268,126 and rent averaging just $896 per month

## Who Should Move to Glendive?

*   Anglers who want to live in the Paddlefish Capital of the World, with 4 fishing access sites on the Yellowstone River and access to the famous paddlefish snagging season that draws visitors from across the country each spring
*   Outdoor and geology enthusiasts who want access to Makoshika State Park, Montana's largest state park with dramatic badlands formations, dinosaur fossils, and 7 viewpoints within 30 miles
*   Buyers who want the most affordable housing market in this comparison, with a typical home value of $185,447 and rent averaging just $832 per month, along with a median household income of $71,063
*   People who want a small eastern Montana community with its own regional airport just 6 miles away, 6 museums, and a 1,000-student school district in a community with a strong Yellowstone River and badlands identity

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Glendive is more affordable than Lewistown in terms of home prices. The typical home value in Glendive is $185,447, compared to $268,126 in Lewistown, about $83,000 less. Rent is slightly lower in Glendive at $832 per month versus $896 in Lewistown.

Median household income is notably higher in Glendive at $71,063 versus $44,195 in Lewistown, a difference that likely reflects Glendive's oil and gas economy and its proximity to the Williston Basin energy sector. Both communities are genuinely affordable by Montana standards, but Glendive offers a lower entry price for buyers and a higher average income.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Lewistown is a quiet, traditional central Montana community where ranching, agriculture, the Judith Basin, and a deep sense of place define daily life. Glendive is a small eastern Montana community where the Yellowstone River, Makoshika State Park's badlands, the paddlefish season, and proximity to the North Dakota oil patch define community character. Lewistown has 17 campgrounds, a hot spring, and 3 nature reserves that reflect its central Montana ranch country setting. Glendive has 7 viewpoints, 6 museums, and Makoshika State Park, which is one of the most visually dramatic state parks in Montana. Both towns are quiet, affordable, and traditional in character. If you want a central Montana community with strong ranching heritage and 17 campgrounds within 30 miles, Lewistown is a solid choice. If you want an eastern Montana community with its own airport, badlands scenery, and world-class paddlefish fishing on the Yellowstone, Glendive offers a genuinely distinctive Montana lifestyle.

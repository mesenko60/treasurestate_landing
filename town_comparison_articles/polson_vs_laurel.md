# Polson vs Laurel, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Polson and Laurel offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Polson is a scenic lakeside community with 5,335 people, its location on the southern shore of Flathead Lake, its cherry orchards, and quick access to Flathead Lake and the Mission Mountains. Laurel, with just 7,215 residents, is a railroad and refinery town known for its industrial economy, its position as a transportation hub, and the Yellowstone River and nearby Beartooth Mountains.

## Who Should Move to Polson?

* Retirees and remote workers seeking a beautiful lakeside setting with a slower pace of life.
* Boaters, anglers, and water sports enthusiasts who want immediate access to the largest natural freshwater lake in the West.
* People looking for a more affordable alternative to the resort towns of the northern Flathead Valley.
* Those who appreciate a community with a strong agricultural heritage, particularly its famous cherry harvest.

## Who Should Move to Laurel?

* Industrial workers, railroad employees, and tradespeople looking for solid employment opportunities.
* Families who want a smaller school district and community feel while remaining just 15 minutes from Billings.
* Homebuyers seeking more affordable real estate than what is typically found in the Billings city limits.
* People who want easy access to the Beartooth Highway and the recreational opportunities south of the Yellowstone River.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Polson is around $415,000, while in Laurel it is closer to $369,826, a difference of roughly $45,174, making Laurel significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Polson averages about $1,050 per month, compared to roughly $1,160 in Laurel.

Polson's overall cost of living differs from Laurel's. Polson offers lower average salaries compared to Laurel, with a median household income of $52,500 compared to Laurel's $66,382.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Polson offers a relaxed, scenic lifestyle defined by its position on Flathead Lake. It lacks the polished resort feel of towns further north, maintaining a more grounded, agricultural character while still providing spectacular water and mountain access. Laurel is a hardworking, blue-collar community where the local economy is driven by the CHS refinery and the Montana Rail Link yard. It offers a slower, more traditional small-town pace than Billings, but residents frequently commute to the larger city for shopping, dining, and entertainment.

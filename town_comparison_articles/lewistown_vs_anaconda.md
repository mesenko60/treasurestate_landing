# Lewistown vs Anaconda: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Lewistown and Anaconda are two of Montana's most affordable small cities, both with strong community identities and a working-class character, but they sit in very different landscapes and carry very different histories. Lewistown is the Geographic Center of Montana, a quiet ranching community of 6,204 in Fergus County with 17 campgrounds within 30 miles and wide-open central Montana skies. Anaconda is the Smelter City, a community of 9,421 in Deer Lodge County at 5,279 feet elevation, with 134 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 48 lakes, Fairmont Hot Springs, and a history shaped by the Anaconda Copper Mining Company and the smelter stack that still dominates the skyline.

## Who Should Move to Lewistown?

*   Retirees and remote workers who want a quiet, affordable small-town lifestyle with a typical home value around $268,126 and a warmer climate, with July highs averaging 82°F versus Anaconda's 78°F
*   People who want a traditional, tight-knit ranching community with a genuine small-town character
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 17 campgrounds, 7 fishing access sites, and the Big Snowy Mountains within 30 miles
*   Anyone who wants to live at the geographic heart of Montana, away from the higher elevations and colder winters of western Montana

## Who Should Move to Anaconda?

*   Budget-conscious buyers who want western Montana at an exceptionally low price, with a median rent of just $661 per month, the lowest of any town in this comparison set
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 48 lakes, 19 campgrounds, 8 fishing access sites, a ski area, and a national forest within 30 miles
*   History enthusiasts drawn to the Anaconda Smelter Stack State Park, the Old Works Golf Course built on a Superfund remediation site, and the community's remarkable copper mining heritage
*   People who want easy access to Butte, Deer Lodge, and the Bitterroot Valley without paying Bozeman or Missoula prices

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Both towns are affordable by Montana standards, but Anaconda has the edge on rent. The typical home value in Lewistown is around $268,126, compared to $283,380 in Anaconda, a difference of just $15,000. Rent diverges more notably: Lewistown averages about $896 per month, while Anaconda comes in at just $661, making it one of the least expensive rental markets in the state.

The median household income in Anaconda is $49,533, compared to $44,195 in Lewistown. Both towns are below the Montana median, but Anaconda residents earn modestly more on average.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Lewistown is a town where the ranching calendar shapes daily life, the pace is slow, and the wide-open central Montana landscape is the main draw. Anaconda is a town with a story, a place where the copper industry built ornate Victorian architecture, the bust left a complex environmental legacy, and the community has responded with a stubborn pride and a growing outdoor recreation identity. If you want affordable, quiet, traditional small-town Montana at the geographic center of the state, Lewistown is a genuine option. If you want western Montana's mountain scenery at some of the lowest housing costs in the state, with a fascinating industrial history and 48 lakes within 30 miles, Anaconda is one of Montana's most underrated communities.

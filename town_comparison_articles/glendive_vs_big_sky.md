# Glendive vs Big Sky: Which Montana Town Is Right for You?

Glendive and Big Sky represent two of Montana's most contrasting communities. Glendive, the Paddlefish Capital of the World on the Yellowstone River in far eastern Montana, is a quiet prairie town of 4,873 people known for Makoshika State Park, paddlefish snagging season, and some of the most affordable housing in the state. Big Sky, The Biggest Skiing in America in the Gallatin Canyon, is a resort community of 3,591 people built around world-class skiing and outdoor recreation, with some of the highest home prices in Montana.

## Who Should Move to Glendive?

- People looking for very affordable housing and a low cost of living in a quiet, unpretentious community
- Anglers who want the Yellowstone River and the unique experience of paddlefish snagging season each spring
- Fossil and geology enthusiasts who want Makoshika State Park, Montana's largest state park, as a backyard
- Those who prefer eastern Montana's wide-open prairie landscapes and a traditional, community-centered way of life

## Who Should Move to Big Sky?

- Skiers and snowboarders who want one of the largest ski resorts in North America as a literal backyard
- High-income remote workers, retirees, or second-home buyers who want a luxury mountain lifestyle
- Outdoor enthusiasts who want dramatic Gallatin Canyon scenery, world-class hiking, hot springs, and proximity to Yellowstone National Park
- People who want a rapidly growing resort community with excellent restaurants and a sophisticated year-round energy

## Cost of Living Breakdown

The housing gap between these two towns is staggering. The typical home value in Big Sky is $1,819,905 compared to just $185,447 in Glendive, a difference of over $1.6 million. Rent is also dramatically higher in Big Sky at $1,712 per month versus $832 in Glendive. Big Sky's median household income of $103,625 is higher than Glendive's $71,063, but the housing cost gap is so extreme that Glendive is in a completely different affordability category. Glendive also has a significant advantage in airport access, with its own small regional airport just 6 miles from town, while Big Sky's nearest airport is in Bozeman, 44 miles away.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

These two towns could hardly be more different. Glendive is a quiet, self-contained prairie community where the Yellowstone River, the badlands of Makoshika, and the annual paddlefish season define the rhythm of life. Big Sky is a resort town that has grown dramatically in recent years, drawing wealthy transplants, remote workers, and outdoor enthusiasts who want a high-end mountain lifestyle in the Gallatin Canyon. One town is among the most affordable in Montana. The other is among the most expensive. Both are genuinely Montana, but they serve entirely different markets and lifestyles.

Glendive has 30 recreational sites within 30 miles, anchored by Makoshika State Park, 4 fishing access points, and 7 scenic viewpoints. Big Sky has 140 sites, including Big Sky Resort, 3 hot springs, 23 trailheads, 17 waterfalls, and the Lee Metcalf Wilderness. For outdoor recreation volume and variety, Big Sky is in a different league. For badlands scenery and river fishing at an accessible price, Glendive offers something genuinely distinctive.

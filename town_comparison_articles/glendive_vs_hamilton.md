# Glendive vs Hamilton: Which Montana Town Is Right for You?

Glendive and Hamilton are two of Montana's most distinctive small towns, separated by the full width of the state and by almost everything else. Glendive, the Paddlefish Capital of the World on the Yellowstone River in far eastern Montana, is a prairie town of 4,873 people known for Makoshika State Park, paddlefish snagging season, and very affordable housing. Hamilton, the Banana Belt of Montana in the Bitterroot Valley, is a town of 4,659 people known for its unusually mild and sunny climate, the legendary Bitterroot River, and direct access to the Bitterroot National Forest.

## Who Should Move to Glendive?

- People looking for very affordable housing and a low cost of living in a friendly, unpretentious community
- Anglers who want the Yellowstone River and the unique experience of paddlefish snagging season each spring
- Fossil and geology enthusiasts who want Makoshika State Park, Montana's largest state park, as a backyard
- Those who prefer eastern Montana's wide-open prairie landscapes and a traditional, community-centered way of life

## Who Should Move to Hamilton?

- Fly fishers who want the Bitterroot River, one of Montana's most celebrated trout streams, as a daily resource
- Remote workers, retirees, or families seeking a mild, sunny climate in a beautiful valley setting
- Hikers and backpackers who want direct access to the Bitterroot National Forest and an exceptional network of trailheads
- People who want a relaxed, unhurried small-town lifestyle with more outdoor variety than most Montana towns of similar size can offer

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Hamilton is significantly more expensive. The typical home value is $516,033 compared to just $185,447 in Glendive, a difference of over $330,000. Rent is similar, with Hamilton at $958 per month and Glendive at $832. Glendive's median household income of $71,063 is actually higher than Hamilton's $52,917, making Glendive notably more affordable in real terms. For buyers on a budget, Glendive offers some of the most accessible housing in Montana.

Glendive has its own small regional airport about 6 miles from town. Hamilton's nearest airport is in Missoula, 56 miles to the north.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Glendive is a quiet, self-contained prairie town where the river and the badlands define daily life. Makoshika's otherworldly landscape and the annual paddlefish season give it a character that is genuinely distinctive in Montana. Hamilton is a valley town with a warmer, sunnier feel than most of western Montana, and the Bitterroot National Forest provides an exceptional outdoor playground right at the edge of town. Both towns are unhurried and community-centered, but they offer very different landscapes and very different price points.

Glendive has 30 recreational sites within 30 miles, anchored by Makoshika State Park, 4 fishing access points, and 7 scenic viewpoints. Hamilton has 194 sites, including 47 trailheads, 55 lakes, 32 campgrounds, 25 fishing access points, and a hot spring. For sheer outdoor variety, Hamilton is in a different league. For badlands scenery and river fishing in eastern Montana, Glendive offers something you cannot find anywhere else.

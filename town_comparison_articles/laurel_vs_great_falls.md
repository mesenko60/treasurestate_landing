# Laurel vs Great Falls: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Laurel and Great Falls are both central Montana towns with similar climates and a shared blue-collar character, but they differ considerably in size and what they offer day to day. Laurel is the City Between the Yellowstone and the Rimrocks, a community of 7,215 in Yellowstone County with affordable housing, 21 fishing access sites within 30 miles, and a quiet, small-town feel just outside Billings. Great Falls is the Electric City, a city of 60,000 on the Missouri River in Cascade County, with 10 museums within 30 miles, 6 waterfalls, a significant military presence at Malmstrom Air Force Base, and a regional economy that serves north-central Montana.

## Who Should Move to Laurel?

*   Budget-conscious families and buyers who want Yellowstone County living with a typical home value around $369,826 and rent averaging about $1,160 per month
*   People who want a small, quiet community with Billings' full-service amenities just 18 miles away
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 21 fishing access sites, 6 state parks, and easy Yellowstone River access within 30 miles
*   Anyone who wants a tight-knit, working-class Montana community with a low-key, unpretentious character

## Who Should Move to Great Falls?

*   Military families stationed at Malmstrom Air Force Base and civilians who support the base economy
*   History and culture enthusiasts drawn to the C.M. Russell Museum, the Lewis and Clark Interpretive Center, and 10 museums within 30 miles
*   Families who want a full-service city with a 10,000-student school district and a broad range of services
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want the Missouri River, 6 waterfalls, and the River's Edge Trail system all within 30 miles

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Laurel is slightly more expensive on housing but cheaper on rent. The typical home value in Laurel is around $369,826, compared to $328,554 in Great Falls, making Great Falls one of the more affordable mid-sized cities in Montana. Rent runs in the opposite direction: Laurel averages about $1,160 per month, while Great Falls comes in at roughly $1,340.

The income figures are nearly identical. The median household income in Laurel is $66,382, compared to $63,934 in Great Falls. Both towns have similar earning profiles, and both offer a cost of living that is well below Montana's resort-corridor cities.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Laurel is a quiet, practical river town where the pace is slow and the Yellowstone is always nearby. Great Falls is a regional hub with a stronger sense of civic identity, a river trail system that winds along the Missouri, and a downtown that has been working to reinvent itself around arts and culture. If you want small-town affordability with Billings close by, Laurel is a solid choice. If you want a mid-sized Montana city with a river, a museum district, and a broader range of services, Great Falls offers more for a similar price.

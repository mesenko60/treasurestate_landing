# Malta vs Belgrade, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Malta and Belgrade offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Malta is a historic agricultural town on the Hi-Line with 2,100 people, its world-class dinosaur fossils, its deep ranching heritage, and quick access to the Bowdoin National Wildlife Refuge and the Missouri River Breaks. Belgrade, with 12,509 residents, is a rapidly growing hub in the Gallatin Valley known for proximity to Bozeman, the Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport, and the Bridger Mountains and Gallatin River.

## Who Should Move to Malta?

* Workers in agriculture, government, or local services looking for a quiet, stable community.
* People seeking very affordable housing and a low overall cost of living.
* Hunters, birders, and outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the vast, uncrowded public lands of northeastern Montana.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle on the high plains.

## Who Should Move to Belgrade?

* Families and professionals who work in Bozeman but want slightly more affordable housing options.
* Frequent travelers who appreciate being minutes away from Montana's busiest airport.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want quick access to the Bridger Mountains, Big Sky, and blue-ribbon trout streams.
* People looking for a community with a strong local identity that still benefits from the economic engine of the Gallatin Valley.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Malta is around $244,540, while in Belgrade it is closer to $544,626, a difference of roughly $300,086, making Malta significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Malta averages about $693 per month, compared to roughly $2,206 in Belgrade.

Malta's overall cost of living differs from Belgrade's. Malta offers lower average salaries compared to Belgrade, with a median household income of $59,449 compared to Belgrade's $88,896.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Malta is a quiet, resilient community on the eastern Hi-Line, defined by agriculture, wide-open spaces, and its unique paleontology attractions. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle far from the state's urban centers. Belgrade feels like a busy, practical town that is absorbing much of the Gallatin Valley's growth. It has its own distinct community pride, centered around local schools and events, but its daily rhythm is closely tied to the economic hum of neighboring Bozeman and the constant activity at the airport.

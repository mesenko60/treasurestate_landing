# Columbus vs Belgrade, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Columbus and Belgrade offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Columbus is a scenic river town in the Stillwater Valley with 2,015 people, its position at the confluence of the Yellowstone and Stillwater rivers, its proximity to the Stillwater Mine, and quick access to the Absaroka-Beartooth Wilderness. Belgrade, with 12,509 residents, is a rapidly growing hub in the Gallatin Valley known for proximity to Bozeman, the Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport, and the Bridger Mountains and Gallatin River.

## Who Should Move to Columbus?

* Workers in the mining or industrial sectors looking for steady, high-paying employment.
* Anglers and boaters who want immediate access to two of Montana's premier rivers.
* People seeking a scenic, small-town lifestyle with easy highway access to the amenities of Billings.
* Outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the rugged beauty of the Absaroka-Beartooth Wilderness.

## Who Should Move to Belgrade?

* Families and professionals who work in Bozeman but want slightly more affordable housing options.
* Frequent travelers who appreciate being minutes away from Montana's busiest airport.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want quick access to the Bridger Mountains, Big Sky, and blue-ribbon trout streams.
* People looking for a community with a strong local identity that still benefits from the economic engine of the Gallatin Valley.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Columbus is around $428,574, while in Belgrade it is closer to $544,626, a difference of roughly $116,052, making Columbus significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Columbus averages about $926 per month, compared to roughly $2,206 in Belgrade.

Columbus's overall cost of living differs from Belgrade's. Columbus offers lower average salaries compared to Belgrade, with a median household income of $62,500 compared to Belgrade's $88,896.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Columbus is a beautiful, thriving town that balances a strong industrial economy driven by the nearby palladium mine with spectacular outdoor recreation. It offers a relaxed, scenic lifestyle with excellent access to both the mountains and the city of Billings. Belgrade feels like a busy, practical town that is absorbing much of the Gallatin Valley's growth. It has its own distinct community pride, centered around local schools and events, but its daily rhythm is closely tied to the economic hum of neighboring Bozeman and the constant activity at the airport.

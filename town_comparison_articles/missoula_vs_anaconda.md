# Missoula vs Anaconda: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Missoula and Anaconda are both western Montana communities, but they represent opposite ends of the spectrum in terms of size, cost, and lifestyle. Missoula is the Garden City, a progressive university city of 77,757 with a vibrant downtown, a rich arts scene, and one of the most impressive outdoor recreation landscapes in the state. Anaconda is the Smelter City, a small, historically rich community of 9,421 at 5,279 feet in the mountains of Deer Lodge County, where the copper-smelting past is still visible in the skyline and the surrounding landscape offers 134 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles.

## Who Should Move to Missoula?

*   Students, academics, and professionals connected to the University of Montana and its broader economy
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 65 fishing access sites, 49 trailheads, and 2 wilderness areas within 30 miles
*   People who value a culturally active, progressive city with independent businesses, live music, and a walkable downtown
*   Anyone who wants the full amenities of a mid-sized city, hospitals, major retailers, a regional airport, in a mountain setting

## Who Should Move to Anaconda?

*   Remote workers and retirees seeking some of Montana's most affordable housing in a scenic mountain setting
*   History enthusiasts drawn to Anaconda's copper-boom heritage, including the iconic Anaconda Smelter Stack State Park
*   Outdoor lovers who want hot springs at Fairmont, mountain lakes, wilderness, and ski access without city prices
*   People who want a quiet, tight-knit community with a strong working-class identity and genuine Montana character

## Cost of Living Breakdown

The difference in cost of living between these two communities is striking. The median home price in Missoula is around $554,389, while in Anaconda it's closer to $283,380, nearly half the cost. The rent gap is even more dramatic: about $1,486 per month in Missoula versus just $661 in Anaconda.

Missoula's median household income of $65,329 is significantly higher than Anaconda's $49,533, but the income gap is far smaller than the housing cost gap. For anyone with remote income, Anaconda's affordability represents a genuine lifestyle opportunity.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Missoula is busy, culturally engaged, and always moving, a city that takes its outdoor culture, its arts scene, and its politics seriously. Anaconda operates at a completely different pace. It's a town that has made peace with its industrial past and found a new identity in its natural surroundings. The Old Works Golf Course, a Jack Nicklaus design built on the old smelter works, using the industrial ruins as course features, is a symbol of that reinvention. Missoula is where you go for the full Montana city experience; Anaconda is where you go for affordable mountain living with a history that most Montana towns simply can't match.

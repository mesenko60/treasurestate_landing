# Hamilton vs Big Sky: Which Montana Town Is Right for You?

Hamilton and Big Sky are both exceptional outdoor destinations in western Montana, but they attract very different residents and carry very different price tags. Hamilton, the Banana Belt of Montana in the Bitterroot Valley, is a town of 4,659 people known for its mild climate, the Bitterroot River, and the Bitterroot National Forest. Big Sky, The Biggest Skiing in America in the Gallatin Canyon, is a resort community of 3,591 people built around one of the largest ski mountains in North America, with home prices that place it among the most expensive communities in the state.

## Who Should Move to Hamilton?

- Outdoor enthusiasts who want the Bitterroot National Forest, 47 trailheads, 55 lakes, and 25 fishing access points all within 30 miles
- Remote workers, retirees, or families who want a mild, sunny climate and a genuine small-town community at a fraction of resort-town prices
- Fly fishers and hikers who want a wide variety of rivers, trails, and backcountry access in one of western Montana's most scenic valleys
- People who want a welcoming, community-centered town with a strong outdoor culture and a growing arts scene

## Who Should Move to Big Sky?

- Skiers and snowboarders who want one of the largest ski resorts in North America as a literal backyard
- High-income remote workers, retirees, or second-home buyers who want a luxury mountain lifestyle in the Gallatin Canyon
- Outdoor enthusiasts who want dramatic canyon scenery, world-class hiking, hot springs, and proximity to Yellowstone National Park
- People who want a rapidly growing resort community with excellent restaurants and a sophisticated year-round energy

## Cost of Living Breakdown

The housing gap between these two towns is enormous. The typical home value in Big Sky is $1,819,905 compared to $516,033 in Hamilton, a difference of over $1.3 million. Rent is also dramatically higher in Big Sky at $1,712 per month versus $958 in Hamilton. Big Sky's median household income of $103,625 is nearly double Hamilton's $52,917, reflecting the resort economy and the high-earning remote workers it attracts. Even so, the housing cost gap is so extreme that Hamilton is in a completely different affordability category. Hamilton's nearest airport is in Missoula at 56 miles. Big Sky's is in Bozeman, 44 miles away.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Hamilton and Big Sky are both outstanding places to live for outdoor enthusiasts, but they serve very different markets. Hamilton is a lush, green valley town with a mild climate, a strong community identity, and a growing but still accessible real estate market. Big Sky is a resort town that has grown dramatically in recent years, drawing wealthy transplants and remote workers who want a high-end mountain lifestyle in the Gallatin Canyon. Hamilton has 194 recreational sites within 30 miles to Big Sky's 140, with a particular advantage in lakes, trailheads, and fishing access. Big Sky leads in skiing, hot springs, and waterfalls. Both towns are exceptional, but Hamilton is the more affordable and more community-centered choice.

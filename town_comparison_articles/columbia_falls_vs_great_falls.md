# Columbia Falls vs Great Falls: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Columbia Falls and Great Falls sit on opposite ends of the Montana experience. Columbia Falls is the Gateway to Glacier, a small community of 5,308 in Flathead County at 3,077 feet elevation, with 214 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 48 campgrounds, 31 trailheads, 19 ski areas, and Glacier National Park just up the road. Great Falls is the Electric City, a city of 60,000 on the Missouri River in Cascade County at 3,340 feet, with 45 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 10 museums, 6 waterfalls, 5 state parks, and a stable economy anchored by Malmstrom Air Force Base and regional healthcare.

## Who Should Move to Columbia Falls?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 48 campgrounds, 31 trailheads, 27 fishing access sites, 19 ski areas, and Glacier National Park all within 30 miles
*   Families and remote workers who want a small Flathead Valley community with Kalispell's services just 6 miles away
*   People who want northwestern Montana's mountain and lake scenery, with 26 lakes within 30 miles, as their daily backdrop
*   Anyone who wants a small-town feel with one of the most extraordinary outdoor recreation footprints in the entire state

## Who Should Move to Great Falls?

*   Military families and government employees drawn to Malmstrom Air Force Base and the stable employment base it anchors
*   Budget-conscious buyers who want a mid-sized Montana city at a significantly lower price, with a typical home value of $328,554 versus Columbia Falls's $584,580
*   History and culture enthusiasts who want 10 museums, including the C.M. Russell Museum, and the Lewis and Clark National Historic Trail along the Missouri River
*   Families who want a full-service city with a 10,000-student school district, a regional airport, and the services of a genuine Montana hub city

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Great Falls is considerably more affordable on home prices. The typical home value in Columbia Falls is around $584,580, compared to $328,554 in Great Falls, a difference of over $256,000. Rent is also lower in Great Falls: Columbia Falls averages about $1,003 per month, while Great Falls comes in at roughly $1,340, about $337 more per month in Columbia Falls.

The median household incomes are close: $65,313 in Columbia Falls and $63,934 in Great Falls. The income difference is modest, but the housing price gap is substantial, making Great Falls a much more accessible purchase market.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Columbia Falls is a small gateway community where Glacier National Park, the Flathead River, and the surrounding mountains shape daily life. Great Falls is a working city on the Missouri River with a strong military presence, a genuine arts community anchored by the C.M. Russell Museum, and a landscape defined by the high plains and the river corridor. If you want northwestern Montana's extraordinary outdoor recreation and the Flathead Valley's natural setting, Columbia Falls is a compelling base. If you want a mid-sized Montana city with affordable housing, a stable economy, and the Missouri River as your backyard, Great Falls offers a quality of life that is easy to underestimate.

# Sidney vs Columbia Falls, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Sidney and Columbia Falls offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Sidney is a bustling agricultural and energy hub with 6,173 people, its position in the Bakken oil field, its rich farming heritage in the Yellowstone Valley, and quick access to the Yellowstone River and eastern Montana badlands. Columbia Falls, with just 5,308 residents, is a gateway community to Glacier National Park known for its timber industry roots, its revitalized downtown, and the Flathead River and Glacier National Park.

## Who Should Move to Sidney?

* Workers in the energy, agriculture, or transportation sectors looking for strong employment opportunities.
* People seeking a hardworking, practical community with a resilient local economy.
* Those who want affordable housing compared to western Montana, with higher average wages driven by the oil and gas industry.
* Hunters and anglers who appreciate the rugged, uncrowded access to the Yellowstone River and surrounding plains.

## Who Should Move to Columbia Falls?

* Outdoor lovers who want to live minutes from the entrance to Glacier National Park.
* Families looking for a community with a strong local spirit and a growing downtown scene.
* People who work in the Flathead Valley but prefer a town with a slightly more blue-collar, unpretentious history than Whitefish.
* Those who want immediate access to the Flathead River for fishing, floating, and recreation.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Sidney is around $241,500, while in Columbia Falls it is closer to $584,580, a difference of roughly $343,080, making Sidney significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Sidney averages about $850 per month, compared to roughly $1,003 in Columbia Falls.

Sidney's overall cost of living differs from Columbia Falls's. Sidney offers higher average salaries compared to Columbia Falls, with a median household income of $75,200 compared to Columbia Falls's $65,313.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Sidney is a dynamic, industrious town where the pace of life is closely tied to the energy markets and agricultural seasons. It has a practical, no-nonsense atmosphere, serving as a major economic engine for eastern Montana while maintaining its roots as a tight-knit farming community. Columbia Falls has transitioned from a traditional timber town into a vibrant gateway community, balancing its working-class roots with a new influx of breweries, markets, and tourism. It feels less resort-focused than neighboring Whitefish, maintaining a strong, independent local character while serving as the front porch to Glacier National Park.

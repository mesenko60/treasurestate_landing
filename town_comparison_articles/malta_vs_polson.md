# Malta vs Polson, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Malta and Polson offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Malta is a historic agricultural town on the Hi-Line with 2,100 people, its world-class dinosaur fossils, its deep ranching heritage, and quick access to the Bowdoin National Wildlife Refuge and the Missouri River Breaks. Polson, with 5,335 residents, is a scenic lakeside community known for its location on the southern shore of Flathead Lake, its cherry orchards, and Flathead Lake and the Mission Mountains.

## Who Should Move to Malta?

* Workers in agriculture, government, or local services looking for a quiet, stable community.
* People seeking very affordable housing and a low overall cost of living.
* Hunters, birders, and outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the vast, uncrowded public lands of northeastern Montana.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle on the high plains.

## Who Should Move to Polson?

* Retirees and remote workers seeking a beautiful lakeside setting with a slower pace of life.
* Boaters, anglers, and water sports enthusiasts who want immediate access to the largest natural freshwater lake in the West.
* People looking for a more affordable alternative to the resort towns of the northern Flathead Valley.
* Those who appreciate a community with a strong agricultural heritage, particularly its famous cherry harvest.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Malta is around $244,540, while in Polson it is closer to $415,000, a difference of roughly $170,460, making Malta significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Malta averages about $693 per month, compared to roughly $1,050 in Polson.

Malta's overall cost of living differs from Polson's. Malta offers higher average salaries compared to Polson, with a median household income of $59,449 compared to Polson's $52,500.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Malta is a quiet, resilient community on the eastern Hi-Line, defined by agriculture, wide-open spaces, and its unique paleontology attractions. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle far from the state's urban centers. Polson offers a relaxed, scenic lifestyle defined by its position on Flathead Lake. It lacks the polished resort feel of towns further north, maintaining a more grounded, agricultural character while still providing spectacular water and mountain access.

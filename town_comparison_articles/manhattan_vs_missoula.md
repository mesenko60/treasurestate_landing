# Manhattan vs Missoula, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Manhattan and Missoula offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Manhattan is a rapidly growing agricultural town in the Gallatin Valley with 2,288 people, its historic role as the Seed Potato Capital of the World, its booming residential growth, and quick access to the Gallatin River and the Bridger Mountains. Missoula, with 77,757 residents, is a vibrant university town known for its craft breweries, its bustling downtown, and world-class hiking and skiing.

## Who Should Move to Manhattan?

* Professionals working in Bozeman who want a smaller-town feel and are willing to commute.
* Families looking for excellent local schools and a safe, tight-knit community.
* People who appreciate a town that still honors its agricultural heritage despite rapid modernization.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want immediate access to the world-class fishing of the Gallatin River.

## Who Should Move to Missoula?

* Young professionals and families who want good job opportunities.
* People who love a lively downtown with restaurants, breweries, and events.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want to be close to the mountains for hiking, skiing, and rafting.
* Anyone who wants a university town vibe with younger energy.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Manhattan is around $652,619, while in Missoula it is closer to $554,389, a difference of roughly $98,230, making Missoula significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Manhattan averages about $1,700 per month, compared to roughly $1,486 in Missoula.

Manhattan's overall cost of living differs from Missoula's. Manhattan offers higher average salaries compared to Missoula, with a median household income of $69,671 compared to Missoula's $65,329.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Manhattan blends its deep agricultural roots with its new reality as a highly desirable, expensive suburb of Bozeman. It maintains a charming, historic downtown and a strong community identity, even as it experiences significant growth and rising costs. Missoula feels like a real city in the mountains, with busy sidewalks, weekly farmers markets, live music, and constant activity.

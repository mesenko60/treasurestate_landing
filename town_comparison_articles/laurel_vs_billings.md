# Laurel vs Billings: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Laurel and Billings share the same county and the same broad Yellowstone River valley, but they offer very different day-to-day experiences. Laurel is the City Between the Yellowstone and the Rimrocks, a community of 7,215 that sits 18 miles west of Billings, with a quiet, working-class character, 21 fishing access sites within 30 miles, and some of the most affordable housing in the Yellowstone corridor. Billings is the Magic City, Montana's largest city at 117,116 people, with a full-service economy, a 16,000-student school district, major hospitals, and a commercial and cultural infrastructure that no other Montana city can match.

## Who Should Move to Laurel?

*   Budget-conscious families and first-time buyers who want Yellowstone County living at a lower price, with a typical home value around $369,826
*   People who want a quiet, small-town feel with easy access to Billings' amenities just 18 miles away
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 21 fishing access sites and 6 state parks within 30 miles
*   Anyone who wants a tight-knit community with a lower cost of living and a practical, unpretentious character

## Who Should Move to Billings?

*   Professionals in healthcare, energy, agriculture, or retail who want Montana's strongest and most diverse job market
*   Families who want a full-service city with major hospitals, big-box retail, and a 16,000-student school district
*   People who want a broad range of restaurants, entertainment, and cultural amenities for a Montana city
*   Anyone who wants the convenience of Montana's largest city at a cost of living that remains reasonable by western standards

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Laurel is the more affordable option, though the gap is not enormous. The typical home value in Laurel is around $369,826, compared to $390,654 in Billings, a difference of about $21,000. Rent is where Laurel's advantage is clearest: Laurel averages about $1,160 per month, compared to roughly $1,383 in Billings, a savings of over $200 per month.

The income gap is modest. The median household income in Laurel is $66,382, compared to $71,855 in Billings. Billings residents earn slightly more on average, but Laurel's lower costs make the two towns fairly comparable in terms of purchasing power.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Laurel is a no-frills, friendly small town where the Yellowstone River is never far away and life moves at an unhurried pace. Billings is a real city with the energy and infrastructure to match, from the Yellowstone Art Museum to the MetraPark events center to a downtown that has been steadily reinventing itself. The two towns are close enough that Laurel residents regularly drive into Billings for work, shopping, and entertainment. If you want small-town affordability with a city nearby, Laurel is a smart base. If you want to be in the middle of everything Montana's largest city has to offer, Billings is the obvious choice.

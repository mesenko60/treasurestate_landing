# Belgrade vs Anaconda: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Belgrade and Anaconda could not be more different in character, cost, or history. Belgrade is the Hub of the Gallatin Valley, a fast-growing community of 12,509 with a booming housing market, strong professional incomes, and the energy of a town that is still building itself. Anaconda is the Smelter City, a community of 9,421 in Deer Lodge County with one of the most dramatic industrial histories in the American West, 48 lakes within 30 miles, the world-famous Old Works Golf Course built on a former copper smelter site, and some of the most affordable housing in western Montana.

## Who Should Move to Belgrade?

*   Families and professionals who want Gallatin Valley access with newer housing, strong schools, and a median household income of $88,896
*   Remote workers who want a fast-growing community with five-minute access to Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want a ski area, 21 fishing access sites, hot springs, and 16 trailheads all within 30 miles
*   Anyone drawn to the energy of a booming small city with a young, professional demographic

## Who Should Move to Anaconda?

*   Budget-conscious buyers and retirees who want western Montana at a dramatically lower price, with a typical home value around $283,380 and rent averaging just $661 per month
*   History enthusiasts drawn to Anaconda's copper smelting heritage, the Anaconda Smelter Stack State Park, and 16 museums within 30 miles
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 48 lakes, 19 campgrounds, and Fairmont Hot Springs all within 30 miles
*   Anyone who wants a quiet, tight-knit small-town community with a strong sense of local identity

## Cost of Living Breakdown

The cost difference between these two towns is among the most dramatic in Montana. The typical home value in Belgrade is around $544,626, compared to just $283,380 in Anaconda, nearly half the price. The rent gap is even more striking: Belgrade averages about $2,206 per month, while Anaconda comes in at roughly $661, making it one of the most affordable rental markets in the state.

The income gap is equally significant. The median household income in Belgrade is $88,896, compared to $49,533 in Anaconda. But in Anaconda, the cost of living is so low that everyday expenses are a fraction of what they are in the Gallatin Valley.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Belgrade is a town in motion, growing fast with new subdivisions and a demographic that skews young and professional. Anaconda moves at a completely different pace, a small city that has reinvented itself after the closure of the Anaconda Copper smelter, with a strong community identity, remarkable outdoor access, and a cost of living that makes it genuinely attractive for retirees and remote workers who want western Montana without the resort-corridor price tag. If you want the Gallatin Valley's economic energy and growth, Belgrade is the clear choice. If you want affordable, historically rich small-town Montana in a spectacular natural setting, Anaconda is one of the state's best-kept secrets.

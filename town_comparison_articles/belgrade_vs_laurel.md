# Belgrade vs Laurel: Which Montana Town is Right for You?

Belgrade and Laurel are both mid-sized Montana communities in the Yellowstone River corridor, but they serve very different residents. Belgrade is the Hub of the Gallatin Valley, a fast-growing suburb of 12,509 in Gallatin County at 4,462 feet, with Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport 5 miles away, 104 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 16 trailheads, 14 waterfalls, 15 lakes, 21 fishing access sites, 2 hot springs, a national forest, a ski area, and a 3,800-student school district. Laurel is the City Between the Yellowstone and the Rimrocks, a working-class railroad and refinery town of 7,215 in Yellowstone County at 3,307 feet, with Billings Logan International Airport 18 miles away, 60 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 21 fishing access sites, 10 museums, 6 state parks, 5 golf courses, and a 1,800-student school district.

## Who Should Move to Belgrade?

*   Families and professionals who want Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport 5 miles away, a 3,800-student school district, and all the amenities of the Bozeman metro area at a slightly lower price than Bozeman itself
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 16 trailheads, 14 waterfalls, 15 lakes, 21 fishing access sites, 2 hot springs, a ski area, and a national forest all within 30 miles, with quick access to the Bridger Mountains and Gallatin Canyon
*   Buyers who want a rapidly growing community with strong job access in the Gallatin Valley, with a typical home value of $544,626 and a median household income of $88,896
*   Remote workers and young professionals who want a suburban Gallatin Valley lifestyle with proximity to Bozeman's restaurants, university, and cultural amenities without paying Bozeman prices

## Who Should Move to Laurel?

*   Working families and blue-collar professionals who want an affordable, no-frills Montana community with strong access to Billings, the largest city in Montana, just 18 miles away
*   Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want 21 fishing access sites on the Yellowstone River, 6 state parks, 10 museums, and 5 golf courses all within 30 miles at a fraction of Gallatin Valley prices
*   Budget-conscious buyers who want to own a home in a real Montana community at a significantly lower cost, with a typical home value of $369,826 and rent averaging just $1,160 per month
*   Families who value a tight-knit small-town community with a 1,800-student school district and easy access to Billings' full range of services, hospitals, and employment

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Belgrade is significantly more expensive than Laurel. The typical home value in Belgrade is $544,626, compared to $369,826 in Laurel, nearly $175,000 more. Rent reflects an even larger gap: Belgrade averages $2,206 per month versus $1,160 in Laurel, almost double.

Median household income is higher in Belgrade at $88,896 versus $66,382 in Laurel. Belgrade's higher costs are driven by its position in the booming Gallatin Valley market. Laurel offers genuine affordability for buyers and renters who want to stay within reach of a major Montana city.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Belgrade feels like a prosperous, growing suburb of Bozeman, with new subdivisions, strong outdoor access, and a community identity tied closely to the Gallatin Valley's outdoor and university culture. Laurel is a working-class railroad town with a different kind of Montana character, where the Yellowstone River, the Rimrocks, and proximity to Billings define daily life. Belgrade has 16 trailheads and 14 waterfalls within 30 miles that Laurel cannot match. Laurel has 10 museums and 6 state parks that reflect its position in the heart of the Yellowstone Valley. If you want a fast-growing community in the Gallatin Valley with outstanding mountain access and a suburban feel, Belgrade is one of the best values in the Bozeman area. If you want an affordable, unpretentious Montana town with strong Yellowstone River access and easy reach of Billings, Laurel is a solid and underrated choice.

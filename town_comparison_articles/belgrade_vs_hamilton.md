# Belgrade vs Hamilton: Which Montana Town is Right for You?

Belgrade and Hamilton are both mid-sized western Montana communities with strong outdoor access, but they sit in different mountain valleys and serve different kinds of residents. Belgrade is the Hub of the Gallatin Valley, a fast-growing suburb of 12,509 in Gallatin County at 4,462 feet, with Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport 5 miles away, 104 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 16 trailheads, 14 waterfalls, 15 lakes, 21 fishing access sites, 2 hot springs, a national forest, a ski area, and a 3,800-student school district. Hamilton is the Banana Belt of Montana, a Bitterroot Valley community of 4,659 in Ravalli County at 3,573 feet, known for its mild winters and proximity to the Bitterroot National Forest, with the nearest airport in Missoula 56 miles away, 194 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 47 trailheads, 55 lakes, 32 campgrounds, 25 fishing access sites, 5 nature reserves, 3 disc golf courses, 3 historic sites, 1 wildlife refuge, and a 1,800-student school district.

## Who Should Move to Belgrade?

*   Families and professionals who want Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport 5 miles away, a 3,800-student school district, and all the amenities of the Bozeman metro area at a slightly lower price than Bozeman itself
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 16 trailheads, 14 waterfalls, 15 lakes, 21 fishing access sites, 2 hot springs, a ski area, and a national forest all within 30 miles, with quick access to the Bridger Mountains and Gallatin Canyon
*   Buyers who want a rapidly growing community with strong job access in the Gallatin Valley, with a typical home value of $544,626 and a median household income of $88,896
*   Remote workers and young professionals who want a suburban Gallatin Valley lifestyle with proximity to Bozeman's restaurants, university, and cultural amenities without paying Bozeman prices

## Who Should Move to Hamilton?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 47 trailheads, 55 lakes, 32 campgrounds, 25 fishing access sites, 5 nature reserves, and a wildlife refuge all within 30 miles in the Bitterroot Valley, one of the most scenically stunning and trail-rich corridors in Montana
*   People who want a mild western Montana climate, with January average highs of 40 degrees and annual snowfall of just 19.8 inches, making it one of the most temperate communities in the state
*   Buyers who want to live in the Bitterroot Valley at a price point similar to Belgrade, with a typical home value of $516,033, but with dramatically lower rent averaging just $958 per month
*   Families and retirees who want a smaller, quieter Bitterroot Valley community with a 1,800-student school district, 6 museums, and easy access to Missoula's full range of services 56 miles north

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Belgrade and Hamilton are surprisingly close in home purchase prices. The typical home value in Belgrade is $544,626, compared to $516,033 in Hamilton, a difference of about $28,000. Rent tells a very different story: Belgrade averages $2,206 per month versus $958 in Hamilton, more than double.

Median household income is significantly higher in Belgrade at $88,896 versus $52,917 in Hamilton. Hamilton's lower income reflects the Bitterroot Valley's more rural, service-oriented economy. For buyers and renters who want Bitterroot Valley outdoor access at a lower monthly cost, Hamilton offers strong value.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Belgrade feels like a prosperous, growing suburb of Bozeman, with new subdivisions, strong outdoor access, and a community identity tied closely to the Gallatin Valley's outdoor and university culture. Hamilton is a quieter, more traditional western Montana community where the Bitterroot River, the Selway-Bitterroot Wilderness, and a mild climate define daily life. Hamilton has 47 trailheads and 55 lakes within 30 miles compared to Belgrade's 16 trailheads and 15 lakes, making it one of the most trail-rich small towns in Montana. Belgrade has 14 waterfalls and a ski area that Hamilton does not. If you want a fast-growing Gallatin Valley community with outstanding mountain access and a suburban feel, Belgrade is one of the best values in the Bozeman area. If you want a mild, trail-rich Bitterroot Valley community with extraordinary outdoor diversity and a quieter pace of life, Hamilton is one of the most compelling small towns in western Montana.

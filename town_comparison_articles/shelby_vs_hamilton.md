# Shelby vs Hamilton, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Shelby and Hamilton offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Shelby is a historic railroad and agricultural town with 3,169 people, its position on the Hi-Line, its famous 1923 Dempsey-Gibbons heavyweight title fight, and quick access to the Sweet Grass Hills and Lake Elwell. Hamilton, with just 4,659 residents, is a scenic hub of the Bitterroot Valley known for its historic downtown, its relatively mild climate, and the Bitterroot Mountains and the Bitterroot River.

## Who Should Move to Shelby?

* Workers in the agriculture, railroad, or energy sectors looking for steady employment on the Hi-Line.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, rural setting.
* Those who appreciate the stark beauty of the northern plains and the close-knit community of a small agricultural town.
* History buffs interested in the unique legacy of the 1923 heavyweight championship fight.

## Who Should Move to Hamilton?

* Retirees and remote workers drawn to the stunning scenery and milder weather of the 'Banana Belt'.
* Fly fishing enthusiasts who want to live near the renowned waters of the Bitterroot River.
* People seeking a vibrant small-town community with a historic main street, local breweries, and community events.
* Outdoor adventurers who want immediate access to the rugged canyons and trails of the Bitterroot Selway Wilderness.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Shelby is around $185,000, while in Hamilton it is closer to $516,033, a difference of roughly $331,033, making Shelby significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Shelby averages about $700 per month, compared to roughly $958 in Hamilton.

Shelby's overall cost of living differs from Hamilton's. Shelby offers higher average salaries compared to Hamilton, with a median household income of $58,000 compared to Hamilton's $52,917.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Shelby is a resilient, hardworking town on the northern plains, where life is shaped by the railroad, agriculture, and the vast open spaces of the Hi-Line. It offers a quiet, practical lifestyle far removed from the state's major urban centers. Hamilton offers a highly desirable blend of spectacular mountain scenery and a charming, active community. The pace is relaxed but the town is lively, with a downtown that serves as a gathering place for locals and visitors alike, all set against the towering peaks of the Bitterroot Range.

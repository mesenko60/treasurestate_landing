# Cut Bank vs Laurel, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Cut Bank and Laurel offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Cut Bank is a windy agricultural town on the Hi-Line with 3,056 people, its proximity to the Blackfeet Indian Reservation, its agricultural and energy economy, and quick access to the eastern edge of Glacier National Park. Laurel, with just 7,215 residents, is a railroad and refinery town known for its industrial economy, its position as a transportation hub, and the Yellowstone River and nearby Beartooth Mountains.

## Who Should Move to Cut Bank?

* Workers in agriculture, energy, or government looking for affordable living on the Hi-Line.
* People seeking a quiet, resilient community with a low cost of living.
* Those who want to live near Glacier National Park without paying resort-town prices.
* Individuals who appreciate the stark, windy beauty of the northern plains.

## Who Should Move to Laurel?

* Industrial workers, railroad employees, and tradespeople looking for solid employment opportunities.
* Families who want a smaller school district and community feel while remaining just 15 minutes from Billings.
* Homebuyers seeking more affordable real estate than what is typically found in the Billings city limits.
* People who want easy access to the Beartooth Highway and the recreational opportunities south of the Yellowstone River.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Cut Bank is around $175,000, while in Laurel it is closer to $369,826, a difference of roughly $194,826, making Cut Bank significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Cut Bank averages about $650 per month, compared to roughly $1,160 in Laurel.

Cut Bank's overall cost of living differs from Laurel's. Cut Bank offers lower average salaries compared to Laurel, with a median household income of $52,000 compared to Laurel's $66,382.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Cut Bank is a tough, practical town known for its wind and its hardworking residents. It serves as a gateway between the vast agricultural plains of the Hi-Line and the dramatic peaks of the Rocky Mountain Front, offering a quiet, affordable lifestyle. Laurel is a hardworking, blue-collar community where the local economy is driven by the CHS refinery and the Montana Rail Link yard. It offers a slower, more traditional small-town pace than Billings, but residents frequently commute to the larger city for shopping, dining, and entertainment.

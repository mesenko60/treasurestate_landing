# Forsyth vs Glendive, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Forsyth and Glendive offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Forsyth is a historic railroad town on the Yellowstone River with 1,630 people, its beautiful, historic county courthouse, its position as a transportation and agricultural hub, and quick access to the Yellowstone River and the eastern plains. Glendive, with 4,873 residents, is a rugged eastern Montana town known for its rich dinosaur fossil beds, its position as an agricultural and energy hub, and Makoshika State Park and the Yellowstone River.

## Who Should Move to Forsyth?

* Workers in agriculture, transportation, or local services looking for a stable community.
* People seeking very affordable housing and a low overall cost of living.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle on the eastern plains.
* Hunters and anglers who want uncrowded access to the Yellowstone River.

## Who Should Move to Glendive?

* Workers in the agriculture, railroad, or energy sectors looking for steady employment in eastern Montana.
* Homebuyers seeking some of the most affordable real estate in the state.
* People who appreciate the stark beauty of the badlands and the quiet isolation of the high plains.
* Paleontology enthusiasts and rockhounds drawn to the fossil-rich terrain of Makoshika State Park.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Forsyth is around $208,690, while in Glendive it is closer to $185,447, a difference of roughly $23,243, making Glendive significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Forsyth averages about $743 per month, compared to roughly $832 in Glendive.

Forsyth's overall cost of living differs from Glendive's. Forsyth offers lower average salaries compared to Glendive, with a median household income of $58,375 compared to Glendive's $71,063.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Forsyth is a quiet, practical community in eastern Montana, defined by the railroad, agriculture, and the Yellowstone River. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle with a strong sense of local history. Glendive is a practical, hardworking town set against the dramatic backdrop of the Montana badlands. It is far removed from the tourist crowds of the western mountains, offering a quiet, resilient community life where the local economy is tied to the land, the railroad, and the river.

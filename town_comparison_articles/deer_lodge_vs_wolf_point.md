# Deer Lodge vs Wolf Point, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Deer Lodge and Wolf Point offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Deer Lodge is a historic ranching and prison town with 2,938 people, the Old Montana Prison Museum, its deep ranching heritage in the Powell Valley, and quick access to the Clark Fork River and the Flint Creek Range. Wolf Point, with just 2,517 residents, is a historic agricultural town on the Fort Peck Indian Reservation known for its position as the largest community on the reservation, its deep roots in farming and ranching, and the Missouri River and the vast eastern plains.

## Who Should Move to Deer Lodge?

* History buffs who appreciate the town's unique museums and historic architecture.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, traditional western town.
* Workers in agriculture, government, or local services looking for a slow-paced lifestyle.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want uncrowded access to the Clark Fork River and surrounding mountains.

## Who Should Move to Wolf Point?

* People seeking some of the most affordable housing in the state.
* Workers in agriculture, tribal government, or local services.
* Those who appreciate a quiet, isolated lifestyle on the high plains.
* Hunters and anglers who want uncrowded access to the Missouri River and surrounding lands.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Deer Lodge is around $225,000, while in Wolf Point it is closer to $145,000, a difference of roughly $80,000, making Wolf Point significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Deer Lodge averages about $800 per month, compared to roughly $600 in Wolf Point.

Deer Lodge's overall cost of living differs from Wolf Point's. Deer Lodge offers higher average salaries compared to Wolf Point, with a median household income of $48,000 compared to Wolf Point's $42,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Deer Lodge is a quiet, deeply historic town that feels like a step back in time. It is defined by its agricultural roots and its unique history as the site of the original territorial prison, offering a slow-paced, affordable lifestyle in a beautiful valley setting. Wolf Point is a quiet, resilient community deeply tied to the Fort Peck Indian Reservation and the agricultural economy of northeastern Montana. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle far removed from the state's major urban centers.

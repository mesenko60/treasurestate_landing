# Bigfork vs Glendive: Which Montana Town Is Right for You?

Bigfork and Glendive sit at opposite ends of Montana in every sense. Bigfork, A Village by the Bay on the eastern shore of Flathead Lake, is a resort-adjacent community of 5,000 people known for its arts scene, summer theater, world-class fishing, and some of the most expensive real estate in the state. Glendive, the Paddlefish Capital of the World in far eastern Montana, is a prairie town of 4,873 people on the Yellowstone River, known for Makoshika State Park, paddlefish season, and a cost of living that is among the lowest in Montana.

## Who Should Move to Bigfork?

- Outdoor enthusiasts who want Flathead Lake, Glacier National Park, and dozens of fishing access sites within easy reach
- Arts lovers and culture seekers who want a vibrant summer arts scene, theater, and galleries in a small-town setting
- Retirees or remote workers who want a beautiful lakeside community with a relaxed but active lifestyle
- Those who want the full western Montana experience, from skiing and hiking to boating and wildlife watching

## Who Should Move to Glendive?

- People looking for very affordable housing and a low cost of living in a friendly, unpretentious community
- Anglers who want the Yellowstone River and the unique experience of paddlefish snagging season each spring
- Fossil and geology enthusiasts who want Makoshika State Park, Montana's largest state park, as a backyard
- Those who prefer wide-open eastern Montana landscapes and a traditional, community-centered way of life

## Cost of Living Breakdown

The housing difference is dramatic. The typical home value in Bigfork is $758,435 compared to just $185,447 in Glendive, a gap of over $570,000. Rent follows the same pattern, with Bigfork at $1,103 per month and Glendive at $832. Bigfork's median household income of $82,648 is notably higher than Glendive's $71,063, but the housing cost gap is so large that Glendive remains far more affordable in real terms.

Both towns have their own airports nearby. Bigfork is served by Glacier Park International in Kalispell, 22 miles away. Glendive has its own small regional airport about 6 miles from town.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Bigfork has the energy of a destination. Summer brings visitors for the theater, the arts festival, and the lake, and the town has a polished, welcoming quality that sets it apart from most small Montana communities. Glendive is quieter and more self-contained, a place where the river and the badlands define the rhythm of life. Makoshika's otherworldly landscape and the annual paddlefish season give Glendive a character that is genuinely distinctive. These two towns share a state but almost nothing else.

Bigfork has 176 recreational sites within 30 miles, including 28 fishing access points, 32 lakes, 25 trailheads, and access to Glacier National Park. Glendive has 30 sites, anchored by Makoshika State Park, 4 fishing access points, and 7 scenic viewpoints. For sheer recreational volume, Bigfork is in a different league. For badlands scenery and river fishing, Glendive offers something you cannot find anywhere else in Montana.

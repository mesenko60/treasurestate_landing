# Libby vs Glendive, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Libby and Glendive offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Libby is a remote timber town in the Kootenai National Forest with 2,775 people, its deep roots in the logging industry, its stunning, rugged isolation, and quick access to the Kootenai River and Cabinet Mountains. Glendive, with just 4,873 residents, is a rugged eastern Montana town known for its rich dinosaur fossil beds, its position as an agricultural and energy hub, and Makoshika State Park and the Yellowstone River.

## Who Should Move to Libby?

* Outdoor enthusiasts seeking uncrowded access to some of Montana's most rugged and remote wilderness.
* Retirees and remote workers looking for affordable mountain living far from major tourist hubs.
* People who appreciate a tight-knit, resilient community with a strong working-class heritage.
* Hunters, anglers, and hikers who want to live in the heart of the Kootenai National Forest.

## Who Should Move to Glendive?

* Workers in the agriculture, railroad, or energy sectors looking for steady employment in eastern Montana.
* Homebuyers seeking some of the most affordable real estate in the state.
* People who appreciate the stark beauty of the badlands and the quiet isolation of the high plains.
* Paleontology enthusiasts and rockhounds drawn to the fossil-rich terrain of Makoshika State Park.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Libby is around $285,000, while in Glendive it is closer to $185,447, a difference of roughly $99,553, making Glendive significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Libby averages about $850 per month, compared to roughly $832 in Glendive.

Libby's overall cost of living differs from Glendive's. Libby offers lower average salaries compared to Glendive, with a median household income of $45,000 compared to Glendive's $71,063.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Libby is a resilient, isolated town tucked into the far northwest corner of the state. It is defined by its timber heritage and its incredible, uncrowded access to the Kootenai River and Cabinet Mountains, offering a quiet, rugged lifestyle. Glendive is a practical, hardworking town set against the dramatic backdrop of the Montana badlands. It is far removed from the tourist crowds of the western mountains, offering a quiet, resilient community life where the local economy is tied to the land, the railroad, and the river.

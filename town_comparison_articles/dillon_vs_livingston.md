# Dillon vs Livingston: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Dillon and Livingston are both small Montana communities with strong outdoor access and deep Montana character, but they sit in very different parts of the state and attract different kinds of residents. Dillon is Montana's Hometown, a small Beaverhead County community of 3,880 at 5,095 feet in the southwest, known for its quiet ranching character, the Beaverhead River, 24 fishing access sites, 16 campgrounds, 5 state parks, 2 hot springs, and the University of Montana Western. Livingston is the Windiest City in Montana, a community of 8,040 in Park County at 4,501 feet along the Yellowstone River in south-central Montana, with 144 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 40 lakes, 24 fishing access sites, 16 campgrounds, 16 waterfalls, 12 trailheads, 8 museums, 7 boat launches, 6 golf courses, 2 hot springs, 2 disc golf courses, a national forest, and a ski area.

## Who Should Move to Dillon?

*   Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want 24 fishing access sites, 16 campgrounds, 5 state parks, 2 hot springs, 8 lakes, and the Beaverhead River all within 30 miles
*   Remote workers, retirees, and families who want a genuinely quiet, small-town Montana lifestyle at a very affordable price, with a typical home value of $375,585 and rent averaging just $821 per month
*   People who want a small university town with the University of Montana Western providing community events, sports, and cultural programming
*   Anyone drawn to southwestern Montana's wide-open spaces, ranching culture, and the Big Hole Valley just to the west

## Who Should Move to Livingston?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 40 lakes, 24 fishing access sites, 16 campgrounds, 16 waterfalls, 12 trailheads, 2 hot springs, a national forest, a ski area, and the Yellowstone River all within 30 miles, plus Yellowstone National Park about 55 miles south
*   Artists, writers, and creative professionals drawn to Livingston's well-known arts community, its historic downtown, and its reputation as one of Montana's most culturally vibrant small towns
*   People who want a small community with a strong sense of identity, a median household income of $65,187, and easy access to Bozeman 35 miles away for additional services
*   Families and remote workers who want a 1,400-student school district, a lively downtown, and a community that balances outdoor adventure with a genuine arts and culture scene

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Livingston is considerably more expensive than Dillon. The typical home value in Dillon is around $375,585, compared to $509,477 in Livingston, about 36% higher. Rent is dramatically higher in Livingston: Dillon averages about $821 per month, while Livingston comes in at roughly $1,783, more than double.

Median household income is higher in Livingston at $65,187 versus $51,458 in Dillon. Even with higher incomes, Livingston's housing costs are substantially greater. Dillon is dramatically more affordable for both buyers and renters.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Dillon is a small southwestern Montana town where the Beaverhead River, ranching culture, and the University of Montana Western define community life. Livingston is a small south-central Montana town where the Yellowstone River, a strong arts community, and proximity to Yellowstone National Park define daily life. Livingston has a creative energy and a cultural density that Dillon does not, with a well-known arts scene, a historic railroad downtown, and a community that has attracted writers, artists, and outdoor adventurers for generations. If you want a quiet, affordable southwestern Montana lifestyle with excellent fishing and outdoor access, Dillon is a compelling choice. If you want a small Montana town with a strong arts community, the Yellowstone River at your doorstep, and Yellowstone National Park within an hour's drive, Livingston is one of Montana's most distinctive and beloved small towns.

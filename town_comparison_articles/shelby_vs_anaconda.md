# Shelby vs Anaconda, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Shelby and Anaconda offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Shelby is a historic railroad and agricultural town with 3,169 people, its position on the Hi-Line, its famous 1923 Dempsey-Gibbons heavyweight title fight, and quick access to the Sweet Grass Hills and Lake Elwell. Anaconda, with just 9,491 residents, is a historic smelting town known for its towering smokestack, its working-class heritage, and Georgetown Lake and the Anaconda-Pintler Wilderness.

## Who Should Move to Shelby?

* Workers in the agriculture, railroad, or energy sectors looking for steady employment on the Hi-Line.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, rural setting.
* Those who appreciate the stark beauty of the northern plains and the close-knit community of a small agricultural town.
* History buffs interested in the unique legacy of the 1923 heavyweight championship fight.

## Who Should Move to Anaconda?

* Retirees and remote workers seeking very low housing costs.
* History buffs fascinated by Montana's copper era.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want uncrowded access to the Pintlers.
* People looking for a quiet, slow-paced mountain community.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Shelby is around $185,000, while in Anaconda it is closer to $210,000, a difference of roughly $25,000, making Shelby significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Shelby averages about $700 per month, compared to roughly $750 in Anaconda.

Shelby's overall cost of living differs from Anaconda's. Shelby offers higher average salaries compared to Anaconda, with a median household income of $58,000 compared to Anaconda's $48,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Shelby is a resilient, hardworking town on the northern plains, where life is shaped by the railroad, agriculture, and the vast open spaces of the Hi-Line. It offers a quiet, practical lifestyle far removed from the state's major urban centers. Anaconda is a quiet, resilient town with a deep industrial history, offering incredibly affordable access to some of Montana's best mountain recreation.

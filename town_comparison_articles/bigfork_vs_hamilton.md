# Bigfork vs Hamilton: Which Montana Town Is Right for You?

Bigfork and Hamilton are both beloved western Montana small towns with strong outdoor identities, but they offer distinctly different lifestyles. Bigfork, A Village by the Bay on Flathead Lake, is a resort-adjacent community of 5,000 people with a thriving arts scene, world-class fishing, and some of the most expensive real estate in the state. Hamilton, the Banana Belt of Montana in the Bitterroot Valley, is a town of 4,659 people known for its unusually mild and sunny climate, the legendary Bitterroot River, and a more affordable, relaxed pace of life.

## Who Should Move to Bigfork?

- Outdoor enthusiasts who want Flathead Lake, Glacier National Park, and a wide variety of fishing, boating, and hiking options
- Arts and culture lovers who want a vibrant summer theater and arts scene in a small-town setting
- Retirees or remote workers who want a beautiful lakeside community with a polished, welcoming atmosphere
- Those who want the full northwestern Montana experience, from skiing to lake recreation to wildlife watching

## Who Should Move to Hamilton?

- People who want a mild, sunny climate by Montana standards, with more sunshine and warmer winters than most of the state
- Fly fishers who want the Bitterroot River, one of Montana's most celebrated trout streams, as a daily resource
- Remote workers, retirees, or families seeking affordability in a beautiful valley with a relaxed, unhurried pace
- Hikers and backpackers who want direct access to the Bitterroot National Forest and an exceptional network of trailheads

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Bigfork is significantly more expensive. The typical home value is $758,435 compared to $516,033 in Hamilton, a difference of about $242,000. Rent follows the same pattern, with Bigfork at $1,103 per month and Hamilton at $958. Bigfork's median household income of $82,648 is considerably higher than Hamilton's $52,917, but the housing cost gap means Hamilton offers meaningfully better affordability for most buyers.

Bigfork is served by Glacier Park International Airport in Kalispell, 22 miles away. Hamilton's nearest airport is in Missoula, 56 miles to the north.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Bigfork has a polished, destination quality that sets it apart from most small Montana towns. The summer arts festival, the Bigfork Summer Playhouse, and the lakeside setting give it a vibrancy that draws visitors and residents alike. Hamilton is quieter and more valley-centered, a place where the Bitterroot River and the surrounding mountains define daily life. The Banana Belt nickname is earned: Hamilton genuinely gets more sunshine and milder temperatures than most of western Montana. Both towns are deeply appealing, but they suit different temperaments and budgets.

Bigfork has 176 recreational sites within 30 miles, including 28 fishing access points, 32 lakes, 25 trailheads, and access to Glacier National Park. Hamilton has 194 sites, including 47 trailheads, 55 lakes, 32 campgrounds, 25 fishing access points, and a hot spring. Hamilton actually edges Bigfork in total site count, with the Bitterroot National Forest providing an exceptional trailhead network.

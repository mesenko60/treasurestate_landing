# Hamilton vs Livingston: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Hamilton and Livingston are both small Montana communities with similar home prices and strong outdoor access, but they sit in very different parts of the state and offer distinct lifestyles. Hamilton is the Banana Belt of Montana, a small Bitterroot Valley community of 4,659 in Ravalli County at 3,573 feet, known for its mild winters, 194 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 55 lakes, 47 trailheads, 32 campgrounds, 25 fishing access sites, and the Bitterroot National Forest at its doorstep. Livingston is the Windiest City in Montana, a community of 8,040 in Park County at 4,501 feet, with 144 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 40 lakes, 24 fishing access sites, 16 waterfalls, 16 campgrounds, 12 trailheads, 7 boat launches, 2 hot springs, a ski area, and Yellowstone National Park 35 miles to the south.

## Who Should Move to Hamilton?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 47 trailheads, 55 lakes, 32 campgrounds, 25 fishing access sites, a hot spring, a national forest, and a wilderness area all within 30 miles, considerably more total sites than Livingston
*   People who want a small, quiet Bitterroot Valley community with a genuinely mild winter climate, where January average highs reach 40 degrees, compared to Livingston's 35 degrees and notably windier conditions
*   Remote workers, retirees, and families who want a peaceful western Montana lifestyle at a lower monthly cost, with Missoula's full range of services 56 miles north
*   Anyone drawn to the Bitterroot Valley's combination of mountain scenery, river access, and the laid-back character of a small agricultural community

## Who Should Move to Livingston?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 24 fishing access sites, 40 lakes, 16 waterfalls, 12 trailheads, 16 campgrounds, 2 hot springs, a ski area, and Yellowstone National Park all within reach
*   Artists, writers, and people drawn to Livingston's well-known creative community, its historic downtown, and a character that has attracted writers and artists for decades
*   Families who want a 1,400-student school district and a community with Bozeman's full range of services just 35 miles away
*   Anyone who wants to fish the Yellowstone River, explore Paradise Valley, and have Yellowstone National Park as a regular weekend destination

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Hamilton and Livingston have nearly identical home prices. The typical home value in Hamilton is around $516,033, compared to $509,477 in Livingston, essentially the same. Rent is considerably lower in Hamilton: Hamilton averages about $958 per month, while Livingston comes in at roughly $1,783, nearly double.

Median household income is higher in Livingston at $65,187 versus $52,917 in Hamilton. Hamilton offers a lower monthly cost of living despite similar home prices, primarily due to its dramatically lower rent.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Hamilton is a small Bitterroot Valley town where the river, the mountains, and the mild climate define daily life. Livingston is a small central Montana town where the Yellowstone River, Paradise Valley, Yellowstone National Park, and a well-known creative and literary community define daily life. Both towns are small, both have strong outdoor access, and both have similar home prices. The key differences are climate (Hamilton is milder and less windy), monthly cost (Hamilton's rent is dramatically lower), and outdoor character (Hamilton has more total recreation sites, while Livingston has Yellowstone access). If you want the Bitterroot Valley's mild climate and outdoor access at a lower monthly cost, Hamilton is a compelling choice. If you want Yellowstone access, Paradise Valley, and Livingston's creative community, Livingston is one of Montana's most distinctive small towns.

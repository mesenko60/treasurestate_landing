# Cut Bank vs Kalispell, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Cut Bank and Kalispell offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Cut Bank is a windy agricultural town on the Hi-Line with 3,056 people, its proximity to the Blackfeet Indian Reservation, its agricultural and energy economy, and quick access to the eastern edge of Glacier National Park. Kalispell, with just 26,110 residents, is a commercial center of the Flathead Valley known for its retail and medical facilities, its historic downtown, and Flathead Lake and Glacier National Park.

## Who Should Move to Cut Bank?

* Workers in agriculture, energy, or government looking for affordable living on the Hi-Line.
* People seeking a quiet, resilient community with a low cost of living.
* Those who want to live near Glacier National Park without paying resort-town prices.
* Individuals who appreciate the stark, windy beauty of the northern plains.

## Who Should Move to Kalispell?

* Families who want access to Glacier National Park without the resort town prices.
* Professionals working in healthcare or retail.
* People who want a mix of historic charm and modern conveniences.
* Outdoor lovers who want to be near Flathead Lake.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Cut Bank is around $175,000, while in Kalispell it is closer to $550,000, a difference of roughly $375,000, making Cut Bank significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Cut Bank averages about $650 per month, compared to roughly $1,400 in Kalispell.

Cut Bank's overall cost of living differs from Kalispell's. Cut Bank offers lower average salaries compared to Kalispell, with a median household income of $52,000 compared to Kalispell's $60,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Cut Bank is a tough, practical town known for its wind and its hardworking residents. It serves as a gateway between the vast agricultural plains of the Hi-Line and the dramatic peaks of the Rocky Mountain Front, offering a quiet, affordable lifestyle. Kalispell is the practical, bustling heart of the Flathead Valley, balancing its role as a commercial hub with incredible access to natural wonders.

# Great Falls vs Livingston: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Great Falls and Livingston are both central Montana communities with strong identities, but they attract very different kinds of people. Great Falls is the Electric City, a city of 60,000 on the Missouri River, anchored by Malmstrom Air Force Base, the C.M. Russell Museum, and a river trail system that winds through the heart of town. Livingston is the Windiest City in Montana, a small, storied town of 8,040 in the Paradise Valley, where the Yellowstone River flows cold and clear through downtown, the Absaroka Range rises to the south, and a long tradition of artists, writers, and fly fishers gives the place a character that is genuinely hard to find elsewhere.

## Who Should Move to Great Falls?

*   Military families stationed at or connected to Malmstrom Air Force Base
*   Families and professionals who want a full-service city with a regional airport, larger job market, and lower housing costs
*   History and art lovers drawn to the C.M. Russell Museum and the Lewis and Clark National Historic Trail
*   People who want a stable, community-oriented Montana city with a broader range of amenities and services

## Who Should Move to Livingston?

*   Artists, writers, and creative professionals drawn to Livingston's long-established arts and literary community
*   Fly fishing enthusiasts who want direct access to the Yellowstone River, one of Montana's finest, right in town
*   Remote workers and retirees who want a small, character-rich community close to Yellowstone National Park
*   People who want the feel of a small Montana town with a surprisingly rich cultural life and stunning mountain scenery

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Great Falls is the more affordable city to buy into. The median home price in Great Falls is around $328,554, compared to $509,477 in Livingston, a difference of about $181,000. Rent also runs higher in Livingston at about $1,783 per month versus $1,340 in Great Falls.

Median household incomes are nearly identical, $63,934 in Great Falls versus $65,187 in Livingston, which makes the housing cost gap all the more significant. Livingston's higher home prices and rents reflect its desirability among remote workers and second-home buyers rather than its local economic base.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Great Falls is a practical, working river city with a military presence and a world-class art museum. It's a city that serves its community without pretension. Livingston is a small town with an outsized reputation, known for its wind, its writers, its fly fishing, and its proximity to Yellowstone. The historic downtown, the railroad heritage, and the backdrop of the Absarokas give Livingston a sense of place that is genuinely irreplaceable. Both are authentically Montana; Great Falls offers stability and affordability, while Livingston offers character, culture, and one of the great rivers in the American West running right through the middle of town.

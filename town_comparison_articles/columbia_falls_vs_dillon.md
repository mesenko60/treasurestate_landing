# Columbia Falls vs Dillon: Which Montana Town Is Right for You?

Columbia Falls and Dillon are both small western Montana towns with deep outdoor identities, but they sit in very different landscapes and serve very different purposes. Columbia Falls, the Gateway to Glacier, is a mountain community of 5,308 people in the lush Flathead Valley, with Glacier National Park at its doorstep and dozens of ski areas within range. Dillon, known as Montana's Hometown, is a high-desert ranching and university town of 3,880 people in the Beaverhead Valley, home to the University of Montana Western and surrounded by world-class fly fishing rivers.

## Who Should Move to Columbia Falls?

- Outdoor enthusiasts who want Glacier National Park, alpine skiing, and mountain wilderness as daily amenities
- Families seeking a growing community with strong schools and a vibrant outdoor culture
- People who want the lush, green scenery of the northern Rockies and the Flathead Valley
- Those who want easy airport access via Glacier Park International in Kalispell, just 6 miles away

## Who Should Move to Dillon?

- Fly fishers who want access to the Beaverhead, Big Hole, and Ruby rivers, among the best trout streams in Montana
- Remote workers, retirees, or students who want a quiet, affordable university town with a genuine small-town feel
- People drawn to wide-open high-desert landscapes, big skies, and a slower pace of life
- History enthusiasts interested in Lewis and Clark country and the rich ranching heritage of southwest Montana

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is significantly more affordable in Dillon. The typical home value is $375,585 compared to $584,580 in Columbia Falls, a difference of over $200,000. Rent follows the same pattern, with Columbia Falls at $1,003 per month and Dillon at $821. Columbia Falls has a higher median household income at $65,313 versus Dillon's $51,458, but the housing cost gap is large enough that Dillon remains the more affordable option in real terms.

Columbia Falls is served by Glacier Park International Airport in Kalispell, 6 miles away. Dillon's nearest airport is in Butte, 62 miles to the north.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Columbia Falls has the momentum and energy of a gateway town, with Glacier-bound visitors in summer and a year-round community of outdoor enthusiasts who take full advantage of the surrounding wilderness. Dillon is quieter and more self-contained. The University of Montana Western adds a youthful undercurrent to a town that is otherwise defined by ranching, fishing, and a deep connection to the land. Both towns are authentically Montana, but they represent different chapters of the state's story.

Columbia Falls has 214 recreational sites within 30 miles, including 48 campgrounds, 19 ski areas, 31 trailheads, and Glacier National Park. Dillon has 71 sites, anchored by 24 fishing access points, 16 campgrounds, 5 state parks, and 2 hot springs. For mountain and alpine recreation, Columbia Falls is in a different category. For river fishing and wide-open southwest Montana landscapes, Dillon is hard to beat.

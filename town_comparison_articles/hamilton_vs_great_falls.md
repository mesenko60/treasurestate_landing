# Hamilton vs Great Falls: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Hamilton and Great Falls are both mid-sized Montana communities with similar January temperatures, but they sit in very different landscapes and offer very different lifestyles. Hamilton is the Banana Belt of Montana, a small Bitterroot Valley community of 4,659 in Ravalli County at 3,573 feet, known for its mild winters, 194 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 55 lakes, 47 trailheads, 32 campgrounds, 25 fishing access sites, and the Bitterroot National Forest at its doorstep. Great Falls is the Electric City, a community of 60,000 in Cascade County at 3,340 feet in central Montana, with 45 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 10 museums, 8 fishing access sites, 6 waterfalls, 5 state parks, 2 rivers, and Malmstrom Air Force Base as a major economic anchor.

## Who Should Move to Hamilton?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 47 trailheads, 55 lakes, 32 campgrounds, 25 fishing access sites, a hot spring, a national forest, and a wilderness area all within 30 miles
*   People who want a small, quiet Bitterroot Valley community with a genuinely mild winter climate and Missoula's full range of services 56 miles north
*   Remote workers, retirees, and families who want a peaceful western Montana lifestyle at a lower monthly cost than Missoula
*   Anyone drawn to the Bitterroot Valley's combination of mountain scenery, river access, and the laid-back character of a small agricultural community

## Who Should Move to Great Falls?

*   Military families and professionals who want a mid-sized Montana city anchored by Malmstrom Air Force Base, with a 10,000-student school district and Great Falls International Airport 5 miles away
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want the Missouri River, 8 fishing access sites, 6 waterfalls, 5 state parks, Giant Springs State Park, and the Lewis and Clark National Historic Trail all within 30 miles
*   Budget-conscious buyers who want a mid-sized Montana city at a moderate price, with a typical home value of $328,554, considerably below Hamilton's $516,033
*   History enthusiasts drawn to the C.M. Russell Museum, the Lewis and Clark Interpretive Center, and a community shaped by the Missouri River

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Great Falls is considerably more affordable than Hamilton for buyers. The typical home value in Hamilton is around $516,033, compared to $328,554 in Great Falls, about 57% higher. Rent is lower in Hamilton: Hamilton averages about $958 per month, while Great Falls comes in at roughly $1,340.

Median household income is higher in Great Falls at $63,934 versus $52,917 in Hamilton. Great Falls residents earn more on average and pay less for housing, making it the better value for buyers.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Hamilton is a small Bitterroot Valley town where the river, the mountains, and the mild climate define daily life. The outdoor access is exceptional and the community is quiet and unhurried. Great Falls is a mid-sized central Montana city where the Missouri River, the military, and a strong arts heritage centered on C.M. Russell define community life. If you want the Bitterroot Valley's outdoor access and a slower pace of life, Hamilton is a compelling choice. If you want a mid-sized Montana city with more services, more employment options, a lower home price, and a river running through it, Great Falls is a solid and often underappreciated option.

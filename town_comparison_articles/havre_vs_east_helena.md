# Havre vs East Helena, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Havre and East Helena offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Havre is a historic railroad and agricultural hub on the Hi-Line with 9,362 people, its deep roots in the railroad industry, the Havre Beneath the Streets underground tour, and quick access to the nearby Bear Paw Mountains and Fresno Reservoir. East Helena, with 2,264 residents, is a growing residential community in the Helena Valley known for its industrial heritage, its rapid recent growth as a suburb of the capital, and Canyon Ferry Lake and the Elkhorn Mountains.

## Who Should Move to Havre?

* Workers in agriculture, education, or the railroad industry looking for steady employment.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a community with all essential amenities.
* Those who appreciate the stark beauty of the northern plains and the close-knit feel of a Hi-Line town.
* History buffs interested in the unique underground history and nearby frontier battlefields.

## Who Should Move to East Helena?

* Families looking for a strong local school district and a tight-knit community feel.
* Professionals working in Helena who want a slightly quieter, more residential environment.
* People who appreciate a town with a strong working-class heritage that is actively reinventing itself.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want quick access to Canyon Ferry Lake and the surrounding national forest.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Havre is around $224,252, while in East Helena it is closer to $430,196, a difference of roughly $205,944, making Havre significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Havre averages about $925 per month, compared to roughly $1,020 in East Helena.

Havre's overall cost of living differs from East Helena's. Havre offers higher average salaries compared to East Helena, with a median household income of $58,000 compared to East Helena's $53,611.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Havre is a resilient, hardworking community that serves as the commercial center for a vast stretch of northern Montana. It offers a practical, unpretentious lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture and the railroad. East Helena is transitioning from its historic roots as a smelter town into a rapidly growing, family-friendly suburb of Helena. It offers a quieter, slightly more affordable alternative to the capital city while maintaining its own distinct, blue-collar identity.

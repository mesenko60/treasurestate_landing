# Dillon vs Whitefish: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Dillon and Whitefish represent two very different Montana lifestyles at opposite ends of the state. Dillon is Montana's Hometown, a small Beaverhead County community of 3,880 at 5,095 feet in the southwest, known for its quiet ranching character, the Beaverhead River, 24 fishing access sites, 16 campgrounds, 5 state parks, 2 hot springs, and the University of Montana Western. Whitefish is Stumptown, a resort community of 8,915 in Flathead County at 3,038 feet in northwestern Montana, with 202 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 46 campgrounds, 33 trailheads, 26 lakes, 22 fishing access sites, 19 ski areas, 8 state parks, 8 museums, 7 golf courses, 7 waterfalls, 4 boat launches, 4 disc golf courses, 4 historic sites, a national forest, and Glacier Park International Airport 9 miles away.

## Who Should Move to Dillon?

*   Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want 24 fishing access sites, 16 campgrounds, 5 state parks, 2 hot springs, 8 lakes, and the Beaverhead River all within 30 miles
*   Remote workers, retirees, and families who want a genuinely quiet, small-town Montana lifestyle at a dramatically lower cost than Whitefish, with a typical home value of $375,585 and rent averaging just $821 per month
*   People who want a small university town with the University of Montana Western providing community events, sports, and cultural programming
*   Anyone drawn to southwestern Montana's wide-open spaces, ranching culture, and the Big Hole Valley just to the west

## Who Should Move to Whitefish?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 46 campgrounds, 33 trailheads, 26 lakes, 22 fishing access sites, 19 ski areas, 8 state parks, a national forest, and Glacier National Park all within 30 miles, making it one of the most outdoor-rich communities in the American West
*   Skiers, mountain bikers, and year-round outdoor adventurers who want Whitefish Mountain Resort, Glacier National Park, and Flathead Lake all within easy reach
*   People who want a vibrant resort town with a lively downtown, excellent restaurants, a strong arts scene, and a community that draws visitors and new residents from across the country
*   Families and remote workers who want a small but dynamic community with a 1,800-student school district, Glacier Park International Airport 9 miles away, and a median household income of $71,110

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Whitefish is dramatically more expensive than Dillon. The typical home value in Dillon is around $375,585, compared to $840,135 in Whitefish, more than double. Rent is dramatically higher in Whitefish: Dillon averages about $821 per month, while Whitefish comes in at roughly $2,639, more than three times higher.

Median household income is higher in Whitefish at $71,110 versus $51,458 in Dillon. Even with higher incomes, Whitefish's housing costs are extraordinary. Dillon is dramatically more affordable for both buyers and renters, with Whitefish being one of the most expensive communities in Montana.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Dillon is a small southwestern Montana town where the Beaverhead River, ranching culture, and the University of Montana Western define community life. Whitefish is a resort town where Whitefish Mountain Resort, Glacier National Park, and a thriving outdoor and arts culture define daily life. Whitefish has an energy and a cosmopolitan character that Dillon does not, with a busy downtown, a strong visitor economy, and a community that attracts people who want the best of Montana's outdoor lifestyle in a small but vibrant setting. If you want a quiet, affordable southwestern Montana lifestyle with excellent fishing and outdoor access, Dillon is one of Montana's most underrated small towns. If you want Montana's premier resort community with world-class skiing, Glacier National Park at your doorstep, and a lively small-town culture, Whitefish is in a class of its own.

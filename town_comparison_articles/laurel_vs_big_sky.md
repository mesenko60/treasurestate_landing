# Laurel vs Big Sky: Which Montana Town is Right for You?

Laurel and Big Sky are both in central Montana, but they represent opposite ends of the Montana lifestyle and price spectrum. Laurel is the City Between the Yellowstone and the Rimrocks, a working-class Yellowstone Valley community of 7,215 in Yellowstone County at 3,307 feet, with Billings Logan International Airport 18 miles away, 60 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 21 fishing access sites, 10 museums, 6 state parks, 5 golf courses, 4 historic sites, 3 nature reserves, 2 disc golf courses, 1 river, and a 1,800-student school district. Big Sky is The Biggest Skiing in America, a resort community of 3,591 in Gallatin County at 6,319 feet in the Gallatin Canyon, with Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport 44 miles away, 140 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 28 campgrounds, 23 trailheads, 19 fishing access sites, 18 lakes, 17 waterfalls, 7 golf courses, 7 nature reserves, 4 museums, 3 boat launches, 3 hot springs, 2 rivers, 2 wilderness areas, 1 ski area, 1 national forest, and a 300-student school district.

## Who Should Move to Laurel?

*   Working families and professionals who want easy access to Billings, the largest city in Montana, just 18 miles away, with its hospitals, employers, shopping, and airport, while living in a more affordable community
*   Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want 21 fishing access sites on the Yellowstone River, 6 state parks, 10 museums, and 5 golf courses within 30 miles, with the Rimrocks and the Yellowstone Valley as a backdrop
*   Buyers who want an affordable Montana community close to a major city, with a typical home value of $369,826 and rent averaging $1,160 per month
*   Families who want a 1,800-student school district, 4 historic sites, and a genuine working-class Montana community with strong Yellowstone Valley character

## Who Should Move to Big Sky?

*   Skiers and snowboarders who want access to Big Sky Resort, one of the largest ski areas in North America with over 5,800 acres of terrain, along with 56.9 inches of annual snowfall and a mountain community built around winter recreation
*   High-income remote workers, executives, and affluent retirees who want a resort-quality mountain lifestyle with a median household income of $103,625, a typical home value of $1,819,905, and 140 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 23 trailheads, 18 lakes, 17 waterfalls, 3 hot springs, 2 wilderness areas, and a national forest all within 30 miles in one of the most scenically dramatic corridors in Montana
*   People who want a small, tight-knit resort community with a 300-student school district and a Gallatin Canyon location that puts Yellowstone National Park within easy reach

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Big Sky is dramatically more expensive than Laurel. The typical home value in Big Sky is $1,819,905, compared to $369,826 in Laurel, nearly five times higher. Rent is also significantly higher in Big Sky at $1,712 per month versus $1,160 in Laurel.

Median household income is much higher in Big Sky at $103,625 versus $66,382 in Laurel, reflecting the resort community's affluent resident base. Big Sky is one of the most expensive communities in Montana and is priced well beyond the reach of most working families. Laurel offers genuine affordability for buyers and renters who want to live in central Montana without resort-town price tags.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Laurel is a working-class Yellowstone Valley community where the railroad, the refinery, the Rimrocks, and proximity to Billings define daily life. Big Sky is a world-class mountain resort community where skiing, hiking, the Gallatin River, and a wealthy seasonal and year-round resident base define daily life. The outdoor recreation gap is substantial: Big Sky has 140 sites within 30 miles versus Laurel's 60, including 23 trailheads, 17 waterfalls, and a ski area that Laurel does not have. Laurel has 21 fishing access sites, 10 museums, and 6 state parks that reflect its deep Yellowstone Valley roots. These two communities are not really comparable as lifestyle choices for most people. If you want an affordable working-class community with easy access to Billings and strong Yellowstone Valley outdoor access, Laurel is a practical and solid choice. If budget is not a constraint and you want to live in one of the most spectacular mountain resort communities in the American West, Big Sky is in a category of its own.

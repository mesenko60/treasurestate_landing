# Great Falls vs Kalispell: Which Montana City is Right for You?

Great Falls and Kalispell are both mid-sized Montana cities that often get overlooked in favor of Bozeman and Missoula, but they each offer a genuine and distinct Montana lifestyle. Great Falls is the Electric City, a city of 60,000 on the Missouri River in central Montana, anchored by Malmstrom Air Force Base, the C.M. Russell Museum, and a river trail system that winds through the heart of town. Kalispell is the Gateway to Glacier Country, a smaller, family-oriented city of 29,886 in the Flathead Valley, where Glacier National Park is less than an hour away, Flathead Lake is just to the south, and the outdoor recreation inventory within 30 miles, 189 sites, dwarfs what most Montana cities can offer.

## Who Should Move to Great Falls?

*   Military families stationed at or connected to Malmstrom Air Force Base
*   Families and professionals who want a larger city with a broader job market, a regional airport, and significantly lower housing costs
*   History and art lovers drawn to the C.M. Russell Museum and the Lewis and Clark National Historic Trail along the Missouri River
*   People who prefer a flatter, more open central Montana setting with a warmer winter climate

## Who Should Move to Kalispell?

*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want Glacier National Park, Flathead Lake, and 19 ski areas within 30 miles
*   Families looking for a growing, family-friendly community in one of Montana's most scenic valleys
*   Remote workers and retirees drawn to the Flathead Valley's natural beauty and expanding amenities
*   People who want a western Montana lifestyle at a lower price point than Whitefish or Missoula

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Great Falls is substantially more affordable. The median home price in Great Falls is around $328,554, compared to $541,638 in Kalispell, a difference of over $213,000. The rent gap is even more striking: about $1,340 per month in Great Falls versus $2,086 in Kalispell.

What makes this comparison particularly interesting is that median household incomes are nearly identical, $63,934 in Great Falls versus $61,590 in Kalispell. Kalispell residents pay significantly more for housing while earning slightly less, reflecting the premium placed on Flathead Valley living.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Great Falls is a steady, unpretentious city with a strong military presence and a surprising cultural depth, the C.M. Russell Museum alone would be a point of pride for a city three times its size. Kalispell is a gateway city in the most literal sense: a practical, family-friendly hub that serves as the base camp for some of the most spectacular outdoor landscapes in North America. If you want affordability and a central Montana river city, Great Falls delivers. If you want Glacier Country access and a western Montana valley setting, Kalispell is hard to beat.

# Laurel vs Hardin: Which Montana Town is Right for You?

Laurel and Hardin are both Yellowstone Valley communities in south-central Montana, but they serve very different residents and offer different access to services, history, and outdoor recreation. Laurel is the City Between the Yellowstone and the Rimrocks, a working-class community of 7,215 in Yellowstone County at 3,307 feet, with Billings Logan International Airport 18 miles away, 60 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 21 fishing access sites, 10 museums, 6 state parks, 5 golf courses, 4 historic sites, 3 nature reserves, 2 disc golf courses, 1 river, and a 1,800-student school district. Hardin is Where the Rivers Meet, a small agricultural and tourism community of 3,742 in Big Horn County at 2,910 feet at the confluence of the Bighorn and Yellowstone rivers, with Billings Logan International Airport 54 miles away, 20 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 10 fishing access sites, 4 historic sites, 2 museums, 2 nature reserves, 1 river, 1 viewpoint, and a 900-student school district.

## Who Should Move to Laurel?

*   Working families and professionals who want easy access to Billings, the largest city in Montana, just 18 miles away, with its hospitals, employers, shopping, and airport, while living in a more affordable community
*   Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want 21 fishing access sites on the Yellowstone River, 6 state parks, 10 museums, and 5 golf courses within 30 miles, with the Rimrocks and the Yellowstone Valley as a backdrop
*   Buyers who want an affordable Montana community close to a major city, with a typical home value of $369,826 and rent averaging $1,160 per month
*   Families who want a 1,800-student school district, 4 historic sites, and a genuine working-class Montana community with strong Yellowstone Valley character

## Who Should Move to Hardin?

*   History enthusiasts and Civil War and Indian Wars buffs who want to live near the Little Bighorn Battlefield National Monument, one of the most significant and visited historic sites in Montana, just 14 miles from town
*   Anglers who want 10 fishing access sites on the Bighorn River, one of the most celebrated blue-ribbon trout fisheries in Montana, along with access to the Yellowstone River confluence
*   Budget-conscious buyers who want one of the most affordable housing markets in the Yellowstone Valley, with a typical home value of $204,573 and rent averaging just $726 per month
*   Agricultural workers, ranchers, and anyone who wants a quiet, affordable eastern Montana community with a 900-student school district and a strong Native American cultural heritage through its proximity to the Crow Agency

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Hardin is significantly more affordable than Laurel. The typical home value in Hardin is $204,573, compared to $369,826 in Laurel, about $165,000 less. Rent is also lower in Hardin at $726 per month versus $1,160 in Laurel, a difference of $434 per month.

Median household income is higher in Laurel at $66,382 versus $60,463 in Hardin. Laurel's higher income reflects its proximity to Billings and its more diverse employment base. Hardin's lower home prices and rents make it one of the most affordable communities in the Yellowstone Valley for buyers and renters alike.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Laurel is a working-class Yellowstone Valley community where the railroad, the refinery, the Rimrocks, and proximity to Billings define daily life. Hardin is a quieter, more traditional community where the Bighorn River, the Little Bighorn Battlefield, the Crow Nation, and the surrounding ranch country define community identity. Laurel has 21 fishing access sites, 10 museums, 6 state parks, and 5 golf courses within 30 miles that reflect its position in the heart of the Yellowstone Valley. Hardin has 10 fishing access sites on the Bighorn River and 4 historic sites including the Little Bighorn Battlefield, which draws visitors from around the world. If you want a working-class community with easy access to Billings and strong Yellowstone Valley outdoor access, Laurel is a practical and solid choice. If you want an affordable Yellowstone Valley community with world-class Bighorn River fishing and one of the most historically significant sites in the American West at your doorstep, Hardin offers a genuinely unique Montana lifestyle.

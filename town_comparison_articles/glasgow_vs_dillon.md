# Glasgow vs Dillon, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Glasgow and Dillon offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Glasgow is a historic railroad town on the Hi-Line with 3,224 people, its position as a regional hub for northeastern Montana, its proximity to Fort Peck Dam, and quick access to Fort Peck Lake and the Charles M. Russell National Wildlife Refuge. Dillon, with just 3,880 residents, is a historic ranching and college town known for the University of Montana Western, its deep agricultural roots, and the Beaverhead River and surrounding Pioneer Mountains.

## Who Should Move to Glasgow?

* Workers in agriculture, government, or the railroad industry looking for stable employment in northeastern Montana.
* Anglers and boaters who want to live near Fort Peck Lake, one of the premier walleye fisheries in the country.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, isolated community.
* Hunters and outdoor enthusiasts drawn to the vast, uncrowded public lands of the Missouri River Breaks.

## Who Should Move to Dillon?

* Students, faculty, and staff associated with the University of Montana Western.
* Ranchers and agricultural workers who want to live in a community that still deeply values its livestock heritage.
* Anglers seeking world-class trout fishing on the Beaverhead and Big Hole rivers.
* People looking for a genuine, unpretentious western town with a stable economy and access to vast public lands.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Glasgow is around $195,000, while in Dillon it is closer to $375,585, a difference of roughly $180,585, making Glasgow significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Glasgow averages about $750 per month, compared to roughly $821 in Dillon.

Glasgow's overall cost of living differs from Dillon's. Glasgow offers higher average salaries compared to Dillon, with a median household income of $55,000 compared to Dillon's $51,458.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Glasgow is a resilient, isolated community that serves as the commercial and social center for a massive swath of northeastern Montana. It offers a quiet, slow-paced lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture, the railroad, and the incredible recreational opportunities at nearby Fort Peck Lake. Dillon strikes a unique balance between a traditional cowboy town and a small college community. It remains deeply tied to the ranching industry, but the presence of the university adds a layer of cultural and educational activity, creating a grounded, authentic Montana atmosphere.

# Forsyth vs Whitefish, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Forsyth and Whitefish offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Forsyth is a historic railroad town on the Yellowstone River with 1,630 people, its beautiful, historic county courthouse, its position as a transportation and agricultural hub, and quick access to the Yellowstone River and the eastern plains. Whitefish, with 8,492 residents, is a premier ski and summer resort town known for Whitefish Mountain Resort, its pristine lake, and Glacier National Park and Whitefish Lake.

## Who Should Move to Forsyth?

* Workers in agriculture, transportation, or local services looking for a stable community.
* People seeking very affordable housing and a low overall cost of living.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle on the eastern plains.
* Hunters and anglers who want uncrowded access to the Yellowstone River.

## Who Should Move to Whitefish?

* Affluent buyers looking for a premium mountain lifestyle.
* Dedicated skiers and snowboarders.
* People who want a lively, walkable downtown with upscale dining.
* Those who desire immediate access to a major ski resort and a beautiful lake.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Forsyth is around $208,690, while in Whitefish it is closer to $850,000, a difference of roughly $641,310, making Forsyth significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Forsyth averages about $743 per month, compared to roughly $1,800 in Whitefish.

Forsyth's overall cost of living differs from Whitefish's. Forsyth offers lower average salaries compared to Whitefish, with a median household income of $58,375 compared to Whitefish's $75,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Forsyth is a quiet, practical community in eastern Montana, defined by the railroad, agriculture, and the Yellowstone River. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle with a strong sense of local history. Whitefish is a polished, high-end resort community with a vibrant downtown, drawing tourists and affluent residents year-round.

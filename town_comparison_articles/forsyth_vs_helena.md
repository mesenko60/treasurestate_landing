# Forsyth vs Helena, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Forsyth and Helena offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Forsyth is a historic railroad town on the Yellowstone River with 1,630 people, its beautiful, historic county courthouse, its position as a transportation and agricultural hub, and quick access to the Yellowstone River and the eastern plains. Helena, with 33,885 residents, is a historic state capital known for its government sector, its gold rush architecture, and Mount Helena and the Missouri River lakes.

## Who Should Move to Forsyth?

* Workers in agriculture, transportation, or local services looking for a stable community.
* People seeking very affordable housing and a low overall cost of living.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle on the eastern plains.
* Hunters and anglers who want uncrowded access to the Yellowstone River.

## Who Should Move to Helena?

* Government workers and professionals.
* Families seeking a stable, safe community.
* Mountain bikers and hikers who want trails right out their back door.
* People who appreciate historic architecture and a central location.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Forsyth is around $208,690, while in Helena it is closer to $425,000, a difference of roughly $216,310, making Forsyth significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Forsyth averages about $743 per month, compared to roughly $1,100 in Helena.

Forsyth's overall cost of living differs from Helena's. Forsyth offers lower average salaries compared to Helena, with a median household income of $58,375 compared to Helena's $68,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Forsyth is a quiet, practical community in eastern Montana, defined by the railroad, agriculture, and the Yellowstone River. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle with a strong sense of local history. Helena is a stable, professional town driven by state government, with a quiet downtown and immediate access to mountain trails.

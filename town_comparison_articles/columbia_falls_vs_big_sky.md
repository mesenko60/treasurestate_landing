# Columbia Falls vs Big Sky: Which Montana Town Is Right for You?

Columbia Falls and Big Sky are both outdoor-focused Montana communities, but they serve very different markets and offer very different lifestyles. Columbia Falls, the Gateway to Glacier in the Flathead Valley, is a working-class mountain town of 5,308 people where families, tradespeople, and outdoor enthusiasts live alongside the tourism economy. Big Sky, known as The Biggest Skiing in America, is a resort community of 3,591 people in Gallatin County, built around world-class skiing and summer recreation, with some of the highest home prices in the state.

## Who Should Move to Columbia Falls?

- Families and working professionals who want genuine mountain-town living near Glacier National Park without resort-town prices
- Outdoor enthusiasts who want access to skiing, hiking, fishing, and wilderness across a wide range of sites within 30 miles
- People who want a real community with schools, local businesses, and year-round residents rather than a seasonal resort atmosphere
- Those who want easy airport access via Glacier Park International in Kalispell, just 6 miles away

## Who Should Move to Big Sky?

- Skiers and snowboarders who want one of the largest ski resorts in North America as a literal backyard
- High-income remote workers, retirees, or second-home buyers who want a luxury mountain lifestyle
- People who want dramatic Gallatin Canyon scenery and proximity to Yellowstone National Park
- Outdoor enthusiasts who want world-class hiking, fly fishing on the Gallatin River, and hot springs nearby

## Cost of Living Breakdown

The housing gap between these two towns is enormous. The typical home value in Big Sky is $1,819,905 compared to $584,580 in Columbia Falls, a difference of over $1.2 million. Rent is also significantly higher in Big Sky at $1,712 per month versus $1,003 in Columbia Falls. Big Sky's median household income of $103,625 is well above Columbia Falls at $65,313, reflecting the resort economy and the high-earning remote workers it attracts. Even so, Big Sky's cost of living is in a category of its own.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Columbia Falls is a genuine small town with a strong local identity. People work in trades, healthcare, and tourism, shop at local businesses, and take their kids to Friday night football games. Big Sky is a resort community that has grown dramatically in recent years, drawing wealthy transplants and remote workers who want a high-end mountain lifestyle. It has excellent restaurants, a growing arts scene, and a year-round energy that is increasingly sophisticated. The two towns share a love of the outdoors but almost nothing else in terms of daily rhythm, cost, or community character.

Columbia Falls has 214 recreational sites within 30 miles, including 48 campgrounds, 19 ski areas, 31 trailheads, and Glacier National Park. Big Sky has 140 sites, anchored by Big Sky Resort, 3 hot springs, 23 trailheads, and the Lee Metcalf Wilderness. Both towns are outstanding for outdoor recreation, but Columbia Falls offers far more variety and volume.

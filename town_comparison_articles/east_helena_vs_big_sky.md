# East Helena vs Big Sky, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

East Helena and Big Sky offer two very different Montana lifestyles. East Helena is a growing residential community in the Helena Valley with 2,264 people, its industrial heritage, its rapid recent growth as a suburb of the capital, and quick access to Canyon Ferry Lake and the Elkhorn Mountains. Big Sky, with 3,591 residents, is a premier alpine resort community known for the massive Big Sky Resort, its luxury real estate market, and Lone Mountain and the Gallatin Canyon.

## Who Should Move to East Helena?

* Families looking for a strong local school district and a tight-knit community feel.
* Professionals working in Helena who want a slightly quieter, more residential environment.
* People who appreciate a town with a strong working-class heritage that is actively reinventing itself.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want quick access to Canyon Ferry Lake and the surrounding national forest.

## Who Should Move to Big Sky?

* Affluent buyers and second-home owners looking for luxury alpine real estate.
* Dedicated skiers and snowboarders who want access to some of the biggest and best terrain in North America.
* Hospitality and resort workers who are willing to navigate the high cost of living for the mountain lifestyle.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want to live in a high-altitude environment with immediate access to wilderness and Yellowstone National Park.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in East Helena is around $430,196, while in Big Sky it is closer to $1,819,905, a difference of roughly $1,389,709, making East Helena significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in East Helena averages about $1,020 per month, compared to roughly $1,712 in Big Sky.

East Helena's overall cost of living differs from Big Sky's. East Helena offers lower average salaries compared to Big Sky, with a median household income of $53,611 compared to Big Sky's $103,625.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

East Helena is transitioning from its historic roots as a smelter town into a rapidly growing, family-friendly suburb of Helena. It offers a quieter, slightly more affordable alternative to the capital city while maintaining its own distinct, blue-collar identity. Big Sky is not a traditional town but a sprawling, high-end resort community built around outdoor recreation and luxury living. The atmosphere is transient and seasonal, driven by tourism, skiing, and wealth, offering spectacular alpine access but lacking the historic, grounded feel of older Montana settlements.

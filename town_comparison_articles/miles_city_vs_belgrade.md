# Miles City vs Belgrade, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Miles City and Belgrade offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Miles City is a ranching community on the eastern plains with 8,412 people, the livestock industry, rodeo culture, and quick access to the wide-open Yellowstone River country. Belgrade, with just 12,509 residents, is a rapidly growing hub in the Gallatin Valley known for proximity to Bozeman, the Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport, and the Bridger Mountains and Gallatin River.

## Who Should Move to Miles City?

* Ranchers, livestock producers, and people whose careers and lifestyle are rooted in Eastern Montana's agricultural economy.
* Those seeking very affordable housing in a community where the typical home costs less than half what it does in many western Montana towns.
* Remote workers or retirees who want quiet, uncrowded living without the cost pressures that come with proximity to major tourist hubs.
* People who appreciate the authentic cowboy heritage of the Yellowstone Valley and the Bucking Horse Sale rodeo tradition.

## Who Should Move to Belgrade?

* Families and professionals who work in Bozeman but want slightly more affordable housing options.
* Frequent travelers who appreciate being minutes away from Montana's busiest airport.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want quick access to the Bridger Mountains, Big Sky, and blue-ribbon trout streams.
* People looking for a community with a strong local identity that still benefits from the economic engine of the Gallatin Valley.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Miles City is around $233,769, while in Belgrade it is closer to $544,626, a difference of roughly $310,857, making Miles City significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Miles City averages about $750 per month, compared to roughly $2,206 in Belgrade.

Miles City's overall cost of living differs from Belgrade's. Miles City offers lower average salaries compared to Belgrade, with a median household income of $60,732 compared to Belgrade's $88,896.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Miles City is quiet and agricultural in character, with a lifestyle shaped by ranching seasons and rodeo. It sits along the Yellowstone River, looking east toward the open plains. Belgrade feels like a busy, practical town that is absorbing much of the Gallatin Valley's growth. It has its own distinct community pride, centered around local schools and events, but its daily rhythm is closely tied to the economic hum of neighboring Bozeman and the constant activity at the airport.

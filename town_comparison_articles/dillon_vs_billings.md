# Dillon vs Billings: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Dillon and Billings represent two very different Montana lifestyles. Dillon is Montana's Hometown, a small Beaverhead County community of 3,880 at 5,095 feet in southwestern Montana, known for its quiet ranching character, the Beaverhead River, 24 fishing access sites, 16 campgrounds, 5 state parks, 2 hot springs, and the University of Montana Western. Billings is the Magic City, Montana's largest city with 117,116 residents in Yellowstone County at 3,123 feet, with 53 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 9 golf courses, 9 museums, 5 historic sites, 4 state parks, Billings Logan International Airport 5 miles away, and the full range of services that come with Montana's only true metropolitan area.

## Who Should Move to Dillon?

*   Anglers and outdoor enthusiasts who want 24 fishing access sites, 16 campgrounds, 5 state parks, 2 hot springs, 8 lakes, and the Beaverhead River all within 30 miles
*   Remote workers, retirees, and families who want a genuinely quiet, small-town Montana lifestyle at a very affordable price, with a typical home value of $375,585 and rent averaging just $821 per month
*   People who want a small university town with the University of Montana Western providing community events, sports, and cultural programming
*   Anyone drawn to southwestern Montana's wide-open spaces, ranching culture, and the Big Hole Valley just to the west

## Who Should Move to Billings?

*   Professionals, families, and anyone who needs Montana's largest city, with a 16,000-student school district, Billings Logan International Airport 5 miles away, and the full range of medical, retail, and employment options that only Montana's largest city can provide
*   People who want a mid-sized western city with 9 museums, 9 golf courses, 5 historic sites, and a downtown that is genuinely active and growing
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want easy access to the Beartooth Highway, the Bighorn Canyon, and Yellowstone National Park within a few hours' drive
*   Anyone who wants Montana's strongest job market, highest median household income at $71,855, and the widest range of services in the state

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Dillon and Billings have similar home prices. The typical home value in Dillon is around $375,585, compared to $390,654 in Billings, only about 4% more. Rent is dramatically lower in Dillon: Dillon averages about $821 per month, while Billings comes in at roughly $1,383, nearly 70% higher.

Median household income is significantly higher in Billings at $71,855 versus $51,458 in Dillon. Billings residents earn considerably more on average, which helps offset the higher rent. Dillon offers a lower monthly cost of living despite similar home prices.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Dillon is a small southwestern Montana town where the Beaverhead River, ranching culture, and the University of Montana Western define community life. Billings is Montana's only true city, where commerce, healthcare, education, and a growing downtown define daily life. The gap between these two towns is not just size but character: Dillon is quiet, slow-paced, and deeply rooted in ranching and river culture, while Billings is active, growing, and connected to the broader economy. If you want a quiet, affordable southwestern Montana lifestyle with excellent fishing and outdoor access, Dillon is one of Montana's most underrated small towns. If you want Montana's largest city with the widest range of services, jobs, and amenities, Billings is the clear choice.

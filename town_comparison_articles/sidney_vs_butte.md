# Sidney vs Butte, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Sidney and Butte offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Sidney is a bustling agricultural and energy hub with 6,173 people, its position in the Bakken oil field, its rich farming heritage in the Yellowstone Valley, and quick access to the Yellowstone River and eastern Montana badlands. Butte, with just 34,768 residents, is a historic mining city known for its rich labor history, its stunning historic architecture, and the Continental Divide and surrounding national forests.

## Who Should Move to Sidney?

* Workers in the energy, agriculture, or transportation sectors looking for strong employment opportunities.
* People seeking a hardworking, practical community with a resilient local economy.
* Those who want affordable housing compared to western Montana, with higher average wages driven by the oil and gas industry.
* Hunters and anglers who appreciate the rugged, uncrowded access to the Yellowstone River and surrounding plains.

## Who Should Move to Butte?

* History lovers who appreciate historic homes and architecture.
* People looking for very affordable mountain living.
* Those who want a town with a strong, independent character.
* Engineering students attending Montana Tech.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Sidney is around $241,500, while in Butte it is closer to $250,000, a difference of roughly $8,500, making Sidney significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Sidney averages about $850 per month, compared to roughly $800 in Butte.

Sidney's overall cost of living differs from Butte's. Sidney offers higher average salaries compared to Butte, with a median household income of $75,200 compared to Butte's $50,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Sidney is a dynamic, industrious town where the pace of life is closely tied to the energy markets and agricultural seasons. It has a practical, no-nonsense atmosphere, serving as a major economic engine for eastern Montana while maintaining its roots as a tight-knit farming community. Butte is fiercely proud of its working-class roots, offering a unique, historic atmosphere that is distinct from the rest of the state.

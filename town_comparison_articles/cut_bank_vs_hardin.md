# Cut Bank vs Hardin, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Cut Bank and Hardin offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Cut Bank is a windy agricultural town on the Hi-Line with 3,056 people, its proximity to the Blackfeet Indian Reservation, its agricultural and energy economy, and quick access to the eastern edge of Glacier National Park. Hardin, with just 3,742 residents, is a agricultural community near the Crow Reservation known for its proximity to the Little Bighorn Battlefield, its farming and ranching economy, and the Bighorn River and Yellowtail Dam.

## Who Should Move to Cut Bank?

* Workers in agriculture, energy, or government looking for affordable living on the Hi-Line.
* People seeking a quiet, resilient community with a low cost of living.
* Those who want to live near Glacier National Park without paying resort-town prices.
* Individuals who appreciate the stark, windy beauty of the northern plains.

## Who Should Move to Hardin?

* Agricultural workers and farmers involved in the sugar beet and wheat industries of the Bighorn Valley.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a quiet, rural setting.
* History enthusiasts interested in the rich Native American culture and the legacy of the Little Bighorn.
* Anglers who want to be near the world-famous trout fishing below Yellowtail Dam.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Cut Bank is around $175,000, while in Hardin it is closer to $204,573, a difference of roughly $29,573, making Cut Bank significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Cut Bank averages about $650 per month, compared to roughly $726 in Hardin.

Cut Bank's overall cost of living differs from Hardin's. Cut Bank offers lower average salaries compared to Hardin, with a median household income of $52,000 compared to Hardin's $60,463.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Cut Bank is a tough, practical town known for its wind and its hardworking residents. It serves as a gateway between the vast agricultural plains of the Hi-Line and the dramatic peaks of the Rocky Mountain Front, offering a quiet, affordable lifestyle. Hardin is a quiet, culturally diverse agricultural center where the plains meet the Bighorn River. It is a town defined by farming, ranching, and its location bordering the Crow Indian Reservation, offering a slow-paced, rural lifestyle far from the state's major urban centers.

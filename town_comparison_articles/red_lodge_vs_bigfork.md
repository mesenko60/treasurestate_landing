# Red Lodge vs Bigfork, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Red Lodge and Bigfork offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Red Lodge is a historic mountain town and gateway to Yellowstone with 2,336 people, its vibrant, historic downtown, its position at the base of the Beartooth Highway, and quick access to Red Lodge Mountain and the Beartooth Mountains. Bigfork, with just 5,000 residents, is a picturesque village on Flathead Lake known for its thriving arts scene, its cherry orchards, and Flathead Lake and the Swan River.

## Who Should Move to Red Lodge?

* Outdoor enthusiasts who want immediate access to skiing, hiking, and the Beartooth Wilderness.
* People seeking a lively, historic mountain town with a strong sense of community.
* Retirees and remote workers drawn to the spectacular scenery and small-town charm.
* Those who want to live in a gateway community to Yellowstone National Park.

## Who Should Move to Bigfork?

* Retirees and affluent professionals looking for a scenic, upscale lakeside community.
* Boaters, sailors, and water sports enthusiasts who want to live on the largest natural freshwater lake in the West.
* Art lovers who appreciate the town's galleries, summer playhouse, and cultural events.
* People who desire a resort-like atmosphere with high-end dining and boutique shopping.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Red Lodge is around $550,000, while in Bigfork it is closer to $758,435, a difference of roughly $208,435, making Red Lodge significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Red Lodge averages about $1,200 per month, compared to roughly $1,103 in Bigfork.

Red Lodge's overall cost of living differs from Bigfork's. Red Lodge offers lower average salaries compared to Bigfork, with a median household income of $58,000 compared to Bigfork's $82,648.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Red Lodge is a vibrant, historic mountain town that balances its role as a tourist destination with a strong, year-round local community. It offers spectacular access to the Beartooth Mountains and a lively downtown filled with shops, restaurants, and local events. Bigfork feels like a curated resort village, where life centers around the water, the arts, and seasonal tourism. The downtown is polished and walkable, filled with galleries and cafes, offering a lifestyle that is significantly more upscale and leisure-focused than most Montana towns.

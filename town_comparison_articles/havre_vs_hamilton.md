# Havre vs Hamilton, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Havre and Hamilton offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Havre is a historic railroad and agricultural hub on the Hi-Line with 9,362 people, its deep roots in the railroad industry, the Havre Beneath the Streets underground tour, and quick access to the nearby Bear Paw Mountains and Fresno Reservoir. Hamilton, with 4,659 residents, is a scenic hub of the Bitterroot Valley known for its historic downtown, its relatively mild climate, and the Bitterroot Mountains and the Bitterroot River.

## Who Should Move to Havre?

* Workers in agriculture, education, or the railroad industry looking for steady employment.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a community with all essential amenities.
* Those who appreciate the stark beauty of the northern plains and the close-knit feel of a Hi-Line town.
* History buffs interested in the unique underground history and nearby frontier battlefields.

## Who Should Move to Hamilton?

* Retirees and remote workers drawn to the stunning scenery and milder weather of the 'Banana Belt'.
* Fly fishing enthusiasts who want to live near the renowned waters of the Bitterroot River.
* People seeking a vibrant small-town community with a historic main street, local breweries, and community events.
* Outdoor adventurers who want immediate access to the rugged canyons and trails of the Bitterroot Selway Wilderness.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Havre is around $224,252, while in Hamilton it is closer to $516,033, a difference of roughly $291,781, making Havre significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Havre averages about $925 per month, compared to roughly $958 in Hamilton.

Havre's overall cost of living differs from Hamilton's. Havre offers higher average salaries compared to Hamilton, with a median household income of $58,000 compared to Hamilton's $52,917.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Havre is a resilient, hardworking community that serves as the commercial center for a vast stretch of northern Montana. It offers a practical, unpretentious lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture and the railroad. Hamilton offers a highly desirable blend of spectacular mountain scenery and a charming, active community. The pace is relaxed but the town is lively, with a downtown that serves as a gathering place for locals and visitors alike, all set against the towering peaks of the Bitterroot Range.

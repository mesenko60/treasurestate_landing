# Havre vs Forsyth, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Havre and Forsyth offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Havre is a historic railroad and agricultural hub on the Hi-Line with 9,362 people, its deep roots in the railroad industry, the Havre Beneath the Streets underground tour, and quick access to the nearby Bear Paw Mountains and Fresno Reservoir. Forsyth, with 1,630 residents, is a historic railroad town on the Yellowstone River known for its beautiful, historic county courthouse, its position as a transportation and agricultural hub, and the Yellowstone River and the eastern plains.

## Who Should Move to Havre?

* Workers in agriculture, education, or the railroad industry looking for steady employment.
* People seeking very affordable housing in a community with all essential amenities.
* Those who appreciate the stark beauty of the northern plains and the close-knit feel of a Hi-Line town.
* History buffs interested in the unique underground history and nearby frontier battlefields.

## Who Should Move to Forsyth?

* Workers in agriculture, transportation, or local services looking for a stable community.
* People seeking very affordable housing and a low overall cost of living.
* Those who appreciate a traditional, slow-paced rural lifestyle on the eastern plains.
* Hunters and anglers who want uncrowded access to the Yellowstone River.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Havre is around $224,252, while in Forsyth it is closer to $208,690, a difference of roughly $15,562, making Forsyth significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Havre averages about $925 per month, compared to roughly $743 in Forsyth.

Havre's overall cost of living differs from Forsyth's. Havre offers lower average salaries compared to Forsyth, with a median household income of $58,000 compared to Forsyth's $58,375.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Havre is a resilient, hardworking community that serves as the commercial center for a vast stretch of northern Montana. It offers a practical, unpretentious lifestyle deeply connected to agriculture and the railroad. Forsyth is a quiet, practical community in eastern Montana, defined by the railroad, agriculture, and the Yellowstone River. It offers a slow-paced, highly affordable lifestyle with a strong sense of local history.

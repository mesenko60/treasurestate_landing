# Lewistown vs Livingston: Which Montana Community is Right for You?

Lewistown and Livingston are both small Montana communities with strong outdoor access and a genuine small-town character, but they differ considerably in price, landscape, and the kind of life they offer. Lewistown is the Geographic Center of Montana, a quiet ranching community of 6,204 in Fergus County with affordable housing, 17 campgrounds within 30 miles, and wide-open central Montana skies. Livingston is the Windiest City in Montana, a community of 8,040 in Park County at 4,501 feet elevation on the Yellowstone River, with 144 outdoor recreation sites within 30 miles, 24 fishing access sites, 40 lakes, 12 trailheads, and the gateway to Paradise Valley and Yellowstone National Park.

## Who Should Move to Lewistown?

*   Retirees and remote workers who want a quiet, affordable small-town lifestyle with a typical home value around $268,126 compared to Livingston's $509,477
*   People who want a traditional, tight-knit ranching community with a genuine small-town character and none of the Yellowstone-corridor pricing pressure
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 17 campgrounds, 7 fishing access sites, and the Big Snowy Mountains within 30 miles
*   Anyone who wants to live at the geographic heart of Montana, where the pace is slow and the cost of living is genuinely manageable

## Who Should Move to Livingston?

*   Fly fishing enthusiasts who want the Yellowstone River at their doorstep, with 24 fishing access sites, 7 boat launches, and 2 rivers within 30 miles
*   Outdoor enthusiasts who want 40 lakes, 12 trailheads, 16 waterfalls, and a ski area all within 30 miles, plus Yellowstone National Park just 55 miles south
*   Artists, writers, and creative professionals drawn to Livingston's well-established arts community and its reputation as one of Montana's most culturally vibrant small towns
*   People who want a small Montana town with a strong sense of place, a historic downtown, and easy access to both Bozeman (35 miles) and Yellowstone

## Cost of Living Breakdown

The gap between these two towns is significant. The typical home value in Lewistown is around $268,126, compared to $509,477 in Livingston, nearly double the price. Rent diverges sharply as well: Lewistown averages about $896 per month, while Livingston comes in at roughly $1,783, nearly twice as much.

The income gap is notable. The median household income in Livingston is $65,187, compared to $44,195 in Lewistown. Livingston residents earn considerably more on average, reflecting the influence of Bozeman's economy and the town's growing appeal to remote workers and retirees.

## Lifestyle & Vibe

Lewistown is a town where the ranching calendar shapes daily life, the pace is slow, and the wide-open central Montana landscape is the main draw. Livingston is a town with a different kind of energy, a place where the Yellowstone River runs through downtown, the wind is a constant companion, and the arts community has given the town a cultural identity that punches well above its size. If you want affordable, quiet, traditional small-town Montana, Lewistown is one of the best values in the state. If you want a small Montana town with world-class fly fishing, a gateway to Yellowstone, and a creative community that has attracted artists and writers for generations, Livingston is a compelling choice.

# Libby vs Columbia Falls, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Libby and Columbia Falls offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Libby is a remote timber town in the Kootenai National Forest with 2,775 people, its deep roots in the logging industry, its stunning, rugged isolation, and quick access to the Kootenai River and Cabinet Mountains. Columbia Falls, with just 5,308 residents, is a gateway community to Glacier National Park known for its timber industry roots, its revitalized downtown, and the Flathead River and Glacier National Park.

## Who Should Move to Libby?

* Outdoor enthusiasts seeking uncrowded access to some of Montana's most rugged and remote wilderness.
* Retirees and remote workers looking for affordable mountain living far from major tourist hubs.
* People who appreciate a tight-knit, resilient community with a strong working-class heritage.
* Hunters, anglers, and hikers who want to live in the heart of the Kootenai National Forest.

## Who Should Move to Columbia Falls?

* Outdoor lovers who want to live minutes from the entrance to Glacier National Park.
* Families looking for a community with a strong local spirit and a growing downtown scene.
* People who work in the Flathead Valley but prefer a town with a slightly more blue-collar, unpretentious history than Whitefish.
* Those who want immediate access to the Flathead River for fishing, floating, and recreation.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Libby is around $285,000, while in Columbia Falls it is closer to $584,580, a difference of roughly $299,580, making Libby significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Libby averages about $850 per month, compared to roughly $1,003 in Columbia Falls.

Libby's overall cost of living differs from Columbia Falls's. Libby offers lower average salaries compared to Columbia Falls, with a median household income of $45,000 compared to Columbia Falls's $65,313.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Libby is a resilient, isolated town tucked into the far northwest corner of the state. It is defined by its timber heritage and its incredible, uncrowded access to the Kootenai River and Cabinet Mountains, offering a quiet, rugged lifestyle. Columbia Falls has transitioned from a traditional timber town into a vibrant gateway community, balancing its working-class roots with a new influx of breweries, markets, and tourism. It feels less resort-focused than neighboring Whitefish, maintaining a strong, independent local character while serving as the front porch to Glacier National Park.

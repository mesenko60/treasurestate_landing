# East Helena vs Cut Bank, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

East Helena and Cut Bank offer two very different Montana lifestyles. East Helena is a growing residential community in the Helena Valley with 2,264 people, its industrial heritage, its rapid recent growth as a suburb of the capital, and quick access to Canyon Ferry Lake and the Elkhorn Mountains. Cut Bank, with 3,056 residents, is a windy agricultural town on the Hi-Line known for its proximity to the Blackfeet Indian Reservation, its agricultural and energy economy, and the eastern edge of Glacier National Park.

## Who Should Move to East Helena?

* Families looking for a strong local school district and a tight-knit community feel.
* Professionals working in Helena who want a slightly quieter, more residential environment.
* People who appreciate a town with a strong working-class heritage that is actively reinventing itself.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want quick access to Canyon Ferry Lake and the surrounding national forest.

## Who Should Move to Cut Bank?

* Workers in agriculture, energy, or government looking for affordable living on the Hi-Line.
* People seeking a quiet, resilient community with a low cost of living.
* Those who want to live near Glacier National Park without paying resort-town prices.
* Individuals who appreciate the stark, windy beauty of the northern plains.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in East Helena is around $430,196, while in Cut Bank it is closer to $175,000, a difference of roughly $255,196, making Cut Bank significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in East Helena averages about $1,020 per month, compared to roughly $650 in Cut Bank.

East Helena's overall cost of living differs from Cut Bank's. East Helena offers higher average salaries compared to Cut Bank, with a median household income of $53,611 compared to Cut Bank's $52,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

East Helena is transitioning from its historic roots as a smelter town into a rapidly growing, family-friendly suburb of Helena. It offers a quieter, slightly more affordable alternative to the capital city while maintaining its own distinct, blue-collar identity. Cut Bank is a tough, practical town known for its wind and its hardworking residents. It serves as a gateway between the vast agricultural plains of the Hi-Line and the dramatic peaks of the Rocky Mountain Front, offering a quiet, affordable lifestyle.

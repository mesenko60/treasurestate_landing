# Manhattan vs Great Falls, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Manhattan and Great Falls offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Manhattan is a rapidly growing agricultural town in the Gallatin Valley with 2,288 people, its historic role as the Seed Potato Capital of the World, its booming residential growth, and quick access to the Gallatin River and the Bridger Mountains. Great Falls, with 60,403 residents, is a historic river city known for its military base, its rich Lewis and Clark history, and the Missouri River and Giant Springs.

## Who Should Move to Manhattan?

* Professionals working in Bozeman who want a smaller-town feel and are willing to commute.
* Families looking for excellent local schools and a safe, tight-knit community.
* People who appreciate a town that still honors its agricultural heritage despite rapid modernization.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want immediate access to the world-class fishing of the Gallatin River.

## Who Should Move to Great Falls?

* Military families stationed at Malmstrom AFB.
* People seeking affordable urban living in Montana.
* History buffs interested in the Lewis and Clark expedition.
* Those who enjoy river recreation right in town.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Manhattan is around $652,619, while in Great Falls it is closer to $285,000, a difference of roughly $367,619, making Great Falls significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Manhattan averages about $1,700 per month, compared to roughly $950 in Great Falls.

Manhattan's overall cost of living differs from Great Falls's. Manhattan offers higher average salaries compared to Great Falls, with a median household income of $69,671 compared to Great Falls's $55,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Manhattan blends its deep agricultural roots with its new reality as a highly desirable, expensive suburb of Bozeman. It maintains a charming, historic downtown and a strong community identity, even as it experiences significant growth and rising costs. Great Falls is a grounded, affordable city with a strong military presence and a deep connection to the Missouri River.

# Sidney vs Lewistown, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Sidney and Lewistown offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Sidney is a bustling agricultural and energy hub with 6,173 people, its position in the Bakken oil field, its rich farming heritage in the Yellowstone Valley, and quick access to the Yellowstone River and eastern Montana badlands. Lewistown, with just 6,204 residents, is a historic agricultural center known for its charming stone architecture, its position at the geographic center of Montana, and the surrounding island mountain ranges and Spring Creek.

## Who Should Move to Sidney?

* Workers in the energy, agriculture, or transportation sectors looking for strong employment opportunities.
* People seeking a hardworking, practical community with a resilient local economy.
* Those who want affordable housing compared to western Montana, with higher average wages driven by the oil and gas industry.
* Hunters and anglers who appreciate the rugged, uncrowded access to the Yellowstone River and surrounding plains.

## Who Should Move to Lewistown?

* Retirees or remote workers who want a quiet, peaceful lifestyle in a classic Montana setting.
* People looking for affordable housing and a lower overall cost of living.
* Those who prefer a tight-knit, traditional small-town community with a strong agricultural heritage.
* History buffs and outdoor enthusiasts who enjoy uncrowded access to the Snowy, Judith, and Moccasin mountains.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Sidney is around $241,500, while in Lewistown it is closer to $268,126, a difference of roughly $26,626, making Sidney significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Sidney averages about $850 per month, compared to roughly $896 in Lewistown.

Sidney's overall cost of living differs from Lewistown's. Sidney offers higher average salaries compared to Lewistown, with a median household income of $75,200 compared to Lewistown's $44,195.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Sidney is a dynamic, industrious town where the pace of life is closely tied to the energy markets and agricultural seasons. It has a practical, no-nonsense atmosphere, serving as a major economic engine for eastern Montana while maintaining its roots as a tight-knit farming community. Lewistown is quiet, slow-paced, and friendly in that classic Montana way. People wave when they drive by, and life revolves around ranching, community events, and the pristine waters of Big Spring Creek that flow right through the center of town.

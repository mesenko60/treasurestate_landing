# Manhattan vs Helena, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Manhattan and Helena offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Manhattan is a rapidly growing agricultural town in the Gallatin Valley with 2,288 people, its historic role as the Seed Potato Capital of the World, its booming residential growth, and quick access to the Gallatin River and the Bridger Mountains. Helena, with 33,885 residents, is a historic state capital known for its government sector, its gold rush architecture, and Mount Helena and the Missouri River lakes.

## Who Should Move to Manhattan?

* Professionals working in Bozeman who want a smaller-town feel and are willing to commute.
* Families looking for excellent local schools and a safe, tight-knit community.
* People who appreciate a town that still honors its agricultural heritage despite rapid modernization.
* Outdoor enthusiasts who want immediate access to the world-class fishing of the Gallatin River.

## Who Should Move to Helena?

* Government workers and professionals.
* Families seeking a stable, safe community.
* Mountain bikers and hikers who want trails right out their back door.
* People who appreciate historic architecture and a central location.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Manhattan is around $652,619, while in Helena it is closer to $425,000, a difference of roughly $227,619, making Helena significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Manhattan averages about $1,700 per month, compared to roughly $1,100 in Helena.

Manhattan's overall cost of living differs from Helena's. Manhattan offers higher average salaries compared to Helena, with a median household income of $69,671 compared to Helena's $68,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Manhattan blends its deep agricultural roots with its new reality as a highly desirable, expensive suburb of Bozeman. It maintains a charming, historic downtown and a strong community identity, even as it experiences significant growth and rising costs. Helena is a stable, professional town driven by state government, with a quiet downtown and immediate access to mountain trails.

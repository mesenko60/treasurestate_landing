# Polson vs Butte, Montana: Which Town Is Right for You?

Polson and Butte offer two very different Montana lifestyles. Polson is a scenic lakeside community with 5,335 people, its location on the southern shore of Flathead Lake, its cherry orchards, and quick access to Flathead Lake and the Mission Mountains. Butte, with just 34,768 residents, is a historic mining city known for its rich labor history, its stunning historic architecture, and the Continental Divide and surrounding national forests.

## Who Should Move to Polson?

* Retirees and remote workers seeking a beautiful lakeside setting with a slower pace of life.
* Boaters, anglers, and water sports enthusiasts who want immediate access to the largest natural freshwater lake in the West.
* People looking for a more affordable alternative to the resort towns of the northern Flathead Valley.
* Those who appreciate a community with a strong agricultural heritage, particularly its famous cherry harvest.

## Who Should Move to Butte?

* History lovers who appreciate historic homes and architecture.
* People looking for very affordable mountain living.
* Those who want a town with a strong, independent character.
* Engineering students attending Montana Tech.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Housing is where the biggest difference shows up. The median home price in Polson is around $415,000, while in Butte it is closer to $250,000, a difference of roughly $165,000, making Butte significantly more affordable. Rent follows the same pattern: a typical rental in Polson averages about $1,050 per month, compared to roughly $800 in Butte.

Polson's overall cost of living differs from Butte's. Polson offers higher average salaries compared to Butte, with a median household income of $52,500 compared to Butte's $50,000.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Polson offers a relaxed, scenic lifestyle defined by its position on Flathead Lake. It lacks the polished resort feel of towns further north, maintaining a more grounded, agricultural character while still providing spectacular water and mountain access. Butte is fiercely proud of its working-class roots, offering a unique, historic atmosphere that is distinct from the rest of the state.

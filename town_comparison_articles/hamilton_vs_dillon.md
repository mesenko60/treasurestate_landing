# Hamilton vs Dillon: Which Montana Town Is Right for You?

Hamilton and Dillon are both western Montana towns with strong outdoor identities and a quiet, community-centered way of life, but they offer very different landscapes and experiences. Hamilton, the Banana Belt of Montana in the Bitterroot Valley, is a town of 4,659 people known for its unusually mild and sunny climate, the Bitterroot River, and direct access to the Bitterroot National Forest. Dillon, Montana's Hometown in the Beaverhead Valley, is a high-desert ranching and university town of 3,880 people, home to the University of Montana Western and surrounded by some of the finest fly fishing rivers in southwest Montana.

## Who Should Move to Hamilton?

- Fly fishers and hikers who want the Bitterroot River and the Bitterroot National Forest's 47 trailheads and 55 lakes within easy reach
- Remote workers, retirees, or families who want a mild, sunny climate in a beautiful valley setting with more amenities than most small Montana towns
- People drawn to a vibrant small-town arts and community scene in one of western Montana's most scenic valleys
- Those who want a wide variety of outdoor recreation options, from fishing and hiking to hot springs and wilderness access

## Who Should Move to Dillon?

- Fly fishers who want the Beaverhead, Big Hole, and Ruby rivers, among the most storied trout streams in Montana, close at hand
- Students or families connected to the University of Montana Western, which gives Dillon a youthful energy unusual for a town of its size
- People looking for affordable housing and a quiet, traditional small-town lifestyle in a wide-open high-desert setting
- History enthusiasts interested in Lewis and Clark country and the ranching heritage of the Beaverhead Valley

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Hamilton is significantly more expensive. The typical home value is $516,033 compared to $375,585 in Dillon, a difference of about $140,000. Rent is similar, with Hamilton at $958 per month and Dillon at $821. Both towns have nearly identical median household incomes, with Hamilton at $52,917 and Dillon at $51,458. Hamilton's higher home prices reflect its popularity as a destination for remote workers and retirees who have discovered the Bitterroot Valley. Both towns have their nearest airports at a significant distance, with Hamilton's in Missoula at 56 miles and Dillon's in Butte at 62 miles.

## Lifestyle and Vibe

Hamilton and Dillon share a relaxed, community-centered pace, but their landscapes and personalities are distinct. Hamilton sits in a wide, green valley flanked by the Bitterroot Range to the west and the Sapphire Mountains to the east, with a mild climate that earns it the Banana Belt nickname. Dillon sits in a drier, more open high-desert valley at over 5,000 feet, with a slower, more traditional feel shaped by ranching and the university. Hamilton has 194 recreational sites within 30 miles to Dillon's 71, but Dillon's rivers are world-class and its 5 state parks and 2 hot springs give it more variety than its size might suggest.

# Granite Mountain Mining Company Office

The Granite Mountain Mining Company Office, built around 1885, was one of the first structures to grace the rocky slopes of Granite, Montana. It stood as the nerve center for an operation that would eventually pull over twenty-one million dollars in silver from the earth. The men who worked the Ruby Shaft and the nearby mills came here to collect their pay, likely feeling the weight of the silver coins in their pockets before heading down to the saloons on Main Street. 

Today, all that remains of this once-bustling office is the brick vault, standing alone against the elements. It is a quiet monument to the wealth that flowed through its iron doors and the suddenness with which it all stopped when the silver market crashed in 1893. The vault held the payroll for a town of three thousand souls, and now it holds nothing but the wind and the memories of a time when Granite was the Silver Queen of Montana.

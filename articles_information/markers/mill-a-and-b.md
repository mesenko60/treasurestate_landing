# Mill A and B

The Granite Mountain Mining Company built two massive structures, known simply as Mill A and Mill B, right next to the Ruby Shaft. Inside these buildings, seventy stamp mills worked six days a week, crushing the silver ore with a deafening roar that could be heard for miles. The process was a messy one, using mercury amalgamation to separate the precious metal from the base rock, and it filled the air with a thick, choking dust that settled over everything in the camp.

By 1889, the Ruby Shaft was pulling up so much ore that even these seventy stamps couldn't keep up, forcing the company to build a third mill down in Rumsey. The men who worked in these mills endured the noise and the dust for their daily wage, knowing that the silver they crushed was making men in St. Louis very rich. When the silver crash of 1893 hit, the stamps finally fell silent, leaving the mills to slowly rot away on the mountainside.

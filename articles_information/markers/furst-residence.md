# Furst Residence

When Anaconda's smelter works expanded into the twentieth century, the town began to outgrow its original perimeters, and the City Council annexed the First Western Addition in 1916. John Furst, a prominent Willow Creek rancher and dairy farmer, purchased two adjoining lots for eight hundred and seventy-five dollars and built this home during the winter of 1917-1918. Furst had come to the United States from Alsace-Lorraine, France, in 1870, learned the trade of shoe cobbling, and eventually made his way to Butte in 1878 before turning to ranching and real estate.

This beautifully maintained residence is an outstanding example of the Craftsman Bungalow, a style that emerged in California and was widely promoted in the first decades of the twentieth century. Square tapered columns, wide bracketed eaves with exposed rafter ends, and a front-gabled dormer are defining stylistic elements of this charming home. After Furst died in 1927, his wife, Theresa, moved into town from the ranch and made her home here until 1949, leaving behind a legacy of hard work and fine craftsmanship.

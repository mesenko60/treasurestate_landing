# Grasshopper Creek

In the midsummer heat of 1862, a group of "Pikes Peakers" led by John White decided to follow a nameless creek leading westward from the Jefferson River. They found the banks swarming with grasshoppers, so they named the water Grasshopper Creek, and while panning the gravels, they hit upon a bonanza that would change the face of the territory forever. The sound of "Eureka!" echoed through the West, setting off the first major gold rush to what is now Montana.

By the fall of that year, five hundred people had descended upon the creek, staking claims and pulling an estimated seven hundred thousand dollars' worth of gold from the ground before winter froze them out. The camp that sprouted three miles upstream was named Bannack, after a local Indian tribe, and it quickly became the first territorial capital of Montana. Today, the creek still flows quietly past the ghost town, holding the memory of the men who came seeking their fortunes in the dirt.

# Kootenai River Waterfowl Home and Highway

**By Mark Twain**

*Libby, Lincoln County, Montana, 1997*

Look and listen for the array of waterfowl who call Kootenai River their home and highway. Mallard ducks, Canadian geese, and common mergansers are year long residents, nesting along the water's edge. Watch for waterfowl close to the river's edge where food is abundant, and where a duck might find a quiet spot to raise a family without being disturbed by the noise of the world.

The arrival of cold weather in the fall brings hundreds of additional birds from Canada, including the common golden eye. Once the lakes and streams freeze, many birds spend the winter feeding on the open waters of the Kootenai River. It is a bustling metropolis of feathers and beaks, a place where a bird can find a meal and a friend, and perhaps a little peace and quiet before the long journey north in the spring.

The river is a highway for these travelers, a ribbon of water that guides them on their way. It is a place of beauty and wonder, a place where a man might sit on the bank and watch the birds fly by, and think about the journey they are on, and the journey he is on, and the journey we are all on, together.

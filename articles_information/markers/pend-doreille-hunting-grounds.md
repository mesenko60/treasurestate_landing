# Pend d'Oreille Hunting Grounds

**By Mark Twain**

*Thompson Falls, Sanders County, Montana, 1809*

For thousands of years, the Olispé, or Pend d'Oreille people, had the good sense to use this valley as a hunting and fishing ground, mostly because it was full of things worth hunting and fishing. They called the area Seqeylqm, which is Salish for the sound of water falling over a drop, a name that is both accurate and entirely lacking in the self-importance that usually accompanies the naming of towns.

According to their own stories, the Salish-speaking people used to be one big happy family until they ran out of food, at which point they did the sensible thing and split up, spreading out across western Montana, Idaho, and Washington. The Clark Fork Valley became their main highway, a place to visit relatives and friends, traveling by foot and birch-bark canoe long before anyone thought to ruin the scenery with a railroad or an automobile.

David Thompson, who never met a river he didn't want to put on a map, noticed how popular the route was and decided it was the perfect place to set up a store. He and the Hudson's Bay Company built Saleesh House nearby in 1809, figuring that if the Indians were going to travel through anyway, they might as well stop and buy something. The trading post did a fine business until the 1820s, when the company closed it down, presumably because they had finally run out of things to sell.

# Palace Bar

**By Editor**

*Whitefish, Flathead County, Montana, November 2020*

There is a certain kind of honesty in a building that has never pretended to be anything but what it is. The structure at 123 Central Avenue in Whitefish has, by all reliable accounts, been dedicated to the distribution of alcohol since the day the first nail was driven. From 1915 to 1919, it served as the home of the Kalispell Malting and Brewing Company, a noble enterprise that was rudely interrupted by the great national experiment in enforced sobriety. For most of its existence since then, it has been known simply as the Palace Bar, a typical Western watering hole where a man could reliably find a drink, a conversation, and occasionally, a mouse race.

In its early days, local lore insists the building featured a brothel on the top floor, an arrangement that surely saved the patrons a great deal of walking in the snow. That era has long since passed into the respectable haze of history, and the Palace has seen a succession of distinguished owners, including a city councilman and an attorney who eventually ascended to the federal bench—proving that a good saloon is an excellent foundation for a career in public service. The decor has never been pretentious, but the Palace boasts a magnificent old wood backbar that is said to have traveled up the Missouri River by steamer to Fort Benton, overland by wagon to Flathead Lake, and up the lake to old Demersville, the valley's original settlement. In more recent times, the establishment became famous for auctioning off live mice and racing them along one-inch ropes stretched over an eight-foot course, with the winning mouse and owner photographed together for posterity. It is exactly the sort of place that makes a town worth visiting.

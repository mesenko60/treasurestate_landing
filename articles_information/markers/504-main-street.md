# 504 Main Street

**By Editor**

*Anaconda, Deer Lodge County, Montana, August 2022*

When grocer Albert MacCallum decided to build a house in 1895, he didn't just want a roof over his head; he wanted a statement of arrival in a town that was busy turning copper into cash. The result was an eclectic piece of architecture that threw together the Queen Anne and French Second Empire styles with the enthusiasm of a man who couldn't decide which he liked better, so he bought both. The house at 504 Main Street features an octagonal tower capped with an intricate ornamental cresting on its dome, a partial Mansard roof at the northeast corner, and a front window with unusual stone and brick surrounds that perfectly reflect the flamboyant tastes of the late Victorian era. It even retains its original, charming multi-paned cottage window, a detail that survived the decades while the town around it boomed and busted with the price of ore.

MacCallum sold the place in 1904 to Dennis Roach, a miner, politician, and businessman who understood that the real money in a mining town wasn't always in the ground. The firm of Roach and Smith offered the citizens of Anaconda everything from billiard parlors and confectionery to flowers, fishing tackle, sporting goods, cigars, and sundries. It was one of the town's longest-established businesses, and Roach himself served as a county commissioner and a representative in the Montana legislature. After his death in 1925, his widow Maude remained in the house for more than thirty years, and their daughter, Katherine "Kash" Felt, a prominent and well-loved Anaconda matron, eventually converted the residence into classrooms where she taught kindergarten. The family held onto the property well into the twenty-first century, proving that while copper kings come and go, a good piece of real estate on Main Street is forever.
